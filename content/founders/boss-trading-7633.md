# Kevin Gong and 5-Minute Futures: Inside Skool's 7,300-Member Algo Trading Community

5-Minute Futures is a free Skool community with roughly 7,331 members, aimed squarely at quants and algorithmic futures traders rather than general retail day traders. It's a narrower niche than most trading communities on the platform, and the person running it, Kevin Gong, has built his public identity around exactly that specialization.

## Who is Kevin Gong

Kevin Gong is listed as the creator of 5-Minute Futures directly on the community's Skool about page, where he appears with a verified badge tying the group to TradeStation futures account holders ([5-Minute Futures on Skool](https://www.skool.com/boss-trading-7633/about)). His LinkedIn profile lists his current focus as "5 Minute Futures" by name, matching the community he runs ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kevinygong/)). His Skool member profile is public under the handle @kevingong ([Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@kevingong)).

That combination, a named LinkedIn presence tied directly to the community, plus a platform-verified badge from TradeStation, is a more concrete identity trail than most trading-focused Skool groups offer, many of which run entirely on pseudonyms or first names only.

## What members actually get

The community is free to join, positioned specifically "for quants and algorithmic futures traders" rather than beginners. That framing, combined with the TradeStation verification badge on the group itself, signals the content leans toward automated and systematic futures trading rather than discretionary chart-reading. Members join to trade ideas, review strategies, and get exposure to the kind of algorithmic approaches that Kevin's own account holder verification implies he trades personally.

Niche framing like this tends to filter membership more tightly than a generic "learn to trade" community would. Someone landing in 5-Minute Futures already knows the difference between discretionary and algorithmic trading, which changes what the group needs to deliver to keep people engaged.

## Why it's grown

A free, narrowly-targeted community built around a specific broker's account holders and a specific trading style (algorithmic futures) doesn't scale the way a broad "trading for beginners" group does, but it doesn't need to. At just over 7,300 members, 5-Minute Futures has grown by staying legible to exactly the audience it named in its own description, quants who already trade futures algorithmically, rather than trying to be everything to everyone.

The verified-badge structure also does real work here: in a space full of anonymous trading gurus promising overnight riches, a community that ties itself to a specific broker relationship and a real, named LinkedIn profile has a built-in credibility signal that's harder to fake than a flashy Instagram trading screenshot.
