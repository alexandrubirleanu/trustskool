# David Scheuer and Academia AMZ: The Miami Bartender Who Became an Amazon Millionaire

Academia AMZ is a free, Spanish-language Skool community aimed at people who want to build an Amazon business generating $10,000 or more a month, without the years of trial and error the founder himself went through first. That founder, David Scheuer, known online as "Ecom Dave," has a career-change story that's been picked up by mainstream lifestyle outlets, not just niche e-commerce blogs.

## Who is David Scheuer

Scheuer studied business at Florida International University in Miami, and before finding success on Amazon, worked as a bartender in the city, pulling shifts five or six nights a week until six in the morning for an after-tax income of a little more than $50,000 a year ([Men's Journal profile](https://www.mensjournal.com/entertainment/meet-e-commerce-expert-and-the-founder-of-amazon-coaching-program-david-scheuer-mens-journal)). His entry into e-commerce reportedly started with a Google search for "earn money online" that led him to a Gary Vaynerchuk video on retail arbitrage, after which he spent more than 900 hours studying Amazon FBA before building his own selling business ([Maxim profile](https://www.maxim.com/partner/david-ecom-dave-scheuer-on-achieving-your-dreams/)).

That research and execution turned into what both outlets describe as self-made millionaire status by age 30, a milestone Men's Journal and Maxim both frame as notable given he built the business as a first-generation immigrant starting from a service-industry income. He now runs The AMZ Academy, also branded Academia AMZ for Spanish-speaking students, teaching the same Amazon FBA and product-sourcing skills he taught himself.

## What members actually get

Inside the free Skool community, the pitch is built around the three obstacles Scheuer says he had to solve himself when starting out: finding profitable products, finding and negotiating with reliable suppliers, and getting new listings to rank on Amazon's first page. Community posts shared by members describe success stories and encouragement around overcoming fear of failure, suggesting an active peer-support culture layered on top of Scheuer's own strategy content.

As a free community in Spanish, it functions as an accessible entry point for Latin American and Spanish-speaking audiences into Amazon FBA education, a niche with fewer well-established Spanish-language teachers than the English-language Amazon coaching space.

## Why it's grown

Scheuer's credibility rests on a documented before-and-after story: mainstream outlets like Men's Journal and Maxim, not just his own marketing, have covered his path from a Miami bartending income to self-made millionaire status through Amazon, which gives his teaching a level of third-party validation many e-commerce coaches lack. That kind of press coverage tends to travel well into paid social and search traffic, feeding a free community that then funnels into his paid AMZ Academy coaching.

Offering the content in Spanish also opens up a large, comparatively underserved audience segment relative to the crowded English-language Amazon FBA coaching market.
