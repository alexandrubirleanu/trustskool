# Kane Kallaway and WavyWorld: How a Billion Views Built a 50,000-Member Skool

WavyWorld describes itself simply as "a digital world designed to help you make better videos," and over 50,000 people have joined for free to learn exactly that: video ideas, formats, scripting, storytelling, editing, and the underlying systems behind a working content machine, rather than one-off tips.

## Who is Kane Kallaway

Kallaway is a content creator and founder who has built his public reputation around short-form video at genuinely large scale, with more than 1 billion cumulative views and over 550,000 followers across platforms, working primarily in tech, AI, and cult-brand content ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kanekallaway/)). He's the founder of Wavy Labs, described as a holding company incubating ventures at the intersection of culture, lifestyle, and emerging technology, and separately leads sandcastles.ai, a content storytelling agent project that applies his content instincts to AI tooling.

Kallaway has discussed his approach directly on the "Uploading..." podcast, in an episode framed around his short-form video content secrets, where he broke down concepts like curiosity loops, strategic hook placement, and platform-specific pacing that inform how he teaches inside WavyWorld ([Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/fr/podcast/rhythm-hooks-and-a-billion-views-kane-kallaways/id1727260996?i=1000666890786)). He also writes a newsletter called Blueprint that extends the same thinking in written form.

## What members actually get

WavyWorld's free tier is built around bite-sized lessons rather than long-form courses, covering the mechanics of building videos that hold attention: how to structure a hook, when to break a scene, how to think about editing rhythm, and how the same idea needs to be reshaped for different platforms rather than reposted as-is. Video teardowns, where Kallaway or contributors break down why a specific piece of content worked, are a recurring format inside the community.

The emphasis throughout is on systems and repeatable process rather than chasing a single viral moment, a philosophy that lines up with what Kallaway has described publicly about his own hit rate: by his own account on the "Uploading..." podcast, roughly half of what he makes clears 100,000 views with top-tier engagement, and about one in four pieces crosses a million.

## Why it's grown

WavyWorld benefits from the same dynamic that drives a lot of large creator-led Skool communities: Kallaway didn't need to build an audience to justify the group, he already had one, built over roughly two years of consistent short-form output across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, and YouTube. The Skool community became a natural next step for people who were already watching his videos and wanted the underlying process rather than another single tutorial.

Because his content itself operates as the ongoing top-of-funnel, WavyWorld doesn't have to rely on paid acquisition to keep growing, the same skill set he teaches inside the community is the mechanism bringing new members to it.
