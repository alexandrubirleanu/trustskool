# Sarah Gibson and VHM Weight Loss-Perimenopause: A Clinician's Free Skool Community for Hormone Health

VHM Weight Loss-Perimenopause is a free Skool community of roughly 3,602 women focused on hormone balance and weight loss during perimenopause and menopause. It's run by Sarah Gibson, whose credentials appear directly in her professional title: MPAS, HWC.

## Who is Sarah Gibson

Gibson is listed on LinkedIn as "Sarah Gibson MPAS, HWC, Vitality Health Matrix," credentials indicating a Master of Physician Assistant Studies alongside a Health and Wellness Coach certification ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sarah-gibson-coaching/)). She is the CEO and founder of Vitality Health Matrix, a company built around what it calls the Matrix Method, an integrative approach combining traditional and functional medicine with DNA testing and personalized nutrition, according to the company's own site and social presence ([vitalityhealthmatrix.com](https://vitalityhealthmatrix.com/); [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/vitalityhealthmatrix/)). Gibson co-founded the company alongside Abbey Walsh, MS, CNS, LDN, a clinical nutrition specialist. The company's own about page states the pair have worked with more than 1,800 women over 40 through their combined coaching and medical weight-loss programs, and the practice sells its own supplement and testing products directly through a companion storefront ([shop.vitalityhealthmatrix.com](https://shop.vitalityhealthmatrix.com/collections/weight-loss)).

## What members actually get

The free Skool community offers weekly Q&A calls with Gibson and Walsh directly, plus discussion around labs, DNA testing, nutrition, fitness, and GLP-1 coaching, all framed around the specific hormonal shifts of perimenopause and menopause rather than general weight-loss advice. It functions as the entry point into Vitality Health Matrix's paid medical weight-loss programs, which the company markets specifically to hormonal women over 35, and members can move from free discussion straight into the same lab-and-DNA-testing pipeline the paid Matrix Method program is built around.

## Why it's grown

Perimenopause and menopause are underserved in a lot of conventional weight-loss content, which tends to default to generic calorie-deficit advice that doesn't account for hormonal shifts. Gibson's clinical credentials, an MPAS alongside a co-founder who's a licensed clinical nutritionist, give the community a level of medical grounding that's uncommon in a space otherwise dominated by wellness influencers without formal training. Free, founder-led Q&A calls give members direct access to that clinical expertise before they ever consider the company's paid medical weight-loss programs, a meaningfully different structure than communities where the free tier is just generic content with no access to the actual practitioner. A claimed track record in the thousands of women, rather than a handful of testimonials, also gives prospective members a scale of prior results to weigh before they book the paid program.
