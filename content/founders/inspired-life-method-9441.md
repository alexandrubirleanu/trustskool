# Lewis Huckstep and Conscious Community: From Three Gyms to Inner-Work Coaching

Conscious Community is a Skool community of just over 4,000 members with a promise aimed squarely at high achievers: break the patterns, heal the wounds, and rewire the identity that's quietly sabotaging relationships, business, and wealth. It's built around the Inspired Life Method, a framework created by its founder, Lewis Huckstep.

## Who is Lewis Huckstep

Lewis Huckstep's path into coaching started far from personal development. He was contracted with professional rugby league teams from age 12 to 18, then moved into business at 18, eventually owning three gyms by the time he was 22. From the outside that looked like success, but by his own account he was burning himself out, neglecting his own needs, and avoiding the inner work he'd later build a business around ([lewishuckstep.com](https://www.lewishuckstep.com/)).

He eventually sold two of the three gyms and restructured the third so it ran without him, reaching a point where it generated $2,000 to $3,000 a week for roughly an hour of his time. That freed him to build a coaching business focused on what he calls Healing and Expanding Consciousness, and he's since coached more than 10,000 clients while investing over $500,000 in his own personal and professional development ([success stories page](https://www.lewishuckstep.com/success-stories)). He also hosts a podcast, "The Lewis Huckstep Experience," where he's interviewed figures like Kerwin Rae, Dr. John Demartini, Sabri Suby, and Jack Delosa, and he's published as an author on Audible ([Audible author page](https://www.audible.com/author/Lewis-Huckstep/B0DK4QLW5V)).

## What members actually get

Inside Conscious Community, the offer centers on the Inspired Life Method itself, Huckstep's framework for helping growth-minded people pursue ambitious goals without sacrificing relationships or personal wellbeing along the way. Members get weekly live coaching and onboarding calls, positioning the community less as a passive content library and more as an ongoing group coaching relationship built around identity work and self-mastery.

The community's own description frames the work as breaking patterns and healing wounds that quietly sabotage relationships, business, and wealth, language that points to inner-work modalities rather than tactical business advice, even though many members are entrepreneurs applying the work to business decisions.

## Why it's grown

Huckstep's growth as a coach rests heavily on the contrast between his outwardly successful gym-owner years and the burnout he says was happening underneath. That kind of "looked successful, wasn't okay" narrative resonates strongly with an audience of entrepreneurs and high achievers who recognize the same pattern in themselves, and it's a more specific, credible hook than generic mindset coaching. Having actually built and exited real businesses, three gyms, rather than only ever selling coaching, also gives him a results-based credibility that pure personal-development coaches without an operating business background often lack.

His podcast interviews with established names in the broader business-and-mindset space also function as an ongoing credibility signal for the community, since prospective members can hear him hold his own alongside guests who already have their own large followings before ever joining Conscious Community themselves.
