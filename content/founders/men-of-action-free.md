# Michael Sartain and the Off-Market Social System: Inside Skool's 7,000-Member Social Dynamics Community

The Off-Market Social System is a 7,094-member Skool community built around forging an "elite social circle" in any city — networking playbooks, event strategies, and status-building tactics aimed at men who want to meet high-status friends and, per the community's own framing, attractive women [Skool](https://www.skool.com/men-of-action-free/off-market-dating-system).

## Who runs it

This free community operates under the Men of Action (MOA) brand, founded by Michael Sartain in 2019 [Grit Daily](https://gritdaily.com/michael-sartain-on-his-purpose-and-vision-for-moa/). Sartain was born and raised in Dallas, Texas, earned a BBA in Management Information Systems from the University of Texas at Austin, and then served roughly seven years in the U.S. Air Force as a KC-135 navigator, reaching the rank of Captain [Medium/Authority Magazine](https://medium.com/authority-magazine/michael-sartain-of-men-of-action-mentoring-on-how-to-navigate-our-complicated-modern-world-to-find-fec9260ba470). After leaving the military amid the dot-com bust, he moved into nightlife work, eventually managing a gentlemen's club in Austin before relocating to Las Vegas, where he built a business hosting nightlife events and later transitioned into coaching men on social skills, networking, and status-building — the foundation of what became Men of Action [ShoutoutLA](https://shoutoutla.com/meet-michael-sartain-ceo-and-founder-of-men-of-action-mentoring/).

Day-to-day, the free Skool community itself is administered under a "Moa Admin" account tied to team member Miguel Colon [Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@miguel-colon-2766), which is typical for larger coaching brands where the founder remains the face and content driver while staff run community operations.

## What members actually get

The Off-Market Social System teaches networking playbooks and a "social circle manifesto" for building relationships with high-status people in a new city, along with a dedicated book club and periodic Q&A sessions that have featured notable guests, including Dan Bilzerian, according to the community's own listing. One-on-one coaching calls are also available for members wanting individualized guidance on status-building and social strategy. As a free tier of the broader MOA ecosystem, it functions as an entry point that leads into MOA's more intensive, paid mentoring programs.

## Why it's grown

Sartain's own background — military service, nightlife management, self-made wealth in Las Vegas — gives him a real, lived version of the "high-status social access" story that the community sells, rather than a purely theoretical one, and MOA has been an established brand in the men's social-development space since 2019, well before this specific Skool community launched. Content niches around social dynamics, networking, and dating draw consistent search and word-of-mouth interest, and offering a genuinely free entry tier (rather than a paid one) removes the biggest barrier to trying the material, which likely explains why this specific community has scaled to over 7,000 members even as a secondary, free arm of a larger paid brand.

**A note on pricing:** the source data for this profile listed the community as free. The current Skool about page shows a $5/month price rather than $0 — a small but real difference worth confirming before joining.
