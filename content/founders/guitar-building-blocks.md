# Taylor Dean Barnett and Guitar Building Blocks: The Muscle Memory Guitar Method on Skool

Guitar Building Blocks is a free Skool community of roughly 2,600 members organized around a single teaching claim: that guitar comes down to seven essential techniques, and once a student drills them into muscle memory, any song becomes playable. The method behind it was built by Taylor Dean Barnett, a guitar teacher whose own beginnings with the instrument were rockier than most of his students would guess.

## Who is Taylor Dean Barnett

Barnett's musical start was discouraging by any measure. A childhood piano teacher reportedly told his parents he had no musical talent at all, and when a friend later talked him into trying guitar, the instrument sat untouched for over a year before a more patient teacher showed him how the instrument actually worked ([New York Business Now profile](https://newyorkbusinessnow.com/taylor/)). That profile describes how, in 2012, Barnett was hospitalized twice in a single month and diagnosed with Crohn's disease, going through ongoing IV treatments and a temporary stomach drain during that period.

It was during that stretch of his life that he founded MusicMatchr, his music education venture, initially getting exposure through a LivingSocial daily-deal promotion for private guitar lessons. Over roughly a decade of private teaching, he estimates he delivered more than 10,000 individual guitar lessons, which became the raw material for the technique-based system he eventually turned into an online course.

## What members actually get

The core offer, both in the free Skool community and Barnett's paid programs at MuscleMemoryGuitar.com, is built around his "muscle memory" framework: seven techniques he argues underlie every style of guitar playing, taught in a sequence designed to build physical habit rather than isolated theory. Barnett's own marketing claims students learn chord changes and technique up to 94% faster than with conventional lesson structures, a figure drawn from his own program material rather than an independent study.

Inside the community, that translates to structured lessons plus access to a group of other guitar students working through the same building-block sequence, positioned as an alternative to scattered YouTube tutorials or generic in-person lessons that don't follow a defined technique ladder.

## Why it's grown

The credibility behind Guitar Building Blocks rests less on marketing and more on volume: ten years and 10,000-plus private lessons is a large enough sample that Barnett's technique choices have been stress-tested against a wide range of real beginners, not just a handful of case studies. That kind of repetition-driven curriculum design tends to travel well once it moves online, since the same sequencing that worked one student at a time in person can be repackaged as a structured course.

His personal story, building a teaching business through a serious chronic illness diagnosis, also gives the brand a resilience narrative that resonates with adult learners who've tried and abandoned guitar before, the same audience segment his free-guide funnel at MuscleMemoryGuitar.com is built to capture before pointing people toward the Skool community and paid course.
