# Sophia Eliana Schulte-Wintrop and UGC Glow Up: Inside Skool's 5,600-Member German UGC Challenge

UGC Glow Up is a free Skool community of roughly 5,673 members built around a 30-day challenge for German-speaking UGC creators, billed as the first challenge of its kind in Germany. It's run by Sophia Eliana Schulte-Wintrop, who left university to build her own content business before turning her process into a teaching brand.

## Who is Sophia Eliana Schulte-Wintrop

Sophia Eliana Schulte-Wintrop is credited by name as the sole creator of UGC Glow Up on the community's Skool about page, which describes the group as a free space for women who want to unlock their full potential through UGC ([UGC Glow Up on Skool](https://www.skool.com/ugc30-challenge-9769/about)). She maintains a public Instagram account, @ugc.sophiaeliana, positioned around her identity as a UGC creator and coach ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/ugc.sophiaeliana/)), and posts UGC content under variations of her name across TikTok as well.

According to her own public bio, she dropped out of university roughly two years before building her current business, choosing self-employment instead, and has since worked with more than 350 brands, including recognizable names like ghd and Treatwell, while helping several thousand women develop their own UGC skills.

## What members actually get

UGC Glow Up is free to join and structured around a 30-day challenge format rather than a standard ongoing curriculum, aimed at helping members improve their UGC skills daily over a defined sprint rather than an open-ended course. The challenge model gives members a clear, time-boxed goal, useful for beginners who might otherwise stall out in a less structured community, and reflects Schulte-Wintrop's own framing of UGC as a skill built through consistent daily practice rather than a one-time course purchase.

Being marketed specifically as Germany's first UGC challenge also gives the community a distinct positioning in an increasingly crowded UGC-education space, differentiating it from broader, less time-bound UGC communities aimed at the same DACH-region audience.

## Why it's grown

At 5,673 members and free to join, UGC Glow Up benefits from Schulte-Wintrop's own visible brand portfolio, work with more than 350 named companies is a concrete, checkable claim that carries real weight with an audience trying to evaluate whether UGC work can actually pay real brand deals. The challenge structure also naturally encourages sharing and public participation, since members working through a 30-day sprint together tend to post progress updates, which can pull in new members who see the challenge happening in real time on social platforms.

Her personal story, leaving university to build the business from nothing, also gives the community relatable stakes for an audience of women considering whether to pursue UGC as a genuine income path rather than a side hobby.
