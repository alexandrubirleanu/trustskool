# Roger C and The Honored Few: The Forearm and Neck Specialist Building a Niche Skool Community

The Honored Few is a $25-a-month Skool community with roughly 4,700 members, built around one of the narrowest niches you'll find on the platform: natural hand, forearm, and neck hypertrophy. The pitch is specific and almost comically literal, add measurable centimeters of thickness to those exact body parts in about 30 days, and it's run by a creator who goes by Roger C.

## Who is Roger C

Roger C posts training content under the handle RelentlessRoger on YouTube and TikTok, where he shares forearm and neck routines, including videos on Belgian wrist curls, cable pronation work, and fat-grip curling aimed at the same niche his Skool community teaches ([RelentlessRoger on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@RelentlessRoger); [TikTok forearm routine, @focusrelentless](https://www.tiktok.com/@focusrelentless/video/7488390250000059650); [TikTok neck routine, @focusrelentless](https://www.tiktok.com/@focusrelentless/video/7470194550703672598)). His Skool profile and the community's own about page brand him as "The #1 Expert on Natural Hand, Forearm, Neck Hypertrophy," and members of the community post progress questions directly to him, including threads asking what his own biceps or forearm routine looks like ([The Honored Few discussion thread](https://www.skool.com/the-honored-few/what-does-roger-do-for-his-biceps-rogerc-pls-tell-us)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own about page, the offer centers on a structured 30-day approach to growing hands, forearms, and neck, muscle groups that get little attention in mainstream bodybuilding programming, which tends to focus on chest, arms, back, and legs. Community threads cover topics like tracking forearm circumference gains, neck training progressions, and members comparing notes on measurable results over weeks and months.

## Why it's grown

What makes The Honored Few work as a paid community is the specificity of the promise. Most fitness content competes on broad claims like "get bigger" or "get leaner," which puts creators up against enormous, well-funded competition. Roger C instead staked out one of the few corners of physique training that's almost entirely unaddressed elsewhere, a strong grip and a visibly thick neck are hard to fake and hard to find good instruction for, which gives a $25 monthly community real differentiation even at a relatively modest size compared to general fitness groups on the platform.
