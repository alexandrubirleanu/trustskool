# Scott English and Combat Fitness: The Ex-Army Medic's Tactical Training Community on Skool

Combat Fitness is a free Skool community built around a simple thesis: military-based training methods produce outsized life transformations for civilians too. With over 14,000 people trained by its own count and a private team hub running on Skool, the community carries the tagline "Motivation. Discipline. Fitness." Behind it is Scott English, whose own path ran through the military before he ever became a coach.

## Who is Scott English

According to Combat Fitness's own site, Scott is a former Army Medic who later became a Navy Diver, and has separately completed Ironman triathlons ([Combat Fitness homepage](https://www.combatfitness.co/home)). He founded Combat Fitness five years ago and, per that same site, has helped improve the fitness of more than 15,000 athletes globally since then. The business is explicit that it has no official partnership with any military branch, but describes its programs as used by people employed by or preparing for demanding roles including Navy SEAL BUD/S, Ranger School, Special Forces Assessment and Selection, and Navy Dive School, alongside civilians like entrepreneurs, corporate executives, doctors, and lawyers.

## What members actually get

The Skool community functions as a hub layered on top of Combat Fitness's broader training ecosystem, which includes the flagship "ONE" program described as an all-in-one tactical fitness training platform, priced around $49 a month for full access. The pitch throughout is training built for "where military optimization philosophies meet real world health & fitness," per the brand's own language, rather than generic gym programming repackaged with military branding.

English also hosts a podcast, "Do Hard Things: The Combat Fitness Podcast," extending the brand's messaging beyond the training programs themselves into longer-form content about discipline and mental toughness, and the brand maintains an active Instagram presence with over 200,000 followers reinforcing the same themes.

## Why it's grown

Combat Fitness's growth traces back to a fairly specific niche: people who want military-style structure and standards without necessarily being in the military, plus actual military-adjacent professionals training for real selections. English's own dual background, medic first, then diver, then endurance athlete, gives the brand's messaging a lived-experience foundation rather than a marketing team's approximation of "tactical" fitness.

The "14,000+ Trained" framing in the community description and the "15,000 athletes" figure on the main site both point to a program that has scaled past a small following into something closer to a full training brand, with the free Skool community likely functioning as a top-of-funnel layer feeding into the paid ONE platform and any coaching English offers directly.
