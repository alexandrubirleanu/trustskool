# Gérard Di-Maccio and Di-Maccio Art Academy: Inside Skool's Guinness-Record Painter's Drawing Community

Di-Maccio Art Academy is a $7-a-month Skool community with just over 5,200 members, built around beginner-level drawing lessons taught directly by its founder. The community's own pitch leans on two credentials: over 1.2 million followers and the title of "Creator of the Largest Painting in the World." Both point to the same person: Gérard Di-Maccio.

## Who is Gérard Di-Maccio

Di-Maccio is a French painter born in 1938, recognized as the leader of the Mouvement Visionnaire, or Visionary Movement, in French art ([ArtMajeur biography](https://www.artmajeur.com/gerard-di-maccio)). Before painting full-time, he taught at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris and wrote a thesis on morphopsychology, the study of reading personality traits from facial structure, an academic background that echoes in the anatomical precision critics associate with his later work. He moved into painting as a full-time career in the late 1970s and has since exhibited across Europe, the United States, North Africa, and Asia.

His best-known achievement dates to 1994-1997, when he painted a single oil canvas measuring 9 meters high by 27 meters wide, a project that took him roughly three years to finish. Guinness World Records officially certified it as the largest professional oil painting by a single artist at a ceremony held on November 14, 2024 ([@Press coverage](https://en.atpress.com/news/416496)). The canvas is permanently housed at the Di-Maccio Art Museum, "The Forest of Taiyo," in Niikappu-cho, Hokkaido, a converted former elementary school that opened in 2010 and now holds more than 200 of his works, displayed with mirrored walls and a recurring sound-and-light program.

## What members actually get

The Skool description promises "in-depth beginner drawing lessons," positioning the community as an entry point into technique rather than a gallery of finished visionary paintings. At $7 a month, the price sits closer to a hobbyist subscription than a professional mastermind, which fits an offer built around fundamentals for people who are just starting to draw rather than working artists chasing a specific style.

## Why it's grown

The pitch works because the credibility behind it is externally verifiable and unusually rare. A Guinness World Record is a specific, checkable claim rather than a vague "master artist" label, and it's paired with a following built over decades of international gallery exhibitions rather than an audience assembled inside Skool itself. For a beginner drawing student, learning from someone whose single painting has its own dedicated museum on the other side of the world is a distinct selling point that's hard for competing art-education communities to replicate, regardless of how solid their own curriculum might be.
