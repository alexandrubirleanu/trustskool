# Jorge Ruvalcaba and Design Turbo IA: Inside Skool's 10,500-Member AI Design Community

Design Turbo IA has grown to more than 10,500 members on Skool with a pitch aimed squarely at Spanish-speaking creatives: go from beginner to confident AI-assisted designer, learning to build professional pieces and real client work step by step. Behind it is Jorge Ruvalcaba, a working brand strategist whose design career predates the current wave of AI tools by well over a decade.

## Who is Jorge Ruvalcaba

Jorge Ruvalcaba Trujillo is founder and CEO of Ruva Estudio, a branding and design studio, and of Ruva Academy, the education arm behind Design Turbo IA. UNIR, the online university publisher, interviewed him specifically about AI's role in design, where he argued that "human judgment, and not AI, will be the great differentiating value in design," a position that runs through his teaching ([UNIR interview](https://www.unir.net/revista/diseno/jorge-ruvalcaba-fundador-ruva-estudio-criterio-humano-y-no-ia-gran-valor-diferencial-diseno/)). His own biography page states Ruva Estudio has created visual identities and brand strategy for more than 300 brands and has worked with companies including Adobe, Formula 1, DHL and Envato, and that he has taught more than 3,000 Spanish-speaking students across his training programs ([ruva.mx biography](https://ruva.mx/semblanza/)). He is the author of "El Viaje del Freelancer" and co-author, with Spanish publisher Parramón, of what's described as the first specialized book on artificial intelligence for designers. His LinkedIn profile confirms the same name and role ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jorgeruva/)).

## What members actually get

Design Turbo IA is framed as a four-day intensive rather than an open-ended course, teaching participants to design faster with AI, build services that sell themselves, and work directly on real client problems from the first session rather than starting with theory. Members get access to what the community describes as a private group of designers already working ahead of the AI curve, alongside Ruvalcaba's broader Ruva Academy curriculum, which his own bio credits with 10-plus specialized training programs across branding, design and AI.

## Why it's grown

Ruvalcaba's advantage over many AI-design communities on Skool is that his design career existed long before generative AI tools did. Client work with recognizable brands like Adobe and Formula 1 gives him a credibility base that pure AI-tool educators, who often have no design portfolio outside of AI-generated work, don't have. That combination, an established design studio plus a clear, teachable stance on where AI fits into the creative process rather than replaces it, is likely a major part of why Design Turbo IA has been able to attract more than 10,000 members in a design-AI niche that's grown crowded fast.

His reported social following, in the hundreds of thousands across YouTube, Instagram and TikTok according to his own biography page, also means the Skool community functions less as a cold-start audience-building exercise and more as a natural extension of an audience he'd already built through years of branding and design content before AI became part of the pitch.
