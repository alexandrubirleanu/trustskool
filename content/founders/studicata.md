# Michael Bar, Joseph Wilson and Studicata: Inside Skool's 19,000-Member Law School Community

Studicata is a free Skool community with more than 19,000 members, built around law school and bar exam preparation, and backed by a case-brief library the company says covers more than 60,000 briefs across 1,000-plus casebooks. It was co-founded by Michael Bar and Joseph Wilson, a pairing of a law-school insider and a product-and-marketing operator that shows up directly in how the platform was built.

## Who are Michael Bar and Joseph Wilson

The Studicata origin story, as told in a feature on The Good Men Project, starts with Michael Bar, a recent law school graduate who created an unusually detailed bar exam outline for his own studying, and Joseph Wilson, who came from a background in digital marketing and product development and immediately recognized the outline's broader potential ([The Good Men Project](https://goodmenproject.com/business-ethics-2/building-studicata-friendship-purpose-and-smarter-law-school-and-bar-prep/); [Joseph Wilson's LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jpwilson90/)). The two describe their shared operating principle as "any job, big or small, do it right or not at all," and built the company together from that starting point rather than one founder bringing on the other as a later hire.

Studicata also describes itself as backed by the team behind YouTube's largest law school channel, a channel with more than 34,000 subscribers and over 4 million total views ([PR Newswire release](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/studicata-unveils-the-largest-free-case-brief-library-for-law-students-302578027.html)), which gave the product a built-in, highly targeted audience of law students before the Skool community itself existed.

## What members actually get

The free tier centers on the case-brief library itself, a genuinely large resource for a law-school-specific product, alongside study guides, outlines, and instructional videos covering law school coursework and bar exam prep. Paid tiers layer on more structured content: $29/month and $99/month subscription options, plus a $995 flat-fee full bar review course for students preparing for the bar exam specifically, which is a materially different price category from the mostly free-or-cheap communities elsewhere on this list, reflecting how much students are typically willing to spend on bar exam prep given the stakes involved.

The Skool community itself functions as a peer network layered on top of that content: more than 19,000 current law students and bar exam takers who can ask questions, share study strategies, and get support during what's widely regarded as one of the most stressful academic and professional certification processes in the US.

## Why it's grown

Studicata's growth traces back to a genuinely underserved problem: law school case briefing is time-consuming and mostly done manually by students who all read the same handful of standard casebooks, which means a well-executed, free case-brief library covering the actual books students are assigned has an unusually direct, obvious value proposition. Pairing that resource with an existing large YouTube audience specifically made up of law students (rather than a general audience) meant the Skool community launched into a pre-qualified, highly relevant pool of potential members rather than needing to build interest from scratch.

The company's continued public product announcements, including new free features like "Simplify" and "Go Deep" launched in 2026 ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/studicata-expands-free-case-brief-experience-with-simplify-go-deep-and-embedded-videos-302782777.html)), suggest an active product team continuing to invest in the free tier specifically, which tends to keep community growth compounding rather than plateauing once initial interest fades.
