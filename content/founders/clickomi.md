# Alán Valdez and Clickomi Academy: The Google Ads Specialist Training Latin America's Advertisers

Clickomi Academy is a private, Spanish-language Skool community with more than 2,800 members, built as the student hub for people who've taken Google Ads courses at clickomi.com. The pitch is direct: master Google Ads like the experts and grow a business alongside what the community calls the best Google Ads community in Latin America. It's led by Alán Valdez, named as the community's founder on its own Skool about page.

## Who is Alán Valdez

Valdez runs Clickomi as both a training academy and a working ad agency, and the company describes itself as a Google Partner Premier, a designation reserved for the top tier of Google Ads agencies by performance, with a team that has managed more than $20,000,000 USD in ad spend across client accounts ([clickomi.com](https://clickomi.com/); [Clickomi on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/clickomi)). A third-party course review site puts his experience at 13 years in marketing and digital advertising, framing his teaching as built on campaigns he's actually run rather than theory alone ([yopagoporclick.com review](https://yopagoporclick.com/curso-google-ads-alan-valdez/)).

He maintains a public YouTube channel and an Instagram account under his own name, where he shares Google Ads tactics alongside his course promotion, and Clickomi states that all of its instructors, not just Valdez, are certified and actively manage live Google Ads campaigns rather than teaching purely from documentation ([Alan Valdez YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@AlanValdez); [Instagram @alanvaldezg](https://www.instagram.com/alanvaldezg/)).

## What members actually get

Inside the private Skool group, access is tied to having gone through one of Clickomi's paid courses, such as Google Ads Factory, Google Ads eComm, or the one-on-one Google Ads consulting track, so the community itself functions as ongoing support rather than a cold-entry free group. Clickomi states its student base spans more than 3,000 people across 37 countries, giving the Skool space a genuinely international, Spanish-speaking membership rather than one concentrated in a single market.

That structure, paid course first, Skool community as the retention layer after, is different from most free-to-join Skool groups, and it means the community's real value proposition is ongoing access to Valdez's team and fellow students working through live campaigns together.

## Why it's grown

Google Ads is a category where credibility is unusually easy to verify or disprove quickly, a bad campaign shows up in wasted spend within days, so a specialist who can point to a Google Partner Premier badge and a track record managing eight-figure ad spend has a real trust advantage over generic "digital marketing" educators. Valdez's positioning leans on exactly that kind of proof rather than hype.

Because the Skool community sits downstream of paid courses rather than functioning as the primary funnel, its growth has tracked the growth of Clickomi's course business directly, each new cohort of students becomes a new wave of community members already invested in the outcome, which likely explains the steady, compounding member growth relative to a free, cold-traffic community in the same niche.
