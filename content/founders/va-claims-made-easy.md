# Reuben Geimah and VA Claims Made Easy: The Army Veteran Building an AI Tool for Disability Claims

VA Claims Made Easy is a free Skool community with nearly 2,900 members, positioned as the number one veteran benefits community with a stated mission to help veterans master the VA claims process. It's run by Reuben Geimah, who is identified on the community's own Skool about page as a VA benefits expert and claims education specialist leading weekly live calls.

## Who is Reuben Geimah

Geimah's LinkedIn profile describes him as an Army veteran and paratrooper who served as a Staff Sergeant, completing deployments to Iraq and Afghanistan before transitioning out of the military ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/reuben-geimah-2b104a349/)). After leaving the Army, he moved into entrepreneurship, first in real estate and later in online fitness coaching, where he built and scaled a coaching business before founding FitnessPreneur Academy, a separate venture teaching that same business model to other coaches.

He posts under his own name on Instagram, where he describes himself as a "Veteran Claims/Relocation Expert" and shares content aimed directly at other veterans navigating the claims system ([Instagram @reubenageimah](https://www.instagram.com/reubenageimah/)). His current company, VA Claims Made Easy, is an AI-powered platform built to simplify the claims process itself, automating claim discovery and helping generate the documentation the VA requires, and the company states it is trusted by more than 15,000 veterans ([vaclaimsmadeasy.com](https://www.vaclaimsmadeasy.com/)).

## What members actually get

The free Skool community functions as the education and support layer around that platform. Members get access to weekly live calls led by Geimah covering benefits strategy, plus a space to ask questions specific to their own claim situation with someone who has both direct military service and a business built specifically around solving this problem. The company's own materials describe an internal 98% success rate figure for the claims processed through its platform, though as with any self-reported statistic from the company itself, that number should be treated as a company claim rather than an independently verified figure.

Because Geimah's path runs through the military himself rather than starting as a civilian advocate, the community reads less like a generic self-help group and more like peer-to-peer help from someone who has personally been through the same VA system his members are navigating.

## Why it's grown

Veteran benefits is a niche with enormous underserved demand, a large share of eligible veterans either never file or file incomplete claims because the paperwork and evidence requirements are genuinely confusing, and there are relatively few builders in the space who combine actual military service with a tech platform built to solve the problem directly. Geimah's background gives him credibility that's hard to fake in this niche specifically, and it likely does more to drive trust and word-of-mouth referrals among veterans than any amount of paid advertising could.

His earlier ventures in real estate and fitness coaching also suggest a founder comfortable building and scaling businesses generally, which may explain why VA Claims Made Easy has grown to thousands of members and tens of thousands of platform users relatively quickly compared to smaller, single-operator advocacy groups in the same space.
