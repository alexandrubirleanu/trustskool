# Agustin Medina and AIlink: Inside Skool's 9,800-Member Spanish AI Agency Community

AIlink Free has pulled in close to 9,800 members on Skool as the free funnel into what its own materials call the largest community in Spanish-speaking regions built around serious AI agencies. The person running it is Agustin Medina, who positions himself as an AI educator and agency founder in his own right.

## Who is Agustin Medina

Agustin Medina describes himself as CEO of LambdaIA and founder of Lambda Automations and the AIlink community, according to his own site, ailinkmastery.com, and his public search footprint across several platforms confirms the same identity: an Instagram account under @agustinmedinaia, a YouTube channel called "Agustín Medina | AI Agency Academy," a Gumroad course on automating and selling AI agents with n8n, and a LinkedIn profile listing him as a speaker and author ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/agustinmedinaia/); [LinkedIn](https://es.linkedin.com/in/conferencianteagustinmedina)). His own site states he has more than two years of experience applying AI to businesses and has trained over 1,000 students, some of whom are cited as already generating revenue from client work.

## What members actually get

AIlink's positioning is "de cero a tu agencia de IA," from zero to your own AI agency, and the paid VIP tier includes eight modules and more than 200 videos covering tools like Claude Code, n8n and Meta Ads, along with a vault of templates and real client contracts, weekly Q&A sessions and technical meetings. The free community serves as the entry point where prospective members can see the results other students claim to have achieved, including specific member success stories that Medina and his site publish as social proof, before deciding whether to upgrade into the paid track.

## Why it's grown

The Spanish-language AI-agency education space on Skool has grown quickly, and Medina's approach, leading with concrete member outcomes and a clearly named backend product rather than a vague promise of "learning AI," follows the same playbook that's worked for several of the larger communities in this niche. Positioning the community as serving Spanish speakers specifically, rather than translating English-language AI content, also gives it a natural advantage with an audience that's underserved relative to the volume of AI business content published in English.

Medina's own multi-platform presence, a working agency in LambdaIA, a YouTube channel, an active Instagram account and a paid course product outside of Skool, mirrors the same pattern seen in other founder-led AI communities: the free Skool group isn't the whole business, it's the visible front door to a broader personal brand and paid education funnel that existed, or was built in parallel, alongside it.
