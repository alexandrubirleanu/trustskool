# Mind Pump and The Elite Trainer Academy: Inside Skool's 2,700-Member Trainer Business Community

The Elite Trainer Academy charges $12 a month and has attracted roughly 2,721 members with a specific pitch: help personal trainers stop undercharging and inconsistently earning, and build toward a reliable six-figure income instead. It's built and run by Mind Pump, a fitness media brand rather than a solo creator.

## Who runs it

Mind Pump is hosted by four people: Sal Di Stefano, Adam Schafer, Justin Andrews, and Doug Egge. Di Stefano started lifting at 13, became a personal trainer at 18, and was running as a general manager at 24 Hour Fitness by 19 before eventually opening his own studio; he's described on the company's own site as "the voice of Mind Pump" ([Mind Pump Podcast story](https://mindpumppodcast.com/mind-pump-podcast-story/)). Schafer managed major fitness clubs across the Bay Area and holds eight national training certifications plus a professional bodybuilding card. Andrews built a decade-plus career as a trainer known for combining exercise science with psychology and behavior change. Egge, who hired Di Stefano as his own trainer, is the one who pushed the group into making content in the first place, and now runs production, finances, and merchandise behind the scenes. The podcast itself, Mind Pump: Raw Fitness Truth, is independently listed and reviewed across Apple Podcasts, Podchaser, and Listen Notes, putting its existence and hosts on record outside Mind Pump's own marketing ([Listen Notes](https://www.listennotes.com/podcasts/mind-pump-raw-fitness-truth-sal-di-stefano-CLpl3iA-ead/)).

## What members actually get

The Academy's own materials describe access to a library of 70-plus live caller sessions, organized by the specific challenges trainers run into with clients: plateaus, objections, pricing pushback, and similar recurring problems, alongside workbooks, guides, and accountability structures aimed at trainers building or scaling their own client base. It's explicitly aimed at people already training clients or planning to, not general fitness enthusiasts, which narrows the audience considerably compared to most fitness communities on the platform.

## Why it's grown

Mind Pump arrives at this Skool community with an existing audience built over years as, by its own description, the top fitness podcast globally, and that pre-existing trust is doing most of the heavy lifting that a brand-new creator would otherwise have to build from zero. The business-of-training angle is also a genuine niche gap: most fitness content on Skool teaches training methodology or nutrition, not the pricing, client retention, and business-building side of running a training career, which is exactly the problem Mind Pump's four hosts have collectively lived through across decades in commercial gyms before any of them touched a podcast microphone. At $12 a month, the price is low enough to be an easy yes for a trainer weighing it against the income increase the community promises, while still filtering out people who aren't seriously building a training business.
