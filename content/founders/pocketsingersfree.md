# Zane Rima and Pocket Singers Free: A Near-Million-Subscriber Vocal Coach's 7-Day Course

Pocket Singers Free promises exactly what its name suggests: learn to sing in a week, no prior experience required. Over 18,600 people have joined the free tier, which used to be sold on its own as a $79 course before being folded into the Skool community at no cost.

## Who is Zane Rima

Rima is the founder of Pocket Singers Elite and CEO of AmaZane Media, working as a vocal coach and singer alongside her role running the AmaZane YouTube channel, which has built a following approaching one million subscribers ([CanvasRebel Magazine profile](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-zane-rima/)). She hosts her own podcast, "Pocket Singers w/ Zane Rima," distributed on Apple Podcasts and Spotify, where she covers vocal training and the business side of building a singing-focused media brand ([Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/pocket-singers-w-zane-rima/id1476439719); [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/show/6W4ZyMb8BOHxI6bbHOcO27)).

Her official Pocket Singers site lists both a free and a paid tier, with Pocket Singers Elite ranked at number two under the music category on Skool as of an April 2025 snapshot, alongside pricing details for the premium offering ([pocketsingers.com](https://pocketsingers.com/pricing/)).

## What members actually get

Pocket Singers Free centers on a 7-Day Singing Course originally sold as a standalone $79 product, now included at no cost for anyone who joins the Skool community. The course targets absolute beginners, focused on developing ear training, pitch accuracy, and basic vocal confidence rather than advanced technique.

For members who want to go further, Pocket Singers Elite runs as a premium tier layered on top, with almost-daily live vocal workshops, feedback sessions, Discord practice rooms, singing challenges, karaoke events, and a fuller curriculum than the free introductory course covers.

## Why it's grown

Rima's near-million-subscriber YouTube audience gives Pocket Singers Free a large, pre-qualified pool of potential members who have already sampled her teaching style through free video content before ever encountering the Skool community. Converting a previously paid $79 course into a free offer removes the last point of friction for that audience, turning warm YouTube viewers into community members with essentially no financial barrier.

The tiered structure, free foundational course feeding into a paid, more intensive Elite tier, is also a well-tested model in creator-led education: it lets Rima capture a large top-of-funnel audience through the free community while monetizing the smaller subset of members who want ongoing, live coaching rather than a one-time course.
