# Shahzad Kayani and Prepper Academy: Inside Skool's 3,400-Member Prepping Community

Prepper Academy bills itself as the number one community for preppers, and with roughly 3,400 members it has built a sizeable free audience around teaching the basics of emergency preparedness in plain, unintimidating language. The person behind it is Shahzad Kayani, who runs the group as "Prepper Academy By Shahzad K."

## Who is Shahzad Kayani

Kayani is a Canadian prepper based in Ontario who has spent roughly two decades building out emergency supplies, a habit that started at age 12 after watching Bear Grylls' Man vs. Wild. By his early thirties, that interest had turned into more than $40,000 worth of stockpiled food, water, backup power, and shelter gear stored across his garage and basement, enough, he says, to keep his family going for six months in a serious disruption ([LadBible profile](https://www.ladbible.com/news/world-news/doomsday-prepper-ww3-global-disaster-stockpile-whats-in-his-kit-638639-20250710)).

Beyond the community itself, Kayani has published multiple prepping guides under his own name, including "The Art of Prepping: The Ultimate Prepping Guide for Food Storage, Security, and Long-Term Preparedness," available on Amazon ([Amazon listing](https://www.amazon.com/ART-PREPPING-Ultimate-Long-Term-Preparedness/dp/B0DZ2TZ8KC)), and he posts regularly about survival gear and preparedness tactics on his own YouTube channel ([Shahzad Kayani on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@shahzadmkayani)) and under his own byline on Medium ([Medium](https://medium.com/@shahzadmkayani/free-prepping-survival-items-by-shahzad-kayani-cba1b9f57872)). That combination of published books, video content, and a personal media profile places him in a small group of preppers who've turned a private hobby into a public, documented body of work rather than an anonymous online persona.

## What members actually get

The community's stated goal is teaching "all the basics of emergency preparedness in a simple, easy way" while connecting members with other like-minded people, a framing that keeps the entry point low for people who are curious about prepping but intimidated by the more extreme corners of the movement. That matches Kayani's own public content, which tends to focus on practical, buyable categories: food storage, water, basic medical supplies, and off-grid power, rather than survivalist tactics aimed at collapse scenarios specifically.

Being free, Prepper Academy functions as the entry point into Kayani's broader body of work, his books and YouTube channel sit alongside it, giving members a next step once they've worked through the community's basics.

## Why it's grown

Prepping has moved from a fringe hobby into mainstream anxiety territory over the last several years, driven by a steady stream of news around supply chain disruptions, extreme weather, and geopolitical uncertainty, and Kayani's messaging leans into that widened audience rather than the older, more militaristic prepper stereotype. His own stockpile and story, documented in mainstream outlets rather than niche survivalist forums, gives the community a relatable entry point: an ordinary Canadian who got serious about preparedness, not a bunker-building extremist.

Free access removes the biggest barrier to trying out a prepping community, and Kayani's parallel output of books and video content gives Prepper Academy a steady stream of new members arriving from outside Skool itself rather than depending purely on organic growth inside the platform.
