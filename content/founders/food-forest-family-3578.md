# Byron Austin and Food Forest Family: From Environmental Science Burnout to New Zealand Food Forests

Food Forest Family (FREE) is a Skool community of roughly 3,780 members built around growing food forest skills, self-sufficient, layered plantings designed to mimic natural forest ecosystems while producing food for the household that tends them. The community's About page credits Byron Austin as its founder.

## Who is Byron Austin

Byron Austin is a food forestry consultant and practitioner who grew up in Oregon, where nature-based summer camps and time at the Jane Goodall Environmental Middle School shaped an early interest in the environment ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/byron-austin-4b5046158/)). He graduated in Environmental Science in 2018, but grew dissatisfied with the field as a career path and turned instead toward permaculture as a more practical, hands-on design approach to building a regenerative future.

That same year he moved to New Zealand for environmental research, and in 2019 he began transforming his own family's orchard into a diverse food forest. By 2020 he'd quit his job entirely to focus on permaculture design, and between 2020 and 2024 he installed food forests for landowners across New Zealand, work that's been recognized locally, including a collaboration with Healthy Families Rotorua under the name "Byron Grows" ([Healthy Families Rotorua](https://healthyfamiliesrotorua.org.nz/healthy-families-rotorua-collaborates-with-byron-grows/)). He documents his design work and installations on his own YouTube channel ([Byron Grows](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC6ju6NIkwAOXtXFHhVITjYg)) and stays active in New Zealand permaculture discussion communities on Facebook.

## What members actually get

Food Forest Family is the free entry point into Austin's teaching, giving members general food forest education and community discussion. For members who want more direct support, he also runs the Food Forest Fellowship, a paid offering that provides 1:1 support with property design and planting plans, weekly coaching, and step-by-step courses covering topics like plant selection, pruning, and layout, the kind of hands-on guidance a written guide alone can't fully replace.

He's also done direct community outreach work beyond his paying clients, including helping a local school, Kaharoa School, install its own mini food forest.

## Why it's grown

Austin's growth rests on a fairly rare combination for this niche: real permaculture design credentials, a formal Environmental Science background, plus several years of hands-on installation work across a country with a genuinely active permaculture and homesteading culture. Unlike a lot of online permaculture content that leans heavily on theory, his free community and YouTube channel are backed by an actual multi-year record of installed food forests for real landowners, which gives the free tier a level of grounded, applied credibility that's harder to fake than general gardening advice.
