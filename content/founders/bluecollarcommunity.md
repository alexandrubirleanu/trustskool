# Logan Sendle and Blue-Collar Biz Free Group: Inside Skool's 5,700-Member Home Services Community

Blue-Collar Biz Free Group is a 5,783-member Skool community aimed at home service business owners — landscapers, hardscapers, lawn care operators, excavators, general contractors, painters, power washers, window cleaners, and similar trades — teaching lead generation tactics they can put to use immediately. It's priced at $1/month [Skool](https://www.skool.com/bluecollarcommunity/about).

## Who runs it

The community is run by Logan Sendle, who previously owned and operated a landscape and hardscape company in Minnesota for seven years before selling the business in May 2024 [Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@logan). That operating history is the community's core credibility marker: Sendle is teaching lead generation as someone who ran a home service business through the actual operational grind — seasonal demand swings, crew management, and local marketing — rather than as an outside marketing consultant who has never run a trade business themselves. His current work is organized under the "Blue Collar Business Accelerator" brand, which maintains its own website separate from the free Skool group.

## What members actually get

The free group's stated hook is "5 free ways to find leads that you can use today in your business," positioning it as a low-friction entry point into Sendle's broader coaching. Content focuses specifically on lead generation for trades businesses — a category most general small-business marketing advice ignores or handles poorly, since home service marketing has its own dynamics (local SEO, seasonal ad timing, referral and review systems, door-to-door and truck-wrap branding) that differ significantly from e-commerce or SaaS marketing. The parent Blue Collar Business Accelerator brand cites more than 300 five-star reviews across Google and Facebook, according to the Skool page.

## Why it's grown

Home service trades are a massive, historically underserved market for business education — most marketing and business content online is built for online creators, coaches, or SaaS founders, leaving trade business owners with comparatively few resources built specifically for their operating reality. Sendle's direct, recent operating history (selling his own company as recently as 2024) makes his advice feel current rather than theoretical, and the $1/month price removes essentially any friction to trying it, which is a common and effective strategy for building a large top-of-funnel audience that can later be converted into a paid accelerator program. It's also part of a broader wave of "Blue Collar" branded communities on Skool (Blue Collar University, Blue-Collar Biz Accelerator, Blue Collar Millionaire among them), reflecting real, growing demand from trade business owners for community and structured guidance.
