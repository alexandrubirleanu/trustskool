# Andreas Weiß and the 3D-Druck Akademie: A Machinist Turned 3D Printing Educator on Skool

The 3D-Druck Akademie is a free, German-language Skool community with roughly 4,380 members offering courses, templates, and community support to help people learn 3D printing from the ground up. It's run by Andreas Weiß, a former CAD-CAM programmer who turned a personal fascination with 3D printing into both a YouTube channel and a company, and whose name appears directly on the community's own about page as its founder.

## Who is Andreas Weiß

Weiß trained as a machinist technician and, starting in 2014, immersed himself in 3D printing technology, studying the manufacturing method closely enough to understand its full commercial potential. Before going independent, he led CAD-CAM programming at Gühring KG, a leading German tooling manufacturer, and worked as a 3D printing consultant on the side ([search coverage via LinkedIn and Frankenpost, cited in reporting on his career](https://de.linkedin.com/in/andreas-wei%C3%9F-4399621b9)). He built a YouTube channel, "3D-Druck ANDI," which grew into one of the larger German-language channels covering 3D printing, and used that audience as the foundation for founding AMPrO 3D GmbH in 2019 ([AMPrO 3D company site](https://ampro-3d.com/3dd-beratung)). The regional paper Frankenpost covered his move to set up shop in the Kulmbach area under the headline describing how he was building his future through 3D printing ([Frankenpost](https://www.frankenpost.de/inhalt.firma-siedelt-sich-an-mit-3d-druck-baut-andreas-weiss-nicht-nur-seine-zukunft.ae100e73-c2e5-4bcd-a19f-b1a7ab533c99.html)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, the 3D-Druck Akademie offers courses spanning modeling and construction software through slicer programs like Bambu Studio, Cura, OrcaSlicer, and PrusaSlicer, plus material science, printer maintenance, and post-processing topics, drawing on more than a dozen specialists to keep the content current for both beginners and experienced users.

## Why it's grown

Weiß's path mirrors a pattern common to successful niche educators: a genuine industrial background in CAD-CAM and manufacturing gave him real technical credibility, and years of consistent YouTube content built the audience before the academy existed as a product. As AMPrO 3D shifted its trading business to a partner company, 3Dmensionals, Weiß has been able to concentrate more fully on growing the academy itself as a dedicated digital learning platform rather than splitting attention between hardware sales and education.
