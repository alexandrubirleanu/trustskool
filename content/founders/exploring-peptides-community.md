# TK Jensen and Exploring Peptides Community: Inside Skool's 19,500-Member Peptide Education Hub

Exploring Peptides Community is a free Skool community with roughly 19,500 members focused on peptide research and practical application — one of the oldest and largest hubs of its kind in the fast-growing peptide and biohacking space. It was founded by TK Jensen, whose own path into peptides started not as a business idea but as a personal medical crisis more than a decade ago.

## Who is TK Jensen

TK Jensen's origin story is unusually specific and well-documented for a wellness-community founder. In his early 20s, while backpacking in Thailand, he collapsed and was hospitalized for three weeks with kidney failure brought on by a combination of severe infection, a viral illness, parasites, and mercury poisoning ([Scantifix founder interview](https://www.scantifix.com/blogs/news/the-story-of-scantifix-interview-with-tk)). He was left bedridden and largely non-functional for years afterward, before discovering the peptide BPC-157 as a treatment for persistent gut issues and leaky gut, with results he describes as dramatic.

That recovery experience led him to start what became Exploring Peptides around 2012, initially as a Facebook group, at a time when almost no consumer-facing information about peptides existed. The community grew to more than 100,000 members across all platforms at its peak. Jensen has since gone on to found Scantifix, a biohacking and skincare company where he serves as CEO, founder, and lead formulator, building on the same peptide and recovery expertise that started with his own illness.

## What members actually get

Inside the Skool community, the stated philosophy is pragmatic rather than ideological: "all we care about is what works and how to apply it to better our health." Content focuses on simplifying peptide science for a general audience, with particular attention to anti-aging applications and, more recently, GLP-1 compounds and how to use them safely — a topic that has exploded in mainstream interest well beyond the original biohacking niche as drugs like semaglutide and tirzepatide have gone mainstream. Members share personal experiences managing chronic conditions and recovering from injury using peptide protocols, and the community explicitly welcomes both newcomers with no background and experienced practitioners.

There's also a separate, more structured paid offering called "Exploring Peptides MEMBERSHIP" that sits alongside the free community for members who want deeper, more curated protocols.

## Why it's grown

Exploring Peptides has a genuine first-mover advantage: Jensen was building peptide education content years before "peptides" became a mainstream wellness and skincare buzzword, which means the community has had roughly a decade to accumulate trust, case studies, and search visibility that newer peptide communities simply haven't had time to build. That head start matters a great deal in a space where credibility and perceived safety are the biggest barriers to entry for cautious newcomers considering unregulated compounds.

The recent mainstreaming of GLP-1 drugs and broader peptide-adjacent wellness content has also expanded the addressable audience well beyond the original biohacker niche Jensen started in, pulling in a much larger population of people newly curious about peptides generally, not just performance-focused early adopters. Jensen's personal story, a documented medical crisis followed by a peptide-driven recovery, also gives the community's core message an authenticity that's harder to fake than typical wellness-influencer positioning.
