# Amaanath Mumtaz and Poppy AI Viral Content Academy: The Founding Team Member Teaching Poppy's Playbook

Poppy AI Viral Content Academy is a Skool community of roughly 3,990 members built around a specific AI tool: Poppy AI, a visual workspace where creators drag in sources, YouTube videos, PDFs, voice notes, images, onto an infinite canvas and let built-in AI cluster ideas and turn them into scripts, social posts, or ads. The community's Skool About page credits Amaanath Mumtaz as its founder.

## Who is Amaanath Mumtaz

Amaanath Mumtaz is part of the founding team at Poppy AI, working as Head of Creator Partnerships, and is based in Colombo, Sri Lanka ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/amaanath/)). Before joining Poppy AI, he brought experience from roles at Creator Forensics, Lemire Digital, and Clever Programmer, and he completed the Nights & Weekends S5 program at buildspace in 2024, a well-known startup-building cohort that has produced a number of independent builders and founders. He maintains an active public presence documenting his work under his own name on Instagram ([@amaanath.me](https://www.instagram.com/amaanath.me/)).

Poppy AI itself was co-founded by Rafeh Qazi and Naz Dumanskyy, who built and grew a YouTube channel to a million subscribers together before pivoting to build software, eventually landing on the combined "Miro plus ChatGPT" concept that became Poppy AI after several earlier product attempts failed ([getpoppy.ai](https://getpoppy.ai/)).

## What members actually get

The Viral Content Academy is positioned as the practical, tactical layer on top of the Poppy AI product itself: teaching content creators, founders, and marketers how to use the tool specifically to make viral content and ads while saving hours of manual work each month. Given Mumtaz's role overseeing creator partnerships at the company, the community likely functions as a direct extension of that relationship-building work, connecting the product's creator user base with structured guidance on getting the most out of it.

That focus on partnerships also shows up in how the community is likely used internally: as a place to surface creators who might be a fit for deeper collaboration with the Poppy AI team, rather than purely a one-way teaching channel.

## Why it's grown

Poppy AI's broader growth has been fast and well documented: the company has said it went from being roughly two months from bankruptcy to making $100,000 in ten days after a TikTok post went viral, and it now serves around 5,000 creators generating roughly $400,000 a month in revenue. A Skool community run by someone whose job is specifically creator partnerships is a natural extension of that growth engine, since it gives the company a direct, ongoing relationship with the creators most likely to advocate for the product, rather than relying purely on paid acquisition to keep growing its user base.

Mumtaz's own background at Clever Programmer and other creator-adjacent companies, plus his buildspace cohort experience, also means he's operating the academy with firsthand knowledge of how fast-moving online creator communities actually function, rather than approaching it purely from a marketing playbook.
