# Bricksdamane and Producer Pathway: Inside Skool's Community for Music Producers

Producer Pathway is a free Skool community for music producers with nearly 8,000 members, hosted by a producer who goes by Bricksdamane. The community's own pages bill it as a place to grow, learn, connect, and simplify the process of producing, and the person behind it has actual major-label production credits to back that up.

## Who is Bricksdamane

Bricksdamane's real name is Charles Driggers, according to his own Skool profile ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/@charles-driggers-8035)). His production website lists him as an award-winning producer and engineer with credits across Hip-Hop, including work with Drake, Migos, Young Thug, Lil Wayne, Meek Mill, and 21 Savage ([bricksaudio.com](https://www.bricksaudio.com/)). Among his more specific credits, he contributed to two tracks on Drake's "More Life" album: "Ice Melts," co-produced with Supah Mario, and "Sacrifices," co-produced with T-Minus.

In tutorial videos, he talks about coming up through the vintage soul-sampling side of production, breaking down how he builds beats from scratch using 1970s soul samples, and covering mixing fundamentals for producers still learning their craft ([YouTube: Platinum Producer Makes VINTAGE 1970s Soul Samples From Scratch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w3ytcZSNU5Q)). According to his own account, he went from making $0 as a producer to more than $100,000, a trajectory that forms the backbone of what he teaches inside Producer Pathway.

## What members actually get

The community is free to join and includes tutorial content pulled directly from Bricksdamane's own workflow, such as a drumkit built around his "I Am Music" sound and lessons on trap drum programming and mixing. It's positioned less as a paid mentorship funnel and more as a shared resource library for producers at different stages, from people making their first beat to others looking to tighten up their mix technique. The community's own posts also warn members directly about impersonation scams using Bricksdamane's name and image, a sign that the group has grown large enough to attract copycat accounts targeting its members.

## Why it's grown

Bricksdamane's pull comes from something a lot of production-education communities can't claim: verifiable placements on records by artists with hundreds of millions of monthly listeners. For an aspiring producer, that's a very different pitch than "I teach production" from someone without label credits. Combining that discography with a steady stream of free YouTube tutorials, he's built enough trust that thousands of producers will join a free Skool community just to be in the same room, digitally, as someone who has actually shipped records for artists at that level.
