# Waqas Ahmed and Go International: Inside Skool's 9,300-Member Pakistani Agency Community

Go International (Free) has built up roughly 9,300 members on Skool around a specific national angle: helping Pakistanis learn and sell digital skills internationally, then build a real SMMA or digital marketing agency rather than staying stuck freelancing project to project. The founder, Waqas Ahmed, describes himself on his own Skool profile as building "Pakistan's Largest Agency Building Platform."

## Who is Waqas Ahmed

Waqas Ahmed's Skool profile identifies him directly as "Founder Of Go International Academy, Pakistan's Largest Agency Building Platform," and lists him as based in Ireland. On the same profile he describes himself as a paid-ads specialist claiming more than $2 million generated for clients. His academy's own site tells a founder story consistent with that positioning: starting out as a Top-Rated Upwork freelancer before transitioning off freelance platforms entirely to run his own agency, eventually landing a 22-project Google Ads and SEO deal with a US client and growing the business past $10,000 a month in recurring revenue from a base in Multan, Pakistan ([gointernationalacademy.com](https://gointernationalacademy.com/)). Cold outreach, rather than referrals or paid lead generation, is credited as the strategy that got him his first clients.

## What members actually get

The free Skool community teaches the fundamentals of running Meta ad campaigns for international clients and building an agency business model rather than a freelance one, framed specifically around Pakistan's opportunity to serve Western clients at competitive rates. Members get access to digital marketing fundamentals, live masterclass sessions, and a stated roadmap that Ahmed positions as normally sold separately at a higher price. A paid tier, Go International Academy, sits above the free community and is marketed toward members who want to build specifically toward a $10,000-a-month international agency.

## Why it's grown

Go International's growth reflects a pattern that's increasingly visible on Skool: a founder builds real, verifiable results in a specific market first, in Ahmed's case a paid-ads agency serving international clients from Pakistan, then packages that journey into an education product targeted at people in the same geography who want the same outcome. Framing the opportunity around national identity, "as a Pakistani," rather than a generic global SMMA pitch, narrows the message but also sharpens it, speaking directly to an audience that often feels locked out of the more US-centric agency and SMMA content dominating English-language business education online.

Meta Ads specifically, rather than a broad menu of digital marketing skills, gives the community a tighter technical focus than many general "build an agency" communities, which likely helps retention among members who came in for one specific, learnable skill rather than a vague promise of online income.
