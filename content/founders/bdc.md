# Keitron Rankin and Billion Dollar Circle: Inside Skool's 27,000-Member Credit and Wealth Community

Billion Dollar Circle is a $25-a-month Skool community with roughly 27,000 members, built around credit repair, business funding, and wealth-building education. It's run by Keitron Rankin, who also goes by "Keo," and who describes his stated mission for the community in blunt, specific terms: help 10,000 people become millionaires.

## Who is Keitron Rankin

Keitron Rankin is a multi-business owner whose primary credential is Ace Credit Club, a credit-repair and financial education business he founded before launching Billion Dollar Circle, alongside King Rankin Consulting Inc ([Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@keitron)). Public biographical detail beyond his business ventures is limited — there isn't an extensive independently reported profile of his background or career history prior to these businesses, and most available information comes from his own platforms and marketing materials rather than third-party reporting. That's a meaningful distinction worth flagging: what's verifiable is that he runs Ace Credit Club and Billion Dollar Circle and describes himself as having built his fortune through those ventures; the specific financial claims about his own success are self-reported rather than independently audited.

## What members actually get

Billion Dollar Circle is structured around four specific outcomes the community says it will help members hit: an 800-plus credit score, their first $100,000 in funding, a six-to-eight-figure business, and the acquisition of "dream" homes, cars, and investment properties. Practically, that translates into access to a proprietary software platform for tracking credit and funding progress, funding pre-qualification services (including options aimed at members with poor credit), personalized business credit-building guidance, customized funding sequences, and mentorship access to a roster the community says includes more than 50 business professionals. The community backs its $25/month price with a 14-day money-back guarantee.

The structure mirrors a well-established niche in the credit-repair and business-funding education space — communities that bundle software tools, done-for-you application support, and peer mentorship rather than relying purely on educational video content.

## Why it's grown

Credit repair and business funding is a durable, high-demand niche independent of any single platform trend, because the underlying problem (limited access to capital, damaged credit) affects a large population continuously, not just during a specific cultural moment. Billion Dollar Circle's growth appears to follow from Rankin's pre-existing Ace Credit Club audience being funneled into a broader, Skool-hosted community that adds software tools and peer community on top of the original credit-repair service — a natural line extension rather than a cold start.

The tiered structure — a large admin team of roughly 28 people managing the community — also suggests Billion Dollar Circle has scaled its support and mentorship capacity in step with membership growth, rather than relying on a single founder to personally handle a community of this size, which matters for retention in a niche where members often need ongoing, individualized help rather than one-time content consumption.

Because financial outcome claims in this space are common but hard to verify from the outside, readers evaluating Billion Dollar Circle should weigh the specific, structured offer (software, funding pre-qualification, mentorship access) more heavily than any individual income or credit-score success story shared inside the community.
