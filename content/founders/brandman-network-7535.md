# Sean Taylor, Kohrey Barkley and No Labels Necessary: Inside Skool's 6,400-Member Music Community

No Labels Necessary is a free Skool community of roughly 6,393 members aimed at helping independent musicians build real fanbases without signing to a label. It's an offshoot of an existing music marketing podcast and agency, run by two named industry professionals rather than a single influencer figure.

## Who runs No Labels Necessary

No Labels Necessary is the Skool arm of a weekly podcast of the same name, hosted by Sean "BrandMan" Taylor and Jacorey "Kohrey" Barkley, co-founders of the music marketing agency ContraBrand Agency ([Spotify podcast page](https://open.spotify.com/show/2QZzZrS616aEVskiQjRSqa); [Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/ng/podcast/no-labels-necessary/id1658301063)). The pair describe themselves as bringing over 15 years of combined experience in music marketing, and the podcast regularly covers artist development, record deal structures, and commentary on shifts in the wider music industry, with episode titles like a deep dive into why new rappers frequently lose their first record deals ([episode example](https://creators.spotify.com/pod/profile/brandman-network/episodes/117--Music-Exec-Reveals-Why-Rappers-ALWAYS-LOSE-Their-First-Record-Deals-Deep-Dive-e2euffg)). Their wider media operation, Brandman Network, runs its own site describing the group as a platform for independent artist education and entertainment ([Brandman Network](https://brandmannetwork.com/)), alongside an active YouTube channel under the same name ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/BrandManNetwork)).

Two co-founders with a running podcast archive and an operating marketing agency gives this community a different footing than most single-creator Skool groups: the content predates the Skool group by years, and the agency work behind it is independently checkable.

## What members actually get

The Skool community is free to join and is built around what it calls the "7 Layers of an Artist Career," a framework for building a sustainable fanbase and career without label backing. Members get access to the strategies and systems Taylor and Barkley discuss on their podcast, applied specifically to the practical mechanics of artist marketing: audience building, content strategy, and understanding how labels and the broader industry actually operate, informed by the pair's agency work advising artists and brands directly.

That agency background matters here. A lot of "how to build a fanbase" advice online comes from people who've never actually run a music marketing campaign for a paying client; ContraBrand Agency's work doing exactly that gives the community's advice a working, tested basis rather than pure theory.

## Why it's grown

At 6,393 members with no cost to join, No Labels Necessary benefits from an audience that already existed around the podcast before the Skool community launched. Podcast listeners who've followed years of episodes on music industry mechanics have a built-in reason to want a more direct, structured version of that advice, and a free Skool group is a low-friction way to convert podcast attention into an active community.

The dual-host structure also broadens the pool of people the content resonates with, since Taylor and Barkley bring somewhat different angles and experience to the same underlying agency work, rather than funneling everything through one person's single perspective.
