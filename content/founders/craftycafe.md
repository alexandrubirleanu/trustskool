# Nancy Joy and Crafty Cafe: Inside Skool's 2,600-Member Cricut Community

Crafty Cafe bills itself as a cozy community for Cricut projects, tutorials, SVGs, and maker challenges, and it has reached roughly 2,672 members without charging for access.

## Who runs it

The community is hosted by Nancy Joy, who runs the crafting blog and YouTube channel Better Life Blog, where she publishes Cricut and Silhouette tutorials, free SVG files, and craft project walkthroughs ([Better Life Blog](https://betterlifeblog.com/)). Her own site's About section describes her as a wife, a mother of four boys, and a self-described serial entrepreneur who built Better Life Blog around the broader idea of living better daily, arts and crafts being one of several categories she covers alongside home DIY, food, business, and parenting content ([Better Life Blog About](https://betterlifeblog.com/about/)). She maintains an active YouTube channel under the Better Life Blog name with ongoing craft tutorial content, including live "Crafts & Coffee Chat" sessions ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/betterlifeblog)), and runs a separate Facebook group for the same crafting audience ([Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/betterlifeblog/)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, Crafty Cafe offers free tutorials, project inspiration, monthly challenges, and ongoing tips aimed at crafters of every skill level, whether their interest is card making, vinyl work, paper crafts, sublimation, or handmade gifts. That range matches Better Life Blog's own content history, which spans multiple crafting techniques and tools rather than specializing narrowly in one Cricut product line. Nancy also runs a newsletter through Kit for subscribers who want new SVG files and project ideas delivered directly rather than searching for them across the blog and YouTube channel separately ([Kit](https://betterlifeblog.kit.com/)).

## Why it's grown

Cricut and Silhouette crafting has a large, engaged hobbyist audience that's used to finding tutorials scattered across YouTube, Pinterest, and Facebook groups, and Skool gives that audience a single, organized home instead. Nancy Joy's multi-year presence across her own blog, a YouTube channel, and a Facebook group gives newcomers a body of prior content to check before committing time to the Skool community, rather than joining a crafting group run by an unfamiliar name with no track record. Free access fits the hobbyist nature of the niche well: most people crafting with a Cricut are looking for project ideas and troubleshooting help rather than a business outcome, so a paywall would cut against the community-and-inspiration angle that keeps a crafting audience engaged long-term.
