# Linden Chasteen and Amplify Views: A 21-Year-Old's Mission to Grow 1,000 YouTube Channels

Amplify Views states its goal in a single line: take 1,000 YouTube channels past 100,000 subscribers. Nearly 28,500 members have joined the free community chasing that number, working with a founder who built his own reputation by treating YouTube growth as a revenue system rather than a popularity contest.

## Who is Linden Chasteen

Chasteen built his track record by helping client channels convert views into actual sales for info-product businesses, reportedly generating over $30 million in combined sales for clients by focusing on monetization rather than vanity metrics like subscriber counts alone. His own posting strategy helped one client's channel cross 300,000 subscribers while generating more than $6 million, according to his LinkedIn profile ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lindenchasteen/)).

He's based in Thailand and was profiled at 21 years old in an interview titled "How This 21-Year-Old Lives The Laptop Lifestyle From Thailand," describing how he built a location-independent business around YouTube strategy work rather than working a traditional job ([YouTube interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kW0Z4bjU2jI)). The Wall Street Times covered his work directly, reporting on the launch of his book on YouTube growth and identifying him by name as the founder of Amplify Views ([Wall Street Times](https://wallstreettimes.com/amplify-views-founder-linden-chasteen-launches-book-on-youtube-growth/)). That book, "YouTube Growth Made Simple: How to Grow Your Channel to 100,000 Subscribers," is available on Amazon and lays out the systems behind growing four separate channels past the 100,000-subscriber mark within a single year ([Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/YouTube-Growth-Made-Simple-Subscribers/dp/B0FJ22WVCM)).

## What members actually get

Inside Amplify Views, the emphasis sits on strategy over shortcuts: understanding what actually converts a viewer into a subscriber and eventually a customer, rather than chasing single viral uploads that don't compound into a sustainable channel. Chasteen has been direct in interviews about his frustration with YouTube advice that optimizes for vanity metrics instead of revenue, and the community's content reflects that bias toward measurable outcomes.

## Why it's grown

Chasteen's own story functions as the product demo. A 21-year-old building a seven-figure-adjacent consulting business from Thailand, backed by a documented $30 million in client sales and a traditionally published book, is an unusually concrete credential set for a YouTube growth community, most of which rely on the founder's own subscriber count as the only proof point.

Because his expertise is about monetization systems rather than content virality specifically, Amplify Views also draws a slightly different audience than pure "how to go viral" communities: creators who already have some traction and want to convert it into income, rather than people starting completely from zero. That focus likely narrows the top of the funnel somewhat, but it produces a more committed, less churn-prone membership base.
