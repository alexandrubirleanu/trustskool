# Chris Bossio and Tomb45 Barber Skool: A Free Classroom Built From a YouTube Channel

Tomb45 Barber Skool is a free community with roughly 3,399 members, positioned simply as "Free Skool for BARBERS." It's run by Chris Bossio, whose path into the barbering business world started, unusually, on YouTube rather than behind a chair.

## Who is Chris Bossio

Bossio is a Tampa-area barber and entrepreneur whose YouTube channel has grown past 350,000 subscribers, a story YouTube's own creator blog covered directly, describing how his tutorial videos on fades and barbering technique built the audience that later became his business ([YouTube Blog](https://blog.youtube/creator-and-artist-stories/chris-bossio-tomb45/)). Al Día News profiled him as a Latino entrepreneur who built his business starting from those video tutorials ([Al Día News](https://www.aldianews.com/en/leadership/entrepreneurs/latinos-success-story)), and he's appeared on the BiggerPockets Business Podcast alongside co-founder Daniel Santana discussing how they approached disrupting the barbering industry ([BiggerPockets](https://www.biggerpockets.com/blog/biggerpockets-business-podcast-05industry-thinking-differently-daniel-santana-chris-bossio)).

That YouTube audience fed two separate businesses: Headlines, a barbershop chain in the Tampa Bay area that Bossio set out to grow to five locations in five years and instead grew to seven, and Tomb45, a barbering products line named after an ancient Egyptian barber honored with his own tomb for his standing in the community. Tomb45's shaving gel line reportedly sold through 70 percent of its first-day inventory and now ships roughly 10,000 products a month across 15 countries, per the YouTube Blog feature.

## What members actually get

The Skool community is the free layer of a bigger operation, Tomb45 Academy, which runs a paid online subscription and in-person workshops for barbers wanting deeper technical training beyond what's posted for free. Inside Skool, members get fade and cutting technique content, business-building advice for barbers running their own chairs or shops, and a direct line to Bossio's team.

## Why it's grown

Bossio's story is a clean example of Skool used as a retention layer rather than an acquisition channel. The audience was already built, 350,000-plus YouTube subscribers who came for cutting tutorials, and the free community exists to keep that audience engaged and gradually introduce them to Tomb45 Academy's paid tier and product line. Because barbering is a trade learned hands-on, a free community anchored by someone with real chair-time credibility (Bossio estimates he now employs around 100 people across his ventures, per the YouTube Blog piece) has an easier time converting attention into paying students than a purely informational channel would.
