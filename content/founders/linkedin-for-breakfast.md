# Richard Woods and LinkedIn For Breakfast: Inside Skool's 6,300-Member LinkedIn Growth Community

LinkedIn For Breakfast is a free Skool community of roughly 6,309 members for people trying to scale a business using LinkedIn. Its founder arrived at online business through an unusually public route: national television.

## Who is Richard Woods

Richard Woods was a finalist on the BBC's "The Apprentice" in 2015, and his official bio describes him as achieving the highest sales performance across all tasks that series, winning eight of ten, a result the bio says is second only on the show's all-time list, along with breaking the show's single-day sales record ([Million Dollar Sprint bio](https://milliondollarsprint.com/about-richard-woods/)). He discussed that experience directly in a podcast interview titled "Interview with 'The Apprentice' Runner Up" ([Fearless.biz podcast](https://podcast.fearless.biz/e/interview-with-the-apprentice-runner-up-richard-woods/)). His LinkedIn profile identifies him as a "BBC Apprentice Finalist" and Million Dollar Sprint Coach ([LinkedIn](https://uk.linkedin.com/in/richardwoodsofficial)), and he maintains an active public following across Facebook and Instagram under variations of his name ([Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/richardwoodsofficial/); [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/richard_woods/)).

Since his television appearance, Woods has published multiple books, including a 2023 title on scaling service businesses without outside investment, and founded the Million Dollar Sprint coaching program for service-based entrepreneurs. LinkedIn For Breakfast is credited to him directly on the Skool community's own about page ([LinkedIn For Breakfast on Skool](https://www.skool.com/linkedin-for-breakfast/about)).

## What members actually get

The community is free to join and centers on what its own description calls "renowned four-day LinkedIn implementation Sprints," alongside weekly events, engagement pods, and community chat. It's built for coaches, consultants, course creators, and agency owners specifically, following the same client focus as Woods' paid Million Dollar Sprint coaching business, positioning LinkedIn growth as a lead-generation tool for service businesses rather than a general personal-branding exercise.

That framing keeps the community's advice grounded in a specific commercial outcome, scaling a service business through LinkedIn-driven leads, rather than generic tips about post frequency or profile optimization disconnected from actual revenue.

## Why it's grown

Woods' path into online business is unusually well-documented for a Skool founder: a nationally broadcast television appearance, records that were publicly reported at the time, and an independent podcast interview discussing that period, together give LinkedIn For Breakfast a founder whose credibility doesn't rest solely on self-reported claims. At 6,309 members and free to join, the community likely draws heavily on the built-in recognition of an "Apprentice" finalist as a credential, which travels further with a general business audience than most niche credentials would.

The free-to-join structure also functions as a natural top-of-funnel for his paid Million Dollar Sprint coaching, giving service business owners a low-risk way to sample his approach to LinkedIn-driven growth before committing to paid coaching.
