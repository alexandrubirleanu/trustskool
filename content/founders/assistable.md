# Jorden Williams and Assistable.ai: The Founder Behind Skool's Top GoHighLevel AI Community

Assistable.ai describes itself as the most installed AI platform built specifically for GoHighLevel, and its Skool community, with well over 3,300 members, sits in the top 1 percent of communities on the platform by its own account. It's the community layer for a company founded by Jorden Williams.

## Who is Jorden Williams

Williams is listed as the founder of Assistable.ai on his LinkedIn profile ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jordenswilliams/)), and he's active under the handle "dubs" on the company's own Skool community, where members reference him directly by first name in posts. He also posts under the handle @j2will_ on X ([X profile](https://x.com/j2will_?lang=en)).

According to a company press release distributed through MarketersMEDIA Newswire, Assistable.ai was founded in 2023 and had, by August 2025, surpassed 7.5 million AI voice calls while expanding through the acquisition of two resellers, LeadIndicator and EverBots, adding industry veterans Bernie White and Mike Copeland to its leadership team as Chief Revenue Officer and Chief Product Officer respectively ([MarketersMEDIA press release](https://news.marketersmedia.com/assistableai-announces-7-million-ai-voice-calls-and-global-expansion/89166545)). That release documents the company's growth and leadership additions without naming Williams directly, but it corroborates the scale and timeline his own profiles describe.

## What members actually get

The Skool community is built around deploying voice and chat AI for GoHighLevel agencies without requiring code, custom snapshots, or manual prompt engineering. Inside, members get access to a classroom covering setup and deployment, GoHighLevel-specific integration guides, and an active discussion space where the Assistable team, including Williams himself, answers configuration questions directly.

Because the tool plugs into an existing agency workflow rather than asking marketers to learn a new platform from scratch, the community doubles as both a support channel and an onboarding path for GoHighLevel agencies adding AI voice and chat to their existing client offerings.

## Why it's grown

Assistable.ai's growth lines up with a broader trend among GoHighLevel agencies looking to resell AI capabilities to their own clients without building the underlying technology themselves, and the reported 7.5 million calls processed by mid-2025 suggests real usage at scale rather than a tool sitting unused after installation. Acquiring established resellers like LeadIndicator and EverBots also gave the company an existing base of agency customers to fold directly into its own Skool community, rather than growing purely through organic Skool discovery.

For agencies already committed to GoHighLevel as their core platform, a tool built specifically for that ecosystem, with a founder actively present in the community answering questions, is a lower-friction option than a general-purpose AI platform that needs custom integration work.
