# Henri Guichané and ExplorIA: An Ex-Docusign Employee's 18,000-Member AI Community

ExplorIA's pitch is disarmingly direct: AI is changing the world, join the community and get ahead of it now. Nearly 19,000 people have taken that offer, joining a free French-language group built around the same YouTube channel that made its founder recognizable in the francophone AI scene in the first place.

## Who is Henri Guichané

Guichané is the cofounder of ExplorIA, a YouTube channel dedicated to making artificial intelligence understandable to a general audience, which has grown to around 40,000 subscribers ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@HenriExplorIA)). Before building the channel, he worked at Docusign, and his LinkedIn profile, listing him as based in the United Arab Emirates, shows a background that includes a Master's degree in Management de la Technologie et de l'Innovation from MINES ParisTech, completed in 2018 ([LinkedIn](https://ae.linkedin.com/in/hguichane)).

Guichané and ExplorIA were named among the ten AI influencers worth following in France by Jedha, a French AI training platform, in a roundup that specifically credits ExplorIA's approach of testing different AI tools and making them accessible to both curious beginners and working professionals ([Jedha, "Les 10 influenceurs IA à suivre"](https://www.jedha.co/formation-ia/les-10-influenceurs-ia-a-suivre-en-2025)). The same list separately recognizes Yassine Sdiri, another Skool community founder in the same French AI education space, which points to a small, identifiable cluster of creators driving much of the francophone AI education market right now.

## What members actually get

ExplorIA's free Skool community gives members access to resources not published on the public YouTube channel: optimized prompts, practical guides, and videos exclusive to the group, along with live events and Q&A sessions for members who want to go deeper on a specific question than a YouTube comment section allows. The tone throughout stays deliberately non-technical, aimed at people who are curious about AI but don't necessarily have a technical background, alongside freelancers and entrepreneurs who want to fold AI tools into daily work.

## Why it's grown

Guichané's Docusign background gives him a specific kind of credibility that's different from a purely content-first AI influencer: he's coming from inside a real software company before pivoting to teaching AI broadly, which shapes a more grounded, less hype-driven teaching style. Being named alongside a small set of recognized peers in the French AI influencer space, rather than claiming to be a lone authority, also lends the community a kind of borrowed credibility from an ecosystem that's actively cross-referencing itself.

For a French-speaking audience that's often underserved by English-first AI education content, a free, well-produced community run by someone with real enterprise software experience is a fairly direct value proposition, and one that doesn't require members to already speak fluent AI-industry English to benefit from it.
