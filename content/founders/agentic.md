# Roman Colman and The Agentic Lab: A National-Lab Researcher's Skool Community for AI Coding

The Agentic Lab bills itself as "the #1 Agentic Coding Community on Skool," and with around 2,300 members it's carved out a specific angle: stop "vibe coding" and learn to build with AI tools like Claude Code from first principles instead. It's run by Roman Colman.

## Who is Roman Colman

Colman's public footprint centers on LinkedIn, where one of his profiles identifies him as a Machine Learning Researcher affiliated with Los Alamos National Laboratory ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/roman-colman-123ml/)). His more active account posts regularly about agentic coding patterns and workflow tools, including posts comparing agentic RAG approaches and discussing focus and tooling for coding agents ([LinkedIn post on agentic RAG](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/roman-colman-2aa3ab3a7_agenticcoding-claudecode-aiworkflow-activity-7474176908738510848-42K6); [LinkedIn post on tools and focus](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/roman-colman-2aa3ab3a7_agenticcoding-claudecode-focus-activity-7466900467370000384-yRpY)). He also runs a consultancy, Manifold Solutions, whose site describes the same "coding agents, built into your business" positioning that anchors The Agentic Lab ([manifoldsolutionsai.com](https://manifoldsolutionsai.com/)).

## What members actually get

According to the community's own about page, Colman built The Agentic Lab because he "couldn't find a community that matched how I think about AI development," so members get direct exposure to his framework for agentic engineering rather than generic AI-tool tips. The group also functions as a talent and knowledge pool for his consulting work, with a cohort he calls "The Trajectory Engineers," business owners and developers he works with directly to fold coding agents into their operations. The stated scale, thousands of members and roughly 2,200 vetted technicals, suggests the group has grown well past a simple discussion board into something closer to a staffed talent network layered on top of a course.

## Why it's grown

The timing here matters as much as the content. Coding agents like Claude Code went from niche to mainstream through 2025 and 2026, and Colman positioned himself early with a research background that differentiates his pitch from the flood of generic "AI coding tips" content elsewhere on Skool. Rather than treating the community purely as a course product, he's using it as a pipeline into his consulting practice, members who stand out become part of the "vetted technicals" pool he draws on for client work, which gives the group a two-way incentive structure: members get access to his frameworks, and he gets a testing ground for talent and ideas he can point clients toward.

His split LinkedIn presence, one account leaning on the national-lab research credential, the other built almost entirely around daily agentic-coding posts, mirrors that dual identity: researcher-turned-practitioner, using the credibility of the first to sell the urgency of the second.
