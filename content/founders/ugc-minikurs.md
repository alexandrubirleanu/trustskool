# Bianca Aßmann and UGC Society: Inside Skool's 6,000-Member German UGC Community

UGC Society (Minikurs) is a free Skool community of about 6,076 members teaching German, Austrian, and Swiss creators how to start earning money as user-generated-content creators, aiming to help members reach their first €1,000. It's run by Bianca Aßmann, who built her own UGC career before turning it into a teaching brand.

## Who is Bianca Aßmann

Bianca Aßmann runs a YouTube channel under the name UGCQUEEN, where her channel description identifies her as founder of UGC Society ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@UGCQUEEN)). Her Skool profile lists her under her full name, Bianca Assmann, tying her personal identity directly to the community ([Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@bianca-assmann-6060)). The community's paid tier, run through UGC Society's own site, has independent customer reviews on Trustpilot under the business name ugc-society.io ([Trustpilot reviews](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/ugc-society.io)).

According to her own community's positioning, Aßmann started doing UGC work herself in 2021, before turning what she learned into a teaching business, which by its own account has since grown into the largest German-language UGC community in the DACH region.

## What members actually get

UGC Society (Minikurs) is free to join and functions as a smaller, introductory version of Aßmann's full UGC Society program, aimed at showing complete beginners how to get started as UGC creators and land their first paid brand deals. The stated goal is modest and specific: help members earn their first €1,000 from UGC work, rather than promising overnight full-time income. Members who want to go deeper can move into the paid version of UGC Society, which includes fuller video training, mentoring, and portfolio-building support.

That structure, a small free community proving the concept before asking for payment, matches how Aßmann describes her own entry into UGC: starting small and building outward, rather than presenting UGC as an instant path to large income.

## Why it's grown

UGC content creation has grown quickly as a side income category, particularly among women looking for flexible, camera-based work that doesn't require an existing social media following, and the free Minikurs format lowers the barrier to trying it. At 6,076 members and no cost to join, the free community works as a funnel toward Aßmann's paid UGC Society program, letting curious beginners test the waters before committing financially.

Her own documented UGC background, starting in 2021 and building the brand from there, combined with a YouTube channel actively teaching the same skills, gives members a founder they can watch working in the same medium she's teaching, rather than a hidden face behind a course.
