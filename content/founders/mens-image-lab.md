# Alex Costa and Men's Image Lab: Inside Skool's 5,500-Member Men's Style Community

Men's Image Lab is a $15-a-month Skool community built around a single, blunt premise captured in its own tagline: "Your image is everything. What does yours say about you?" With over 5,500 members, it's run by Alex Costa, a men's style content creator whose reach spans well beyond Skool itself.

## Who is Alex Costa

Costa was born in Belo Horizonte, Brazil, and started his YouTube channel in September 2011, initially posting gameplay footage for Call of Duty before pivoting into the men's fashion, grooming, and lifestyle content that built his current following. A biography site describing his career says Google later hired him as a strategic content engagement manager before he moved into full-time content creation ([The City Celeb biography](https://www.thecityceleb.com/biography/personality/content-creator/youtuber/alex-costa-biography-siblings-age-net-worth-girlfriend-wikipedia-youtube/)). His own Skool profile lists him as based in New York, NY, and describes him as a content creator with more than 8 million followers across platforms ([Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@alexcosta)). His LinkedIn profile identifies him as the founder of Forte Series, described elsewhere as a men's haircare manufacturing company based in New York ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexcostaofficial/)).

## What members actually get

Alongside the flagship Men's Image Lab, Costa also runs a smaller, separately priced premium tier called Men's Image Lab Premium, aimed at members who want a deeper system for personal style, physique, and what its own materials describe as building "magnetic charisma." The core community's positioning, entirely built around personal image and what it signals to other people, mirrors the hairstyle tutorials, product reviews, and style breakdowns Costa has been publishing on YouTube for over a decade. That same content pipeline doubles as a distribution channel for Forte Series, the haircare line he built under the Forte Series manufacturing company, which shows up repeatedly across his own social posts as he demonstrates the products in use.

## Why it's grown

Costa isn't building an audience from inside Skool, he's porting one over. A decade-plus YouTube career, a cross-platform following in the millions, and a haircare product line already on the market give Men's Image Lab a ready-made customer base that trusts his taste before they ever see a Skool invite link. That's the same growth pattern behind most large creator-led Skool communities: the platform organizes and monetizes an audience that already exists rather than building one from scratch. For members, the appeal is straightforward: they're paying to follow the same style playbook that already carried Costa from a gaming channel to a men's grooming brand with millions of followers watching.
