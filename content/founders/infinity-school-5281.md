# Kai Nemzer and Geometry Smash: Inside Skool's 6,200-Member Geometry Dash Community

Geometry Smash bills itself as the number one Geometry Dash community on Skool, with roughly 6,269 members gathered around a single mobile rhythm-platformer game. It's run by a content creator whose real identity is easy to trace, because his fame predates the Skool community by years.

## Who is Kai Nemzer

Kai Nemzer, who goes by KaiGuy online, is a YouTuber known specifically for short-form content about Geometry Dash, and his channel has passed 2 million subscribers according to fan-documented profiles of his career ([Famous Birthdays profile](https://www.famousbirthdays.com/people/kai-nemzer.html); [Wikitubia fan wiki](https://youtube.fandom.com/wiki/KaiGuy)). His Skool activity is tied directly to his real name: his Skool profile lists him as Kai Nemzer under the handle @nemzer ([Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@nemzer)), and he's been covered by the Skool Games' own "Skool Stories" podcast in an episode describing the community he built ([Skool Stories episode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0-SvsnFOWyM)).

That coverage describes Nemzer running Geometry Smash alongside an earlier community called Dash Academy, together drawing thousands of Geometry Dash players, and notes his groups have placed in the Skool Games competition multiple times and hosted in-person meetups for members.

## What members actually get

Geometry Smash is free to join and is built specifically for players of Geometry Dash, the rhythm-based platforming game originally created by Swedish developer Robert Topala. As the self-described number one community for the game, it functions as a hub for players to discuss levels, share content, and connect with other fans, drawing on Nemzer's own standing as one of the most recognized creators in the Geometry Dash content space.

Niche gaming communities like this succeed less on formal curriculum and more on the founder's credibility within that specific fandom, and Nemzer's YouTube following, built entirely around Geometry Dash content, gives him exactly that kind of standing with the exact audience the community targets.

## Why it's grown

At 6,269 members and free to join, Geometry Smash benefits from Nemzer's YouTube audience functioning as a direct, ready-made pipeline: fans already watching his Geometry Dash videos have an obvious next step in joining a community run by the same person. The Skool Stories podcast coverage of his communities also points to repeated success in the Skool Games competition and in-person mastermind events, both signals of an actively engaged membership rather than a passive follower count.

Because Geometry Dash has a famously dedicated global fanbase built around level creation, speedrunning, and competitive play, a creator with genuine standing in that scene, like Nemzer's multi-million subscriber channel, can convert a fraction of that audience into a sizable, active Skool community relatively quickly.
