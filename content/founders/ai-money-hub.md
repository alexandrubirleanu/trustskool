# Lindsay Gonzales and AI Money Hub: The Consultant Teaching Skool Members to Resell AI Services

AI Money Hub sells itself on a specific angle: exclusive affiliate offers where the company builds the product and members resell it, rather than a course on how to build AI tools from scratch. At $49 a month with just over 3,040 members, it's run by Lindsay Gonzales.

## Who is Lindsay Gonzales

Gonzales runs Automate AI Consulting, a business reflected in her own YouTube channel of the same name, where she publishes short-form content on AI automation ([Automate AI Consulting on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@AutomateAIConsulting)). She's listed in the IAAIC's public directory of AI consultants, based in Fresno, which independently verifies her positioning as a certified AI consultant and process automation specialist ([IAAIC directory listing](https://www.iaaic.org/united-states/fresno/ai-consultant/lindsay-gonzales)). Her LinkedIn profile ties that same Automate AI Consulting identity to her name directly ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lindsaygonzales33/)).

According to her own account, Gonzales built automations for free early on, then as a freelancer on Upwork, before transitioning into higher-ticket AI consulting retainers, a progression that lines up with the resell-focused, monetization-first framing of AI Money Hub itself.

## What members actually get

Rather than teaching members to build custom AI tools, AI Money Hub is built around done-for-you offers, meaning Gonzales and her team have already built the underlying product or service, and members are trained to sell and deliver it to their own clients. The stated goal is landing a first paying client as quickly as possible, with training and a network specifically oriented toward closing deals rather than technical AI education for its own sake.

## Why it's grown

A "we build it, you resell it" model lowers the technical barrier to entry considerably compared to communities that expect members to learn AI development themselves before they can earn anything, which likely broadens AI Money Hub's addressable audience to people with sales or marketing backgrounds rather than only technical ones. Gonzales's own documented path, from free automations, to freelance platform work, to consulting retainers, gives the pitch a specific, gradual trajectory that members can map their own progress against rather than a vague promise of AI riches.

Being listed independently in a professional AI consultant directory alongside her own YouTube and LinkedIn presence also gives Gonzales a level of external verification that a lot of "AI money" communities on Skool don't have behind their founders.

The "AI money" category on Skool is crowded with communities promising vague riches from tools members never actually get access to, and a model built around reselling an already-built product removes a lot of the guesswork members would otherwise face trying to package and price their own AI service from scratch. That done-for-you structure is likely the biggest single differentiator setting AI Money Hub apart from purely educational AI communities in the same broad niche.
