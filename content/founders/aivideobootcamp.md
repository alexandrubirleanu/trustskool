# Daniel Riley and AI Video Bootcamp: Inside Skool's 25,000-Member AI Video Community

AI Video Bootcamp is a $9-a-month Skool community with more than 25,000 members focused entirely on AI-generated video and image creation — AI influencers, UGC-style ad content, and AI "clones." It's co-founded by Daniel Riley, a British AI content creator whose own viral video work is the community's best proof of concept, alongside Matt Stark, who runs the business and infrastructure side.

## Who is Daniel Riley

Daniel Riley is a 27-year-old British creator whose primary public portfolio is his TikTok channel, @timetravellerpov, which produces AI-generated historical POV clips — the kind of "POV: you wake up in Pompeii on eruption day" content that has become a recognizable AI video subgenre. That channel has accumulated more than 586,000 followers and 12.8 million likes ([Skoolmakers review](https://skoolmakers.com/communities/ai-video-bootcamp/)). He also posts under @promptedbyriley on Instagram, and inside Skool he's listed under the handle @oracle as the community's founder/owner.

His co-founder, Matt Stark, is described on the AI Video Bootcamp marketing materials as the founder of Brightdock, described there as a "multi-million dollar creative agency," with claims of having been ranked #1 on Freelancer.com and having worked with clients including Rita Ora and Mike Tyson ([Community Hunter review](https://communityhunter.com/reviews/ai-video-bootcamp-daniel-riley/)). Those specific client and ranking claims come from the business's own promotional materials rather than independent verification, so they're worth treating as self-reported rather than confirmed facts — a distinction that matters more than usual in a niche (AI content, agency services) where inflated case-study claims are common.

## What members actually get

The stated focus is building practical AI video and image skills: creating consistent AI-generated characters (a persistent technical challenge in AI video, since most models struggle to keep a "character" looking the same across shots), producing AI-generated UGC-style ad content for brands, and building "AI clones" of a creator's own likeness for content scaling. Members get personal 1-to-1 feedback, weekly updates on new AI tools as the underlying models change rapidly, and instruction on advanced prompting technique, positioned as accessible to complete beginners with no technical background required.

The price is set to step up from $9/month to $50/month once the community reaches 25,300 members — the same scarcity-driven pricing mechanic used by several of the largest AI-focused Skool communities, designed to create urgency for people on the fence about joining.

## Why it's grown

AI Video Bootcamp benefits from riding one of the fastest-moving trends in the creator economy: AI video generation tools improved dramatically through 2025 and into 2026, and demand for people who can actually use them well (for ads, for content, for building AI-driven personas) has outpaced the supply of credible teachers. Riley's personal TikTok success gives the community an unusually direct, visible proof point — prospective members can watch his own AI-generated content go viral before deciding whether to pay to learn the process, which is a much shorter trust chain than most educational communities can offer.

Pairing a creator with a large organic audience (Riley) alongside an operator with claimed agency and client-services experience (Stark) is a common structure for scaling these communities past the point where a single creator's bandwidth becomes the bottleneck.
