# Noelle Russell and I Love AI Community: Inside Skool's 2,800-Member Responsible AI Community

I Love AI Community promises to turn AI disruption into a competitive advantage rather than a threat, aimed at leaders trying to unlock AI's potential for their career or business. The free community has grown to roughly 2,804 members.

## Who is Noelle Russell

Russell built her career inside the AI divisions of some of the largest technology companies in the world, holding roles at NPR, Microsoft, IBM, Accenture, AWS, and Amazon Alexa before going independent ([NoelleRussell.ai](https://noellerussell.ai/)). She says she has built more than 100 conversational AI applications since 2014, work that at Amazon Alexa alone reached roughly 2 million unique users and generated some 30,000 four- and five-star reviews, and that her work at Microsoft speaking to Fortune 500 executives and running internal AI hackathons influenced close to $1 billion in Microsoft AI revenue during her tenure there. Microsoft has recognized her as a Most Valuable Professional for Responsible AI for four consecutive years. She's listed as a keynote speaker by two independent speaker bureaus, Ragan Communications and Leading Authorities ([Ragan](https://www.ragan.com/ragan_speakers/noelle-russell/); [Leading Authorities](https://www.leadingauthorities.com/speakers/noelle-russell)), is profiled on the analyst network Thinkers360 as founder of the AI Leadership Institute ([Thinkers360](https://www.thinkers360.com/tl/noelle)), and published the book *Scaling Responsible AI: From Enthusiasm to Execution*.

## What members actually get

The community's own pitch centers on giving business owners a path to increase the value of their business, build automations, and deploy "AI employees" safely and at scale, aimed squarely at leaders rather than developers. It sits alongside Russell's separate, more structured AI Leadership Institute, positioning the free Skool community as an accessible front door into her broader responsible-AI curriculum and consulting work.

## Why it's grown

Most AI-focused Skool communities are run by people whose credibility rests entirely on recent AI-tool experimentation, which makes Russell's decade-plus inside the AI divisions of Amazon, Microsoft, IBM, and Accenture, corroborated by independent speaker bureaus and an analyst network rather than resting solely on her own community's marketing, an unusually well-documented background for this niche. Her specific angle, responsible and safe deployment rather than purely speed and hacks, also differentiates the community from the much larger cluster of "AI hustle" groups on the platform, appealing instead to business leaders who are cautious about AI risk and want a credentialed guide rather than the fastest possible adoption. Free access lowers the barrier for exactly that risk-averse leadership audience to test the waters before committing to her paid Institute or consulting engagements.
