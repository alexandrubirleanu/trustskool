# Brad Lea and REAL SUCCESS: Inside Skool's 15,000-Member Sales Mindset Community

REAL SUCCESS is a free Skool community, sitting at roughly 15,000 members, built around a single promise: teach people the mindset, skill sets and habits it takes to become unstoppable. The name on the door is Brad Lea, a sales trainer whose reach extends well beyond Skool into podcasting, software and a separate paid closing program.

## Who is Brad Lea

Brad Lea was born January 27, 1969, in Cottage Grove, Oregon, and by his own retelling didn't finish school, instead going straight into car sales, where he sharpened the persuasion skills that would define his career. He later went through a period of real financial hardship, including a stretch of homelessness, before rebuilding around sales training as a business ([Sramana Mitra interview](https://www.sramanamitra.com/2016/05/11/from-high-school-drop-out-to-20m-in-revenue-brad-leas-journey-with-lightspeed-vt-part-3/)).

In 1999 he founded LightSpeed VT, a virtual training platform that Sramana Mitra's interview series reports was generating over $13 million a year, with clients that have included Tony Robbins and Grant Cardone. He later launched Closer School, an on-demand program teaching high-ticket closing, and has run a joint mastermind called The Revolution with sales trainer Andy Elliott. A review of Closer School on Ippei.com describes Lea as having closed more than 800 deals across companies including GM, UFC and Tony Robbins' organization ([Ippei review](https://ippei.com/closer-school-brad-lea/)). He also hosts the long-running "Dropping Bombs" podcast and posts under Brad Lea TV.

## What members actually get

REAL SUCCESS is positioned as the free, no-cost front door to Lea's broader sales and mindset teaching. The community description frames it plainly: mindset, skill sets and habits, the three levers Lea says separate people who talk about success from people who build it. Unlike Closer School, which is a paid, structured curriculum, REAL SUCCESS on Skool functions as an open space where his existing audience, drawn from Dropping Bombs listeners and his social following, can interact directly, ask questions and get exposed to the same closing and persuasion frameworks he sells at a higher ticket elsewhere.

That structure, free community feeding a paid backend, is one of the more common patterns on Skool, but it works especially well for someone like Lea who already has a built-in audience conditioned to his voice through years of podcasting.

## Why it's grown

Lea didn't need Skool to build an audience. He needed a place to put one he'd already spent two decades building, first through LightSpeed VT's corporate clients, then through Dropping Bombs and its guest list of entrepreneurs and closers. Skool gives that audience a lower-friction, community-style entry point than a podcast feed or a paid course, and a free tier removes the biggest objection for listeners who are curious about his sales philosophy but not yet ready to pay for Closer School.

The other piece is credibility transfer. A high-ticket closing trainer whose own biography includes selling into GM, UFC and Tony Robbins' companies carries a kind of proof that's hard to manufacture inside a community platform alone. REAL SUCCESS borrows that proof rather than trying to build it from scratch, which is a large part of why it was able to reach 15,000 members without needing to be the primary product in Lea's business.
