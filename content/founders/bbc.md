# David Iya and Business Builders Club: Inside Skool's 8,000-Member Founder Community

Business Builders Club is an 8,037-member Skool community for first-time founders and SaaS builders working toward their first consistent revenue. It runs at $33/month for current members, a locked-in rate the community says will rise to $89/month once it hits 8,100 members [Skool](https://www.skool.com/bbc/about).

## Who runs it

The community is founded and led by David Iya. Iya's LinkedIn identifies him as co-founder and CTO of Eze, a Y Combinator W20 company [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/davidiya/), and that YC connection is independently corroborated by a 2020 Forbes article covering Eze, the secondhand-smartphone marketplace Iya built with co-founder Josh Nzewi to fix inefficiencies in the used-phone trading market [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/frederickdaso/2020/09/08/its-eze-to-trade-smartphones-thanks-to-these-two-y-combinator-founders/). Iya's own materials and bio describe him as a Forbes 30 Under 30 honoree who has built companies across 30+ countries and generated over $70 million in online GMV [nocap.blog](https://nocap.blog/founder/david-iya/); the Y Combinator and Eze details are independently verifiable via Forbes, while the specific Forbes 30 Under 30 honor and the $70M GMV figure are self-reported and not independently confirmed in this research.

Iya has also been described as someone who built two Top #10 Skool communities in under 50 days, a claim that appears in Skool's own creator community discussions [Skool](https://www.skool.com/thedirectory/the-guy-who-built-two-top-10-skools-in-50-days-is-taking-on-new-clients) rather than in independent press.

## What members actually get

Business Builders Club is structured around what it calls three proven paths to a first $5,000 month: product launches, Upwork-based service systems, and templated SaaS builds. The community gives members access to 300+ N8N automation templates and more than 100 validated SaaS ideas, alongside live weekly classes, according to the group's own listing. The pitch is less "learn business theory" and more "copy a system that has already worked for someone else," which is a common structure for Skool communities in the founder/SaaS space but is executed here at unusually large scale for the price point.

## Why it's grown

Two factors stand out. First, member reviews are genuinely strong: Business Builders Club sits at a 5.0/5 rating across its published reviews, with essentially all reviewers saying they'd recommend it [ProveWorth](https://proveworth.com/community/business-builders-club). Second, Iya's own YC and startup-building background gives him more substantive founder credibility than many "how to make money online" Skool operators, since Eze is a real, previously covered company rather than a business built solely to sell a course about business. For a community explicitly targeting first-time founders, having a leader who has actually built and sold real companies — not just taught about building them — is a meaningful differentiator, and it shows up in how the community frames its content: fewer motivational posts, more shipped templates and specific dollar-amount playbooks.

Prospective members should still treat the more sweeping bio claims (30 Under 30, exact GMV figures) as self-reported until independently verified, while weighing the community against its member reviews and its verifiable YC/Eze track record.
