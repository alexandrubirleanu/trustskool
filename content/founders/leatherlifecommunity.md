# Joe Meling and Leather Life Community: The Signature Artist Teaching Skool's Leathercrafters

Leather Life Community has grown to roughly 3,200 members around a simple pitch: connect with other people in the leathercraft industry, learn from those ahead of you, and help those coming up behind you. It's run by Joe Meling, a working leathercraft business owner rather than a hobbyist teaching on the side.

## Who is Joe Meling

Meling is the owner and designer behind 23+, a family-run leathercraft business he's operated with his wife Marti since 2013, after transitioning out of a career riding bulls ([23+ About Us](https://twentythreeplus.com/pages/about-us)). Weaver Leather Supply, a major supplier in the leathercraft industry, features Meling on its own site as a signature artist, a relationship that began in late 2018 and gives him a recognized, third-party-endorsed position within the craft rather than a self-declared one ([Weaver Leather Supply featured craftsman page](https://www.weaverleathersupply.com/pages/featured-craftsman-joe-meling)). Through that partnership, Weaver also sells his leather tooling pattern books directly, a product line built specifically around his own original designs.

He also runs a YouTube channel under his own name, where he shares tooling techniques and pattern-drawing instruction aimed at people learning the craft from scratch ([Joe Meling on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@joemeling3216)).

## What members actually get

Leather Life Community offers monthly patterns, instructional videos, and a support network spanning skill levels, from total beginners to advanced leatherworkers refining custom techniques. Meling has narrowed his own commercial work over the years to focus specifically on custom belts, and the patterns and instruction he brings into the community reflect that same specialization in tooling and pattern design rather than broader leather goods manufacturing.

## Why it's grown

Meling's credibility in this community comes from a genuinely unusual career arc, competitive bull riding into full-time leathercraft, and it's backed by a real commercial relationship with Weaver Leather Supply, one of the larger names in leathercraft materials, rather than resting purely on self-promotion. That endorsement matters in a craft hobby space where buyers and students can be understandably skeptical of anyone claiming expertise without a visible body of sold work behind them.

Leathercraft is also a slower-growing, more niche hobby than most categories on Skool, which tends to produce smaller but more genuinely engaged communities built around mentorship between skill levels rather than rapid, marketing-driven growth.

Meling's own pattern books, sold through Weaver and through his 23+ storefront, also mean members joining the Skool community already have a tangible product they can point to when deciding whether his teaching style suits them, rather than having to take his instructional ability purely on faith. That's a real advantage in a craft niche where technique is highly visual and easy to evaluate before ever paying for coaching.
