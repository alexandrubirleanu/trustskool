# Jake Van Clief and Clief Notes: A Marine Veteran's 42,000-Member AI Systems Community

Clief Notes describes itself with a line that's easy to write off as marketing until you check it: "What we give away free beats most paid courses. Build durable AI systems with a Marine vet and Edinburgh researcher." Over 42,000 people have joined a free community built around exactly that combination of backgrounds.

## Who is Jake Van Clief

Van Clief spent eight years in the U.S. Marine Corps as an Avionics and Cryptographic Technician, working on F-35 and F-18 fighter jets, a role confirmed on his own LinkedIn profile, which lists him as a U.S. Marine Corps veteran ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jake-van-clief-74b66915a/)). That military role is also where he says he first encountered AI systems in a practical setting, through the Autonomic Logistics Information System used to maintain the F-35.

After leaving the Marines, he earned a BA in Political Science and Philosophy from Flagler College before completing an MSc in AI Governance at the University of Edinburgh, where he conducted published research. He went on to found Eduba, a technical consultancy, and has since trained more than 1,000 employees at Fortune 500 companies including Pacific Life, Colgate-Palmolive, and KPMG UK. In March 2026, he published a paper on arXiv titled "Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agentic Architecture," which underpins much of what he teaches inside the community ([Substack](https://jakevanclief.substack.com/)).

## What members actually get

Clief Notes offers more than 40 free lessons covering the fundamentals Van Clief considers durable rather than trend-dependent: folder architecture for AI projects, prompt frameworks, computing basics, and hands-on work with tools like Claude Code. The framing throughout is explicitly systems-first, aimed at people who want methods that hold up over a decade rather than tutorials chasing whichever tool is trending that month.

Paid tiers above the free level unlock enterprise consulting case studies and more advanced applied material, drawing directly on the corporate training work Van Clief has done through Eduba, giving members a look at how the same frameworks get deployed inside large organizations rather than just personal projects.

## Why it's grown

Clief Notes reportedly reached 16,000 members in under a month with zero paid advertising, an unusually fast start for a Skool community, and the growth since has continued along the same trajectory. Part of that speed comes from the specificity of Van Clief's positioning: "Marine vet turned AI governance researcher" is a distinct enough story that it travels well, and it's backed by verifiable credentials rather than vague claims of expertise.

The other part is timing and restraint. Rather than promising shortcuts in a space full of hype, Van Clief's systems-first pitch, folder structures as agentic architecture, methods over tool-of-the-week tutorials, appeals directly to people who are already burned out on AI content that doesn't hold up past a single use case. That's a narrower audience than "learn AI," but it's one that sticks around and refers others in.
