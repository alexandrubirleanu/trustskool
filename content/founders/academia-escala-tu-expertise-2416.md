# Agustin Capalbo and Escala Tu Expertise: Inside Skool's Free Agency-Scaling Community

Escala Tu Expertise is a free Skool community with roughly 2,227 members aimed at Spanish-speaking agency owners, consultants, coaches, and course creators who already have paying clients but lack the systems to grow past their current ceiling. It's run by Agustin Capalbo, alongside co-founder Tobias Blousson, who built the business from a support-desk job at Google into a multi-client marketing agency of his own.

## Who is Agustin Capalbo

According to the community's own about page and his company's site, Capalbo took a position on Google's Ads support team in 2019, then concluded that a job there wouldn't get him to his actual goals and left to start offering digital marketing services directly to private clients, first under the name @marketingclasificado ([escalatuexpertise.com, "Sobre Nosotros"](https://escalatuexpertise.com/sobre-nosotros/)). From there he moved from general freelance services into specialized consulting, and eventually from one-on-one service work into a structured, course-based program he calls "Acelera," which combines information, coaching, and community rather than trading his own hours for client work. The Skool community's about page adds that he now runs an agency with more than 80 active clients, has personally put over $1 million into advertising, and says he has helped more than 80 experts scale their businesses, with some surpassing $80,000 a month ([skool.com/academia-escala-tu-expertise-2416](https://www.skool.com/academia-escala-tu-expertise-2416)). Tobias Blousson is credited on the same about page as admissions and operations director for the business.

## What members actually get

Because the community itself is free to join, it functions as the top of a funnel rather than the full product: the pitch is "scale free with proven strategies" aimed specifically at people who already bill at least $2,000 a month and have real client results, not beginners still figuring out their offer. The stated focus for that audience is building a genuinely high-value offer, a customer acquisition system, a reliable traffic channel, and an efficient team, the same four pillars Capalbo describes shaping his own transition from freelancer to agency owner. Free members get access to that framework and the community itself, with the paid "Acelera" program presumably available as the next step for those who want closer, structured support.

## Why it's grown

Capalbo's story maps cleanly onto the exact audience he's recruiting: agency owners and consultants stuck at the same "too big to freelance, too small to scale" stage he was once in, being taught by someone who documents having walked that specific path rather than an outside consultant theorizing about it. Making the Skool community itself free removes the biggest point of friction for that audience, who are typically wary of committing budget to yet another course before seeing whether the operator's advice actually applies to their situation. A free front door paired with a named back-end program ("Acelera") is a fairly standard but effective structure for converting a large free audience into a smaller base of paying clients over time, especially in the Spanish-language coaching and consulting space, where trust in the specific operator's track record tends to matter more than generic scaling advice.
