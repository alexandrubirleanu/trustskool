# Meg the Creator and the Old AIM Community: Inside Skool's Migrating Content Creators Club

This particular Skool listing, now labeled "OLD AIM (Migrating)," is a $277 one-time-payment community with roughly 3,290 members that's actively being wound down in favor of a new home, with its own about page pointing members to go.megthecreator.com/aim-sales. The person behind it is Megan Spencer, who publishes under the name Meg the Creator.

## Who is Meg the Creator

Spencer describes herself as an introvert-friendly entrepreneur, mentor, and content strategist who teaches everyday people how to build sustainable online income without becoming a traditional, follower-chasing influencer. In an interview with Authority Magazine, she walked through the thinking behind what she calls her Anti-Influencer Method, an approach built around Amazon Onsite Commissions, user-generated content work, freelancing, and TikTok Shop rather than viral growth or personal branding ([Authority Magazine interview](https://medium.com/authority-magazine/content-creators-megan-spencer-meg-the-creator-on-5-things-you-need-to-make-compelling-and-4fd19673b282)). She maintains a YouTube channel under the Meg the Creator name where she publishes tutorials aligned with that same approach ([Meg the Creator on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@megthecreator)), and her LinkedIn profile under Megan Spencer confirms the same professional identity behind both ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/megan-r-spencer/)).

Her personal site, megthecreator.com, is also the destination this Skool listing is actively migrating members toward, which ties the "AIM" community directly back to her as its operator rather than leaving that connection to inference ([Meg the Creator](https://megthecreator.com/)).

## What members actually get

While it was active under its original name, the Content Creators Club paid community centered on Spencer's Anti-Introvert content strategies, teaching students to earn through Amazon Onsite Commissions, UGC work, freelancing, and TikTok Shop, the same four pillars she describes publicly as her core teaching framework. The one-time $277 price point suggests a course-style structure rather than an ongoing coaching subscription.

Members currently landing on this listing are being redirected off Skool entirely, toward a sales page on Spencer's own domain, which suggests she's consolidating her paid community onto owned infrastructure rather than continuing to run it through a third-party platform.

## Why it's moving

Migrating an established, paying community away from Skool is a real decision with real churn risk, and creators generally only take that step when they want tighter control over billing, branding, or the member experience than a third-party platform allows. Spencer's public profile, an Authority Magazine interview, an active YouTube channel, and her own branded site, gives her an audience base sturdy enough to make that kind of platform move without starting over from zero.

It's also a useful reminder for anyone researching a Skool community before joining: a listing's current name and status can shift quickly when the creator behind it is actively restructuring their business, and checking whether a community is being actively maintained versus wound down is worth doing before paying a one-time fee like the $277 asked here.
