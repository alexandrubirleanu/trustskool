# Tim Han and Heartfluencer Family: Inside Skool's Private Success Insider Alumni Community

Heartfluencer Family isn't a public Skool community in the usual sense. With around 3,050 members, it's a private space reserved for students who've already gone through specific Tim Han programs, LMA, UYFP, ELA, or WCA, making it more of an alumni home base than an open discovery community.

## Who is Tim Han

Han built Success Insider starting in 2016, when, according to his own about page, it began as what he calls the world's fastest growing personal development YouTube channel before expanding into a broader business of coaching, courses, and live events ([About Tim Han](https://www.successinsider.com/about-tim-han/)). He's described on that page as a coach, author, entrepreneur, and international speaker, and Success Insider now runs a full slate of named programs, including Life Mastery Achievers (LMA), an Unlock Your Full Potential package (UYFP), an Extraordinary Living Accelerator (ELA), and a World Class Achievers retreat (WCA), all of which feed into the Heartfluencer Family community as a shared alumni space.

Han also hosts the Success Insider Podcast, distributed on Spotify under his name ([Success Insider Podcast](https://open.spotify.com/show/1B2h1AOexKXk86DDa4Ld02)), and the business has accumulated well over a hundred pages of customer reviews on Trustpilot, a mix of praise and criticism typical of a large, long-running coaching operation ([Trustpilot](https://ca.trustpilot.com/review/successinsider.com)).

## What members actually get

Because entry is restricted to graduates of specific paid programs, Heartfluencer Family functions less as a lead-generation tool and more as retention infrastructure, a place for Han to keep past students engaged, run challenges like the community's namesake Heartfluencer Challenge, and surface testimonials and continued participation from people who've already paid for one of his flagship courses.

That's a different model from most Skool communities on this list, which are typically built to attract new members directly. Here, Skool is being used as the "after" layer for a coaching business that already runs its acquisition funnel elsewhere, through YouTube, the podcast, and Han's own programs.

## Why it's grown

Success Insider's foundation goes back nearly a decade of consistent YouTube output, which gave Han a large audience to draw from long before Skool existed as a platform. Feeding graduates of LMA, UYFP, ELA, and WCA into a single shared community rather than leaving them scattered across separate program-specific groups also means the alumni base compounds over time, every new cohort adds to the same pool instead of starting a fresh, disconnected group.

For a personal development brand built on repeat program purchases, that kind of unified alumni space gives Han a direct channel for promoting new offers and events to people who've already demonstrated they're willing to pay for his coaching, which is a meaningfully different growth lever than the open, first-touch communities most of Skool is built around.
