# Brad Goh and Trading Tribe: Inside Skool's 26,000-Member Trading Community

Trading Tribe is a Skool community of roughly 26,000 traders built by Brad Goh, a Singapore-based full-time trader who describes himself as having gone from retail beginner to seven-figure trader before building an education and software business around the process. Note upfront: while this community is sometimes referenced as free, Skool's current listing and Trading Tribe's own about page show a $1/month entry price — worth knowing before you assume zero-cost access.

## Who is Brad Goh

Brad Goh, who also posts as "The Trading Geek," describes himself on X as a full-time seven-figure trader and founder of The 1% Club, "a tight-knit community helping traders become consistent," as well as the founder of EdgeFlo, which he calls a "trading superapp reshaping how retail traders trade, journal, and manage risk" ([Brad Goh on X](https://x.com/Bradgohtrades/status/1983394876973621655)). He's also profiled as "the anti-guru trader" in a piece on TechBullion focused on his emphasis on proof and discipline over hype ([TechBullion profile](https://techbullion.com/brad-goh-the-anti-guru-trader-redefining-success-through-proof-and-discipline/)), and has a Crunchbase person profile tied to EdgeFlo ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/brad-goh)).

Goh has built a small portfolio of trading-education brands rather than a single product: Trading Tribe functions as the accessible, low-cost entry community, while The 1% Club (which he calls "The Harvard of Trading") is positioned as a more selective, higher-commitment training ground, and EdgeFlo is a separate SaaS product for trade journaling and risk management rather than an education product at all.

## What members actually get

Inside Trading Tribe, members get access to a Market Mechanics Bootcamp, a Price Action Mastery Course, something called the "1% Challenge," a "$10,000/Month Blueprint," and a library of past lecture recordings. The framing throughout is aspirational but specific: the community targets "young men and women forging their path to become profitable traders," with the $1/month price positioned as a low bar to entry that Goh's own materials say is meant to filter out casual browsers while still keeping the community accessible.

The 1% Club (Elite), by contrast, is a much smaller, invitation-feeling community of around 600 members, positioned as the more advanced, disciplined tier above Trading Tribe.

## Why it's grown

Retail trading education is one of the most consistently large niches across every creator platform, not just Skool, because the appeal (learn to trade, generate income from markets) is evergreen and the barrier to trying is low. Goh's specific positioning — built around discipline, behavioral accountability, and proof rather than lifestyle marketing or unrealistic screenshots — differentiates Trading Tribe from a crowded field of trading "guru" communities that lean heavily on flashy results. Building EdgeFlo as an actual software product for journaling and risk enforcement, rather than just selling courses, also gives Goh's brand a more substantive claim to being about trader behavior and process than pure signal-selling.

The near-zero price point for Trading Tribe itself functions as a wide top-of-funnel: it's cheap enough that virtually anyone curious about trading can join, with the real business model presumably resting on graduating engaged members toward EdgeFlo's software subscription or the more selective 1% Club.
