# Riri Manzat and Speak Italian with Riri: A TikTok Teacher's Skool Classroom for Conversational Italian

Speak Italian with Riri is a free Skool community, home to around 2,290 members, promising "a place where you ACTUALLY speak Italian from day one." That's a pointed pitch in a category full of grammar-drill apps and textbook-style courses, and it's built entirely around one instructor: Riri.

## Who is Riri Manzat

The community's own about page names her in full as Riri Manzat, and describes her background in her own words: "Hi, I'm Riri, and I've taught Italian to hundreds of students from all over the world." That teaching persona lives primarily on short-form video rather than a traditional classroom. She posts lessons, vocabulary quizzes and grammar breakdowns under the brand "Learn Italian With Riri" on TikTok, at @learnitalianwithriri ([TikTok profile](https://www.tiktok.com/@learnitalianwithriri)), where individual lesson clips and quiz-format videos regularly rack up views from learners testing their vocabulary in real time. On Instagram, posting as @learnitalian.withriri, she's built a following of roughly 95,000 ([Instagram profile](https://www.instagram.com/learnitalian.withriri/)) around the same short, bite-sized lesson format, covering everyday topics like family vocabulary and common beginner phrases. She also runs a course sales site at italianwithriri.com, where her Italian Starter Challenge is positioned as a 90-day path to conversational fluency, and a LinkedIn profile under the name Riri (Ana) Manzat is publicly listed as well ([LinkedIn profile](https://it.linkedin.com/in/riraim)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's description, the group is built around getting members speaking Italian immediately rather than starting with passive grammar study, with the stated goal of making fluency "inevitable" through ongoing group practice rather than solo study. That framing lines up with the teaching style visible across her TikTok and Instagram content, short, interactive, quiz-driven exchanges rather than long-form lecture videos.

## Why it's grown

Riri's TikTok and Instagram lesson clips function as an extended free sample of her teaching style before anyone joins anything, which is a fairly efficient funnel for a language-learning offer: prospective members can watch dozens of short lessons and get a feel for whether her explanations click before committing to a free community, let alone her paid course. The free Skool group then sits as a lower-commitment landing spot for that existing short-form audience, tens of thousands of followers across platforms, giving her a way to convert casual video viewers into a more engaged, ongoing community before asking anyone to pay for the full 90-day program. It's a pattern that shows up repeatedly among language-learning creators on Skool: the platform-native short video builds trust and demonstrates teaching style at scale, while the Skool group converts the most motivated fraction of that audience into a space where accountability and repetition, the two things solo app-based learning tends to lack, actually happen.
