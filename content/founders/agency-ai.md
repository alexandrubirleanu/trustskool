# Arseny Shatokhin and AI Agent Developer Academy: Inside Skool's 2,800-Member Builder Community

AI Agent Developer Academy pitches itself on a narrow, concrete promise: deliver a real AI agent to a real paying client within a month, not just complete a course. The free community has reached roughly 2,836 members on Skool, positioned against the wave of AI communities charging monthly fees for what its founder frames as theory over practice.

## Who is Arseny Shatokhin

The academy is run by Arseny Shatokhin, a software builder who founded the AI agent platform Agencii.ai in 2022 under the parent brand VRSEN ([ippei.com review](https://ippei.com/agencii-ai/)). Before building Agencii, Shatokhin ran an AI automation agency that was generating around $80,000 a month, and he shut it down deliberately, choosing to build a more scalable product business around agent frameworks instead of continuing to trade his own time for client work ([ippei.com review](https://ippei.com/agencii-ai/)). That platform runs on the Agency Swarm framework, which lets a builder assemble a team of specialized AI agents into what Shatokhin calls an "agency," and it's built on top of OpenAI's Agents SDK and Responses API.

Shatokhin maintains an active public presence across several independently verifiable channels: a LinkedIn profile under Agency AI Solutions ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/arseny-shatokhin-074358187/)), an X account posting under @__vrsen__ ([X](https://x.com/__vrsen__)), and a YouTube channel where he publishes tutorials on building production AI agents ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCSv4qL8vmoSH7GaPjuqRiCQ)). That combination of a named company, a documented prior business, and a multi-year public build-in-public trail is a step beyond the anonymous "AI guru" pattern common in this niche.

## What members actually get

The community's stated offer is an AI Agent Developer Course and playbooks, weekly Q&A calls, direct developer support, and reusable agent templates, all framed around shipping a working agent for an actual client rather than a portfolio project. The pitch draws a sharp line against competing Skool communities that charge around $97 a month for what Shatokhin describes as instruction from people without real deployment experience, positioning his own background running production agent systems at Agencii as the differentiator.

## Why it's grown

The AI agent space on Skool is crowded, and most of the communities competing for the same audience are led by people whose only credential is having taken someone else's course first. Shatokhin's background cuts against that pattern in a specific way: he had already built and then intentionally wound down a real, revenue-generating agency before building the product that now anchors his teaching, which gives the "we do this for a living, not just teach it" claim something concrete behind it. Free access also matters here. Where much of the AI agent education market has moved toward $50 to $200 monthly subscriptions, a free community with a working developer as the visible face lowers the barrier for beginners trying to figure out whether agent development is a viable path before they commit money to a paid product like Agencii itself.
