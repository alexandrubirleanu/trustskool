# Gaëtan Bertuit and SuperHote: The Co-Founder Behind France's Top Short-Term Rental Community

SuperHote's Skool community, with close to 4,200 members, describes itself plainly as "Le Groupe du TOP 1% des loueurs en courte durée," the group for the top 1% of short-term rental hosts in France. It sits alongside SuperHote's core product: a channel manager built specifically to automate calendar syncing, pricing and messaging across platforms like Airbnb, Booking.com and Expedia.

## Who is Gaëtan Bertuit

Gaëtan Bertuit is credited as the founder on the community's Skool About page, and he's publicly known as SuperHote's co-founder and Chief Marketing Officer. He studied at Montpellier Management and is based in Montreal, and he's described his own path into SuperHote as starting with building marketing funnels before moving toward a more scalable SaaS model alongside his co-founder ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/gaetanbertuit/)). He's spoken about that transition in detail on the French business podcast Secrets d'agence, in an episode titled "Passer du service au produit," roughly translating to "moving from service to product" ([YouTube interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pRHB3Zo7re4); [podcast listing](https://podcast.ausha.co/secrets-d-agence-by-the-quest/9-gaetan-bertuit-co-fondateur-atsuperhost-passer-du-service-au-produit)).

SuperHote itself was co-founded in 2016 by Bertuit alongside Mathieu, an investor who started out in the Airbnb rental business himself before the pair built the channel manager together ([background on Mathieu's origin story](https://luckey.fr/blog/decouvrez-l-histoire-de-mathieu-notre-hote-airbnb-experimente)). That founding pair, one coming from marketing and growth, the other from hands-on property investing, shapes how the product and its surrounding community are positioned: built by people who were actually managing short-term rentals themselves before they started selling software to other hosts.

## What members actually get

Inside the Skool community, members get access to a network built around optimizing and automating short-term rental operations: discussion with other hosts and concierge operators managing dozens or hundreds of properties, plus proximity to SuperHote's own tools and training. The company's academy content and live tour events (branded "SuperHote LIVE Tour") extend that same education into in-person and structured formats beyond the free Skool tier.

The community's positioning as a space for the "top 1%" of hosts, rather than beginners, signals that it's aimed at operators already running real short-term rental businesses and looking to scale further, not people exploring Airbnb hosting for the first time.

## Why it's grown

SuperHote's Skool community benefits from a product that already has real market traction: the company describes itself as the number one channel manager software in France for short-term rental automation, with more than 40,000 owners and operators having used the platform. A free community layered on top of an established software product with a large existing user base is a natural, low-cost way to deepen engagement among people already paying for the tool, and Bertuit's own marketing background is a clear fit for building exactly that kind of layered growth funnel.
