# Amaury Witrant and His Skool Community: France's Faceless YouTube Classroom

Communauté Amaury Witrant bills itself as a top free French-language Skool community for "faceless YouTube," the practice of running channels without ever appearing on camera. With around 3,645 members, it's built around the story and business of Amaury Witrant, a former quantitative finance professional who now runs a portfolio of automated YouTube channels.

## Who is Amaury Witrant

Witrant's LinkedIn profile lists him as CEO at Vaulgard and a Columbia University graduate ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/amaury-witrant/)). A review of his programs on jean-marie-corda.com describes him going through a demanding French preparatory track (PCSI/PSI*) before École Centrale, then completing a master's in quantitative finance and operations research at Columbia University in New York ([jean-marie-corda.com](https://www.jean-marie-corda.com/amaury-witrant/)). He worked in quantitative finance in Manhattan before shifting into building automated, faceless YouTube channels, a business the same review says now spans more than 30 channels.

Witrant posts regularly to his own YouTube channel about the mechanics of that business, including videos walking through revenue figures from individual automated channels ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCOOPKDnDq2TMIIoSEgFiJjA)). He also co-founded a paid program called Vaulgard with Amaury Dumas, through which the pair say they've trained more than 1,000 French-speaking entrepreneurs in the faceless YouTube model, per the same third-party review.

## What members actually get

The free Skool community functions as the top-of-funnel layer for Witrant's paid Vaulgard program. Members get his process for finding a profitable niche, structuring video production without appearing on camera, delegating scripting and editing to freelancers or AI tools, and, per the community description, turning YouTube channels into owned digital assets rather than personal brands.

## Why it's grown

Witrant's pitch works because his own story doubles as the proof of concept: a finance background that signals analytical rigor, paired with transparent, numbers-forward YouTube videos about what his automated channels actually earn. That combination, a credentialed background plus specific, checkable claims about channel revenue, is a stronger trust signal in the French YouTube-automation space than the vaguer "I quit my job" framing common to the niche. The free Skool community lets his audience sample the framework before deciding whether to pay for Vaulgard's ongoing coaching.
