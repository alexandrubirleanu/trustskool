# Daniel Contreras and The New Era Community: Inside Skool's 10,700-Member Barber Business Community

The New Era Community has grown to more than 10,700 members on Skool around a specific, practical promise: a step-by-step playbook for barbers who want to execute, optimize and scale the business they already have, rather than just get better with clippers. It's the community arm of New Era Of Barbering, led by Daniel Contreras, known online as @dlucs_.

## Who is Daniel Contreras

Daniel Contreras identifies himself as a barber, entrepreneur and leader of what he calls the New Era movement in the barber industry, a description that appears across his brand's own site, theneweraofbarbering.com. He hosts a podcast called "No Bullsh*t w/ Dlucs_," now running well past 300 episodes with a 4.9-star rating on Apple Podcasts, where the official show description states that listeners will hear "how to scale your barber business, charge more for your services while generating new clients, and the many lessons Daniel has learned on his path to helping barbers scale to $20k/m" ([Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/no-bullsh-t-w-dlucs/id1450221733)). His organization's own materials describe the New Era Community as having already helped more than 6,300 barbers worldwide, alongside a roster of 1,400-plus private coaching clients ([theneweraofbarbering.com](https://theneweraofbarbering.com/)).

## What members actually get

The free Skool community functions as the on-ramp into New Era Of Barbering's broader coaching business. Rather than teaching cutting technique, the focus is squarely on the business side of running a barbershop or booth: pricing services higher, attracting and retaining higher-paying clients, and building repeatable systems instead of relying on walk-in volume. That business-first framing is a deliberate departure from most barber-focused content online, which tends to center on technical skill rather than commercial strategy.

## Why it's grown

Contreras built his audience the way a lot of successful niche Skool founders do: years of consistent podcasting under his own name and handle, long before Skool communities were a common tool, gave him a built-in, trusting audience of barbers who already knew his voice and his specific angle on scaling a barbering business. The New Era Community then became the natural next step, a free space where that podcast audience could interact directly and get pulled toward paid coaching.

Barbering is also a trade with a large working population but relatively little business-specific education aimed at it, which leaves room for a focused offer like this to stand out. Combining a long-running, well-reviewed podcast with a stated track record of thousands of barbers already coached gives the community a credibility foundation that's difficult for a brand-new entrant in the same niche to match quickly.
