# George McConnell and E365 Academy: The Etsy Seller Who Turned $100 Into a Multi-Million-Dollar Shop

E365 Academy is a $7-a-month Skool community with roughly 3,150 members, marketed as the top Etsy community on the platform. It's run by George McConnell, an Etsy seller whose own store growth is the backbone of the curriculum.

## Who is George McConnell

McConnell's route to Etsy wasn't a straight line. According to a feature on Ecommerce Fastlane, he spent 15 years in corporate roles and made nine separate business attempts, including plenty of failed ventures and long nights working out of warehouses, before an Etsy side hustle finally took ([Ecommerce Fastlane profile](https://ecommercefastlane.com/from-100-to-1-million-how-george-mcconnell-turned-setbacks-into-a-thriving-etsy-business/)). He left his corporate job in 2019 and turned an initial $100 investment into a multi-million-dollar print-on-demand business, a story Printify also covered in detail on its own blog, crediting his data-driven approach to testing designs and doubling down only on what actually sells rather than continuously launching new products ([Printify blog](https://printify.com/blog/how-george-mcconnell-turned-setbacks-into-a-thriving-etsy-business/)).

He's discussed that same trajectory on the Etsy Seller Podcast, in an episode built around the $4 million he'd generated over six years of selling, and he lists himself as Owner of Etsy Mentor on LinkedIn ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/george-mcconnell-10a106325/)). Across both interviews, the details line up consistently: corporate burnout, repeated business failures, then a specific, documented Etsy success built on print-on-demand and disciplined testing.

## What members actually get

Inside E365 Academy, McConnell teaches the same fundamentals covered in his outside interviews: an approach built on print-on-demand, ordering physical samples and taking his own product photos rather than relying purely on supplier images, and treating Etsy's algorithm as something that rewards consistent, high-quality listings over volume alone. The low $7 monthly price point positions it as an accessible entry point rather than a high-ticket mastermind, with the goal of helping members "fast-track" past the mistakes he made across his earlier business attempts.

## Why it's grown

McConnell's story works as marketing precisely because it's been independently verified outside his own community, both Printify and Ecommerce Fastlane, plus a dedicated podcast episode, corroborate the same $100-to-multi-million-dollar arc, which gives prospective members a way to check his credibility before paying anything. A $7 monthly price also removes almost all the friction typically associated with joining a paid mastermind, letting curious Etsy sellers try the community at essentially no risk.

Etsy sellers are also a naturally underserved audience for structured education compared to bigger ecommerce platforms like Shopify, so a low-cost, founder-led community built around one person's documented, specific results has room to stand out in a niche with comparatively few dedicated communities.
