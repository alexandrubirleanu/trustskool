# Haris Hussaini and Player Accelerator: Inside Skool's 6,000-Member Youth Soccer Community

Player Accelerator is a 6,057-member Skool community helping soccer players aged 13-18 get recruited into professional academies, ECNL, MLS Next, and Division 1 college programs. Membership runs $397/year, discounted from a stated $697 regular price [Skool](https://www.skool.com/zth-champions-club/about).

## Who runs it

The community is part of ZTH Training ("Zero To Hero Training"), founded by Haris Hussaini. Hussaini started playing organized football at age 12, and by 18 had signed with the Vancouver Whitecaps' MLS academy — the same academy pathway that produced Alphonso Davies [Double Tap](https://www.doubletapcontent.com/blog/from-zero-to-hero-zth-training-joins-double-tap). He has described going from a benchwarmer at 16 to a professional academy signing at 18, and built ZTH Training's coaching methodology directly out of that personal turnaround [Haris's own funnel page](https://haris.clickfunnels.com/masterclass1). Habas Jawansheer later joined the initiative as a partner, and the two built out the training program together; the Skool community itself is administered under Jawansheer's account [Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@habas), while Hussaini remains the brand's founder and the origin of its coaching methodology.

## What members actually get

Player Accelerator includes more than 40 hours of technical, tactical, physical, and mental training content, plus a stated 48 coaching calls delivered by a current professional footballer, direct introductions to contacts at professional clubs and academies worldwide, and three private one-on-one calls with pro players. The program's stated track record includes players signing with clubs like West Ham, LAFC, Watford, and the Vancouver Whitecaps, along with trials at clubs including RB Leipzig and Benfica, according to the community's own materials. ZTH's broader training platform claims to have worked with more than 3,800 young players across its various programs.

## Why it's grown

Youth soccer development is an intensely competitive, high-stakes niche for families — a professional academy signing can change a teenager's entire trajectory — and Hussaini's personal story (an overlooked player who worked his way into a real MLS academy) gives the program a credibility anchor that most youth-sports coaching content lacks. Rather than a generic "become a better player" pitch, Player Accelerator sells a specific outcome (academy or college signings) with named clubs and a codified methodology behind it, which is a stronger, more checkable promise than most sports-training communities make. The annual pricing model, at nearly $400/year, also signals a program built for serious, committed families rather than casual hobbyists, which likely supports both retention and word-of-mouth referral among competitive youth soccer circles.
