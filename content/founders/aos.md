# Ming Fung and Agents of Speech: A Former ABA Therapist's Free Community for 18,500 Parents

Agents of Speech exists for a specific, often anxious audience: parents of children with speech delays who want practical tools rather than a waitlist for a speech pathologist. Over 18,500 parents have joined the free community to learn, vent, and collaborate around exactly that problem.

## Who is Ming Fung

Fung is the founder and CEO of Agents of Speech, born and raised in Hong Kong, and began her career working with children on the autism spectrum starting in 2014 as an ABA (Applied Behavior Analysis) therapist, according to her own team bio on the organization's site ([agentsofspeech.com/team/ming-fung](https://www.agentsofspeech.com/team/ming-fung)). Her professional background as a speech therapist is also documented on LinkedIn, where she's listed under that title in connection with Agents of Speech ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ming-fung/)).

Fung maintains an active public presence under her own name and face, running an Instagram account, a YouTube channel, and hosting live global Q&A sessions specifically for parents, a format the organization has promoted directly on its own blog ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/mingfung.speech/); [Agents of Speech blog](https://www.agentsofspeech.com/blog-posts/join-our-live-global-q-a-with-speech-therapist-ming)). Her organization's "Our Story" page lays out the founding rationale in more detail, framing the free education model as a direct response to the cost barriers many families face when trying to access speech therapy support ([agentsofspeech.com/our-story](https://www.agentsofspeech.com/our-story)).

## What members actually get

The Skool community gives parents free access to practical, at-home tools: a home therapy checklist, structured exercises, and strategies for building speech and language development into everyday routines rather than requiring a separate, scheduled therapy session for every skill. The approach leans on Fung's clinical ABA background, focused on structured, repeatable behavioral techniques parents can apply themselves.

Paid coaching programs exist alongside the free tier for families who want more direct, ongoing support, but the free education layer, checklists, exercises, and community Q&A, remains a core part of the offer rather than a stripped-down teaser.

## Why it's grown

Fung's clinical background as a working ABA therapist, rather than a content creator who studied the subject secondhand, gives Agents of Speech a credibility foundation that's directly relevant to the anxious, information-hungry audience it serves. Parents dealing with a child's speech delay are typically looking for expert-backed guidance, not general parenting content, and Fung's professional history speaks directly to that need.

The explicitly free model also removes a real barrier in this niche: speech therapy access is frequently limited by cost and waitlists, and a free community run by someone with genuine clinical experience fills a gap that many families can't otherwise close quickly.
