# Richard Mugica and The Closers Network: From $20-an-Hour Truck Driver to $300M in Sales

The Closers Network positions itself as a training ground for remote high-ticket closers chasing impact, freedom, and income, and it's grown to nearly 22,000 members on the back of a founder whose own career reversal is unusually well documented.

## Who is Richard Mugica

Mugica is the founder of 1 Call Closers, an Inc. 5000-ranked company (listed at #1,173) that builds high-ticket coaching upsell systems for fitness and coaching brands. According to his own site, the company has generated more than $300 million in verified sales and serves over 100,000 coaching clients across its partner brands, operating with roughly 400 team members after seven years in business ([richardmugica.com](https://richardmugica.com/)). His LinkedIn and Crunchbase profiles both list him as founder of 1 Call Closers and the Closers Network ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/richardmugica/); [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/richard-mugica-130e)).

Mugica has spoken publicly about starting out earning $20 an hour as a truck driver before building his sales career into a business doing over $100 million in high-ticket sales, a story he's told directly on his own Closers Network Podcast, which he co-hosts with Mike Sadikian ([podcast episode](https://richardzp.podbean.com/e/20hour-to-100m-in-high-ticket-sales-cn-podcast-e006/)). Outside of business, he's been married to his wife Hailey since 2014, has four children, and co-hosts a separate podcast for Christian couples called "Married with the Mugicas," which he's described as reflecting how his faith and family shape his priorities around the business rather than the other way around.

## What members actually get

The Closers Network gives members access to weekly coaching, call reviews where members' actual sales calls get broken down, and sales seminars covering the mechanics of high-ticket closing. According to 1 Call Closers, the underlying agency has sold for well-known names in the coaching and fitness space, including Bedros Keuilian, Dean Graziosi, and Robby Blanchard, and the community draws directly on frameworks built for that agency work rather than generic sales theory.

The Closers Network Podcast, distributed on iHeart, Apple, and Amazon Music, extends the same content into interview form, featuring stories from closers who've built successful careers inside the network.

## Why it's grown

Mugica's personal turnaround story, from a $20-an-hour truck driving job to founding an Inc. 5000 company, gives The Closers Network a credibility hook that's both dramatic and independently verifiable through Inc.'s own ranking rather than resting solely on self-reported numbers. That combination of a compelling personal arc and a legitimately large, ranked business is relatively rare in the high-ticket sales training niche, which is often crowded with unverifiable income claims.

The community also benefits from being built on top of an operating agency rather than existing purely as an education product. Members training inside The Closers Network are learning from a system that's actively closing real sales for name-brand fitness clients, not a theoretical framework built only for teaching purposes.
