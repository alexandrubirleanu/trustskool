# Mike Pantile and the Masculinity Project: A Former Addict Turned Catholic Men's Coach on Skool

The Masculinity Project is a free Skool community with around 2,330 members, built to connect Catholic men around what its description calls "Faith. Fellowship. Fortitude. Fitness. Finances." It was founded by Mike Pantile, who runs it alongside co-leader Brian Fehringer.

## Who is Mike Pantile

Pantile's public story is built around a specific redemption arc that he's told across his own site and multiple Catholic media outlets. By his own account on mikepantile.com, he spent years struggling with pornography, drugs, alcohol, depression and suicidal ideation before converting, and has since lost more than 100 pounds while setting a personal powerlifting deadlift record above 800 pounds. He describes himself as a six-figure fitness coach and gym owner with more than a decade in the industry, and as a husband and father who has since reoriented his coaching business around Catholic masculinity and virtue. That story has carried him onto established Catholic media: he's a guest on the "Purposely Catholic" podcast in an episode titled "Catholic Masculinity w/ Mike Pantile" ([Spotify episode](https://open.spotify.com/episode/2waDMokUNSDhL984LKW7w9)), was interviewed on the widely followed "Pints With Aquinas" podcast under the headline "Red Pill to Catholic: Embracing True Patriarchy" ([Libsyn episode](https://sites.libsyn.com/79956/red-pill-to-catholic-embracing-true-patriarchy-mike-pantile)), and was profiled directly by Catholic Stand in a feature titled "Mike Pantile: How to Build a Man" ([Catholic Stand feature](https://catholicstand.com/mike-pantile-how-to-build-a-man/)). His co-leader, Brian Fehringer, separately runs a coaching business called Fehringer Fitness aimed at helping skinny men bulk up.

## What members actually get

Per the community's own framing, members get bi-weekly men's group meetings, Bible studies paired with faith-based accountability, and a set of mini-courses spanning devotion, masculinity and mindset, physical strength, marital leadership, courtship and dating, financial stewardship, and overcoming lust, essentially a structured curriculum wrapping Catholic teaching around the fitness-and-discipline framework Pantile built his coaching career on. The breadth of that curriculum, faith, fitness and finances treated as a single integrated program rather than separate offerings, mirrors the way Pantile describes his own turnaround: not just a physical transformation, but a full reordering of habits across every part of life at once.

## Why it's grown

Pantile's redemption narrative, from addiction and suicidal ideation to a national-caliber powerlifting total and a coaching business built on Catholic values, is a distinctive hook in a crowded men's self-improvement space, and it's been amplified through appearances on podcasts with substantial, dedicated audiences rather than through paid ads. Pints With Aquinas in particular has built a large following among practicing Catholics specifically interested in faith-and-culture conversations, which makes it a strong top-of-funnel source for a values-aligned free community like this one. From there, the free Skool group functions as the entry point to his paid 1:1 coaching.
