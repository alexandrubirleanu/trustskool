# Ghadi Abboud and Timeless Jump Club: Inside Skool's 2,700-Member Jump Rope Community

Timeless Jump Club sells itself against a common failure mode in jump rope training: most people jump with no plan and plateau on basics. The free-to-join Skool community teaches every skill in a defined order instead, and has reached roughly 2,720 members.

## Who is Ghadi Abboud

The club is led by Ghadi Theo Abboud, who posts under the handle @alwaysghadi and is listed as the founder of Timeless Jump, the Dubai-based rope and gear brand the club sits under ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/alwaysghadi/)). Abboud is a combat sport athlete with more than a decade of training experience, and the Timeless Jump brand's own site dates the company's founding to 2020, built around the idea that most jump ropes on the market tangle, crack, or use cheap materials ([Timeless Jump](https://timelessjump.com/)). He maintains a public LinkedIn profile under his name tied to the company ([LinkedIn](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/ghadi-abboud-304577200)), and the underlying Timeless Jump business has accumulated customer reviews on Trustpilot, giving it a paper trail independent of the brand's own marketing ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/timelessjump.com)).

## What members actually get

The Skool community's stated structure covers more than 100 skills, moving from beginner to advanced, each broken down with detailed demos, slow-motion angles, and live coaching aimed at correcting form in real time. That structured, skill-by-skill approach is the direct answer to the plateau problem the group's own marketing calls out: rather than a single workout routine, members get an explicit skill tree to progress through. The underlying Timeless Jump brand says it now serves more than 50,000 athletes worldwide, designs its ropes in Dubai, and ships internationally, which gives the Skool club a built-in customer base to draw new members from rather than needing to build a jump rope audience from scratch.

## Why it's grown

Jump rope has become a popular low-cost, low-space alternative to bigger home-gym setups, but most content in the space is either straightforward cardio workouts or advanced freestyle tricks with no path connecting the two. A community that explicitly sequences skills end to end fills that specific gap, and Abboud's combat sport background gives the coaching a credibility angle distinct from a generic fitness influencer picking up a rope for content. Trustpilot reviews tied to the underlying Timeless Jump business also give prospective members a way to sanity-check the brand's claims against real customer feedback rather than relying solely on the Skool community's own description, which is a meaningfully different trust signal than most product-adjacent communities on the platform offer.
