# WGM Dina Belenkaya and Chess Courses by RCS: A Woman Grandmaster's Skool Classroom

Chess Courses by RCS sits on Skool with just under 4,600 members and a one-time $1 entry fee, a low-friction front door into a much bigger chess education business. It's run by Dina Belenkaya, a FIDE-titled Woman Grandmaster who has spent the last several years building an online chess brand across YouTube, Twitch, Instagram, TikTok, and X in addition to her over-the-board playing career.

## Who is Dina Belenkaya

Belenkaya holds the WGM title and a FIDE rating profile that puts her among titled women players competing internationally. Beyond tournament play, she's built what her own site describes as a global media presence, and she was profiled at length on the Perpetual Chess Podcast about her tournament schedule, her social media growth, and the thinking behind launching a dedicated chess school ([Perpetual Chess Podcast, episode 422](https://www.perpetualchesspod.com/new-blog/2025/2/18/ep-422-wgm-dina-belenkaya-on-her-recent-tournies-her-continued-social-media-growth-and-the-vision-behind-her-new-chess-school)). She also wrote a beginner-to-intermediate study guide for players in the 1000 to 1400 Elo range for Chess.com, the kind of contributor slot the site reserves for recognized coaches and titled players ([Chess.com](https://www.chess.com/article/view/wgm-dina-belenkayas-beginner-intermediate-study-guide-1000-1400-elo)). The Adult Chess Academy blog separately covered her as, in their words, "a born chess entertainer" building out new coursework ([adultchessacademy.com](https://www.adultchessacademy.com/blog/090-wgm-dina-belenkaya-a-born-chess-entertainer-her-new-course-chasing-the-im-title)).

Belenkaya founded the Russian Chess School as her main training operation for adult improvers, offering structured programs from complete beginner through roughly 2000 rating, built around weekly group lessons, game reviews, and one-on-one coaching calls rather than a pile of disconnected video lessons ([russianchessschool.com/about](https://www.russianchessschool.com/about)).

## What members actually get

Inside Chess Courses by RCS, the pitch is narrow and direct: fix specific, identifiable weaknesses in your game through Belenkaya's own courses rather than generic instructional content. The community functions as an access point to her coursework and to the broader Russian Chess School ecosystem, where paying students get game reviews and coaching calls layered on top of the course library.

## Why it's grown

Belenkaya's growth pattern follows a path that's become common among chess creators since the pandemic-era streaming boom: build a large, free audience through entertaining, personality-driven content on YouTube and Twitch, then convert a slice of that audience into paying students through structured coaching rather than one-off video sales. Her $1 entry point on Skool functions less as a revenue line and more as a low-risk way for her social following to sample the classroom before deciding whether to go deeper into the paid Russian Chess School programs or in-person training camps she and her team have started running in the US.
