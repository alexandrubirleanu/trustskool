# Sam Matla and EDMProd Mastermind: The Founder Who Turned Electronic Music Teaching into a Real Business

EDMProd Mastermind Community is a Skool community with nearly 2,900 members built around learning electronic music production "the smart way," offering bi-weekly 90-minute feedback sessions, free samples and Ableton project files, and access to EDMProd's paid course library. It's led by Sam Matla, credited directly as the founder on the community's Skool about page, alongside Aden, listed as EDMProd's Director, who runs the recurring feedback sessions.

## Who is Sam Matla

Sam Matla founded EDMProd and co-authored its flagship course, EDM Foundations, which the company says has helped more than 5,000 people learn the fundamentals of electronic music production ([EDMProd About](https://www.edmprod.com/about/)). His personal site describes him as having spent the last decade in online business, primarily in e-learning, and frames EDMProd as a company he built and has since folded into a broader portfolio of ventures rather than treating it as a single, static project ([sammatla.com](https://sammatla.com/)).

That same personal site shows Matla has since expanded beyond music education into productivity coaching for solopreneurs and knowledge workers, running a newsletter with several thousand subscribers and a goal-setting program he calls Focused90. He describes himself there, somewhat self-deprecatingly, as the "anti-guru of the productivity space," arguing that most of the productivity industry causes more distraction than it resolves.

## What members actually get

Inside the Skool community, the core offer centers on the bi-weekly 90-minute feedback sessions, live critique on members' actual tracks, alongside free resources like sample packs and starter Ableton projects meant to lower the barrier to finishing a first track. Members also get access to EDMProd's paid course catalog, built around the same fundamentals-first approach as EDM Foundations, aimed at beginners who want a structured path rather than scattered YouTube tutorials.

With Aden running day-to-day sessions as Director, the community appears to have moved past being a single-founder operation, letting Matla's original teaching framework scale to more members without requiring his direct involvement in every feedback call.

## Why it's grown

Electronic music production is a notoriously overwhelming space for beginners, with software complexity and genre-specific technique creating a steep initial learning curve, and EDMProd's core pitch since its founding has been reducing that overwhelm through a fundamentals-first curriculum rather than genre-chasing tutorials. Having already built EDM Foundations into a course with a five-thousand-plus track record before this Skool community existed gives the group instant credibility with new members who've likely encountered EDMProd content on YouTube or through the course itself first.

The live, small-group feedback format is also a harder thing to replicate for free anywhere else online, which gives members a concrete reason to stay engaged in the community beyond passively consuming content.
