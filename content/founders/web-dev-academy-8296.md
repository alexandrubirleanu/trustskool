# Hayk Simonyan and Dev Mastery Roadmap: A Senior Engineer's Skool Classroom for Mid-Level Developers

Dev Mastery Roadmap is a Skool community of roughly 3,399 developers built around a specific promise: help mid-level engineers become senior engineers. It's run by Hayk Simonyan, a software engineer who says he reached senior-level status within two years of starting his career.

## Who is Hayk Simonyan

Simonyan's LinkedIn lists him working in software engineering, and his personal site, hayksimonyan.com, describes a career that includes shipping products generating $5.3 million in revenue with more than 100,000 active users, work he attributes to Globaldev Group while based in Switzerland ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/hayksimonyan/); [hayksimonyan.com](https://hayksimonyan.com/)). He publishes regularly on Substack under his own name, including posts like "Senior Software Engineer Roadmap 2026" and "Why Most Developers Stay 'Mid' Forever," and maintains a Udemy instructor profile and an active X account ([Substack](https://hayksimonyan.substack.com/p/senior-software-engineer-roadmap); [Udemy](https://www.udemy.com/user/hayk-simonyan-112/); [X](https://x.com/hayksimonyann)). He states on his own site that he never worked at a FAANG company and built his engineering career independently of that path.

## What members actually get

Simonyan's Dev Mastery program, which the Skool community feeds into, is built around a 180-day mentorship structure: weekly one-on-one calls, pair programming sessions, code reviews, and mock interviews focused on system design and production-level architecture rather than algorithmic interview trivia. The paid program comes with a stated guarantee, continued mentorship at no additional cost until a member lands a senior role, or a refund if that hasn't happened within six months.

## Why it's grown

The pitch here is direct mentorship from a named, verifiable engineer rather than a pre-recorded course library, a meaningfully different structure than most "learn to code" Skool communities. Simonyan's public Substack writing and LinkedIn presence give prospective members a way to evaluate his thinking and communication style before paying for 1:1 mentorship, which matters in a niche where the core product is largely intangible, career guidance and code review, rather than a fixed curriculum. For developers stuck in the "mid-level trap" he writes about, a mentor whose own claimed trajectory, senior status within two years without traditional FAANG credentials, mirrors the exact outcome members are paying for, which is a more specific trust signal than a generic "learn to code" brand.
