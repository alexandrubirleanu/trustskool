# Julien Massey and Communauté Mentorya: Turning a 3,000€ AI Agency Playbook Into a Free Skool Course

Communauté Mentorya is a free, French-language Skool community with around 2,350 members, pitched around launching a profitable AI agency. The offer is specific: access to the AI agents its founder normally sells to companies for 3,000€ each, and a copy of the client-acquisition system he used to build his own business. That founder is Julien Massey.

## Who is Julien Massey

Massey runs Agencya.io, an agency specializing in operational AI automation for growing companies, building things like CRM and sales-pipeline automation, and billing and payment-reminder workflows, for paying clients ([Agencya.io](https://agencya.io/)). He's built a YouTube channel with more than 35,000 subscribers teaching AI automation and agent-building ([YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC2R8m4VdylpissDeVEaeXYw)), and posts regularly on LinkedIn about AI agents, automation, and agency-building, including announcements of agents he's built for clients ([LinkedIn post](https://fr.linkedin.com/posts/julien-ia_je-tai-cr%C3%A9%C3%A9-lagent-ia-qui-va-r%C3%A9diger-tous-activity-7318566047819714560-8WzW)). He also publishes tutorials and resources through his personal site, julien-ia.com.

## What members actually get

Per the community's own about page, members get access to the AI agent templates Massey otherwise sells at enterprise pricing, structured training on the underlying business models, technical n8n tutorials, and real-world case studies from his own client work, complete with what the page describes as "numbers and proofs" of results. That last part matters for positioning: rather than teaching AI automation in the abstract, the community is framed around agents he has actually deployed and billed for, which is a different pitch than most "learn AI agents" content built purely from tutorials with no paying-client track record behind them.

## Why it's grown

The free Skool group functions as a lead-generation layer sitting underneath a service Massey charges thousands of euros for per client. His existing YouTube audience, north of 35,000 subscribers built around AI automation content, is the obvious acquisition channel feeding new members into the group, and the case-study-driven content inside then does the work of building enough credibility to convert some of those free members into paying agency clients or push them toward a higher-ticket offer. He's also mentored young entrepreneurs directly, helping structure their acquisition strategies and secure their first clients, according to summaries of his coaching work, which suggests the community functions as one layer of a broader mentorship pipeline rather than a standalone product. It's a fairly common structure among AI-agency Skool communities right now: use free community content to prove the model works, then monetize the more ambitious members downstream rather than charging for the community itself.
