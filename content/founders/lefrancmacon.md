# Le Franc-Maçon and Le Parvis du Temple: Inside Skool's 6,400-Member Freemasonry Community

Le Parvis du Temple is a free Skool community of about 6,477 members built around understanding freemasonry, choosing whether to pursue it, and beginning what its description calls a genuine inner work. It's an unusual entry on Skool in one specific way: its founder is real, interviewed, and biographically documented, but goes by a pseudonym rather than a public legal name.

## Who is Le Franc-Maçon

The person behind the community identifies publicly only as "lefrancmacon," or Le Franc-Maçon, and gave a long-format interview to 450.fm, a French publication covering freemasonry, in March 2026 ([450.fm interview](https://450.fm/2026/03/28/exclusif-interview-de-linfluenceur-le-franc-macon-qui-est-il-et-que-veut-il/)). In that interview he describes himself as a graduate of France's École Polytechnique, initiated into freemasonry roughly twelve years ago, and currently holding the grade of master mason. He describes Le Parvis du Temple as his "véritable refuge virtuel," a space where freemasons and non-members can discuss the craft in a spirit of goodwill, and states the private group had already reached 5,000 members within two months of launch, about half of them practicing masons.

Choosing not to publish a legal name is unusual for a Skool founder, but it's consistent with wider freemasonry norms around discretion, and the interview itself, along with his active public social media presence discussing the subject, is what makes the person behind the account verifiable rather than anonymous.

## What members actually get

The community is free to join and is split into general discussion spaces along with sections reserved specifically for freemasons and for masters, according to the founder's own description in the 450.fm interview. Members get a mix of public-facing education about freemasonry, symbolism, and philosophy, alongside paid workshops offered within the private group, while Le Franc-Maçon keeps his broader social media content free.

That two-tier structure, free public content plus paid workshops for the most engaged members, maps closely to how he describes monetizing his following outside of Skool as well: building trust through openly answering common public questions about freemasonry, then offering deeper paid access to those who want it.

## Why it's grown

Le Parvis du Temple grew quickly, by the founder's own account reaching 5,000 members within its first two months, largely because it filled a specific gap: a space where curious non-masons could ask basic questions about freemasonry without the gatekeeping that surrounds most lodges, while masons themselves got a private space to discuss the craft outside formal lodge structures. The 450.fm interview describes him as having become, in recent years, the most publicly recognizable face of freemasonry to the general French public, which gave the community an unusually large and ready-made audience the moment it launched.
