# Banah Winn and Harmony: Inside Skool's Largest Private Spiritual Community

Harmony describes itself as the largest private spiritual community on Skool, with close to 37,000 members paying $27 a month for access to something called the Golden Ratio Frequencies — a proprietary set of audio frequencies its founder says are unavailable anywhere else, including mainstream streaming platforms. The person behind it is Banah Winn, a musician and self-described sound healer.

## Who is Banah Winn

Banah Winn's public background is as a music producer who moved into sound healing and frequency work after a period of meditation and energy-healing practice, including time spent in Sedona, Arizona — a location well known in the wellness and spiritual community for its associations with meditation retreats and so-called energy vortexes. Winn describes the Golden Ratio Frequencies as a formula built on mathematical relationships that show up in nature, from DNA structure to breathing patterns to the geometry of galaxies, and says the discovery process came out of that meditation practice ([Banah Winn's own site](https://www.banahwinn.com/)).

Winn has talked about the frequencies publicly on wellness podcasts, including an appearance on the "Born to Heal" podcast with Dr. Katie Deming, in an episode titled "The Golden Ratio: The Healing Sound Pattern Behind Nature, Breath, and Stillness" ([episode page](https://www.katiedeming.com/episode-129-the-golden-ratio-the-healing-sound-pattern-behind-nature-breath-and-stillness/)), and runs a YouTube channel under the same name ([youtube.com/@Banahwinn](https://www.youtube.com/@Banahwinn)).

It's worth being direct about what's independently verifiable here versus what's a claim made by the founder: the musical background, the YouTube and podcast presence, and the framing of the Golden Ratio Frequencies as a proprietary formula are all publicly documented. Any specific physiological or health claims attached to the frequencies (better sleep, reduced stress, sharper intuition) are the community's own marketing language rather than claims that have been independently verified, and readers evaluating the community should treat them as such.

## What members actually get

Inside Harmony, members get access to a growing library of audio frequencies targeted at specific outcomes — sleep, stress reduction, mental clarity, grounding, intuition, and emotional regulation — plus guided meditations and a weekly live session described as a group "activation." The core differentiator the community leans on is exclusivity: the frequencies are positioned as only available inside Harmony, not on Spotify or other mainstream audio platforms, which gives paying members a reason the $27/month price can't simply be replaced by a free YouTube search.

## Why it's grown

Harmony's growth sits at the intersection of two larger trends: the mainstreaming of sound and frequency-based wellness content (binaural beats, solfeggio frequencies, and similar audio wellness products have a large, pre-existing online audience), and the format advantage Skool gives a subscription content community — a single home for both the audio library and the live communal component (group meditations, weekly activations) that a simple podcast or YouTube channel can't replicate. Winn's own description of having "helped millions find coherence" through the frequency work suggests the audience was already substantial before the Skool community consolidated it into a paid, recurring structure — a familiar Skool pattern where the platform becomes the monetization layer for an audience built somewhere else first.
