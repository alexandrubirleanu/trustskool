# Curtis Beach and Track & Field by Curtis Beach: A Decathlon Champion's Skool Classroom

Track & Field by Curtis Beach is a free Skool community with roughly 3,445 members built around one premise: get instruction from someone who has actually competed and coached at every level the sport offers, from state high school meets to the Olympic Training Center.

## Who is Curtis Beach

Curtis Beach is a two-time NCAA heptathlon champion (2012, 2014) at Duke University and a four-time Team USA member who once held the national high school decathlon record with 7,907 points, a mark DyeStat called the best any US high schooler had produced ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curtis_Beach)). He also holds the world heptathlon best in the 1000 meters and won a US indoor national championship in 2016, finishing fourth at that year's World Indoor Championships.

Since retiring from competition, Beach has built a full coaching career. He has coached at Duke as a volunteer assistant ([goduke.com](https://goduke.com/sports/track-and-field/roster/coaches/curtis-beach/1032)), at the US Olympic Training Center and Spire Academy, and was hired as an assistant coach for Point Loma Nazarene University's women's track and field program ahead of the 2025 season ([plnusealions.com](https://plnusealions.com/sports/track-and-field/roster/coaches/curtis-beach/1293)). He runs his own coaching business, Beach Performance, according to his LinkedIn profile ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/curtis-beach-9a616418/)), and his own site, curtisbeach.com, serves as the hub for that private coaching work ([curtisbeach.com](https://curtisbeach.com/)). That's more than a decade of coaching across sprints, hurdles, jumps, throws, and mid-distance events, spanning high school, college, and professional athletes.

## What members actually get

The Skool community's own description keeps the pitch simple: "Master your Track & Field events. Lessons and resources built from real-world experiences at the high school, college, and professional levels." Given Beach's actual résumé, that reads as a credible claim rather than marketing gloss. He's coached at three distinct competitive tiers himself, which is a narrower and more specific credential than most fitness-adjacent Skool creators can point to.

## Why it's grown

Beach's growth path looks less like a typical influencer funnel and more like a coach converting an existing professional reputation into a scalable teaching product. A two-time NCAA champion with an Olympic Training Center coaching credit doesn't need to manufacture credibility online. What Skool adds is a way to package that expertise for athletes and coaches who can't hire him directly, at a much lower price point than 1:1 coaching. For a sport where good technical coaching is unevenly distributed, a community built by someone with Beach's competitive and coaching background fills a real gap for club athletes without access to that level of instruction locally.
