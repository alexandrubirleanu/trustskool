# Albert Shiney and AI Automation (A-Z): Inside Skool's 164,000-Member AI Agency Community

AI Automation (A-Z) is one of the largest AI-focused communities on Skool, with more than 164,000 members paying $7 a month to learn how to build and sell AI automation services. Its promise is blunt: go from zero experience to landing a first client inside a 7-day challenge. Behind it is Albert Shiney, a Danish entrepreneur who has built his public brand almost entirely around making AI automation approachable for beginners.

## Who is Albert Shiney

Albert Shiney is based in Cyprus and runs his automation business under the name Shiney AI Solutions ([Shiney.ai on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/shiney-ai-solutions)), alongside a YouTube channel under the same name ([youtube.com/@AlbertShiney](https://www.youtube.com/@AlbertShiney)) where he publishes tutorials and case studies on AI agencies. He joined Skool in December 2023 and has since built out a small ecosystem of communities around the same theme: AI Automation (A-Z) as the free, top-of-funnel community, plus paid programs called "The 1% in AI" and "The 0.1% in AI" for members who want deeper, done-with-you coaching on building agencies that serve traditional, non-tech businesses.

Public information on his background before Skool is thin — he doesn't appear to have a well-documented prior career the way many creator-economy founders do, and his online presence is built almost entirely around the Shiney AI brand itself. That's worth noting plainly rather than papering over: what's verifiable is the current business (Shiney AI Solutions, the YouTube channel, and the tiered Skool ecosystem), not a pre-AI career history.

## What members actually get

Inside AI Automation (A-Z), the pitch centers on a seven-day path from "beginner" to "AI Automation Agency operator." That includes guidance on setting up Claude-based skills and workflows, building a first AI agent, sales scripts for landing a first client, and running commentary on which industries are worth targeting heading into 2026. The community also runs recurring giveaways of software and cash prizes to keep daily engagement high, a common Skool retention tactic for large free-to-cheap communities.

Because the entry price is only $7 a month, the free-to-paid community functions mostly as a top-of-funnel: members who want closer coaching or a done-with-you build process are funneled toward Shiney's higher-ticket "1% in AI" and "0.1% in AI" programs. This tiered structure — cheap mass community feeding pricier cohort-based coaching — is one of the more common playbooks among the largest AI communities on Skool right now, and Shiney has executed it at meaningful scale.

## Why it's grown

Two forces explain the size of this community. First, timing: AI automation agencies (using tools like n8n, Make, and Claude to sell workflow automation to small businesses) became one of the fastest-growing "learn to earn online" niches through 2025 and into 2026, and a genuinely free, low-friction entry point (a 7-day challenge rather than a long paid course) removes almost all the friction to joining. Second, the built-in scarcity mechanic — the community's own materials note that pricing steps up from $7/month to $50/month once membership hits 165,000 — creates a visible growth countdown that gives existing members a reason to talk about the community publicly, which in turn feeds new sign-ups.

It's worth flagging that some AI-automation "Skool Games" and creator round-up content online blends Albert Shiney's AI Automation (A-Z) together with other similarly-named, similarly-themed AI automation communities run by other creators — the space is crowded and the branding overlaps heavily (AI Automation Society, AI Automation Mastery, and others all exist as separate Skool communities with different founders).
