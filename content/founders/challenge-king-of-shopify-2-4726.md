# Marcus Lawrence and Challenge King of Shopify: France's Free 3-Day Dropshipping Sprint

Challenge King of Shopify compresses its entire pitch into a single line: a free 3-day challenge to become a King of Ecommerce. Over 23,000 members have joined this specific edition of the challenge, which the community's own page identifies as its third run, hosted by its creator, Marcus Lawrence.

## Who is Marcus Lawrence

Lawrence, also known online as Marcuus Lawrence, is a French entrepreneur and digital influencer born in 1995 in Roubaix, France, who built his public profile through dropshipping content before packaging his methods into the King of Shopify program ([lejournaltech.fr profile](https://lejournaltech.fr/marcus-lawrence-2/); [frenchhub.fr profile](https://frenchhub.fr/marcus-lawrence-parcours-formation-influence)). The Skool community's own about page directly credits him as the challenge's creator, describing this run as the "Troisième édition du Challenge de Marcuus," the third edition of his challenge.

His broader King of Shopify program, sold separately from the free Skool challenge, has been reviewed by French-language ecommerce education sites as a structured course covering the full path from store setup to customer relationship management and sales optimization ([Top Avis Formation review](https://top-avis-formations.fr/avis/king-of-shopify-formation-de-marcus-lawrence/)).

## What members actually get

The free challenge runs as a live, three-day format rather than a self-paced video dump: each day includes a live session with specific, concrete actions to execute immediately, followed by homework before the next session. Participants are assigned a dedicated King of Shopify coach who answers questions before, during, and after each day's session, and the challenge functions as a structured on-ramp into the broader dropshipping and ecommerce community around Lawrence's brand.

The format is designed to produce a tangible result inside three days rather than simply delivering information, which is a deliberate contrast to longer, more theoretical ecommerce courses.

## Why it's grown

Lawrence built his following as a visible figure in the French dropshipping space before launching King of Shopify, and that pre-existing recognition as a "thought leader in the dropshipping space," as French coverage of his program describes it, gave the free challenge an audience willing to show up for a live, unpaid commitment rather than a passive course purchase.

Running the challenge as a recurring, numbered event (this being its third edition) also lets Lawrence keep re-activating past participants and drawing in new ones each time it relaunches, rather than relying on a single evergreen funnel that slowly loses momentum. For a niche as saturated with dropshipping courses as ecommerce is, that combination of a known personal brand and a repeatable live format is a meaningful differentiator.
