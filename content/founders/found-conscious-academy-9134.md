# Azeem Ali and Found Conscious Academy: A Detox Practitioner's Free Skool Community

Found Conscious Academy is a free Skool community of roughly 3,655 members organized around holistic detoxification and what its own description calls health sovereignty for freedom-minded activists. It's run by Azeem Ali, a certified detox practitioner who built a following around iridology and herbalism-based protocols.

## Who is Azeem Ali

Ali operates under the handle @foundconsciousness across platforms, including an Instagram account and a podcast, Found Consciousness, distributed on Spotify ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/foundconsciousness/); [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/show/0gTs7l5JA9awa5u7br3J6i)). His own site describes him as a certified detox practitioner who uses iridology, detoxification, and herbalism to build personalized health roadmaps for clients, and lists him as the author of a nutrition guide called "The 10 Principles of Eating Wisely" ([foundconsciousness.com](https://www.foundconsciousness.com)). He has also appeared as a guest discussing iridology and detoxification on independent platforms like Rumble, where he covered topics including the body's electrical signaling and how trauma manifests physically ([Rumble](https://rumble.com/v4p8xn0-azeem-ali-on-iridology-the-electric-body-rhythm-as-health-crystallization-o.html)). His own site cites a newsletter audience of more than 14,000 people, and it frames his mission in explicit terms, describing his goal as taking "a million people from sick to superhuman" through what he calls the path to "WellVille."

## What members actually get

The free Skool community gives members monthly hangout sessions, courses on detoxification principles, and a structured 14-day gut cleanse protocol using binders and herbs, which Ali describes as targeting mucoid plaque, toxins, heavy metals, and biofilm. It functions as the free layer sitting alongside Ali's paid iridology readings and one-on-one detox consulting, where members can go from free community discussion into a personalized, paid health roadmap without switching platforms.

## Why it's grown

Ali's community sits inside the broader holistic-health and health-sovereignty space that has grown quickly on Skool, appealing to people distrustful of conventional medical institutions. His use of iridology and detox protocols puts him well outside mainstream clinical practice, a positioning he leans into rather than away from, marketing directly to freedom-minded activists rather than a general wellness audience. That specificity, plus a consistent personal brand across Instagram, podcast, and his own site rather than an anonymous group page, is likely why the free community has scaled past 3,600 members: it reads as a single practitioner's personal practice rather than an impersonal content mill. A podcast in particular gives potential members a long-form way to hear Ali's actual reasoning before they ever pay for a reading, which is a slower but more durable trust-building path than a single high-production sales page.
