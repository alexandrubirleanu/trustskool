# Bashiri Smith and Become an Ai Engineer: Inside Skool's 6,200-Member AI Career Community

Become an Ai Engineer is a free Skool community of about 6,213 members, aimed squarely at software engineers who want to move into higher-paying AI engineering roles. The founder, Bashiri Smith, positions himself as living proof of the transition the community teaches.

## Who is Bashiri Smith

Bashiri Smith is named directly on the community's Skool about page as its creator, described there as helping students land their first $100K-plus AI and software engineering roles ([Become an Ai Engineer on Skool](https://www.skool.com/become-an-ai-engineer/about)). His LinkedIn profile lists him as a software engineer and entrepreneur who posts regularly about software engineering, coding, and AI career topics under his own name ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bashiri-smith/)), including posts breaking down six-figure salary structures for engineers moving into AI-adjacent roles. He also runs BASWE.AI, an AI engineer accelerator program built on the same premise, and maintains a public presence on TikTok and Instagram under variations of his name and handle discussing software and AI career transitions.

Having a consistent, named identity across LinkedIn, the Skool community itself, and a separate branded accelerator program gives Smith's community a firmer identity trail than many AI-career groups on Skool, which frequently run behind logos or generic brand names with no attached individual.

## What members actually get

The community is free to join and targets software engineers with roughly two to six years of experience who want to move into AI engineering, a track the group's own description says has produced over $1 million in placed engineer salaries in the prior year. The pitch centers on a structured roadmap, hands-on projects, and interview preparation aimed at converting existing software engineering experience into an AI specialization, rather than teaching AI or coding from zero.

That's a meaningfully narrower target than most "learn AI" communities on Skool, most of which are open to complete beginners. Restricting the pitch to engineers who already have professional coding experience lets the community assume a baseline of technical competency and focus specifically on the AI-specific gap.

## Why it's grown

At 6,213 members and free to join, Become an Ai Engineer taps into one of the more in-demand career shifts of the last few years: experienced software engineers looking to reposition themselves as AI engineers to capture higher salaries and stronger job security. Smith's own public content, LinkedIn posts breaking down real salary numbers and interview prep, gives the community a practical, results-oriented framing rather than abstract AI hype, which likely resonates with an audience of working engineers evaluating whether the career shift is worth their time.
