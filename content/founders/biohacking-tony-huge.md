# Tony Huge and Skool's Biohacking Community: Peptides, TRT, and a Decade of Self-Experimentation

BIOHACKING | TONY HUGE is a free Skool community of roughly 4,200 members built around peptides, TRT and advanced human optimization, all filtered through the persona and protocols of its founder, Tony Huge. The community's own About page describes him as "a pioneer in modern biohacking with over a decade of real-world experimentation and protocol development," and it positions itself as the number one free biohacking community on the platform.

## Who is Tony Huge

Tony Huge's real name is Anthony Hughes, and before he became a biohacking personality he practiced as a bankruptcy lawyer in Sacramento ([Tauler Smith LLP legal coverage](https://taulersmith.com/sacramento-lawyer-dr-tony-huge-faces-lawsuit-over-supplement-claims)). In 2016 he became publicly associated with Enhanced Athlete, a supplement brand focused on performance optimization, and he has spent more than a decade since running self-experiments involving peptides, SARMs, hormones and recovery protocols, tracking his own bloodwork and publishing the results along the way ([Generation Iron profile](https://generationiron.com/tony-huge-profile-stats/)).

His public profile grew substantially through the 2019 Generation Iron documentary "Enhanced," which followed his self-testing and also chronicled legal scrutiny of Enhanced Athlete, including an FDA product seizure at the company's Sacramento facility in December 2017. There has been ongoing public debate, including in litigation, over whether Tony Huge is the founder of Enhanced Athlete or an unpaid brand ambassador for it, though he is unambiguously the company's public face ([SupplySide reporting](https://www.supplysidesj.com/healthy-living/-doctor-tony-huge-brand-ambassador-or-founder-boss-of-enhanced-athlete-); [lawsuit coverage](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/430880571/dr-tony-huge-of-enhanced-athlete-hiding-from-deposition-court-filing-says)). By late 2025, coverage of his work described his combined following across TikTok, Instagram and YouTube as pushing well into the millions ([FinancialContent](https://markets.financialcontent.com/wral/article/marketersmedia-2025-10-22-tony-huge-pushes-biohacking-into-the-fitness-mainstream)).

## What members actually get

The Skool community itself is free, and its content centers on the same territory Tony Huge has built his public persona around: peptide protocols, TRT, SARMs, and hormone optimization framed as tools for advanced human performance rather than mainstream fitness advice. His own site lays out detailed protocol breakdowns, including things like his "Complete Vilon Stack Protocol," aimed at an audience already familiar with, or actively curious about, compounds well outside typical gym-and-diet content ([tonyhuge.is](https://tonyhuge.is/about-tony-huge/)).

The community functions as a funnel and gathering point for that existing audience rather than an entry-level fitness course. It assumes members are already interested in the more experimental end of biohacking and want ongoing access to his protocol updates and community discussion.

## Why it's grown

Tony Huge's community didn't need to build an audience from a standing start. He arrived with a decade-plus of documented self-experimentation, a Generation Iron documentary, and millions of followers built through years of blunt, controversial commentary on peptides and performance enhancement. That's a similar dynamic to other large fitness-adjacent Skool communities: when a founder already has a substantial off-platform audience and years of visible track record, a free Skool community becomes a low-friction way to consolidate that audience into a single, engaged home rather than a way to build a following from zero.

It's worth noting that his path here isn't without real controversy. The FDA seizure, the "Dr." title dispute, and the ongoing legal questions around his exact role at Enhanced Athlete are all part of the public record, and anyone evaluating this specific corner of Skool should weigh that alongside the scale of his following.
