# Robb Quinn and The Sales Agency: Inside Skool's Sales Training and Placement Community

Sales Training & Placement is a $27-a-month Skool community with more than 8,100 members, built as a network for sales reps who want both training and a route into new remote sales jobs. It's run by Robb Quinn, who founded The Sales Agency after building his own career from the ground up in door-to-door and B2B sales.

## Who is Robb Quinn

Robb's own company site describes him as someone who has "actually carried a bag, closed deals, and built teams from scratch," a direct contrast with trainers who teach sales theory without having sold anything themselves ([thesalesagency.com](https://thesalesagency.com/)). Before founding the company, he spent years in door-to-door, B2C, and B2B sales, a period he's credited elsewhere with earning him multiple president's club honors between 2010 and 2017.

In the company's early days, Robb sold for other online service providers while live-streaming his own sales calls publicly to demonstrate his approach. That visibility led other sales reps to start asking him for training directly, and by 2020 he'd made his first hire, Frank DeMaio, who left a corporate role at ADP to join full time. Frank became Robb's business partner in 2022, and the two have since scaled The Sales Agency into what the company describes as a multi-7-figure business. Customer reviews on Trustpilot for thesalesagency.com are mixed, with some praising the training and others raising concerns about placement outcomes ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/thesalesagency.com)).

## What members actually get

For $27 a month, members get access to sales training drawn from Robb's own methodology, live sessions, and a network of other reps in remote and B2B sales roles. The community also functions as a recruiting pool, connecting members with job openings and companies looking to hire trained reps, which is the placement half of the community's name.

## Why it's grown

Sales training communities are common on Skool, but pairing training with an actual recruiting pipeline is a more specific value proposition. Members aren't just learning technique in the abstract, they're being positioned for real job openings that the company already has relationships with. Robb's own quota-carrying background, rather than a purely coaching-and-consulting resume, gives him credibility with an audience that tends to be skeptical of anyone who hasn't personally closed deals under pressure. Growing to over 8,100 paying members at $27 a month suggests the combination of training plus placement is resonating more than training alone typically does in this category.
