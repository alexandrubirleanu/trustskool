# Sascha Huber and Team Fire Adler: Inside Skool's 5,200-Member German Health and Freedom Community

Team Fire Adler is a German-language Skool community of roughly 5,264 members organized around the tagline "Wir inspirieren und helfen Menschen dabei, in ein Leben voller Gesundheit und Freiheit auszubrechen," or, we inspire and help people break into a life full of health and freedom. It's the community arm of a distributor network built around the supplement company Zinzino by Sascha Huber.

## Who is Sascha Huber

Huber's own account of his early life describes an underperforming student with a video game habit who lost both grandfathers to cancer, at ages 10 and 13, an experience he credits with turning his focus toward health prevention ([sascha-huber.at/about](https://sascha-huber.at/about)). After finishing secondary school he quickly earned certifications as a fitness trainer, health trainer, nutrition trainer, personal trainer, and sports mental trainer, then spent his late teens working in a gym while six separate online business attempts of his own failed.

He started with Zinzino in January 2021, earned nothing in his first four months, and relaunched in May 2021. By January 2022 he was clearing €7,400 a month and left his other work to go full-time; by May 2022, at age 22, he passed €10,000 in a single month for the first time. In September 2023, at 23, he became Zinzino's youngest-ever member of its worldwide Leader Council, part of an organization he'd built to roughly 1,500 partners and more than 12,000 customers ([sascha-huber.at/about](https://sascha-huber.at/about)). He founded Team Fire Adler in August 2021 and separately wrote a health-prevention book at age 19 ([sascha-huber.at/projekte/team-fire-adler](https://sascha-huber.at/projekte/team-fire-adler)).

## What members actually get

The Skool community centers on the Zinzino model, described on Huber's own site as a network built around test-based, personalized nutritional supplements, wrapped in the "#breakouttogether" framing that runs through the group's marketing. Members get access to team calls, incentive trips, and offline events tied to the broader Zinzino partner structure Huber runs alongside the Skool page.

## Why it's grown

Team Fire Adler's growth traces directly to Huber's own results. A founder who went from zero income to a five-figure month and a Leader Council seat within roughly two years gives the group a concrete, recent proof point instead of an abstract promise. In direct-sales-style communities, the sponsor's own visible income trajectory tends to function as the actual product being sold, and Huber's specific public numbers, exact euro figures tied to exact ages and an exact promotion date, work as that proof in a way a vaguer "financial freedom" pitch never could.
