# Jack Wood and Shredders University (Lite): Fifteen Years of Lead Guitar Distilled Into a Free Program

SHREDDERS UNIVERSITY (LITE) is a free Skool community of roughly 3,760 members, framed around structure, personalization, and results, and pitched as the completely free starting point for rock and metal guitarists who want help with technique, solos, practice routines, or getting out of a rut. The community's About page credits Jack Wood as its founder.

## Who is Jack Wood

Jack Wood is a UK-based guitarist, based in Southampton, who picked up the instrument at age 5 and has spent roughly 15 years building a career around it: touring internationally, composing and tracking award-winning music, and teaching guitar in person for more than a decade ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/jackwood_guitar/); [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@Jackwoodguitar); [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/jackwoodgtr/)). Alongside his teaching platforms, he also runs a Patreon for guitarists who want closer, ongoing access to his lessons and build process ([Patreon](https://www.patreon.com/Jackwoodguitar)).

Over that time he's built a personal brand of more than 200,000 followers across his platforms, positioning himself specifically around lead guitar and shredding, faster, technically demanding solo playing, rather than general beginner guitar instruction.

## What members actually get

Shredders University (Lite) functions as the free version of Wood's broader Shredders University program, which has also run as a paid membership community (Shredders University 2.0) elsewhere on Skool, reportedly priced around $127 a month for its roughly 140 members. The Lite tier is explicitly built for guitarists who want direct help with technique, working through specific solos, structuring practice time more effectively, and breaking out of a plateau, without the cost of the fuller paid membership. That two-tier structure lets Wood keep a wide, no-cost door open for casual players while reserving deeper, more personalized coaching for the members willing to pay for closer feedback.

## Why it's grown

Wood's community benefits from a genuinely rare combination in online guitar education: real touring and recording experience layered under more than ten years of direct teaching practice, plus an already-large personal following built before Skool became part of his platform mix. Offering a free "Lite" tier alongside a more expensive full membership lets him serve casual or budget-conscious guitarists at no cost while still monetizing more serious students through the paid version, and it gives newer followers from his Instagram and YouTube content a natural, low-risk next step into the sharper feedback and structure a full lead-guitar curriculum could offer.

Because so much of Wood's audience discovers him first through short-form video, riff breakdowns, gear reviews, quick technique fixes, the free Skool tier gives that audience somewhere to land that isn't just another algorithm-fed feed. It's a community built for people who already like his playing and want a more structured, ongoing relationship with his teaching rather than scattered clips.
