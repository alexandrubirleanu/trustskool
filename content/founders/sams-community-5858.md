# Sam Zia and Optimal Living: A Million-Follower TikToker's Skool Brotherhood

Optimal Living is a free Skool community, home to around 2,300 members, pitched as the system for "becoming THAT Guy," leveling up physique, style and mindset through accountability, challenges and direct mentorship. Its URL still carries the old working name, sams-community, a small clue to who's actually running the show.

## Who is Sam Zia

Sam Zia is a fitness and lifestyle creator who built his following on short-form video, most visibly on TikTok under the handle @samm.zia, where he's amassed roughly 1.1 million followers and 76.4 million likes ([TikTok stats via IDCrawl](https://www.idcrawl.com/sam-zia)). He's also active on Snapchat as @samm_zia, where he's posted documented physical transformations, including a widely viewed two-month before-and-after series charting a shift from a lean frame to a more muscular, defined physique ([Snapchat profile](https://www.snapchat.com/@samm_zia)).

Inside his own community, that transformation content isn't just marketing, it's the curriculum. Members reference following "Sam's split," a nod to his personal workout routine that's become a shared reference point inside the group, according to posts on the community's own discussion board ([Optimal Living community post](https://www.skool.com/sams-community-5858/sam-zia-split)).

## What members actually get

Per the group's own about page, Optimal Living runs on weekly community calls where anyone who requests to speak gets the chance to, plus direct 1-on-1 calls with Sam's team and peer groups for accountability. The pitch leans hard on his own words: "I built this because it's what I wish I had when I started," positioning the group as a substitute for the structure and mentorship he didn't have during his own transformation. That framing, a creator building the resource he wishes existed rather than a generic coaching program, is a common thread across his social content, where the transformation story is told and retold as the origin point for everything that followed.

## Why it's grown

The mechanics here are familiar from creator-led Skool groups: Sam already had a large, engaged short-form audience before he opened the community, built over years of transformation and lifestyle content on TikTok and Snapchat. That existing audience, rather than any Skool-specific marketing, is almost certainly what's pushed Optimal Living past 2,300 members on a free tier. The personal-transformation narrative also does double duty as both proof of concept and recruiting material, every new post showing his own physique progress functions as an implicit testimonial for the system members are being asked to follow. Weekly open-mic-style calls, where any member can request to speak rather than only a select few, is also a fairly low-cost way to keep a free community feeling personal at scale, even once membership climbs into the thousands.
