# Trevor Calais and Lets Geaux Hustle: Inside Skool's Veteran Wealth-Building Community

Lets Geaux Hustle is a free Skool community with more than 7,800 members, aimed squarely at veterans, active-duty service members, and civilians who want to escape the traditional 9-to-5. It's run by Trevor Calais, a US military veteran who built a company around helping other veterans use benefits they're often not maximizing.

## Who is Trevor Calais

Trevor lists himself as the founder of Let's Geaux Hustle on LinkedIn, positioning himself as a veteran turned entrepreneur focused on financial freedom for other service members ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/trevor-calais-a1271258/)). His company's own site describes his mission as helping active duty military, veterans, and side-hustling 9-to-5 workers use their VA benefits to escape reliance on a single paycheck, with a stated track record of more than 10,000 clients served ([letsgeauxhustle.com](https://www.letsgeauxhustle.com/)). He also maintains a YouTube channel and a TikTok account under the Let's Geaux Hustle name, both used to publish testimonial-style content from people going through the same three-phase process ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCeOwwaUcGHb6lgbgpHfKOTg); [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@letsgeauxhustle)).

The company runs a structured three-phase system built around that mission: first increasing a veteran's VA disability rating, then improving credit and securing business funding, and finally using that funding to acquire income-generating assets, everything from rental property to vehicles listed on platforms like Turo.

## What members actually get

Inside the free Skool community, members get access to training built around each phase of that system, along with case studies like Trevor's own "Turo Playbook," a guide specifically about listing a financed vehicle on the car-sharing platform Turo to turn a monthly payment into a cash-flowing asset. The community also functions as a lead generation layer for the company's paid strategy calls and funding-support services, with free content demonstrating the approach before any paid engagement.

## Why it's grown

Veterans are a well-defined, findable audience with a real, underused resource, VA disability ratings and related benefits, that most veteran-focused financial content doesn't address directly. By naming that specific mechanism instead of making a generic financial-freedom pitch, Trevor differentiates the community from the much larger pool of general side-hustle and wealth groups on Skool. His own background as a veteran gives him instant credibility with the target audience, and the free entry point, paired with a track record he can point to in thousands of past clients, makes joining an easy first step before any funding or coaching services get pitched.
