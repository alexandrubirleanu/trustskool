# James Dickerson and The Vibe Marketers: Inside Skool's 2,700-Member AI Marketing Community

The Vibe Marketers pitches itself as access to thousands of collaborators, ideas, and workflows for anyone trying to become what it calls a "vibe marketer." The free community has reached roughly 2,680 members.

## Who runs it

The community was created by James Dickerson, who also goes by "The Boring Marketer," and is credited with pioneering the term "vibe marketing" itself: applying the same rapid-iteration, AI-assisted approach that defined "vibe coding" to marketing work instead ([IM Rhys review](https://www.imrhys.com/the-vibe-marketers-review-best-skool-course-for-vibe-coders/)). Dickerson runs the community with a team of ten admins, and the group enforces what an independent, unsponsored review describes as a "no noise, all signal" culture, where self-promotion is limited and every post is expected to provide real value rather than function as an ad for the poster's own offer ([IM Rhys review](https://www.imrhys.com/the-vibe-marketers-review-best-skool-course-for-vibe-coders/)). A separate community review site, Skoolmakers, independently profiles The Vibe Marketers as well, corroborating that the community and its stated positioning are recognized outside Dickerson's own marketing ([Skoolmakers](https://skoolmakers.com/communities/vibe-marketers/)).

## What members actually get

Per the IM Rhys review, which discloses it was not sponsored or incentivized by Dickerson or the community, members get access to AI-assisted marketing workflows, frameworks for rapid iteration and testing, and a community culture that actively surfaces case studies from other members, including examples of participants cutting customer acquisition costs by 60% and automating the bulk of client reporting work. The review's author reported a personal result of roughly 10x efficiency gains and an 80% reduction in workload after using the group's AI workflows over six months of active membership.

## Why it's grown

"Vibe coding" became one of the most-discussed terms in tech through 2025, and Dickerson's bet was that the same underlying behavior, using AI tools to move fast and iterate rather than working from rigid playbooks, would resonate just as strongly applied to marketing specifically. Being early to name and claim that specific framing, rather than joining a crowded "AI marketing" category with no distinct angle, gives The Vibe Marketers a sharper identity than most adjacent Skool communities. The heavy moderation against self-promotion is also a differentiator worth noting: in a niche where many free communities function mostly as lead-generation funnels for admins' paid offers, a genuinely enforced no-noise policy is the kind of detail that shows up in independent reviews rather than the community's own marketing, which is likely part of why it retains members rather than just accumulating sign-ups.
