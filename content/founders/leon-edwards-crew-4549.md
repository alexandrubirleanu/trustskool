# Mario and Martin Rios: The Twin Coaches Behind the Leon Edwards Crew on Skool

The Leon Edwards Crew is a free Skool community with just over 5,000 members, built around athletic bodybuilding coaching aimed at lifters who want an aesthetic, athletic physique rather than pure powerlifting size. Despite the name, it isn't run by the UFC welterweight champion himself. It's run by Mario Rios and Martin Rios, identical twin coaches who've built a following partly through content about, and inspired by, Edwards' own physique.

## Who are Mario and Martin Rios

Mario Rios posts as Coach Mario Rios on YouTube, where the channel had accumulated roughly 438,000 subscribers and more than 780 million total video views as of mid-2026, publishing sports and fitness content since 2022 ([youtubers.me channel stats](https://in.youtubers.me/mario-rios/youtuber-stats/en)). His brother Martin runs a separate channel, MartinRiosFit, covering similar athletic bodybuilding ground. Their content frequently references Leon Edwards directly, including videos built around Edwards' fights and physique, which is where the crew's name and branding come from ([Mario Rios on TikTok, @coach_mariorios](https://www.tiktok.com/@coach_mariorios/video/7241270632686865710); [YouTube: Mario Rios meets Leon Edwards](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2OaLuA8KxFc)).

Outside the free content, the twins run paid one-on-one coaching under the Rios Twins Fitness brand, including structured programs like a 90-day aesthetic body plan and a 120-day peak athleticism package aimed at building both muscle and functional athletic performance rather than just size ([riostwinsfitness.mykajabi.com](https://riostwinsfitness.mykajabi.com/120-day-peak-athleticism-package)).

## What members actually get

The Skool community's own description frames it as a coaching hub focused on helping lifters improve both aesthetics and athletic performance, positioning itself against the more static, size-only version of bodybuilding. For a free community at this size, it functions primarily as a funnel and content hub tied to the brothers' YouTube output, where the paid coaching programs are the actual product.

## Why it's grown

The growth logic here is the same pattern seen across a lot of fitness-adjacent Skool communities: build the audience first on YouTube and TikTok with high-volume, algorithm-friendly content, then use a free Skool group as the place where that audience becomes a list you can talk to directly. Tying the brand to Leon Edwards, a genuinely famous, currently relevant UFC champion, gives the community a recognizable hook that a generic "athletic bodybuilding" name wouldn't carry on its own, even though the community itself is run entirely by the Rios twins rather than by Edwards.
