# Jonas Winkler and Winkler Gilde: Germany's Best-Known Carpentry Influencer Builds a Skool Guild

Winkler Gilde is a free, German-language Skool community with around 2,350 members, described on its own page as a home for hobbyists through professional carpenters, covering "Projekte, Tipps, First-Buyer Rights uvm." The name is not a coincidence: it's built around Jonas Winkler.

## Who is Jonas Winkler

Winkler is a trained Tischlermeister (master carpenter) and product designer from the Hanover region who has spent years turning that trade knowledge into one of Germany's biggest woodworking media brands ([HolzWerken profile](https://www.holzwerken.net/blog/jonas-winkler/das-bin-ich-jonas-winkler/)). His YouTube channel has grown past 500,000 subscribers, alongside roughly 428,000 followers on Instagram, built on tutorials and workshop content ([Baufluencer.de profile](https://baufluencer.de/influencer/jonas-winkler/)). Tool manufacturer Festool has featured him directly, running an interview with him as a "Tischlermeister & YouTuber" ([Festool interview](https://www.festool.de/blog/work/jonas-winkler-interview)), and German business magazine brand eins profiled him as one of the industry's "Botschafter," ambassadors making skilled trades visible to a younger audience through influencer-style content ([brand eins feature](https://www.brandeins.de/magazine/brand-eins-wirtschaftsmagazin/2024/handwerk/jonaswinklerofficial-influencer-im-handwerk-die-botschafter)). He also hosts a podcast covering the same craft-and-business territory ([brand eins podcast episode](https://detektor.fm/wirtschaft/brand-eins-podcast-jonas-winkler)).

## What members actually get

The community's own pitch is short and personal: "Direkter Austausch mit mir, persönliche Tipps, Projekte und Inspiration," direct exchange with Winkler himself rather than a generic curriculum. The mention of "First-Buyer Rights" in the community description suggests members get early access to something tied to his product or tool recommendations ahead of the general public, though the specifics aren't detailed on the page itself. Given that his public content already spans everything from beginner projects to advanced workshop technique, the community likely functions as the space where questions that don't fit neatly into a YouTube comment section actually get answered directly.

## Why it's grown

Winkler didn't build this group from zero. He already commanded one of the largest owned audiences of any tradesperson in Germany, half a million YouTube subscribers and over 400,000 on Instagram, accumulated over years of free tutorial content before Skool entered the picture. The brand eins feature is instructive here too: it frames him as part of a small cohort of craftspeople using influencer tactics specifically to make traditional trades feel current again, which likely pulls in a younger audience already primed to want closer access to him. Winkler Gilde reads less like a group built to attract strangers and more like a direct-access tier layered on top of an audience he'd already spent years earning elsewhere.

His podcast work adds a further channel that doesn't show up in follower counts at all: it lets him go deeper on the business side of running a trade, not just the how-to side, which likely appeals to the segment of Winkler Gilde members who are craftspeople themselves rather than hobbyists looking for weekend project tips.
