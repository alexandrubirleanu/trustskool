# Javier García and Mundo Amazon: The Spanish-Language Amazon FBA Community Built From One Failed Routine

Mundo Amazon is a Spanish-language Skool community with roughly 4,200 members, offering exclusive resources, PDFs, and live training sessions around building an Amazon FBA business. It's aimed squarely at the U.S. and Latin American Spanish-speaking market, a segment that's often underserved by English-first e-commerce education.

## Who is Javier García

Javier García built a career in international business before deciding it wasn't for him. A Univision Miami feature on his career change describes the transformation from a stable corporate track into entrepreneurship, driven by a sense that his job wasn't giving him personal or professional satisfaction ([Univision profile](https://www.univision.com/local/miami-wltv/de-negocios-internacionales-a-emprendedor-la-transformacion-de-javier-garcia)).

In 2018 he opened his first Amazon FBA store, and after testing multiple approaches he settled on the wholesale model, buying and reselling products from established brands like Apple, Microsoft and Nike in bulk quantities through Amazon rather than building his own private-label products from scratch. According to his LinkedIn profile, that approach has generated more than $25 million in cumulative Amazon sales ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/javier-garcia-4b483840/)).

## What members actually get

García founded Mundo Amazon in 2020 as a way to teach the specific wholesale FBA method he'd developed, rather than general e-commerce theory. The community's own description promises exclusive resources, PDFs, regular updates, and live training sessions, and it's built specifically for Spanish speakers, a positioning choice that matters given how much English-language noise exists in the Amazon FBA education space.

The stated goal is helping members reach $10,000 a month in Amazon income, and García has said his broader mission is giving the Latin community the tools to build their own businesses and gain more control over their financial future. He's also built out a paid arm of the brand, Mundo Amazon Academy, which offers a bootcamp and more structured, intensive training beyond the free community.

## Why it's grown

Mundo Amazon's growth is a fairly clean case of a founder building credibility through actual sales results before turning to teaching. García didn't launch a course on Amazon FBA theory; he spent years running an eight-figure wholesale operation first, then built the community and academy around the specific playbook that got him there. That sequencing, results before curriculum, tends to travel well in any language, but it particularly matters in the Spanish-speaking e-commerce education market, where students often have fewer trusted, native-language options to choose from.

The free Skool community also acts as a natural top-of-funnel for the paid academy: anyone can join, see the caliber of training and community discussion, and then decide whether to invest further in the bootcamp or intensive coaching track.
