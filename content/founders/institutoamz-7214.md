# Rafel Mayol and Instituto AMZ: Inside Skool's Free Amazon FBA Community

Instituto AMZ is a free Skool community with nearly 7,920 members, built around teaching Amazon FBA and e-commerce to Spanish-speaking entrepreneurs. It's the official group tied to Emprendedores.com, whose founder Rafel Mayol has publicly positioned the community as a no-cost version of a course that previously sold for between 1,000 and 2,000 euros.

## Who is Rafel Mayol

Rafel was born in Barcelona, Spain, and trained as an engineer before moving to the United States, where he initially took a conventional corporate job ([Oberlo profile](https://www.oberlo.com/blog/rafel-mayols-success-an-entrepreneurship-journey-full-of-learnings)). He later shifted into entrepreneurship, and by his own account went from zero revenue to more than 20 million euros across several businesses, including Wiohair.com, a Spanish e-commerce brand focused on hair-loss products, and Sonrisa.com, which he describes as the top invisible-aligner brand in Uruguay. He has discussed that path in more depth on his own podcast and in guest interviews, including an episode of the "Visión Inteligente de Negocios" podcast on Spotify ([Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/episode/2J9y7Qygi18ROy24zvNKLn)), and he maintains an active YouTube channel under his own name ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/rafelmayol)).

Through Emprendedores.com, he also runs an e-commerce incubator program that, according to his own figures, has generated more than 4 million euros in member sales and helped over 40 entrepreneurs reach what he calls the Club 10K milestone. He talks about that broader journey, from 0 euros to more than 20 million euros in revenue, on his own podcast and on X, where he frames sharing what he's learned as the point rather than any single course ([X](https://x.com/Rafel_Mayol/status/1767913949964497037)).

## What members actually get

Instituto AMZ offers what was originally sold as a paid Amazon FBA master course, entirely free inside the Skool community. The stated intent, repeated in the group's own posts, is to remove the tuition barrier that Rafel says has historically locked people out of e-commerce education, with members encouraged to help each other with questions rather than relying solely on paid support.

## Why it's grown

Converting a formerly $1,000-plus course into a free offer is a strong, specific hook in a niche where most competitors, including several other AMZ-branded academies searchable online, still charge for similar material. Rafel's own track record, a verifiable jump from a standard corporate job to a multi-million-euro portfolio of e-commerce brands, gives the free course more weight than it would carry from an anonymous creator. Removing the price tag also lowers the bar for a Spanish-speaking audience that may be wary of high-ticket guru courses, letting Emprendedores.com build a large top-of-funnel community first and monetize later through other parts of the business.
