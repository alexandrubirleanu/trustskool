# Ebed Jarrell and Iron Faith Wrestling Academy: Inside Skool's Wrestling Coaching Community

Iron Faith Wrestling Academy is a $39-a-month Skool community with roughly 8,780 members, built to help wrestlers train smarter rather than just harder. It's run by Ebed Jarrell, a coach whose own wrestling career took him from feeling stuck as a young athlete to becoming a state champion and Division I-level competitor.

## Who is Ebed Jarrell

Ebed is one of the coaches behind Iron Faith, a wrestling club based in Warwick, Rhode Island, which also runs an in-person program alongside its online Skool community ([Iron Faith about page](https://www.ironfaith.com/about-us)). He and Stephen Jarrell are credited as the founders of Iron Faith Grappling, with the broader Iron Faith brand originally founded by Stephen in 2019. Ebed maintains an active coaching presence on LinkedIn and Instagram under the Iron Faith name, where he posts training content and updates from the club ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ebed-jarrell-4383b8119/); [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/ebedjarrell/)).

According to the club's own materials, Ebed's coaching philosophy centers on the idea that his own breakthrough as a wrestler came not from training harder but from simplifying his approach and building a clear, repeatable system, the same framework he now applies with the wrestlers he coaches.

## What members actually get

For $39 a month, members get instructional wrestling content built around that same simplified system, along with supplementary material like wrestling-specific cardio programming designed to build in-season conditioning without extra mat time. The academy operates alongside Iron Faith's physical club in Rhode Island and a separate free tier that offers a lighter sample of the same content, so the paid online community functions as a way to extend the full coaching relationship to wrestlers who can't train in person.

## Why it's grown

Wrestling is a sport where credibility is unusually easy to verify: either you competed at a real level or you didn't, and Ebed's own record as a state champion and D1 competitor gives the academy a foundation that's hard to fake. Charging a modest but real monthly fee, rather than making the community free, also signals a more serious training product aimed at wrestlers and parents who are already invested in the sport rather than casual browsers. Combined with an existing physical club that's been operating since 2019, the online academy gives Iron Faith a way to reach wrestlers well outside driving distance of Warwick, Rhode Island.
