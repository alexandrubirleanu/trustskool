# Jean-Philippe Lavoie and BBQ Nation: The Brothers Behind Quebec's Biggest French-Language BBQ Community

BBQ Nation is a Skool community of nearly 2,900 members, priced at a token one dollar a month, built for people who want to learn, share, and become masters of fire in the tradition of BBQ Québec. Its own about page calls it the official community for learning, sharing, and mastering grilling, with courses, challenges, and unlimited BBQ talk. The community is credited to Jp Lavoie on its Skool about page, one half of the Lavoie brothers who founded BBQ Québec.

## Who is Jean-Philippe Lavoie

Jean-Philippe, known as JP, co-founded BBQ Québec with his older brother Maxime, and the company's own history page traces the business back to their family's hardware store, where Max started assembling barbecues for customers at age 12 and JP later developed his own passion for the craft by watching his brother work ([BBQ Québec: Notre Histoire](https://bbqquebec.com/pages/notre-histoire); [Les Frères Lavoie](https://bbqquebec.com/pages/freres-lavoie)). The brand launched in 2013, and the brothers opened their first dedicated retail location in Ste-Foy the following year, later expanding to Boucherville, Laval, and Montreal.

Within the business, the brothers split roles, Max operates as the primary grill master and product expert, while JP focuses on the educational side of the company, including courses, corporate events, web content, and product testing for the brand's sauce and spice line. That division lines up directly with JP being the one credited as the Skool community's founder, since a community built on courses and shared learning is squarely his side of the business.

## What members actually get

For a dollar a month, members get access to an ongoing space built around BBQ Québec's broader educational output, which over the years has included two published books, an annual BBQ Fest de Québec, a training program called BBQ Camp, and reported instruction of more than 20,000 people through in-person classes. The Skool community functions as a digital extension of that in-person teaching, giving members a place to ask questions, share their own grilling results, and stay connected to a brand that's been building BBQ culture in Quebec for well over a decade.

The extremely low price point, effectively a nominal fee rather than a meaningful subscription cost, suggests the community's real purpose is deepening engagement with an already-loyal customer base rather than functioning as a standalone revenue product.

## Why it's grown

BBQ Québec had already built a substantial off-platform reputation before this Skool community existed, competing internationally, appearing on television, and being compared by grilling authority Steven Raichlen to a young version of himself, so the Skool group inherits trust the brothers built over more than a decade rather than starting from zero. French-language BBQ content is also a genuinely underserved niche compared to the flooded English-language grilling space, giving BBQ Nation a clearer path to becoming the default gathering place for Quebec's grilling community specifically.

Combined with the near-free price of entry, that existing brand strength likely explains why a niche this specific has still managed to approach 2,900 members on Skool.
