# Nsima Inyang and The Stronger Human Movement: Inside Skool's 28,000-Member Movement Community

The Stronger Human Movement is a $5-a-month Skool community with more than 28,000 members built around unconventional strength and movement training — kettlebells, maces, sandbags, and "rope flow" rather than standard gym programming. It's run by Nsima Inyang, a coach whose credentials span competitive bodybuilding, Brazilian jiu-jitsu, and powerlifting, and who has appeared as a guest on one of the biggest interview podcasts in the world.

## Who is Nsima Inyang

Nsima Inyang holds a black belt in Brazilian jiu-jitsu, competed as a professional natural bodybuilder placing in the top five in the world, and competes at an elite level in powerlifting ([Tim Ferriss Show episode #816](https://tim.blog/2025/06/19/nsima-inyang/)). He's also a co-host of Mark Bell's Power Project, one of the most-followed strength and fitness podcasts in the world, which has given him a built-in audience of people already interested in unconventional training methods.

In June 2025, Inyang was a guest on The Tim Ferriss Show for an episode titled "Mutant and Movement Coach — True Athleticism at Any Age, Microdosing Movement, 'Rope Flow' as a Key Unlock, Why Sleds and Sandbags Matter, and Much More," where he discussed his training philosophy of blending traditional strength sports with tools like kettlebells and rope flow to build what he calls more natural, playful movement capacity ([tim.blog transcript](https://tim.blog/2025/06/19/nsima-inyang-transcript/)). That kind of placement — a full-length feature on one of the most listened-to interview shows in the English-speaking world — is a meaningfully different validation signal than most fitness creators on Skool can point to.

## What members actually get

The Stronger Human Movement is organized around a training progression Inyang calls Breathe → Foundation → Bounce → Control → Flow, which moves members from breathing and movement-quality basics through balance and coordination work ("bounce" and "control") toward more advanced, playful movement patterns ("flow"). The community has been described as one of the biggest movement-and-health communities on Skool, and it functions as the entry point into Inyang's broader business, which includes The Stronger Human's e-commerce site and its own dedicated podcast.

At $5 a month, it's priced as a mass-market entry product rather than a premium coaching offer, consistent with a strategy of using a large, cheap community as the top of a funnel that likely leads to higher-ticket coaching or products elsewhere in Inyang's business.

## Why it's grown

Two things stand out. First, credibility stacking: Inyang isn't a single-sport specialist claiming general fitness expertise — he has genuine competitive results in bodybuilding, jiu-jitsu, and powerlifting, plus a co-hosting role on an established, high-reach podcast (Power Project), which gave the Skool community a running head start in audience and trust before it needed to grow organically on its own. Second, differentiation: "movement" training built around tools like sandbags, maces, and rope flow occupies a distinct niche from both mainstream bodybuilding content and mainstream mobility/yoga content, which has let the community grow without competing head-on with the largest fitness creators on the platform.

The Tim Ferriss feature in particular functions as a durable piece of third-party validation that keeps sending new, higher-intent visitors to Inyang's ecosystem well after the episode aired.
