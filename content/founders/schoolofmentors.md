# Jack Dumoulin and School of Mentors: Inside Skool's 6,100-Member Entrepreneurship Community

School of Mentors calls itself the "#1 Entrepreneurship Community in the World," built around weekly live calls where members get to question millionaires and billionaires directly. It runs at $49/month with 6,167 members [Skool](https://www.skool.com/schoolofmentors/about).

## Who runs it

School of Mentors is an extension of School of Hard Knocks, a media brand co-founded in 2021 by brothers James and Jack Dumoulin along with their childhood friend Joshua Smith [ScamRisk](https://www.scamrisk.com/school-of-mentors-review-is-james-and-jack-dumoulin-legit/). Jack Dumoulin manages the Skool community day to day [Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@jackdumoulin), and per coverage of the community, his background includes UT Austin's Red McCombs School of Business and winning an Excel World Championship at age 17 [beehiiv](https://bawz.beehiiv.com/p/school-of-mentors-review-2025-jack-dumoulin-s-weekly-calls-with-millionaires-billionaires). School of Hard Knocks, the parent brand, has built a large independent following through its interview content — reporting on the order of 6+ million TikTok followers and over 1 million YouTube subscribers, per the same coverage.

## What members actually get

School of Mentors functions as the community layer on top of School of Hard Knocks' interview show: rather than just watching interviews with successful founders, members get weekly live sessions where they can ask the same guests questions directly. The community also offers a library described as 1,000+ hours of existing content, plus workshops for members working through their own businesses. It's been described as a "Netflix for Entrepreneurs" model — less a structured curriculum, more an ongoing feed of access to people who've actually built large businesses [CommunityHunter](https://communityhunter.com/reviews/school-of-mentors-school-of-hard-knocks/).

## Why it's grown

The Dumoulin brothers built a genuinely large independent media audience (millions of TikTok and YouTube followers) before turning it into a paid Skool community, which is a materially different growth path than most "entrepreneurship community" operators who start the community first and try to build an audience around it. That existing audience gives School of Mentors a natural, low-cost-of-acquisition funnel, and the access-to-real-guests format is harder for competitors to replicate than a generic course library, since it depends on the founders' existing relationships and interview pipeline. Reviews reflect this: the community holds 180+ Trustpilot reviews averaging 5 stars, with feedback consistently citing mentor quality and access as the standout [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/schoolofmentors.com).

One thing worth noting for prospective members: pricing on the Skool page has moved over time (from an earlier $25/month reported in some reviews to $49/month currently), so it's worth checking the live price before joining rather than relying on older reviews.
