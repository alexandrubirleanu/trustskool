# John Marshall and Full Carnivore Challenge: Inside Skool's Animal-Based Diet Community

Full Carnivore Challenge is a Skool community with just over 3,050 members, pitched as a way to build a carnivore or animal-based lifestyle that members won't need a break from. It's run by John Marshall, who operates under the brand name Carnivore Mentor.

## Who is John Marshall

Marshall publishes as "carnivorementor" on Instagram, where his profile identifies him directly as "John Marshall // Animal Based Coach," and where he's built an audience of roughly 328,000 followers around carnivore diet content, results videos, and coaching promotion ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/carnivorementor/)). His branded site, carnivorementor.com, runs under the name "ABLE by Carnivore Mentor" and hosts his coaching programs, including a Primal Fitness Challenge and the Full Carnivore Challenge itself, alongside meal plans, bodyweight and gym workout programs, and a dedicated Animal Based Masterclass ([carnivorementor.com](https://www.carnivorementor.com/pages/home)).

That combination, a large, name-attached Instagram following plus a matching branded website offering the same programs found inside the Skool community, ties the account directly to a real, identifiable coach rather than an anonymous diet-content page.

## What members actually get

Inside the community, the curriculum follows the same structure Marshall promotes publicly: nutrition principles training and a "how to eat" course aimed at people transitioning into or refining a carnivore or animal-based diet. The positioning throughout is built around sustainability, framing the diet as a long-term lifestyle change rather than a short-term reset, which lines up with the community's own tagline about never needing a break from it.

## Why it's grown

Carnivore and animal-based eating has grown from a fringe idea into a much more mainstream corner of the diet and wellness world over the past several years, driven partly by high-profile advocates and partly by a broader backlash against processed food. Marshall's substantial, verifiable Instagram following gives Full Carnivore Challenge a real top-of-funnel audience feeding into Skool rather than relying on cold discovery inside the platform itself.

Having a consistent, name-attached brand across Instagram, his own site, and the Skool community also helps in a niche where a lot of carnivore-diet content online comes from anonymous or pseudonymous accounts, since prospective members can verify Marshall's identity and following independently before joining.

Restrictive diets also tend to see high early dropout without ongoing accountability, which is part of why a live community structure, rather than a one-time meal plan PDF, tends to outperform in this category specifically. Bundling a challenge format with an existing large following gives Marshall a way to funnel people who are already curious about carnivore eating into a structured program with peer support built in from day one.
