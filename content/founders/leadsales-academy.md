# Roberto Peñacastro and Leadsales Academy: The WhatsApp CRM Founder's Skool Classroom

Leadsales Academy is a free, Spanish-language Skool community with around 2,290 members, built to help users "dominar Leadsales" (master Leadsales) through guides, webinars and sales hacks. On Skool itself, the group is administered under the company's own account rather than a personal name, but the business behind it has a clearly documented founder: Roberto Peñacastro.

## Who is Roberto Peñacastro

Peñacastro, who goes by Roby, previously worked at Google helping small businesses scale their advertising-driven sales before founding Leadsales in 2020 alongside co-founder David Villa Cañez, who had earlier built the Latin American logistics platform Mienvio ([TechCrunch coverage](https://techcrunch.com/2023/08/28/whatsapp-leadsales-conversational-commerce/)). Per that same TechCrunch reporting, the pair noticed small businesses shifting to sell through WhatsApp without the tooling to scale it, and bootstrapped Leadsales to more than 1,400 customers across Mexico, Colombia and Peru and $1.6 million in annual recurring revenue before raising a $3.7 million seed round led by Ulu Ventures and Blue Pointe Ventures to expand into Brazil. He's discussed the company's growth on his LinkedIn, where he describes helping "+2,800 empresas a triplicar sus ventas a través de WhatsApp" ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/robertopenacastroortega/)), and has appeared as a guest on entrepreneurship podcasts including School for Startups Radio ([School for Startups Radio episode](https://schoolforstartupsradio.com/crm_for_whatsapp/)) and a Spotify interview titled "Un Whatsapp en Esteroides?" ([Spotify episode](https://creators.spotify.com/pod/profile/sebastiangomz1/episodes/Un-Whatsapp-en-Esteroides--c-Roberto-Peacastro-CEO--Co-Founder-de-Leadsales-e2ug9aj)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, members get exclusive guides, webinars, and sales tactics for using Leadsales, plus activities with prizes to keep engagement going, all oriented around getting more out of the CRM product itself rather than general business coaching. That's a narrower promise than most Skool business communities make, and it's a sensible one for a group whose members are already paying for the underlying software: the content only has to make an existing tool more useful, not sell an entire new business model from scratch.

## Why it's grown

Leadsales Academy's membership tracks the underlying company's own growth rather than an individual creator's personal audience, it's the education and retention layer for a real, venture-backed SaaS product with thousands of paying customers across several countries. That gives it a different growth engine than most Skool groups: new members arrive because they've signed up for the CRM itself, not because they discovered Peñacastro on social media. His own public profile, TechCrunch coverage, founder interviews, an active LinkedIn, still matters here because it's what makes the underlying product (and by extension the free academy built around it) credible to prospective customers evaluating WhatsApp CRM options in a crowded Latin American market.
