# Jake Whan and Foundations of Ascension: Inside Skool's 9,700-Member Breathwork Community

Foundations of Ascension has attracted nearly 9,700 members on Skool as a free entry point into breathwork and somatic healing, framed around what the community calls the beginning of a personal "ascension process." Behind it is Jake Whan, a breathwork facilitator and podcaster who also runs a paid membership called Ascension University.

## Who is Jake Whan

Jake Whan describes himself as a spiritual leader and speaker devoted to what he calls restoring "Christ Consciousness through embodied relationship with God," a framing that runs through his personal site and his teaching ([jakewhan.com](https://www.jakewhan.com/)). He hosts "The Second Coming," a podcast focused on spiritual leadership, and has appeared as a guest discussing breathwork facilitation on other shows, including an episode of "A State of Mind" on Apple Podcasts titled around his work as a breathwork facilitator and channel ([Apple Podcasts guest episode](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/spirit-in-action-jake-whan-breathwork-facilitator-channel/id1462588220?i=1000703087863)). He maintains an active Instagram account and a YouTube channel under his own name, and his teaching blends breathwork, nervous-system-focused practices and energy work rather than sitting in a single conventional wellness category ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/jake_whan/)).

## What members actually get

Foundations of Ascension is positioned as a free space for spiritual seekers and "heart-led leaders" working on healing, inner transformation and what the community describes as the rise of a "New Earth." It leans on breathwork, somatics and energy-healing practices rather than traditional talk-therapy or clinical wellness framing. For members who want to go deeper, Whan offers Ascension University, a paid six-figure-framed membership hosted separately that includes bi-weekly live teachings and breathwork journeys, according to his own promotional posts ([Facebook post](https://www.facebook.com/jake.whan.7/posts/inside-of-ascension-university-i-teach-you-exactly-how-to-heal-remember-and-live/24976321831993783/)).

## Why it's grown

Whan's growth path follows a pattern common among spiritually-oriented Skool communities: build a following through consistent podcasting and guest appearances on aligned shows, then use a free community as the first, low-commitment step before inviting the most engaged members into paid teaching. Appearing as a guest on other breathwork and spirituality podcasts, rather than only hosting his own, has likely extended his reach into audiences that wouldn't have found him through his own channel alone.

The specificity of the spiritual framing, centered on Christ Consciousness and breathwork rather than a more generic "manifestation" or "healing" pitch, also gives Foundations of Ascension a distinct identity in a crowded spiritual-wellness segment of Skool, which may explain why it has grown as large as it has despite competing against dozens of similarly themed free communities.
