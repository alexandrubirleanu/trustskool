# Eric Seto and Invest & Retire Community: A CPA's Free Skool Classroom for Long-Term Investors

Invest & Retire Community is a free Skool community of roughly 3,684 members focused on long-term investing and retirement strategy for working professionals. It's run by Eric Seto, a Chartered Professional Accountant and Chartered Investment Manager based in Vancouver.

## Who is Eric Seto

Seto's LinkedIn lists him as a CPA and CIM associated with BranchPoint Asset Management in Vancouver, BC, with professional experience spanning Vancouver and Hong Kong and CPA Canada certification completed between 2013 and 2017 ([LinkedIn](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/setoeric)). He's also listed on Crunchbase as a finance professional and entrepreneur ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/eric-seto-7948)), and he's the founder of 5mininvesting.com, a separate investing-education site where he publishes shorter-form content ([5mininvesting.com](https://5mininvesting.com/)). A press release distributed through Marketers Media covers his retirement-investing guide and training work under his own name ([Marketers Media](https://news.marketersmedia.com/eric-setos-guide-to-investing-for-retirement-plus-comprehensive-training/89081155)).

Separately, Seto is the founder and CEO of Investing Accelerator, a paid financial education platform aimed at professionals without a finance background, which the Skool community's own discussion threads reference directly, including posts about angel investors and hedge funds run inside that program.

## What members actually get

Inside the free community, members get long-term investing discussion, monthly passive-income ideas, and threads where Seto and other members post real portfolio moves, including year-to-date return tracking on stocks and indices and posts from parents discussing how they're investing on behalf of their kids. It functions as an open discussion layer sitting alongside Seto's paid Investing Accelerator program, rather than a hard sales funnel.

## Why it's grown

Seto's combination of formal credentials (CPA, CIM) and a public, named professional history is a stronger trust signal in the finance-education space than the anonymous or pseudonymous accounts common among "passive income" communities. Because personal finance carries real downside risk for members who act on bad advice, a free community anchored by someone with licensed, checkable credentials in accounting and investment management has an easier time building trust than an equivalent community run by an unverified persona, even before members ever consider upgrading to Seto's paid program.
