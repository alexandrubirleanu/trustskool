# Sara Brewer and Overcome Pornography For Good: The Shame-Focused Coach Behind Skool's Recovery Community

Overcome Pornography For Good is a free Skool community with nearly 2,850 members built around a specific, contrarian premise, that quitting pornography compulsively doesn't require willpower, it requires addressing the shame underneath the behavior. It's led by Sara Brewer, credited as the founder on the community's own Skool about page and the author whose name the whole program is built around.

## Who is Sara Brewer

Sara Brewer is a certified life and faith-based coach who founded the Center for Overcoming Pornography, and her own site describes how she developed the method after coming to see shame, rather than lack of discipline, as the central driver of compulsive pornography use ([Center for Overcoming Pornography, About](https://centerforovercoming.com/about)). Once she built a coaching process focused on addressing that root cause directly, she reports her clients began seeing results that a purely willpower-based approach hadn't produced.

She has since published a book under the same name as her program, "Overcome Pornography for Good," available on Amazon, and hosts a podcast of the same title that has reportedly passed two million downloads with more than 650 five-star reviews ([Amazon: Overcome Pornography for Good](https://www.amazon.com/Overcome-Pornography-for-Good/dp/B08K5BPNTB); [Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/overcome-pornography-for-good/id1549605485)). According to her site, her direct coaching has supported more than 1,800 individuals, separate from the much larger audience reached through the podcast and book.

## What members actually get

Inside the free Skool community, members get access to the same shame-focused framework Brewer developed in her paid coaching, applied at group scale, plus the ability to interact with other coaches in her program, including team members who went through the process themselves before becoming coaches. That peer-coach structure, people who overcame the same compulsive pattern now helping others through it, gives the community a lived-experience credibility that's harder to manufacture than credentials alone.

The free tier functions as an accessible front door into Brewer's broader ecosystem of paid coaching, the book, and the podcast, letting people test the shame-focused framework in a group setting before committing to more individualized paid support.

## Why it's grown

Pornography recovery is a category where most existing content leans on either religious moralizing or blunt willpower-and-accountability apps, so a framework built specifically around shame as the mechanism, backed by a coach who has spent years refining that specific angle across a book, a long-running podcast, and thousands of paying clients, stands out as more clinically grounded than the average recovery community. Brewer's faith-based framing also gives the community a clear audience fit within Latter-day Saint and broader religious communities where this topic carries particular stigma and where existing secular recovery resources may feel like a poor fit.

The multi-year output across the podcast and published book gives Brewer a large amount of pre-existing content to point new Skool members toward immediately, which likely accelerates trust building compared to a coach starting a community with no prior public track record.
