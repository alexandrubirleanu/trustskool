# Arthur Cyiza and ArtFx: Inside Skool's Videography and Cinematography Community

ArtFx Videography Community is a free Skool group with close to 8,840 members focused on teaching videography, cinematography, and filmmaking. It's run by Arthur Cyiza, a working filmmaker and content creator with an established presence well beyond Skool.

## Who is Arthur Cyiza

Arthur is listed as a brand ambassador on Sigma Corporation of America's own site, where he's described as a self-taught filmmaker and creative mentor who specializes in cinematic storytelling and high-impact videography, and who has partnered with brands including Sigma, DJI, Adobe, ASUS, and Amaran early in his career ([Sigma Corporation ambassador page](https://www.sigmaphoto.com/learn/ambassadors/arthur-cyiza)). Outside of camera-gear sponsorships, he has built a following of more than 280,000 on Instagram, where he posts tech, fitness, and lifestyle content alongside his filmmaking work ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/its.arthur7/)), and he runs a parallel TikTok account under a similar handle covering the same mix of creative and lifestyle content ([TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@iam_arthur7)).

That combination of brand partnerships and a large personal following is what he draws on inside ArtFx: rather than teaching from theory alone, the lessons and community discussion are built around gear and techniques he's actually used on paid and sponsored shoots. His profile on the creator network Intch lists him under the same filmmaker and influencer positioning ([Intch](https://intch.org/15135207)), reinforcing that the ArtFx community grew out of an already-established creator business rather than starting from a blank slate.

## What members actually get

The community is free and structured as an ongoing space for people learning videography and cinematography rather than a single fixed course. Posts inside cover cinematic technique, camera and lens choices, and general filmmaking craft, with Arthur periodically dropping in with updates framed as the start of members' cinematic journey. Given his sponsorship ties to Sigma and DJI, the equipment discussion inside the group tends to reference gear he has an actual working relationship with rather than generic buying-guide advice.

## Why it's grown

Arthur's advantage is that he isn't teaching filmmaking as a side hustle. He's an actively working, sponsored creator whose name shows up on a major camera manufacturer's own ambassador roster. For an audience of aspiring videographers, that's a stronger trust signal than most creator-education communities can offer, since it demonstrates the exact outcome many members want: getting recognized and paid by brands for camera work. Layering a free Skool community on top of an existing quarter-million-follower Instagram audience gives him a low-friction way to convert casual followers into a more engaged group he can teach directly, which likely explains the community's growth toward 9,000 members without a paid tier.
