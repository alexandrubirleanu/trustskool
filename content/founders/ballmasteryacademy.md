# Brandon Hima and Ball Mastery Academy: Inside Skool's Soccer Skills Community

Ball Mastery Academy is a $7-a-month Skool community with more than 7,480 members, built to help soccer players sharpen ball control without needing a full training setup, just space and a ball. It's run by Brandon Hima, a US Soccer Federation-licensed coach who has spent over a decade building skills programs for youth and competitive players.

## Who is Brandon Hima

Brandon played competitive soccer through high school in Watertown, Connecticut, where his own recruiting profile documents his path toward a Division I college scholarship ([NCSA recruiting profile](https://www.ncsasports.org/mens-soccer-recruiting/connecticut/watertown/watertown-high-school6/brandon-hima)). He has since coached with clubs including CT Rush Central and Cheshire United, and lists himself as a soccer coach available for private sessions through CoachUp, where his profile describes him as a national licensed coach with the US Soccer Federation ([CoachUp](https://www.coachup.com/coaches/brandonh-68)).

His training method, known as the Ball Mastery Bootcamp, grew out of a plan he built with his father, the same plan he credits with helping him earn his own college scholarship and later train with professional teams in Europe. According to his own program materials, that method now reaches thousands of players across dozens of countries through his online coaching content ([Gumroad](https://brandonhima.gumroad.com/l/elite)). He also maintains a Facebook page under his own name where he posts training clips and updates directly to parents and players following the program ([Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/BrandonHimaOfficial)).

## What members actually get

For $7 a month, Ball Mastery Academy gives players a structured, daily ball-control curriculum built around short sessions, roughly 20 minutes at a time, with clear progression from beginner touches to more advanced footwork. The pitch is specifically about consistency: parents and players who've tried other programs and quit within weeks are the target audience, and the low price point is designed to make daily practice an easy habit rather than a big financial commitment.

## Why it's grown

Youth and amateur soccer training is a crowded space online, but Brandon's angle works because it solves a narrow, well-defined problem. Most players know they need better first touch and closer control, and most existing content either assumes access to a full training facility or isn't broken into a program players will actually stick with. A $7 monthly price removes the friction of asking parents to commit to an expensive academy before they know if their kid will follow through, while his own playing history and coaching license give the program credibility beyond just being another skills-drills channel.
