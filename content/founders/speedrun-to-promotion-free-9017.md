# Steve Huynh and Speedrun To Promotion: An Ex-Amazon Principal Engineer's Career Coaching Community

Speedrun To Promotion is a free Skool community with roughly 4,480 members dedicated to helping tech workers get promoted as fast as possible. It's run by Steve Huynh, a former Amazon Principal Engineer who spent close to two decades inside the company before leaving to coach engineers on career growth full time.

## Who is Steve Huynh

Huynh entered tech with a liberal arts degree rather than a computer science background, starting as a support engineer at Amazon and eventually climbing to Principal Engineer, a level his own bio describes as roughly the top one percent of engineers at the company. He moved from Junior Engineer to Senior Engineer in about three and a half years, then spent another eight years reaching Principal, and along the way conducted close to a thousand hiring-bar interviews as an Amazon bar raiser ([alifeengineered.substack.com/about](https://alifeengineered.substack.com/about)). He writes and publishes career-growth content through his "A Life Engineered" newsletter and YouTube channel, and has discussed layoffs, interviewing, and career growth in interviews including an appearance on The Peterman Pod ([Peterman Pod episode](https://creators.spotify.com/pod/profile/peterman-pod/episodes/Amazon-Principal-Engineer-On-Layoffs--Interviewing--Career-Growth--Steve-Huynh-e349kmr)). He's also published under his own name on Amazon, including a book titled "A Life Engineered" ([Amazon author page](https://www.amazon.com/A-Life-Engineered/dp/B0FNPPVCZ4)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, Speedrun To Promotion is built as a launchpad specifically for getting to the next level in tech as quickly as possible, drawing on Huynh's own path through Amazon's engineering ladder plus his experience sitting on the other side of the promotion process as a bar raiser. The free community pairs with more intensive, smaller-group and one-on-one coaching options for members who want closer support.

## Why it's grown

Huynh's credibility here comes from having actually done the specific thing he's now coaching people through, reaching a senior technical level at one of the most demanding engineering cultures in the industry, while also having sat on the interview panels that decide who gets promoted. That dual perspective, both candidate and evaluator, is a differentiator that generic "career coach" content can't easily replicate. Leaving Amazon to spend more time with his family after having a second child gave him the bandwidth to build this out into a full coaching practice rather than a side project.
