# Razvan Paraschiv and Grow Channels Accelerator: Skool's YouTube Automation Powerhouse

Grow Channels Accelerator calls itself "the best & biggest YouTube Automation community on earth," and with roughly 4,300 free members it has the scale to back that up, at least within its niche. The community teaches YouTube automation: building and monetizing faceless video channels by outsourcing scripting, voiceover, and editing to freelancers rather than appearing on camera yourself.

## Who is Razvan Paraschiv

Razvan Paraschiv is credited directly on the community's Skool About page as its founder, and he's built a public profile around exactly this business model. He grew up in a small city in Romania in a low-income family, and his entrepreneurial track record started young: flipping phone cases at school at 15, then moving through Shopify dropshipping, an SMMA, affiliate marketing and Amazon FBA before landing on YouTube automation as his focus ([nyweekly feature](https://nyweekly.com/business/romanian-teenagers-youtube-empire-razvan-paraschivs-story/)).

He began publicly documenting his YouTube automation journey on his own channel in 2021, showing the process of delegating scriptwriting, editing, and voiceover work to freelancers rather than doing it himself. That channel has since grown past 50,000 subscribers, and Grow Channels, his flagship paid program, has been reported to have attracted more than 7,000 students across over 50 countries.

## What members actually get

The free Skool community is positioned as the top-of-funnel version of Paraschiv's teaching: free YouTube automation content, access to a large peer community, and exposure to the same faceless-channel framework he uses in his paid Grow Channels Accelerator program, which reportedly runs into the thousands of dollars for full access. Inside the free tier, the pitch is straightforward community access and content rather than the full paid curriculum, which is the standard structure for creators using Skool as an acquisition layer above a higher-ticket offer.

The core mechanic being taught throughout is delegation: identifying video ideas and niches, then hiring freelance scriptwriters, voiceover artists and editors to produce content at scale, with the channel owner acting more as a producer and manager than a creator in the traditional sense.

## Why it's grown

Grow Channels' growth traces cleanly back to Paraschiv's own transparency about his numbers and process starting in 2021. Rather than positioning himself as a guru teaching a system he invented from theory, he built an audience by publicly documenting his own channels as they scaled, which gave prospective students something concrete to evaluate before paying for the more expensive program. The free Skool community extends that same trust-building approach: it costs nothing to join, and it gives Paraschiv a large, low-friction pool of engaged YouTube automation enthusiasts he can nurture toward the paid Grow Channels Accelerator over time.

It's also arriving at a moment when YouTube automation as a category has drawn a lot of both genuine interest and skepticism, with independent reviewers publishing detailed breakdowns of programs like his to help buyers separate substance from hype ([program review](https://deliveredsocial.com/the-growchannels-review-is-their-youtube-automation-program-worth-the-hype/)). That level of outside scrutiny, combined with a free community that anyone can join and judge for themselves, is part of what has let Grow Channels scale past thousands of members while competitors in the same space struggle for credibility.
