# Jason Hamilton and Story Hacker STARTER: Inside Skool's 7,600-Member AI Fiction-Writing Community

Story Hacker STARTER is a 7,645-member Skool community that teaches aspiring novelists how to write books faster using AI tools, without — as the community puts it — "compromising your creativity or ethics." It's the entry-level tier of a larger Story Hacker ecosystem, priced at a $7 one-time fee rather than a subscription [Skool](https://www.skool.com/story-hacker-silver/about).

## Who runs it

The community is run by Jason Hamilton, who teaches and publishes under the name "The Nerdy Novelist." Hamilton holds a BA in English from Brigham Young University and a Master's degree in creative media from a UK university [CommunityHunter](https://communityhunter.com/reviews/story-hacker-ai-jason-hamilton/). Before building his own author-education business, he worked as Content Manager at Kindlepreneur.com under Dave Chesson, one of the most recognized names in self-publishing education — a background that gives him direct experience on the marketing and business side of authorship, not just the craft side. He is active on Skool under his own profile as well [Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@jason-hamilton-2564).

A notable detail: the Story Hacker curriculum includes more than a dozen licensed courses from the late David Farland, a bestselling fantasy author who personally mentored Brandon Sanderson early in his career, per the same review source. Licensing Farland's material gives the community a craft-teaching pedigree that goes beyond Hamilton's own content.

## What members actually get

STARTER is the free-to-low-cost entry point into the broader Story Hacker system (a "Silver" tier that has previously been referred to as Story Hacker Silver, sitting below paid "Gold" tiers). For the one-time $7 fee, members get an AI Foundations course, a 40-chapter plot module, a writing-prompts library, monthly Q&A calls, and access to a system for producing complete AI-assisted short stories within an hour. The pitch is squarely aimed at writers who want to use AI as a drafting and ideation accelerator while keeping human creative judgment in the driver's seat — a middle position between "AI will replace writers" and "AI has no place in fiction," which is where most working novelists actually land.

## Why it's grown

AI-assisted fiction writing is a genuinely contentious topic inside author communities, and Hamilton's positioning — formal English/creative-writing credentials, a real publishing-industry job history, and licensed material from a writer who mentored one of fantasy's biggest current names — gives him more credibility with skeptical writers than most "AI writing" communities can claim. The low, one-time entry price for STARTER functions as a funnel: it lets curious but cautious writers try the system cheaply before deciding whether to move into paid tiers, which likely explains why membership here has scaled well past what a typical niche-craft community reaches.

**A note on pricing:** the source data for this profile listed Story Hacker STARTER as free. The current Skool about page shows a $7 one-time fee rather than $0 — a small but real difference worth confirming directly on Skool if free access is the deciding factor for you.
