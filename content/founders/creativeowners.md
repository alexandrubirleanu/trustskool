# Samuel G and Creative Owners Club: Inside Skool's 8,000-Member Creative Real Estate Community

Creative Owners Club is a 7,993-member Skool community teaching "creative financing" real estate strategies — ways to buy property with no traditional bank loan, including deals structured for buyers with bad credit or little capital. It costs $25/month, which the group frames as "less than $1 a day" [Skool](https://www.skool.com/creativeowners/about).

## Who runs it

The community is run by Samuel G, who posts under the handle @ownwithsam. His Skool profile describes him as a "Professional Problem Solver" who became a millionaire at 24 and has personally completed more than 3,000 real estate deals using no-money-down and creative-financing techniques [Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@ownwithsam). He has 13.8k followers on his Skool profile alone and is active across Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook. Beyond these self-reported figures and his Skool activity, independent (non-Skool) biographical detail on Samuel G — his full name, location, or a documented deal history outside his own channels — is not readily available in public records, which is worth noting for readers who want third-party verification of the specific numbers.

## What members actually get

Creative Owners Club's stated mission is to help 10,000 everyday people buy a home with no money down. The curriculum centers on techniques like seller financing, subject-to deals, and lease options — structures that let a buyer take control of a property without qualifying for a conventional mortgage. Members get weekly live coaching calls, contract templates, negotiation scripts, and a 30-day action plan, and the group also teaches members how to get paid finder's fees for deals they source but don't personally buy, turning the skill into a source of income even without capital to invest directly.

## Why it's grown

Creative financing is a niche that spikes in interest whenever mortgage rates rise and traditional financing becomes harder to qualify for — exactly the environment of the past few years. Communities that can show real, specific deal structures (rather than generic "real estate investing" motivation content) tend to retain members better in this niche, because the tactics are genuinely different from what most people assume real estate investing requires. Samuel G's positioning — a personal track record measured in thousands of completed deals rather than a theoretical framework — is the kind of proof point that travels well on short-form video, which is likely how much of this community's growth has been driven given his active presence on Instagram and YouTube.

As with any creative-financing education product, prospective members should weigh the specific legal and financial risk of the strategies taught (seller financing and subject-to deals carry real contractual and lending risk depending on jurisdiction) against the track record claimed, and treat self-reported deal counts as marketing claims rather than audited figures until shown otherwise.
