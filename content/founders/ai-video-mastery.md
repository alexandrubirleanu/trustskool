# Mahmut Kasimoglu and AI Video Mastery: Inside Skool's 8,800-Member AI Video Community

AI Video Mastery is an 8,839-member Skool community charging $9/month to teach creators and small businesses how to produce AI-generated video content, from UGC-style ads to AI influencers and AI clones. It has grown into one of the larger AI-video hubs on the platform by promising a structured path through a fast-moving, hype-heavy niche.

## Who runs it

The community is built and run by Mahmut Kasimoglu, who posts online under the handle "mahmutthedev." Kasimoglu maintains an active YouTube channel under that name where he covers AI video tools, AI automation workflows, and hands-on tutorials, including recent coverage of tools like Higgsfield Canvas [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@mahmutthedev). He is also a working developer: his GitHub profile lists projects spanning web automation and productivity tooling [GitHub](https://github.com/mahmutthedev), and he writes about AI and automation topics on Medium and LinkedIn. That combination of hands-on coding background and daily AI-video experimentation is the throughline of his public content, and it's the same skillset the Skool community is built around, per the group's own about page [Skool](https://www.skool.com/ai-video-mastery/about).

Beyond his own channels, detailed independent biographical information on Kasimoglu (education, prior employers, company history) is not publicly documented — his public footprint is almost entirely built through his content itself rather than press coverage, which is typical for creators in the fast-growing "AI content creator" niche.

## What members actually get

According to the community's own description, AI Video Mastery is structured as a step-by-step roadmap that starts from zero and builds toward "real AI creative skills for content, client work, ads, and paid projects." The stated focus areas are UGC-style ads, AI influencers, AI clones, and viral content formats, with an emphasis on frameworks that produce realistic-looking output rather than the obviously synthetic look many early AI video tools produced. Members get curated frameworks and step-by-step guidance rather than a raw tool list, positioning the community as a shortcut through the trial-and-error most solo creators go through when they try to keep up with a tool landscape that changes weekly.

## Why it's grown

Two things explain the size of this community relative to its low price point. First, the underlying skill is monetizable in a very direct way: businesses are actively paying for AI-generated UGC ads and AI avatar content right now, so members can plausibly turn what they learn into freelance or agency income rather than treating it as a hobby course. Second, the $9/month price point (with a limited-time yearly discount) removes almost all friction to trying it, which matters enormously in a category where dozens of competing "AI video" Skool communities launched in the same 12–18 month window — AI Video Bootcamp, AI Film Academy, AI Filmmaker Lab, and others are all fighting for the same audience. Being cheap, active, and led by someone who is visibly still doing the work himself (rather than someone purely teaching from the sidelines) has been enough to pull AI Video Mastery ahead of much of that pack.

For anyone evaluating whether to join, the real signal isn't the sales page — it's whether the community is still growing and how members are actually rating it month over month.
