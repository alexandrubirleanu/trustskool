# Braiden Shaw and Amplifi Launchpad: Inside Skool's 5,900-Member Financial Independence Community

Amplifi Launchpad is a free Skool community of roughly 5,897 members aimed at people just starting their path toward financial independence, whether they're tackling debt or building their first budget. Its founder, Braiden Shaw, took an unusual road into personal finance: college athletics.

## Who is Braiden Shaw

Braiden Shaw is listed as a member of BYU's men's basketball roster for the 2017-2018 season on the university's official athletics site, documenting his time as a Division 1 college athlete ([BYU Cougars roster](https://byucougars.com/sports/mens-basketball/roster/player/braiden-shaw)). He's since built a career in finance and real estate, and his personal site describes him building a net worth exceeding $10 million before age 30, alongside co-founding Fulcrum Home Loans, which he describes scaling into one of the fastest-growing brokerages in its space ([braidenshaw.com](https://braidenshaw.com/grow-your-net-worth)). His LinkedIn profile lists him as CEO of Amplifi LLC and owner of Fulcrum Home Loans, with a focus on venture capital, private equity, and real estate syndication ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/braiden-shaw-47873a122/)). Amplifi Launchpad's Skool about page credits him directly as its creator ([Amplifi Launchpad on Skool](https://www.skool.com/amplifi-launchpad-5580/about)).

Shaw also runs Amplifi's Growth Circle, a private investment community, and by his own account has built a social media following that includes 1.4 million followers on Instagram.

## What members actually get

Amplifi Launchpad is free to join and positions itself as the entry point into Shaw's broader Amplifi ecosystem, offering personal finance basics like debt payoff strategies and budgeting frameworks, along with community support and accountability for people at the earliest stage of building financial stability. It's explicitly framed as a starting point rather than the advanced offering: members who progress further can move into Amplifi's more involved programs, like Foundations or the private Growth Circle, which focus on wealth building strategies and private investment deal access for a more established audience.

That tiered structure, free basics feeding into paid, more advanced programs, mirrors Shaw's own described path: starting from a standard income and building outward through real estate and lending businesses rather than an inherited head start.

## Why it's grown

At 5,897 members and free to join, Amplifi Launchpad benefits from Shaw's documented and specific financial track record, a real net worth figure, a named mortgage brokerage he co-founded, and college athletics credentials that are independently verifiable through BYU's own athletics site. Personal finance content is crowded with vague "financial freedom" messaging from people who've never built anything concrete; a founder who can point to a specific, named business (Fulcrum Home Loans) and specific numbers gives the free community a more tangible reason to trust the advice.

The former-athlete framing also broadens his appeal beyond a typical finance-bro audience, positioning financial independence as achievable through discipline and system-building rather than industry insider connections.
