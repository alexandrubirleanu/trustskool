# Mike Navarrete and Tradetopia Exchange: Inside Skool's 2,800-Member Free Trading Community

Tradetopia Exchange is a free Skool community built around live trading sessions Monday through Friday, with a stated goal of teaching members to build their own trading system rather than hand them someone else's setups to copy. It has grown to roughly 2,837 members on a no-cost model, which is unusual in a niche where most serious trading education sits behind a paywall.

## Who is Mike Navarrete

The community is run by Mike Navarrete, whose path into markets did not start on a trading desk. According to an interview published on Darwinex's own blog, Navarrete was raised by immigrant parents, went to business school on scholarship, and then found the corporate track unfulfilling enough that he spent two and a half years in the U.S. military before ever placing a trade ([Darwinex interview](https://www.darwinexzero.com/blog/trading-act-discipline-mike-navarrete)). He got into trading after a friend showed him MetaTrader 4 profits, opened a live account soon after, and by his own account spent roughly a year losing money before he found consistency.

That consistency eventually produced a verifiable result: his DARWIN, ticker SLCF, took first place in the January 2024 edition of DarwinIA SILVER, one of Darwinex's investor-facing performance competitions, and pulled in six consecutive seed capital allocations between October 2023 and March 2024 totaling roughly €615,000 in cumulative funding ([Darwinex interview](https://www.darwinexzero.com/blog/trading-act-discipline-mike-navarrete)). He posts regularly to a YouTube channel under his own name ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@officialmikenavarrete)) and to Instagram as @mikenavarrete ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/mikenavarrete/)), where he trades under the nickname "El Mago."

## What members actually get

Per the community's own framing, Tradetopia Exchange centers on watching Navarrete trade in real time rather than studying pre-recorded lessons in isolation. The pitch explicitly positions itself against communities that hand out a single strategy for everyone to copy: members are meant to leave with the process for building a system suited to their own risk tolerance and schedule, across the currency, commodity, and index markets Navarrete trades himself. Free live giveaways and weekly sessions are part of the standard offering, alongside a companion newsletter distributed through Beehiiv for members who want written breakdowns between sessions.

## Why it's grown

Two things make this community's growth story more concrete than most trading groups on Skool. First, Navarrete's headline claim, finishing first in a DarwinIA competition, is not just a self-reported number: DarwinIA rankings are published by Darwinex itself, which makes the claim independently checkable rather than a screenshot of a personal P&L statement. Second, his backstory, military service followed by a documented year of losses before profitability, is the kind of detail that tends to resonate with retail traders who are skeptical of "I made it look easy" narratives; it sets up the free community as proof of a process rather than a highlight reel.

The free price point also removes the biggest point of friction in trading education, where skepticism about paid signal groups and prop-firm funnels runs high. By running the paid parts of his business (mentorship, funded-account coaching) alongside a genuinely free live-trading room, Navarrete gets a large top-of-funnel audience while letting the daily sessions themselves serve as the proof of competence that trading educators usually have to claim rather than demonstrate.
