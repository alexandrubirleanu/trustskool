# Aly Yu and Aly's 3D Printing Masterclass: The 80K-Follower Creator Teaching a 3D Printing Business

Aly's 3D Printing Masterclass is a Skool community of roughly 2,900 members priced as a paid subscription, built around a knowledge and innovation hub for starting, growing, and scaling a 3D printing business. The community is currently paused, listed as "on break until October 8th" on its own about page, a detail that's unusual to see stated so plainly but consistent with a founder who is still actively running the underlying business herself. It's led by Aly Yu, named directly as the founder on the community's Skool about page.

## Who is Aly Yu

Aly Yu runs her 3D printing business publicly under the handle @aly3dprints on Instagram, where she has built an audience of roughly 80,000 followers documenting her work ([Instagram @aly3dprints](https://www.instagram.com/aly3dprints/)). According to her own account on the Skool community page, she started 3D printing with a single Bambu Lab A1 mini printer and scaled the operation to 45 printers funded entirely through reinvested profits, now serving customers in more than 60 countries.

Her content leans into her background outside of engineering, in one Instagram reel caption she describes coming to 3D printing from an art history background rather than a technical one, which she frames as an asset in designing products that actually sell rather than just print well ([Instagram reel](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DGbYXNbyIKa/)). She has also run giveaways in partnership with Bambu Lab, the 3D printer manufacturer whose entry-level A1 mini she credits as her starting point ([TikTok giveaway post](https://www.tiktok.com/@scottyujan/video/7447994622988537119)).

## What members actually get

Inside the paid community, the core offering is a structured curriculum Yu calls "The 3D Blueprint," covering product development, customer acquisition, and marketing systems specifically for a 3D printing business rather than generic maker content. Given her description of scaling from one printer to 45 through reinvested profit, the community's implicit pitch is that the same playbook is replicable for members starting from a single printer of their own.

Yu has also talked publicly about building AI agents to help automate parts of the 3D printing workflow, suggesting the community's content is evolving alongside her own business rather than staying static once it launched.

## Why it's grown

3D printing sits at an interesting intersection right now, consumer printers have gotten cheap and capable enough that starting a small print-on-demand business is genuinely accessible, but most existing content in the space is about the hardware and slicer settings rather than the business of selling what you print. Yu's angle, a non-technical founder scaling a real operation to dozens of machines and international customers, gives her content a business-first framing that's rarer in a niche otherwise dominated by hobbyist tutorials.

Her existing Instagram following also gives the Skool community a built-in audience that's already seen proof of the model working before ever being asked to pay for the curriculum behind it.
