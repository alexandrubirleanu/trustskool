# Isaiah Rivera, John Evans, and THP Jump Training: Skool's 79,000-Member Vertical Jump Lab

Nearly 80,000 people have joined THP Jump Training for one specific reason: they want to jump higher, and they've decided to learn from someone who has already done it at an elite level. The free community pairs a vertical jump training course with structured programming, built around the training system of professional dunker Isaiah Rivera and his longtime coach John Evans.

## Who is Isaiah Rivera

Rivera is widely known in the dunk and vertical jump training world for his own numbers: a documented 50.5-inch vertical jump, among the highest publicly recorded by any athlete training the skill directly rather than as a byproduct of another sport ([Grokipedia profile](https://grokipedia.com/page/Isaiah_Rivera)). He stands 6'2" and has built his reputation through international dunk competitions and a large body of training content built around his own progress, filmed and coached primarily by John Evans.

His path into serious strength training has personal roots. Rivera's early family life involved helping care for younger siblings affected by spinal muscular atrophy, which by his own account shaped the discipline and work ethic he later brought to athletic training. That combination of a hard personal story and a genuinely elite, verifiable physical result (jump height is about as objectively measurable as athletic claims get) is a large part of why his coaching carries credibility that's hard to fake ([VerticalJumpWorld review](https://verticaljumpworld.com/isaiah-rivera-jump-program-review/); [A1Athlete review](https://a1athlete.com/thp-strength-review/)).

## What members actually get

The free Skool community includes a week-long training program with specific sets, reps, and video demonstrations, an isometric training block aimed at patellar tendon health (a common injury risk in jump-heavy training), and a broader jump training course built around the same principles Rivera used to take his own vertical from the low twenties to over 50 inches. Coaching content is typically delivered with Evans explaining the mechanics behind each movement, not just Rivera performing it.

For athletes who want more than the free tier, THP Strength sits alongside the Skool community as a paid coaching product, priced in the $79 to $139 per month range according to third-party reviews, offering more direct, customized programming and access to the coaches themselves ([THP Strength official site](https://www.thpstrength.com/)).

## Why it's grown

THP's growth is a straightforward case of proof preceding promotion. Rivera didn't build an audience by claiming expertise, he built it by publishing his own jump numbers alongside his training process, in a niche where results are unusually easy to verify on video. A vertical jump either happened on camera or it didn't, and Rivera and Evans have years of that documentation, including a podcast episode specifically built around chasing a 51-inch vertical ([THP150, Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/episode/1TI1RSCSqgR9dSkEsYp1zM)).

That verifiability lowers the trust barrier that most fitness communities have to overcome. Combined with a free entry point into the Skool community itself, it means new members can watch the exact methodology that produced Rivera's own results before ever paying for anything, which is a stronger sales mechanism than testimonials in a niche this specific.
