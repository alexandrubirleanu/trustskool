# David Pierce Hallahan and the Relationship Creator Community: An Executive Recruiter's Pivot on Skool

The Relationship Creator Community is a free Skool space with roughly 2,500 members, positioned as a place for successful women to master what founder David Pierce Hallahan calls "the art of attracting your committed masculine man." What makes this community unusual among relationship-coaching Skool groups is the founder's prior career: two decades in executive search, not dating coaching, before he ever built this brand.

## Who is David Pierce Hallahan

Hallahan is Irish, and spent more than 20 years in executive recruitment, placing C-suite executives globally, according to his own account of his career ([RelationshipCreator.com, our story](https://www.relationshipcreator.com/story)). He founded Team Capital and serves as a Director at The Alexander Group, a firm Forbes has ranked among America's top executive search firms. That professional background sits behind his self-positioning as someone who understands "the psychology of leadership, change and negotiation," having mentored, in his own telling, hundreds of business leaders and high achievers over his recruiting career.

His pivot into relationship coaching traces to 2013, when a divorce and a string of failed relationships with other career-focused women pushed him to try to understand what was actually happening beneath the surface of those relationship patterns, per his site's account. That period of personal upheaval led him into extensive reading, coaching, and mastermind groups, and eventually into building RelationshipCreator.com around the resulting framework. His LinkedIn and X accounts, both active under his own name, back up the professional history described on his site.

## What members actually get

The free Skool community is built specifically for women, with the stated goal of helping them attract and keep what Hallahan calls a "committed masculine man." His central claim to authority is a simple one repeated across his materials: as a man himself, he argues he can decode male behavior and psychology in a way female-only dating coaches can't. Reviews of RelationshipCreator.com on Trustpilot suggest the broader coaching business has an established customer base beyond just the free Skool tier.

The community likely functions as the top-of-funnel layer for his paid coaching programs, giving prospective clients a taste of his framework before any paid engagement.

## Why it's grown

Hallahan's pitch stands out in a crowded relationship-coaching market because his authority claim rests on a specific, verifiable professional background, two decades assessing and placing senior executives, rather than a generic "dating coach" credential. That recruiting-industry pedigree, combined with a Forbes-recognized firm on his resume, gives his relationship content a different flavor than typical influencer-style dating advice, leaning more on structured psychological framing than pickup tactics.

His openness about his own 2013 divorce as the catalyst for the whole business also gives the brand a credible origin story, positioning him as someone who lived through the problem before turning it into a teaching framework.
