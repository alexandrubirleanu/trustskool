# JD Denham, Will Haas and Peptide of The Week Community: Inside Skool's 13,000-Member Peptide Podcast Hub

Peptide of The Week Community is a free Skool community of roughly 13,200 members that functions as the official companion hub for the Peptide of The Week podcast. It's run by the podcast's two co-hosts, JD Denham and Will Haas, in collaboration with the Peptide Research Institute, and it sits at the intersection of two fast-growing niches: peptide-based health optimization and podcast-driven community building.

## Who are JD Denham and Will Haas

The Peptide of The Week podcast is described as "your no-BS guide to peptides, performance, and total body optimization for athletes, high performers, or those wanting to feel better, move better, and live stronger" ([podcast listing on Listen Notes](https://www.listennotes.com/podcasts/peptide-of-the-week-jd-denham-and-will-haas-5HWu9X6xFKb/)), with Denham and Haas covering real-world protocols, hard-earned personal lessons, and the underlying science in conversation with expert guests.

JD Denham's public profile extends beyond the podcast: he's an entrepreneur and owner at Warrior Makers, based in Costa Mesa, California, where his stated focus is helping men get back in shape so they can spend more time with their kids, and he has spoken publicly about his own journey with sobriety as part of his broader message on discipline and self-improvement ([Jeremy Fouts podcast, Episode 35](https://jeremyfouts.com/episode-35-men-standing-up-with-jd-denham/)). Detailed independent biographical information on Will Haas specifically is more limited in public sources beyond his co-hosting role, which is worth noting rather than filling in with speculation.

## What members actually get

The community is built to extend the podcast's content into a more structured, ongoing format: compound breakdowns and research summaries on specific peptides, protocol and dosing frameworks, and updates on new developments in peptide science as the field moves quickly. The free tier covers general access, while paid Premium and VIP tiers unlock exclusive content and, at the VIP level, direct access to Denham and Haas for personalized guidance on individual protocols and live Q&A sessions — a structure that mirrors how a lot of podcast-first communities monetize: free content stays free, and the paid tier sells direct access to the hosts rather than gated information.

## Why it's grown

Peptide of The Week benefits from two converging trends. First, peptide-based protocols for recovery, performance, and anti-aging have moved from a niche biohacker interest into much broader mainstream curiosity over the past few years, expanding the pool of people searching for reliable, non-scammy information. Second, podcast-to-community conversion is a well-proven growth model: listeners who already trust a show's hosts convert into community members at a much higher rate than cold audience acquisition, because the trust-building work has already happened in the podcast feed before anyone ever visits the Skool page.

Because peptides remain a category with real regulatory and safety complexity, and the hosts' personal biographical detail beyond the podcast and Denham's Warrior Makers business is limited in independent sources, prospective members should weigh the community's protocol and dosing content the way they would any non-medical wellness source: useful for orientation, not a substitute for guidance from a qualified clinician.
