# Francisca Duarte and Comunidad1350: Inside Skool's 2,700-Member Weight Loss Community

Comunidad1350 opens with a direct pitch to women who've tried and failed at losing weight through conventional dieting: it offers, in the founder's own words, "the most powerful tool," knowledge rather than another restrictive plan. The free community has reached roughly 2,776 members.

## Who is behind it

The community is built by Francisca Duarte, a Chilean content creator known publicly as SweetFran, who posts fitness-oriented recipes across TikTok under @sweetfran0 and Instagram under @sweetfran ([TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@sweetfran0); [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/sweetfran/)). Her recipe content also runs on a dedicated YouTube channel and has been featured by Paris.cl, a major Chilean retail and media platform, which republished a collection of her recipes under her own name, a placement that sits outside her own self-published channels ([Paris.cl](https://www.paris.cl/las-recetas-de-sweetfran-MKHXHPFDWA.html)). Comunidad1350 is built around what she calls "Método 1350," her framework for sustainable weight loss built on food knowledge rather than calorie deprivation.

## What members actually get

Per the community's own framing, members join a space aimed specifically at women who have tried multiple diets without the results they wanted, and are offered Duarte's approach to weight loss centered on understanding food and nutrition rather than following a rigid restrictive plan. That matches the broader content she publishes across TikTok and Instagram, where her videos consistently focus on nutritional information about specific foods and recipes, calorie counts of everyday dishes, ingredient swaps, portioning, rather than generic workout content. Her recipe archive also lives on a dedicated WordPress blog and has circulated across Pinterest boards curated by other users, extending her reach beyond the platforms she posts to directly.

## Why it's grown

Weight loss content aimed at women in Latin America is an enormous and crowded category, and Duarte's angle, positioning her method against the fatigue of having tried "everything" already, speaks directly to an audience that has likely already burned out on restrictive diet culture. Being featured by a mainstream retailer like Paris.cl gives her a layer of visibility and legitimacy beyond her own social channels, which matters in a niche saturated with anonymous or unverifiable "transformation coaches." A free Skool community serves as a natural extension of an already-large short-form content following, converting TikTok and Instagram viewers into a more structured space where Duarte can teach Método 1350 in more depth than a single video allows, likely as a step toward any paid programs she offers beyond the free tier.
