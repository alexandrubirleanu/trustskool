# Alicia Lyttle and Ai Fueled Profits: Live, Skool's AI Conference Community

Ai Fueled Profits: Live is the official Skool community attached to a recurring conference of the same name, and it has grown to roughly 4,250 members. Rather than being a standalone course, it functions as the home base for attendees and followers of the live event, hosted by Alicia Lyttle.

## Who is Alicia Lyttle

Alicia Lyttle has more than two decades of experience in digital marketing, and her path into the AI space is well documented across her own site and independent press. Before becoming known as an AI consultant, she worked inside government, including stints at the White House, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and the City of New Orleans Mayor's Office, before moving into entrepreneurship and digital marketing ([alicialyttle.com](https://alicialyttle.com/)).

Her turn toward AI specifically has a clear origin point: when ChatGPT launched in late 2022, she canceled her appointments and took a two-week sabbatical to learn the tool inside and out, a decision a Bossip feature describes as curiosity rather than burnout ([Bossip profile](https://bossip.com/3915318/alicia-lyttle-queen-of-ai/)). That deep dive turned into a second act as a Certified AI Consultant and TEDx speaker now nicknamed "The Queen of AI," and she went on to co-found Monetized Marketing, a training company focused on teaching businesses to use AI, along with AI InnoVision and the International Association of Artificial Intelligence Consultants ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alicialyttle/)). Her accolades include a Bronze Stevie Award for Mentor or Coach of the Year in Business, along with INC 5000 recognition and multiple ClickFunnels Two Comma Club awards.

## What members actually get

The Skool community itself is structured as the connective tissue around the live conference: a place for attendees to stay in touch between events, get updates on upcoming sessions, and access whatever supporting material Lyttle and her team release around the "AI Fueled Profits" brand, which also exists as a standalone paid course teaching people with no coding background how to identify profitable AI niches and automate their marketing and content workflows.

Because the community is tied to a live event rather than a single evergreen curriculum, its content skews toward announcements, community connection among conference-goers, and the kind of ongoing engagement that keeps an audience warm between in-person or livestreamed sessions.

## Why it's grown

The growth here is best explained by timing plus an already-built following. Lyttle wasn't a AI newcomer trying to manufacture authority from scratch. She arrived in the ChatGPT-era AI boom with two decades of marketing credibility, a TEDx talk, and a government résumé that lent her an unusual amount of institutional credibility compared to many self-taught AI educators. That combination made her a natural draw for a conference-style community, where the value proposition isn't just curriculum but access to a recognized name and her network of AI practitioners.

The free Skool tier also lowers the barrier for anyone curious about the conference to get a taste of the community before committing to a ticket or the paid course, which is a standard and effective structure for event-based communities on the platform.
