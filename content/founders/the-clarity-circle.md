# Armina Sîrbu and The Clarity Circle: Inside Skool's 23,000-Member Career Skills Community

The Clarity Circle is a free Skool community with more than 23,000 members, built around a specific idea: as AI reshapes jobs, the skills worth building aren't technical ones that AI can replicate, but "meta-skills" — things like adaptability, judgment, and learning agility that hold up regardless of which tools or roles change around them. It's run by Armina Sîrbu, a career development coach with two decades in talent development.

## Who is Armina Sîrbu

Armina Sîrbu is a career development coach and international trainer with more than 20 years of experience in talent development ([The Clarity Circle's own about page](https://www.skool.com/the-clarity-circle-6460/about)). She's been profiled discussing her broader philosophy on learning and self-development, including an interview with The CEO Library on how reading functions as a kind of "virus" that builds courage and problem-solving capacity ([The CEO Library interview](https://theceolibrary.com/armina-sirbu-10183.html)). She also maintains a personal training site and has published training presentations on SlideShare, consistent with a long-running career as an independent corporate trainer rather than someone who built an audience primarily through social media first ([SlideShare presentations](https://www.slideshare.net/ArminaSrbu)).

That background, career coach and corporate trainer first, community builder second, is a different founder profile than most of the largest communities on this list, which tend to be led by people who built an audience through content (YouTube, TikTok, podcasts) before launching a Skool community. Sîrbu's path looks more like someone porting an existing corporate training practice into a scalable, direct-to-individual format.

## What members actually get

The core program is a "15-Day Meta-Skills Challenge" built around what the community calls the 15 core meta-skills of the future — the specific list isn't fully public outside the community, but the framing consistently centers on capabilities like learning agility, adaptability, and judgment rather than any single technical skill. Members get live and recorded workshops, practical tools and templates, and monthly sessions led by guest experts, plus peer discussion for career-related problem solving. The community offers a 7-day free trial period as an onboarding mechanic, though the base community itself is free to join.

## Why it's grown

The Clarity Circle's growth timing lines up closely with a broader anxiety cycle around AI and job displacement that intensified through 2025 and 2026 — as more roles get touched by AI tools, "future-proof your career" messaging resonates with a much wider audience than it would have a few years earlier, and meta-skills framing (as opposed to "learn to code" or "learn to prompt") appeals specifically to people who don't want to bet their career on any single technical skill staying relevant.

Because the community is free, its growth ceiling is set less by price friction and more by how well the content actually delivers on a fairly abstract promise ("meta-skills") that's harder to demonstrate concretely than a course teaching a specific, checkable skill. That the community has scaled past 23,000 members on that more abstract pitch suggests Sîrbu's two decades of corporate training experience are translating into content people find genuinely useful, not just an appealing tagline.
