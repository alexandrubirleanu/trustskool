# Yassine Sdiri and Communauté IA: France's Free AI Classroom of 69,000 Members

Communauté IA is one of the largest French-language AI communities on Skool, with nearly 70,000 members and a description that names its creator directly: "Apprenez à Maîtriser et Exploiter l'IA, créé par Yassine Sdiri." The pitch is simple, learn to master and put AI to work, without hiding who is teaching or why.

## Who is Yassine Sdiri

Sdiri identifies himself professionally as an entrepreneur and AI creator, with a LinkedIn profile listing him under Chatflow AI, an AI-focused venture separate from the Skool community itself ([LinkedIn](https://fr.linkedin.com/in/yassine-sdiri-2793711b2)). He runs a YouTube channel under his own name, publishing tutorials and demonstrations on subjects ranging from building and selling AI agents to using tools like Claude for automation, which functions as the main top-of-funnel content feeding the free community ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@yassine-sdiri)).

A roundup of French AI influencers published by the training platform Jedha specifically names both Sdiri and ExplorIA's Henri Guichané among the creators worth following in the francophone AI space, describing Sdiri as one of the more widely followed voices for tutorials and AI demonstrations in that scene ([Jedha, "Les 10 influenceurs IA à suivre"](https://www.jedha.co/formation-ia/les-10-influenceurs-ia-a-suivre-en-2025)).

## What members actually get

Inside the community, members get access to the resources, templates, and guides referenced across Sdiri's YouTube content, effectively turning scattered video tutorials into an organized library. The stated focus is squarely practical: helping people move from consuming AI content passively to applying it, whether that means automating parts of a business, building simple AI agents, or just understanding tools well enough to stop feeling behind.

Because the community is free, there's no tiered content strategy holding back the fundamentals, the value proposition is closer to a well-organized extension of an already-active YouTube channel than a separate paid product layered on top of it.

## Why it's grown

Communauté IA's size traces directly back to Sdiri's decision, by his own account, to commit fully to AI content starting in early 2023, ahead of the wave of interest that followed. Being early with consistent output in a specific language market gave him a head start that's hard to replicate once a niche gets crowded, and French-language AI education has grown into a genuinely competitive space since then.

The community's growth also benefits from something simpler: naming the founder directly in the group description removes any ambiguity about who members are learning from, which matters in a market where a lot of AI-branded Skool groups are anonymous or team-run. For a niche built on trust in a fast-moving, hype-prone subject, that clarity is worth more than it might seem.
