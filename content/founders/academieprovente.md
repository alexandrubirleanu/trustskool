# Olivier Lafleur and Académie Provente: Inside Skool's 9,600-Member Sales Community

Académie Provente calls itself Quebec's number one group for learning premium sales, and with roughly 9,600 members on Skool, it has built a sizeable following among French-speaking entrepreneurs and salespeople. Behind the brand is Groupe Provente Inc., a registered Quebec company, with Olivier Lafleur serving as its public face and CEO.

## Who is Olivier Lafleur

Olivier Lafleur is co-founder of Groupe Provente and CEO of Académie Provente, according to the company's own site, which credits him with over six years of experience in premium sales and more than 3 million Canadian dollars in closed premium service contracts ([academieprovente.com](https://academieprovente.com/)). The same source describes him consulting for more than a hundred companies on their sales processes and training over 200 salespeople directly, positioning him as what the company calls "the vision" behind Groupe Provente's direction. He maintains a public Facebook presence under his own name, where he posts about the business and its Montreal-area roots ([Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/olivierlafleurGC/)). Groupe Provente is headquartered in Laval, Quebec, and lists a second co-founder, known publicly as Santiago, as Chief Coach, credited with a similar multi-year track record in premium sales training.

## What members actually get

Académie Provente's pitch centers on ethical, structured selling, teaching what the group frames as discipline and clarity in the sales process rather than aggressive, high-pressure tactics. The stated goal across the group's materials is helping members generate $5,000 or more per month online through premium sales, with training delivered both online and in person. The Skool community itself functions as the entry point into that broader curriculum, with posts from Lafleur and the team touching on everything from sales fundamentals to time management for small business owners trying to juggle client work and a growing team.

## Why it's grown

Groupe Provente's growth in Quebec's French-language business education market follows a pattern that's common among regional Skool communities: build credibility in a specific geography and language first, then use verifiable, denominated results, in this case a stated $3 million CAD in contracts closed personally by the founder, as social proof that travels faster than generic testimonials. Quebec's sales-training market is smaller and more tightly networked than the broader English-language SMMA and coaching space on Skool, which means word of mouth and personal reputation carry more weight relative to paid advertising.

The dual-founder structure, a CEO focused on vision and business development alongside a Chief Coach focused on delivery, also lets Académie Provente scale training without either founder becoming a bottleneck, a structural choice that shows up in how consistently the community publishes new content under both names.
