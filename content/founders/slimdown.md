# Nagina Abdullah and Slim Down Midlife Women: The Berkeley Biologist Behind Masala Body

Slim Down Midlife Women is a free Skool community with nearly 2,900 members, pitched around helping midlife women increase metabolism, burn fat, and lose weight through lifestyle changes rather than extreme dieting. Its founder is Nagina Abdullah, credited directly on the community's Skool about page and better known publicly as the woman behind the health coaching brand Masala Body.

## Who is Nagina Abdullah

Abdullah holds a degree in Molecular and Cell Biology from UC Berkeley and spent years working as a management consultant at a top-four consulting firm in New York City, a career that involved 12-hour workdays and frequent overnight travel ([Masala Body, About](https://masalabody.com/about/)). That same site describes her own weight struggle going back to childhood and years of yo-yo dieting before she lost 40 pounds and kept it off for over a decade without calorie counting or extreme exercise, using her biology background to research global nutrition and develop her own anti-inflammatory, spice-based recipes.

She has continued to update her own story publicly, describing on her podcast appearances how she faced a second round of metabolic changes around age 45 as belly fat appeared despite following the habits that had worked for years, and how she used the same research-driven approach to get into what she describes as the best shape of her life by 48 ([Ep. 76, Your Biggest Vision](https://urban20something.com/biggest-vision-ep-76-nagina-abdullah-founder-masala-body/)). She has been featured across a wide range of outlets and podcasts, including Fox News, Business Insider, Mind Body Green, and a case study published by Interview Connections describing a podcast-guesting campaign that generated over $24,000 in sales for her coaching business ([Interview Connections case study](https://interviewconnections.com/case-studies/nagina-abdullah/)).

## What members actually get

Inside the free Skool community, members get Abdullah's core metabolism-focused framework applied at scale, built around the same principles she used personally: research-backed food choices, anti-inflammatory spices, and habit changes rather than tracking calories or punishing exercise routines. The free group functions as a top-of-funnel introduction to her broader Masala Body coaching business, where she works more directly with women in their 40s through 70s dealing with midlife metabolic changes.

Her own two-stage weight loss story, the first 40 pounds in her 30s, then a second metabolic shift she had to solve again at 45, gives the community a built-in answer to one of the most common objections in this niche, that what worked in your 30s stops working in midlife, since she experienced and solved that exact problem herself.

## Why it's grown

Midlife weight loss is one of the most searched, most skeptical audiences in the health space, women in this demographic have typically tried multiple approaches that worked temporarily before stalling, so a founder with an actual science background and a documented, decade-plus personal track record has a credibility advantage over generic fitness influencers. Abdullah's consistent podcast guesting strategy, appearing across dozens of shows over several years, has also functioned as a long-running, low-cost customer acquisition channel that continues feeding new members into both the free community and her paid coaching programs.

That combination, real science credentials, a relatable and specific personal story, and years of consistent media appearances, gives the community a foundation that's difficult for newer entrants in the niche to replicate quickly.
