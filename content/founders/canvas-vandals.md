# Tom Wells and The Canvas Vandals: Turning Spray Paint Art Into a Skool Business

The Canvas Vandals is a $49-a-month Skool community with close to 4,850 members, teaching people to make $500 to $1,000 a month from spray paint art within eight weeks. It's run by Tom Wells, an artist who built his following by posting spray paint tutorials and speed-painting content online before turning that audience into a paid coaching community.

## Who is Tom Wells

Wells posts as tomwellywells across TikTok and YouTube, where he shares short spray paint art tutorials, including pieces built around pop culture references like Indiana Jones, and describes himself in his own captions as a full-time artist ([TikTok, @tomwellywells](https://www.tiktok.com/@tomwellywells/video/7456584695040576800); [YouTube Shorts tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/bXkJvHYQzPs)). On his own coaching page, Wells describes having spent years developing his techniques and processes for spray paint art, and says he's already taken complete beginners with no creative background through his teaching to the point where some are now selling their own work ([tomwellywells.com/art-coaching](https://tomwellywells.com/en-us/pages/art-coaching)).

## What members actually get

According to the Skool community's own description, The Canvas Vandals bills itself as the most comprehensive spray paint art learning platform and community online, with courses covering the full painting process from technique fundamentals through business and marketing skills for selling finished pieces. Members also get monthly free stencil designs, live tutorials, and Q&A access, positioning it for anyone between total beginner and someone trying to turn the hobby into a side income.

## Why it's grown

Spray paint art is a visually dramatic, short-video-friendly skill, which makes it well suited to the TikTok and YouTube Shorts formats Wells built his audience on in the first place. Rather than just selling finished paintings, he packaged the actual how-to knowledge, plus the business side of pricing and selling that knowledge is often the harder part for creative hobbyists, into a monthly membership. Bundling technique instruction with the commercial side of the craft is what separates The Canvas Vandals from a typical art tutorial channel, and it's likely a big part of why members are willing to pay monthly rather than just watching free clips.
