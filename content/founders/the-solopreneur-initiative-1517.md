# Brandon Rhee, Chantelle Morrison, and The Solopreneur Initiative: Two Founders, 3,100 Members

The Solopreneur Initiative pitches itself as the place where solopreneurs stop building alone, with roughly 3,155 members focused on getting real support and real progress rather than isolated hustle. It's co-founded by Brandon Rhee and Chantelle Morrison.

## Who runs it

Rhee brings more than 20 years of B2B sales, lead generation, digital marketing, and copywriting experience into the community, a background documented on his personal site, brandonrhee.com, which describes four consecutive years as top sales representative at Arran Access, along with consulting work that doubled one client's annual revenue and cut another's cost-per-click in half ([brandonrhee.com](https://brandonrhee.com/home)). He's also listed on Himalayas as a business coach tied specifically to The Solopreneur Initiative.

Morrison is a certified life coach and NLP practitioner running her own coaching practice at chantellemorrison.com, working across life, career, business, and recovery coaching for entrepreneurial women ([chantellemorrison.com](https://chantellemorrison.com/)). Outside of business coaching, she also founded Silent Rights in 2020, a platform built around sharing her personal story of resilience after domestic abuse, a project profiled directly by Rise Against Domestic Violence South Africa ([Rise Against Domestic Violence SA feature](https://www.riseagainstdomesticviolence.co.za/chantelle-morrison)).

## What members actually get

The community teaches three core competencies aimed at getting a solopreneur their first paying client: offer creation, audience building, and sales conversion. Rhee handles much of the marketing engine and daily content, authoring direct-response posts built on classic copywriting frameworks, while Morrison brings the coaching and audience-building side, drawing on her existing following built through her own brand.

According to Rhee's own account, the pair grew the community from zero to more than 3,100 members in 42 weeks using purely organic Facebook content, rather than paid acquisition, a specific, checkable growth claim rather than a vague success story.

## Why it's grown

Pairing a sales-and-marketing specialist with two decades of B2B experience alongside a certified coach with an existing following and a separate, independently profiled personal brand gives The Solopreneur Initiative two distinct audiences to draw from rather than one. Both founders maintain personal, professionally documented identities outside the community itself, which matters for a solopreneur audience that's often wary of coaches whose only credential is the community they're currently selling.

A reported 42-week run to over 3,100 members through organic content alone also suggests a genuinely engaged, share-worthy community rather than one propped up by paid ads, which tends to produce more durable, longer-term member retention.

Solopreneurship is also a category defined almost entirely by isolation, people building alone specifically because there's no team to lean on, which makes peer community arguably more central to the value proposition here than in communities built around a single technical skill. Having two founders rather than one also lets the community credibly cover both the front-end marketing work and the back-end coaching and mindset side, rather than one person stretching across both.
