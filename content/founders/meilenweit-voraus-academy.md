# Natalie and Lukas Frankenberger and Meilenweit Voraus: Germany's Points and Miles Community on Skool

Meilenweit Voraus Basic is a free Skool community with close to 4,490 members, describing itself as the largest German-language miles and travel-hacking community, where members trade tips on points programs, credit cards, and news for collecting airline miles and hotel points. It's run by Natalie and Lukas Frankenberger, a couple who built the project out of their own backgrounds in tourism and banking.

## Who are Natalie and Lukas Frankenberger

According to Meilenweit Voraus's own about page, Natalie brings more than 15 years of experience from the travel industry, including nine years working across travel agencies such as Frankfurt Airport, LCC, ADAC, and L'Tur, before retraining in finance at the Frankfurt School of Finance and moving into banking. Together, she and Lukas describe founding Meilenweit Voraus in 2023 because the demand they kept encountering wasn't generic budget travel advice, it was specifically how to fly business class or stay in luxury resorts for a fraction of the normal cost, using points and miles strategy ([meilenweitvoraus.com/about-us](https://www.meilenweitvoraus.com/about-us)). The couple also runs a YouTube channel under the Meilenweit Voraus name and is listed as the founding partnership on the O&M Club site, a separate collaboration page profiling the pair ([O&M Club profile](https://omclub.world/pages/natalie-frankenberger-lukas-frankenberger); [Meilenweit Voraus on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@MeilenweitVoraus)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, Meilenweit Voraus Basic functions as the free entry tier into a broader ecosystem that includes paid Premium and VIP levels. Inside, members get access to a network of experienced travel hackers, ongoing news on points and miles opportunities, and workshops covering how to build a mileage strategy around everyday spending rather than only around travel-specific purchases.

## Why it's grown

Travel hacking is a naturally tiered niche: casual travelers want basic tips, while serious point collectors want ongoing, timely intelligence on shifting card offers and airline partnerships, which rewards a community model over a one-off course. By combining Natalie's tourism-industry background with Lukas's banking and finance angle, the pair cover both halves of what travel hacking actually requires: knowing how the loyalty programs work operationally, and knowing how to structure the credit and banking side to earn points efficiently. That combination, plus being one of the more established German-language voices in the space since 2023, has let the free Basic tier act as a steady funnel into their paid programs.
