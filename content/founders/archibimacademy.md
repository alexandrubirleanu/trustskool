# Lloni Rodriguez and ArchiBIM Academy: Inside Skool's 2,600-Member Architecture Community

ArchiBIM Academy calls itself the number one architecture academy in the Spanish-speaking world, promising to help members master ArchiCAD and BIM workflows through live classes Monday through Friday. It's free to join and has reached roughly 2,635 members.

## Who runs it

The academy is run by Lloni Rodriguez, who identifies himself across his public profiles as an architect, planner, and BIM manager. He posts architecture and ArchiCAD content under the handle @lloni_fernando_11 on Instagram, where he has built a following of more than 51,000 people, and mirrors that content on TikTok under the same handle ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/lloni_fernando_11/); [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@lloni_fernando_11)). The academy itself maintains a separate Instagram account under @archibim_academy with short-form videos explaining what the community does and how members generate income from it ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/archibim_academy/)). A companion Skool community post describes the group as the largest Spanish-speaking digital architects' community on the platform, built around a shared push to learn, create, and transform digital architecture practice rather than collect certificates ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/llonis-workshop-8945/somos-la-comunidad-de-arquitectos-digitales-mas-grande-de-habla-hispana-en-skool)).

## What members actually get

The community's stated offering covers architectural design (ARQ), structural work (ESTR), MEP systems, and interiors (INT), delivered through live daily classes plus round-the-clock workshop support. The emphasis, repeated across both the academy's Skool description and its Instagram content, is on completing real projects from modeling through technical documentation rather than accumulating course certificates that don't reflect deliverable work architecture firms actually pay for. A related community post describes more than 400 architects and students already connected inside the group, sharing progress on real buildings such as the "Edificio Igara" project that shows up repeatedly in the community's own posts.

## Why it's grown

ArchiCAD and BIM training is a genuine professional skills gap in much of the Spanish-speaking architecture world, where formal university programs often lag behind the software tools firms expect graduates to already know. Rodriguez's own visible identity as a working architect and BIM manager, rather than an anonymous "academy" brand, gives the community a face members can evaluate directly through his Instagram and TikTok content before joining. Free access matters here too: architecture students and junior professionals are a price-sensitive audience relative to the software licenses and continuing education they're often already paying for, so a no-cost live-class community is positioned to capture that audience before nudging them toward whatever paid mentorship or project-based coaching Rodriguez may offer separately.
