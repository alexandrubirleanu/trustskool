# Isa Ulubaev and Master Your Mind: From Refugee Childhood to German Mindset Coaching on Skool

Master Your Mind is a free, German-language Skool community with roughly 4,380 members, pitched around helping men become emotionally independent, disciplined, and self-assured rather than needy in relationships and life generally. It's run by Isa Ulubaev, a mindset and success coach who built a large following through weekly video content before turning that audience into a mentoring business.

## Who is Isa Ulubaev

Ulubaev's own site describes him as someone who came to Germany as a refugee, grew up under difficult circumstances, and went down a self-destructive path as a young man before turning his life around and moving into coaching. Since then he's published weekly video content under the Master Your Mind brand, and his site credits him with around 730,000 followers across social platforms and millions of video views ([master-your-mind.de](https://www.master-your-mind.de/)). He hosts a YouTube channel under the Master Your Mind name and is registered on the platform as the community's owner, confirmed directly on the Skool group's own about page, which lists him by name as its founder ([Master Your Mind on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/masteryourmind)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, Master Your Mind is aimed at men working on emotional independence, discipline, communication, and what the group frames as financial freedom, teaching members to build what the brand calls the "highest version" of themselves rather than staying dependent on external validation, particularly from relationships. The free Skool group functions as the top-of-funnel layer for Ulubaev's paid "2.0 Mentoring" program, which he runs with a small team through his main site.

## Why it's grown

Ulubaev's story, a difficult refugee childhood followed by a self-destructive period and then a deliberate turnaround, is the kind of personal narrative that travels well in short-form mindset and self-improvement content, and it gives his coaching a level of lived credibility that's harder to manufacture than pure theory. Publishing consistently on a weekly cadence for years, rather than in bursts, is a large part of how he built the following his community now draws from, and the free Skool tier lets that audience sample his approach before committing to the paid mentoring program.
