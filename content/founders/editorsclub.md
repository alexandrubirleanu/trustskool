# Chalitha Weerakkody and Editors Club: A Sri Lankan Filmmaker's Agency-Backed Community for Editors

Editors Club is a free Skool community of roughly 3,840 members aimed at creative professionals, video editors, graphic designers, and other content creators, offering paid work opportunities, cash-prize games, and business-building education. The community's About page credits Chalitha Weerakkody as its founder.

## Who is Chalitha Weerakkody

Chalitha Weerakkody is a filmmaker from Sri Lanka who has built a career working with online creators around the world, and he's the owner of Editoz Club, an agency focused on producing viral content for figures in the personal growth and creator space ([LinkedIn](https://au.linkedin.com/in/chaliwee)). Through that agency, he has worked with recognized creators including Davie Fogarty, Jane Lu, Goose McGrath, and Simon Beard, applying a mix of business strategy, marketing, and graphic design to build content and social advertising campaigns, with particular focus on TikTok marketing.

His professional portfolio, listed as a Senior Multimedia Producer, is documented on an Australian production-industry directory, alongside his own YouTube channel and Vimeo profile showcasing his editing and production work ([The Loop portfolio](https://www.theloop.com.au/Chalitha/portfolio/Video-Producer/Melbourne); [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCi_8h4-2yCvUINiFfJJEiig); [Vimeo](https://vimeo.com/user70389798)).

## What members actually get

Editors Club gives members a place to get hired for real editing and content work, including opportunities connected to prominent creators such as entrepreneur Robert Herjavec, alongside games where members can win up to $2,000 a month in cash prizes. There's also a paid $99 course focused on learning to build and grow a creative business, and members can submit their work for review from established pro creators, giving newer editors a feedback loop that's often hard to access outside of an agency environment.

The community also functions as a discussion space for the broader craft: members trade notes on viral editing trends, software workflows, and the business side of freelancing, from pricing a first client to structuring a small editing team, topics that sit closer to Weerakkody's own day-to-day running Editoz Club than to a typical tutorial-only editing course.

## Why it's grown

Weerakkody's community draws its credibility directly from his agency work: rather than teaching editing purely as a skill in the abstract, Editors Club is built by someone who is actively running paid content campaigns for well-known creators through Editoz Club, giving him real, ongoing visibility into what creators are actually paying for and hiring editors to produce. That agency-backed pipeline also gives the community a genuine job-placement angle that pure educational communities can't easily replicate, since members aren't only learning technique, they're potentially a hire away from working directly with the kind of creators Weerakkody's agency already serves.

That pipeline is also a two-way filter: Weerakkody gets a pool of vetted, community-tested editors to draw from when Editoz Club needs to staff up for a new client campaign, and members get a realistic shot at paid work that doesn't depend on cold outreach to strangers.
