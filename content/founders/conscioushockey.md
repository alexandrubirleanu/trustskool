# Xander Mavrogiannis and Hockey Instincts Mastery: An NCAA Player's Hockey IQ Community

Hockey Instincts Mastery, also known as Conscious Hockey, is a free Skool community of roughly 2,500 members with a pitch aimed squarely at ambitious young players: score more goals and assists by learning to think the game the way NHL players do, rather than just skating harder or shooting more pucks. The coach behind it, Xander Mavrogiannis, built the framework out of his own path through competitive hockey.

## Who is Xander Mavrogiannis

Mavrogiannis, born February 12, 1998 in Dover, Massachusetts, played junior hockey for the East Coast Wizards and Walpole Express before moving on to NCAA Division III hockey at Assumption University ([Elite Prospects player profile](https://www.eliteprospects.com/player/321094/xander-mavrogiannis)). His LinkedIn profile lists him as a student at Assumption University, and he separately works as a hockey coach in the Dover, Massachusetts area, listed on the coaching platform CoachUp ([CoachUp profile](https://www.coachup.com/coaches/alexanderm-19)).

Beyond his own playing career, he built Conscious Hockey around a specific niche within hockey instruction: decision-making and what he calls "Hockey IQ," distinct from the more common focus on skating mechanics or shooting technique. Content tied to the brand includes breakdowns of specific plays inspired by NHL stars, including modules referencing Connor Bedard's deception and playmaking, distributed through the "conscious_hockey" Instagram account and an associated podcast appearance discussing his coaching philosophy.

## What members actually get

Inside the free Skool community, members get access to instructional breakdowns built around real-game situations rather than isolated drills, with course titles like "Exposing Goalies on Breakaways" and "Patrick Kane Patience" reflecting the pattern-recognition-based approach Mavrogiannis teaches. The core promise is that players can train hockey sense the same deliberate way they train physical skills, using video breakdown and situational teaching rather than generic skill-and-drill content.

That positioning sets Hockey Instincts Mastery apart from most youth hockey training content online, which tends to focus almost entirely on physical mechanics rather than in-game reads and anticipation.

## Why it's grown

Mavrogiannis's own NCAA playing background gives him a credibility floor that pure content creators without playing experience don't have, and the Hockey IQ angle fills a real gap in a youth hockey training market saturated with skating and shooting content. Parents and players searching for something beyond stickhandling drills have relatively few well-known options teaching in-game decision-making specifically, which helps a niche, free community like this stand out even at a modest member count.

Tying specific lessons to recognizable NHL players like Bedard and Kane also gives the content an immediate hook for younger players who already study those players' highlight clips, turning passive fandom into a structured learning frame.
