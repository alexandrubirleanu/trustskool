# Emiliano Arroyo and RicFit Pro Trial Portal: From Client to Team Member Inside a FIFA Agent's Football Academy

RicFit Pro Trial Portal is a Skool community of roughly 4,200 members built around a single, direct pitch: "Helping You Become the Best Footballer Possible & Helping You Get Signed!" It's the Skool front door to RicFit, a football development system built by FIFA-licensed agent Eric Friedlander, but the community itself is credited on Skool to a different name: Emiliano Arroyo.

## Who is Emiliano Arroyo

Emiliano Arroyo's own story with RicFit started as a client, not an employee. According to RicFit's own team page, Arroyo went through the program as a player and consistently showed the discipline and professionalism that RicFit tries to instill, eventually earning a trial with a club in Latvia's second division and gaining real experience inside a professional football environment ([RicFit team page](https://ricfittraining.com/meettheteam)). Before that, as a youth player, he was profiled on a recruiting platform used by college programs to evaluate prospects ([NCSA recruiting profile](https://www.ncsasports.org/mens-soccer-recruiting/missouri/harrisonville/harrisonville-high-school/emiliano-arroyo)).

When RicFit began expanding, Arroyo was brought on full-time, moving from client to team member, and he now plays a role in RicFit's operations and player support systems, work he continues to talk about under the handle Coach Arroyo across social platforms.

## The organization behind him

RicFit as a business was founded by Eric Friedlander, a FIFA Licensed Agent and former professional footballer who played across five countries over an 11-year career and served as General Manager of FK Metta before turning to coaching and career development for ambitious players ([RicFit's about page](https://ricfittraining.com/about-eric); [Medium profile](https://medium.com/@siawynn/eric-friedlander-is-bringing-a-new-level-of-structure-to-football-through-ricfit-61b96467d7b8)). Friedlander built RicFit to give players the structured performance development and career strategy system he wishes he'd had earlier in his own playing career.

## What members actually get

The Pro Trial Portal sits inside that broader RicFit ecosystem, giving members access to training content and a community aimed at players who want structured development and a real shot at getting noticed by clubs, rather than generic fitness advice. Given the "Not for the lazy" framing on the community's own page, the target audience is explicitly hungry, ambitious players rather than casual hobbyists.

## Why it's grown

The growth story here works on two levels. Friedlander brought the credibility: a genuine FIFA agent license, years playing professionally across multiple countries, and a GM role that gives the whole operation real football-industry standing rather than just marketing claims. Arroyo, meanwhile, brings something a founder alone can't easily replicate: proof that the system works, having gone through it himself as a client before joining the team. A community run in part by someone who was recently in the exact position current members are in tends to build trust quickly, since Arroyo isn't asking members to trust a system in the abstract, he's someone who used it, got a real trial with a professional club, and stayed on to help others do the same.
