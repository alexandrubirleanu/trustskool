# Tyler Smerz and Outlaw Golf Club: The Junior Champion Building Affordable Golf Coaching on Skool

Outlaw Golf Club calls itself the number one online golf community connecting players worldwide, and at $9 a month with roughly 3,060 members, it's priced well below typical private golf instruction. It's run by Tyler Smerz.

## Who is Tyler Smerz

Smerz played competitive junior golf out of Las Vegas before winning a Future Champions Golf Tour Arizona Boys Championship as a 2019 prospect ([Future Champions Golf feature](https://futurechampionsgolf.com/witb-fcg-arizona-boys-champion-tyler-smerz/)), and he went on to play collegiate golf at Grand Canyon University, where he's listed on the men's golf roster as a junior from Las Vegas, Nevada ([GCU Club Sports roster](https://gcuclubsports.com/sports/mens-golf/roster/tyler-smerz/1400)). That competitive record, verified through both a tournament win writeup and an official college roster, backs up the community's own description of him as a player with 15-plus years of tournament experience and a career-best handicap of plus-3.

Beyond competitive play, Smerz has built a fairly large instructional following, publishing swing breakdowns and golf content across YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat under variations of his own name ([Tyler Smerz on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@tylersmerz)), reaching roughly 15,000 followers on Instagram with lesson-style content aimed at everyday golfers rather than other competitive players.

## What members actually get

Outlaw Golf Club is built around Smerz's stated goal of making quality golf coaching accessible and affordable, rather than gatekept behind expensive private lessons. At $9 a month, it's priced closer to a streaming subscription than traditional coaching, giving members swing instruction and a community of other golfers working on their game, drawing on Smerz's own competitive and collegiate playing background as the basis for the instruction.

## Why it's grown

Golf instruction has historically been expensive and geographically limited to whoever's available at a member's local course, and a $9-a-month online alternative removes both of those barriers for players who want structured coaching without a country club budget. Smerz's own competitive record, backed by an independent tournament feature and a verifiable college roster rather than just claimed credentials, gives the community real coaching authority behind the low price point.

His existing following across YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat also gives Outlaw Golf Club a built-in discovery channel outside of Skool itself, which matters for reaching golfers who aren't actively browsing the Skool community directory.

Golf also carries a reputation, fair or not, as an expensive, exclusive sport, and a young instructor building his brand around affordability rather than exclusivity gives Outlaw Golf Club a distinct position relative to traditional club pros charging far more per lesson. Pairing that pricing with a genuine junior and collegiate competitive record, rather than only social media following, gives the coaching itself more weight than a purely content-driven golf influencer could offer.
