# Dan De Luis and the Abundance Breathwork Community: A Wim Hof-Trained Coach on Skool

The Abundance Breathwork Community, listed on Skool as "Hacking Your Happiness," charges $10 a month for access to breathwork, mindfulness, and biohacking content aimed at nervous-system regulation and stress relief, with a 7-day free trial built into the offer. The coach running it, Dan De Luis, brings a genuinely unusual credential to the space: he trained directly under Wim Hof, the Dutch extreme-athlete known as "The Iceman," and is a certified instructor in his method.

## Who is Dan De Luis

De Luis is a Canada-based performance and breath coach who has spent close to two decades working with clients ranging from professional athletes in the NFL, NHL, and NCAA to Canadian special forces personnel and corporate executives ([Dan De Luis Wellness, About](https://dandeluis.com/about/)). He is listed as a certified instructor on the official Wim Hof Method instructor directory ([Wim Hof Method instructors](https://activities.wimhofmethod.com/instructors/dande-luis)), having trained under Hof directly and assisted with advanced instructor certification programs.

His path into the field started around 16 years ago, when chronic physical and mental health issues, including depression, anxiety, and chronic back pain, led him to explore yoga and breathwork, including time spent at an ashram in India under a guru's guidance. A separate injury reportedly left him bedridden for roughly two years earlier in his career, and breathwork became his primary rehabilitation tool during that stretch, an origin story that a press release on his 2026 meditation-club launch also references ([MarketersMedia release](https://news.marketersmedia.com/world-renowned-breath-work-and-performance-coach-dan-de-luis-launches-online-meditation-club-to-make-breathwork-accessible-to-thousands/89187893)). He now runs Dan De Luis Wellness, the Breath Work Meditation Club, and Nature's Elements Wellness, a thermal spa retreat in Mallorytown, Ontario.

## What members actually get

The paid Skool community offers a growing library described as more than 100 free videos on breathwork, mindset, and recovery, alongside a 7-day free trial of the $10-a-month tier. Content spans breathwork techniques, biohacking strategies, sleep optimization, and anxiety management, with the stated aim of helping members perform under pressure rather than just relax in the abstract.

Given De Luis's background coaching elite athletes and tactical personnel, the material draws on techniques originally built for people operating under genuinely high-stakes physical and mental stress, not just general wellness content repackaged for a broader audience.

## Why it's grown

The credibility anchor here is specific and verifiable: being a certified Wim Hof Method instructor is a real, checkable credential, not a self-assigned title, and it puts De Luis in a smaller pool of breathwork coaches with an internationally recognized certification behind their teaching. Combined with two decades of work across professional sports leagues and military-adjacent clients, that gives the $10 entry price an unusually strong credibility-to-cost ratio for a wellness Skool community.

His own rehabilitation story, going from bedridden to building a career around the same practice, also gives the brand's messaging a personal foundation that pure technique-focused breathwork content often lacks.
