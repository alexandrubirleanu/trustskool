# Levi Patton and Music Money: A Skool Games Winner Teaching Musicians to Build Communities

Music Money is a free Skool community with roughly 4,760 members that teaches people how to build a profitable music-focused community on Skool in 40 days or less. It's run by Levi Patton, a Nashville-based community builder who describes himself as a winner of the Skool Games, the platform's own recurring growth competition, and who co-founded the project alongside Christian Crenshaw.

## Who is Levi Patton

Patton's own Skool profile bio reads "Winner of the Skool Games. I help Community Owners Scale. (Co-Founder of Muzic Money + Skool Launch)," and he's listed as based in Nashville, Tennessee, having joined the platform in September 2023 ([Levi Patton on Skool](https://www.skool.com/@levi)). The Music Money community's own about page credits him directly as its founder and describes him as a "Community Expert and Skool Games Winner." The Muzic Money marketing site also lists Roland, who has managed producer Zaytoven for more than 20 years, as part of the team alongside Patton, giving the project a direct connection to working music industry management rather than just online marketing credentials ([muzic-money.com](https://muzic-money.com/)).

## What members actually get

According to the community's own description, Music Money walks members through a structured 40-day system for launching a paid music community from scratch, covering everything from initial offer positioning through the mechanics of running weekly calls and retaining paying members. The community pairs free content with paid one-on-one strategy calls and a more intensive VIP tier for people who want direct, ongoing support from Patton and his team.

## Why it's grown

Music is a crowded, hit-driven industry where most independent artists and producers struggle to build predictable income, which makes a recurring community model an attractive alternative to one-off sales of beats, lessons, or merchandise. By specifically targeting the music niche rather than teaching generic community building, Patton is competing in a narrower, more addressable market than the broad "how to start a Skool community" category most Skool-focused creators chase, and the Skool Games win itself functions as a credibility signal to musicians who don't know the platform's internal ecosystem.
