# Jonathan Courtney and Facilitator Club: Inside Skool's 10,000-Member Facilitation Community

Facilitator Club bills itself as the biggest facilitation community on the internet, and with around 10,000 members on Skool, that claim is hard to argue with. It exists to connect facilitators and aspiring facilitators around workshop design, Design Sprints and the business of building a facilitation career, and it's run by Jonathan Courtney, a name well known in the product design and workshop-facilitation world.

## Who is Jonathan Courtney

Jonathan Courtney is CEO and founder of AJ&Smart, an award-winning product design, strategy and innovation studio built around Design Sprints, a methodology he's taught to companies worldwide. He co-founded the studio after connecting with a collaborator over a shared enthusiasm for design thinking, and has since expanded AJ&Smart into three related businesses: Facilitator.com, a Studio arm and a Partners arm ([Unscheduled CEO newsletter](https://www.unscheduledceo.com/p/introducing-the-new-aj-and-smart)). He's interviewed extensively about his path into workshop facilitation, including a detailed conversation with Facilitator.com's own blog about how workshops reshaped his career ([facilitator.com interview](https://facilitator.com/blog/interview-jonathan-courtney)), and he maintains a public facilitator profile on SessionLab, a well-known tool in the workshop-design space ([SessionLab profile](https://www.sessionlab.com/profiles/jonathan-courtney)). AJ&Smart also runs Facilitation Fundamentals, a course hosted on Teachable, which serves as a paid complement to the free Skool community ([Teachable course](https://aj-smart.teachable.com/p/facilitation-fundamentals)).

## What members actually get

Facilitator Club gives members a space to talk shop about facilitation and workshop design, including AJ&Smart's signature Design Sprint methodology, share resources and insights from real client work, and ask the AJ&Smart team direct questions about building a facilitation career. The Skool community includes a classroom with training material, regular community events, and a leaderboard that surfaces the most active contributors. Alongside the free club, Courtney also runs Facilitator Pro, a smaller, more advanced paid community for people further along in their facilitation careers.

## Why it's grown

AJ&Smart had already built a substantial reputation in the design and product world well before Skool existed, largely through its YouTube channel and its role popularizing the Design Sprint format for product teams. That existing authority is the real engine behind Facilitator Club's size: Courtney isn't building trust from zero inside the community, he's redirecting an audience that already associates AJ&Smart with credible, practical workshop expertise.

The free-to-paid structure, Facilitator Club as the open community and Facilitator Pro and Facilitation Fundamentals as the monetized layers, also mirrors what tends to work well for niche professional communities on Skool: a wide, free space for peer discussion and career questions, with clearly separated paid products for people who want structured training rather than open conversation. For a niche as specific as professional facilitation, reaching 10,000 members is a strong signal of just how much of that niche AJ&Smart's name already commands.
