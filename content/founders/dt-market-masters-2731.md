# Dion van der Made and DT Market Masters: Inside Skool's Funded Trader Community

DT Market Masters is a free Skool community with more than 9,270 members, built around one specific goal: getting traders "funded" by a proprietary trading firm so they can trade with the firm's capital instead of their own. It's run by a trader known online as DionTrades, whose real name is Dion van der Made.

## Who is Dion van der Made

Dion finished law school and went straight into corporate consulting, a path that looked stable on paper but left him drained, according to an interview with TheTradingPit ([TheTradingPit success story](https://www.thetradingpit.com/blog/dion-success-story-2025)). During the pandemic he started looking for ways to make money online, and by early 2024 he had gone full time as a trader. That first year was brutal: he lost more than $60,000 of his own money, mostly to ego-driven overtrading and blown prop-firm evaluations.

The turning point, according to an interview with Alpha Capital, came when a mentor told him to stop moving his stop losses and stop taking partial profits early ([Alpha Capital interview](https://alphacapitalgroup.uk/interviews/he-failed-every-evaluation-until-he-fixed-his-ego-how-dion-trades-earned-30k-in-)). He quit his consulting job, started coaching other traders, and built a YouTube channel by streaming his own live trading sessions for 80 to 90 days straight one summer. Since then he says he has earned over $100,000 in payouts across several funded-trader programs, trading NASDAQ and gold on higher time frames using ICT-style concepts and risking around 1% of the account per trade. He currently holds a $200,000 simulated allocation with Alpha Capital and splits his time between Asia and Europe to match his preferred trading sessions ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/diontrades/)).

## What members actually get

Inside DT Market Masters, the core offer is a 9-step roadmap toward passing a funded-trader evaluation, paired with a 5-day mindset challenge that leans heavily on the psychology lessons Dion says cost him $60,000 to learn the hard way. Members get instruction in ICT concepts, access to live trading sessions, and a weekly market outlook, all framed around the same three pillars he talks about publicly: knowledge, community, and psychology.

## Why it's grown

The appeal of a funded-trader community is structural before it's personal: members aren't being asked to risk their own savings, they're being coached toward qualifying for someone else's capital, which lowers the perceived downside of joining. Dion's own loss-then-recovery story does the rest of the selling, giving him credibility with an audience that has likely already blown an account or two of its own. He built his following the slow way, streaming live trades for months before ever charging for coaching, and DT Market Masters looks like the free front door to that audience: a no-cost Skool group that lets thousands of traders watch and learn before any paid coaching relationship exists.
