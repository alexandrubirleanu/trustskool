# Jonathan Migan and Team3DAlpha: A Testosterone Coach's Classroom for Skinny-Fat Young Men

Team3DAlpha is a $25-a-month Skool community with roughly 3,417 members, aimed at young men dealing with low testosterone, low confidence, and what the community describes as a "skinny-fat" body type. It's run by Jonathan Migan, who built the brand around his own account of turning around his physical and mental health.

## Who is Jonathan Migan

Migan runs Team3DAlpha across multiple platforms under the brand handle, including an active Instagram, a Threads account, an X account posting as Team3DAlphaCEO, and a YouTube channel, Team 3D Alpha ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/team3dalpha/); [X](https://x.com/Team3DAlpha); [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCj0nUqdm-3dzRfivqv1n1kQ)). His official site describes his own history as including low testosterone, being "skinny fat," and struggling with low confidence and depression before developing what he calls his Nucleus Overload training program ([team3dalpha.com](https://www.team3dalpha.com/)). The same site is explicit that Migan is "not a Doctor or Dietitian," positioning him as a self-described natural testosterone expert building his approach from personal experience rather than clinical credentials. His site frames that personal history in specific terms, describing past struggles with bullying, video game addiction, and ADHD alongside the low-confidence and low-testosterone narrative that anchors the brand, and presents his current program as the reversal of that same story.

## What members actually get

Members get eBooks on testosterone-focused diet and supplementation, a training and nutrition program built around his Nucleus Overload and HSP Training methods, and access to a private group and a "Team3DAlpha Daily Routine" Migan says is designed to produce visible physical and mental changes within 30 days or less. The community also covers dating and relationships as part of its confidence-building angle, tying physical training to the social confidence problems Migan says he struggled with himself, rather than treating fitness and dating as separate topics.

## Why it's grown

Team3DAlpha's growth rests on a specific, narrow audience, young men who feel physically underdeveloped and lack confidence, paired with a founder who's built a consistent, personal-story-driven presence across Instagram, X, Threads, and YouTube rather than a single platform. Because Migan explicitly disclaims medical credentials on his own site, the pitch relies entirely on his personal transformation story and the coherence of that story across platforms, a real trust signal for this kind of body-recomposition coaching community, but a different one than a community anchored by clinical credentials. Members joining $25-a-month communities like this one are typically buying into a relatable before-and-after narrative as much as a specific protocol, and Migan's site is built to reinforce that narrative at every turn rather than lead with lab values or peer-reviewed research.
