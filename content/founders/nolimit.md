# Alex Elfi and NOLIMIT: Inside Skool's 2,800-Member French E-Commerce Community

NOLIMIT is a free, French-language Skool community that pitches itself on scale: learn to build and grow an e-commerce brand in the US and internationally using systems its founders say generate more than $1,000,000 a month. It has reached roughly 2,827 members.

## Who runs it

NOLIMIT's own about page names Alex Elfi as the community's creator, alongside a co-founder identified as Givenche, describing both as "real brand operators" with a combined track record of more than $40 million generated across their ventures ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/nolimit/about)). Elfi maintains a public identity beyond the community itself: he posts under the handle @shadox_a on X, where he discusses e-commerce revenue benchmarks and strategy with his following ([X](https://x.com/shadox_a/status/1910004726411723263)), runs a YouTube channel under his own name featuring interviews about scaling to seven figures a month ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=INYfrjH75hs)), and has documented six years of e-commerce work through his content. NOLIMIT's about page states that Elfi and Givenche personally handle member support rather than delegating it to virtual assistants, which the group frames as direct founder access rather than a layer of hired coaches.

## What members actually get

The stated curriculum runs more than 30 hours of video modules covering product research, media buying and ads, recruitment, finance, and day-to-day brand management, alongside supplier contacts and templates the founders say they use in their own operations ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/nolimit/about)). Weekly live sessions with Elfi and Givenche are positioned as the core of the offer, giving members a direct view into decisions the founders are making in their own e-commerce businesses rather than case studies pulled from other people's brands.

## Why it's grown

French-language e-commerce education on Skool is a thinner market than its English-language equivalent, and NOLIMIT's positioning leans into that gap directly, targeting French-speaking entrepreneurs who want US-market e-commerce tactics without translating English-first content themselves. Free access removes the friction that comes with committing money to a niche where returns are unproven for a beginner, and the founders' own public content, regular X posts and YouTube interviews discussing specific revenue numbers, gives prospective members something to evaluate before joining rather than asking them to take the community's own claims at face value.

The reliance on founder-reported revenue figures is worth flagging plainly: the $40 million combined figure and the "$1,000,000 per month systems" framing both originate from Elfi and Givenche's own channels rather than an audited or third-party source, which is standard for the e-commerce education niche but still means prospective members should treat the numbers as marketing claims until they see the underlying operations for themselves.
