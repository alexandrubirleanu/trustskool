# Dan Holman and The Daily Pursuit: The Ex-Pro Striker Coaching Skool's Football Community

The Daily Pursuit is a free Skool community with roughly 3,370 members, built around the tagline "Live The Standard" for players chasing goals in football and in life. It's run by Dan Holman, who built the community's parent brand under the name Goalman Football.

## Who is Dan Holman

Holman worked his way into professional football without coming up through a traditional academy system, going on to play 10 years in the professional game, a path detailed on his own Goalman Football coaching site ([Goalman Football](https://goalman.co.uk/)). He's a UEFA-qualified coach, and a stats profile confirms his playing career, listing his time as a striker for clubs including Tavistock ([365Scores player profile](https://www.365scores.com/en-us/football/player/dan-holman-178742)). His LinkedIn profile describes him specifically as a "Specialist Attacking & Goalscoring Coach," consistent with the finishing and striker-focused training he promotes publicly ([LinkedIn](https://uk.linkedin.com/in/danholman1)).

Holman posts instructional football content under the handle @goalman_ on both Instagram and X, the same "Goalman" name that ties directly back to his coaching brand and the "goalman-5746" address of this Skool community, linking his public coaching identity directly to the group.

## What members actually get

The Daily Pursuit is framed as a home for serious, driven players, teaching what Holman describes as the mindset, habits, and daily actions that separate top performers from everyone else, alongside football-specific training content drawn from his own goalscoring and attacking specialty. Being free, it functions as an entry point into Holman's broader coaching business, which includes more structured, presumably paid, mentorship programs built around his playing and coaching background.

## Why it's grown

Holman's own path, reaching professional football without an academy pedigree, gives The Daily Pursuit real credibility with players and parents who feel shut out by traditional academy gatekeeping, since he's living proof that path isn't the only route to a pro career. A UEFA coaching qualification layered on top of that playing background also signals a level of formal coaching credential that a lot of "ex-player turned content creator" accounts in football don't actually have.

Free access removes the barrier for young players and parents who are curious but not ready to commit financially, while Holman's existing Instagram and X following under the Goalman name gives the community a steady discovery channel outside Skool's own search and directory.

Youth football is also an environment where parents are frequently pitched expensive academy programs and private coaching packages with uncertain results, and a coach who reached the professional game through an unconventional route offers a genuinely different value proposition than the standard academy-pipeline pitch. That contrast, plus the free entry point, likely explains why a fairly narrow niche, goalscoring and attacking-specific coaching, has still been able to build a community of this size.
