# Stefan Masnikovic and 6 Figure Tech Freelancer: Inside Skool's 6,500-Member Freelancing Community

6 Figure Tech Freelancer, hosted at Skool's 9-5-to-dream-life community, is a free group of about 6,527 members pitched around escaping a traditional job for freelance tech work. It's run by Stefan Masnikovic, whose own move from corporate employee to freelancer is the story the whole community is built on.

## Who is Stefan Masnikovic

The Skool community's about page credits Stefan Masnikovic as its founder, listing him by name as the creator of the group ([9-5 to Dream Life on Skool](https://www.skool.com/9-5-to-dream-life/about)). His LinkedIn profile documents his own transition out of a corporate role into freelance data work, describing quitting a job to become a freelance data analyst and building out a coaching business around that shift ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/stefanmasnikovic/)). He also runs Freedom Freelancing, a coaching business for IT and tech professionals looking to freelance, with its own dedicated website ([Freedom Freelancing](https://freedom-freelancing.com/)), and posts regularly under his name on YouTube ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@Stefan-Masnikovic)).

Masnikovic is based in Amsterdam, and his public content, LinkedIn posts, YouTube videos, and the Freedom Freelancing brand all consistently tell the same story: someone who left a standard employment path for tech freelancing and has since built a business teaching others to do the same.

## What members actually get

The community is free to join and frames itself as a path from a traditional 9-to-5 job to a "6-figure tech freelancer" career, offering coaching, community support, and resources at no upfront cost. The core promise leans on Masnikovic's own transition: rather than teaching an abstract freelancing framework, the content is built around the specific playbook he used to move from a corporate data role into higher-paying freelance data and tech work.

That's a narrower pitch than most generic "escape your job" communities, which tend to stay vague about what kind of freelance work members should actually pursue. Anchoring the pitch specifically to tech and data freelancing, a field with well-documented hourly rates and demand, gives members a more concrete target than "become your own boss."

## Why it's grown

At 6,527 members and free to join, 9-5 to Dream Life benefits from a founder whose own career change is documented in public, across LinkedIn posts, YouTube videos, and a standalone coaching website, rather than asserted without evidence. Freelance coaching is a crowded niche online, and a lot of it comes from people who've never actually freelanced themselves; Masnikovic's LinkedIn history of posts about his own freelance income and client work gives the pitch a paper trail that's unusually easy to check.

Free access also lowers the barrier for people still weighing whether to leave a stable job, which is exactly the audience most likely to want proof, in the form of a real person's documented transition, before committing further.
