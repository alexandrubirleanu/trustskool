# Tim Adam and Pinterest Skool: Inside Skool's 5,500-Member Pinterest Marketing Community

Pinterest Skool is a 5,521-member community teaching organic Pinterest marketing — how to drive traffic and sales through the platform without paid ads — for $7/month [Skool](https://www.skool.com/pinterest/about).

## Who runs it

The community is run by Tim Adam, who states he has more than 16 years of Pinterest marketing experience and has personally generated over 425 million Pinterest views through his own work, according to the community's about page. That length of tenure predates most of the current wave of "Pinterest marketing" educators, many of whom started teaching the platform only after it saw a resurgence in creator interest over the past few years. Beyond his own Skool materials, this research did not find extensive independent, third-party biographical coverage of Adam (press features, external interviews) — his public track record is presented primarily through his own community and course materials rather than outside verification, which is worth keeping in mind alongside the specific view-count figure he cites.

## What members actually get

Pinterest Skool includes an AI-assisted Pinterest agent tool, a structured "profit roadmap," weekly live Q&A sessions with Adam directly, trending keyword reports to guide content timing, video pin templates, and engagement games designed to keep the community active. There's also a built-in promotional element where members can get exposure to the rest of the community's membership base — useful for members selling their own products or services who want additional organic reach beyond Pinterest itself.

## Why it's grown

Pinterest occupies an unusual position in the creator-marketing world: it's one of the few major platforms where content has a genuinely long shelf life (a well-optimized pin can drive traffic for years, unlike a tweet or a TikTok that's largely dead within days), which makes it especially attractive to bloggers, e-commerce sellers, and content creators looking for durable, low-maintenance traffic. That makes Adam's specific, long-tenure claim — 16+ years and 425 million views — a meaningful differentiator in a platform-specific niche where most competing communities are led by people who picked up Pinterest marketing only recently. The low $7/month price point and weekly direct access to Adam further lower the barrier for creators curious about diversifying their traffic sources away from an increasingly saturated Instagram/TikTok/YouTube landscape.

As with any platform-specific marketing community, the value depends heavily on how current the tactics stay as Pinterest's own algorithm and ad products change, so an actively posting, still-active founder matters more here than the raw years-of-experience number.
