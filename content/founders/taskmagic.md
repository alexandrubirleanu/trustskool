# Josh Sturgeon and TaskMagic Empire: Inside Skool's 4,300-Member Automation Community

TaskMagic Empire is a free Skool community built around a single no-code automation tool: TaskMagic, which lets non-technical users automate repetitive browser tasks without writing scripts. With roughly 4,300 members, the community bills itself as the place for "tips, tricks and shortcuts for making money with TaskMagic," and new joiners are promised a free automation the moment they sign up.

## Who runs it

The community's About page on Skool credits Josh Sturgeon as its founder. Sturgeon is a Boston-based growth strategist and co-founder of EmberTribe, a customer-acquisition agency of around 40 people that works with early and growth-stage startups ([The Org profile](https://theorg.com/org/embertribe/org-chart/josh-sturgeon); [personal site](https://joshsturgeon.com/about-me/)). He holds a BA in Business Administration from Gordon College and an MA in Religious Studies from Gordon-Conwell Theological Seminary, and outside of EmberTribe he mentors founders through Techstars and has guest-lectured at MIT ([Crunchbase profile](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/josh-sturgeon)).

That background, acquisition and growth marketing rather than software engineering, fits the shape of TaskMagic Empire: it isn't a place where the underlying tool gets built, it's a place where people already using it trade the specific shortcuts and workflows that make it pay off. The Skool page itself was announced publicly as "OFFICIAL: TaskMagic's Lazy Empire skool community" in a launch video published to YouTube ([video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MxRnDEXC7Gg)).

## What TaskMagic is, and what members get

TaskMagic itself was founded by Jeremy Redman, who by his own account and outside reporting scaled the product to seven figures in revenue within a couple of years of launch, going from roughly $15/hour and six figures of personal debt to running a company with thousands of paying customers ([Starter Story breakdown](https://www.starterstory.com/taskmagic-breakdown)). The tool's pitch is straightforward: point-and-click automation for people who can't code, aimed at agency owners, freelancers and small operators who need to automate repetitive browser work like data entry, lead scraping, or report generation.

Inside TaskMagic Empire, the promise is narrower and more tactical than a general course. Members get a free automation on joining, and the ongoing content is framed explicitly around "making money with TaskMagic," meaning workflow templates, use-case breakdowns, and shortcuts for squeezing more value out of the tool rather than teaching automation from first principles.

## Why it's grown

TaskMagic Empire's growth is really downstream of two separate growth stories converging: TaskMagic's own rapid rise as a product, which created a large base of users hungry for practical tips beyond the tool's own documentation, and Sturgeon's growth-marketing background, which is exactly the skill set needed to turn that user base into an engaged free community. A free tips-and-shortcuts community is a classic acquisition-funnel play for a SaaS product, and it's the kind of structure someone with an agency background built around customer acquisition would recognize immediately.

It's also a useful data point for anyone studying Skool communities generally: not every community tied to a well-known tool or personality is run day-to-day by that tool's original founder. Sometimes the public-facing name and the person actually building the community engine behind it are two different people, each bringing a different kind of expertise to the table.
