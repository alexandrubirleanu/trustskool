# Davie Fogarty and Davie's Free Ecom Course: A $600M Founder Teaches Skool for Free

It's unusual to find a nine-figure operator teaching a completely free course to over 73,000 people, but that's exactly the setup behind Davie's Free Ecom Course. The community releases weekly video training on picking products, running ads, and building a store, backed by forum access to ask questions directly, with no paywall anywhere in the funnel.

## Who is Davie Fogarty

Fogarty is the founder and CEO of The Davie Group, the parent company behind The Oodie, the oversized wearable blanket brand that turned into one of the defining direct-to-consumer breakout stories of the pandemic era. Forbes Australia profiled him in its inaugural 30 Under 30 list with the headline "Australia's $600 million retail shark," describing a business that had scaled to that revenue figure through The Oodie and a portfolio of related brands including Calming Blankets, Pup Naps, and Fydoo ([Forbes Australia](https://www.forbes.com.au/news/30-under-30/meet-davie-fogarty-australias-600-million-retail-shark/)).

Fogarty's public profile grew further after he joined Shark Tank Australia as an investor in 2023, returning for the 2024 season, a move that gave a direct-to-consumer operator unusual mainstream visibility in a market normally dominated by traditional retail and franchise pitches ([Yahoo Lifestyle Australia](https://au.lifestyle.yahoo.com/oodie-founder-and-shark-tank-judge-davie-fogarty-spills-on-reality-tv-debut-015101728.html)). He has also spoken publicly about scaling The Oodie to roughly $250 million in revenue within three years of launch, detailing the operational and marketing decisions behind that growth on industry podcasts ([Chronos Agency podcast](https://chronos.agency/podcast/frameworks-for-scaling-a-250m-dtc-business-in-3-years/)).

## What members actually get

The free course walks through the fundamentals of starting and growing an ecommerce brand: choosing the right product, building out a Shopify store, running Facebook and Google ad campaigns, and tracking profit rather than just top-line revenue. It includes a free store builder to help beginners get moving immediately rather than getting stuck on setup, and it's structured as ongoing weekly releases rather than a single upfront course dump.

Fogarty also runs a separate, paid offering called Daily Mentor for operators who've moved past the fundamentals, an ecommerce community aimed at people already running seven-figure-plus brands who want mentorship from others who've built to nine figures. The free Skool course functions as the entry point for anyone earlier in that journey.

## Why it's grown

The growth logic here doesn't need much explanation: Fogarty already had a public, verifiable success story before the course existed. A Forbes profile, a Shark Tank seat, and a real brand generating hundreds of millions in sales all pre-date the Skool community, and the course exists as a way to give back to (and capture the attention of) an audience that would otherwise pay for that same credibility signal elsewhere.

Making the course entirely free also removes the single biggest objection new ecommerce students have, which is not knowing whether the person teaching them has actually built something at scale. When the answer is independently verifiable through mainstream business press rather than just self-reported numbers, the free price point becomes a trust accelerator rather than a signal of lower quality.
