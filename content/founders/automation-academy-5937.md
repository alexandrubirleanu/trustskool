# Ardit Sulce and Vibe Coding Pro Club: Inside Skool's 2,800-Member AI Coding Community

Vibe Coding Pro Club charges $29 a month to guide members through building real applications with AI coding tools like Claude Code and Gemini CLI, from a blank project through databases and Stripe payments. It has reached roughly 2,789 members.

## Who runs it

The club is run by Ardit Sulce, whose Udemy instructor page credits him with teaching Python programming to more than 600,000 students over roughly a decade ([Udemy](https://www.udemy.com/user/adiune/)). His flagship course, the Python Mega Course, has enrolled more than 362,000 students and carries over 73,000 ratings at a 4.6-star average, making it one of the most reviewed Python courses on the platform, and it explicitly covers building real-world apps including AI agents with LangChain ([Udemy course](https://www.udemy.com/course/the-python-mega-course/)). Sulce holds a Master of Science in Geospatial Technologies from the University of Muenster in Germany, and before focusing on teaching, he worked on applied data projects including ecosystem mapping for the Center for Conservation Geography in Australia and drone image processing for the Swiss firm in-Terra. He also publishes the Daily Python Projects newsletter on Substack, describing it as the platform's most-read Python newsletter ([Substack](https://substack.com/@arditsulce)), and maintains a public GitHub organization hosting course materials for his teaching ([GitHub](https://github.com/arditsulceteaching/thepythonmegacourse)).

## What members actually get

The Skool community extends Sulce's teaching style, building complete, working applications rather than isolated syntax lessons, into the newer "vibe coding" workflow of building software primarily by directing AI coding assistants. Members are guided from a zero-code starting point through adding real infrastructure like databases and Stripe payment processing, mirroring the project-based structure that made his Udemy courses successful at scale.

## Why it's grown

Vibe coding communities on Skool are numerous, but most are run by people who only recently discovered AI coding tools themselves. Sulce brings a genuinely different starting point: a decade of measurable teaching results at Udemy scale, with hundreds of thousands of students and tens of thousands of public ratings serving as an independent, platform-verified track record rather than a self-reported claim. That teaching background, plus a real academic and applied technical resume behind the Python expertise, gives the $29 monthly price point a credibility anchor that newer AI-coding educators without a comparable teaching history don't have.
