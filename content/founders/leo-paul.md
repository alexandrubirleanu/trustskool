# Leo-Paul Klein: The French Stock Investor Behind Skool's Free Small Caps Community

Léo-Paul is a free, French-language Skool community with just over 3,100 members, built around one specific promise: free training on small cap stocks, company valuation, and options trading, all delivered by a single named creator rather than a team of contributors.

## Who is Leo-Paul Klein

Klein runs the investing-focused YouTube channel that shares his name, Léo-Paul, where he publishes deep-dive stock analysis videos, small cap "tier lists," and full breakdowns of his own portfolio moves ([Léo-Paul on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@LeoPaulKlein)). His videos aren't generic market commentary, they're built around specific, disclosed positions: one video walks through re-valuing his entire portfolio after a market move, another discloses a roughly 350,000 euro portfolio in detail, and another covers a 40,000 dollar allocation across two undervalued stocks.

He also runs a personal site, leopaulvalue.com, positioning himself under a value-investing banner distinct from his YouTube persona ([leopaulvalue.com](https://www.leopaulvalue.com/)). Between the channel and the community, he presents a consistent, name-attached investing identity rather than an anonymous "finance guru" account, which is relatively uncommon in a niche where a lot of creators operate under handles instead of their real names.

## What members actually get

Inside the Skool group, Klein offers what he describes as over five hours of exclusive, entirely free training, organized into modules that move from beginner to advanced level. A recurring feature is a proprietary checklist built around five categories and thirty-six criteria, the same framework he says he personally uses to screen small caps worth deeper research.

The stated goal is helping members become autonomous investors: able to read an annual report, value a company themselves, and understand options mechanics, rather than depending on someone else's stock picks.

## Why it's grown

Free access removes friction for retail investors who are curious about small cap investing but wary of paying upfront for a stranger's system, and Klein's YouTube channel, with videos analyzing specific, named stocks and disclosing his own real portfolio, gives the community a track record members can actually check against his public calls over time. That transparency, showing the portfolio rather than just claiming results, is a meaningfully different trust signal than most finance communities offer.

Small cap investing is also a niche where most free content online is either extremely surface-level or paywalled behind expensive newsletters, so a structured, free, criteria-based framework fills a real gap for French-speaking retail investors trying to go deeper than headline stock tips.

His video output is also unusually frequent for someone running a free community rather than selling a paid product, with new stock breakdowns and portfolio updates published on a regular cadence, which keeps the YouTube channel functioning as an ongoing top-of-funnel feed into the Skool group rather than a one-time introduction.
