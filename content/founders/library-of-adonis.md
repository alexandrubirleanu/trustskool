# Hamza Ahmed and Library of Adonis: The 2.3 Million-Subscriber Self-Improvement School on Skool

Library of Adonis is a $37-a-month Skool community with roughly 2,500 members, pitched as a school for men covering everything from discipline and fitness to "looksmaxing," dating, and business. Unlike many niche self-improvement Skool founders, Hamza Ahmed arrived with an already-massive audience: a YouTube channel with over 2.3 million subscribers built over more than a decade of content.

## Who is Hamza Ahmed

Ahmed was born June 10, 1997 in the UK, comes from a Pakistani family background, and now lives in Dubai. He studied Psychology, a background that shows up throughout his content's framing around mindset and behavior change. He started his YouTube channel on August 28, 2013, and has since built it to over 1,067 videos and more than 311 million total views, positioning him as a mentor to what's frequently cited as more than 3 million men across his platforms.

His content spans self-development, fitness, relationships, and making money online, generally aimed at a young male audience. Industry commentary on his growth, including a LinkedIn post analyzing how he scaled his Skool community, has specifically highlighted his existing YouTube reach as the engine behind that growth ([Sean Akyildiz, LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/seanakyildiz_how-hamza-adonis-scaled-his-skool-community-activity-7186802291260227584-3AGb)).

## What members actually get

Library of Adonis is structured as a full curriculum rather than a single course: step-by-step material on mindset, fitness, social skills, relationships, and career direction, plus a 24/7 accountability community, according to the platform's own positioning. Ahmed personally offers what's described as life audits, giving members direct plans covering body, money, dating, habits, and goals, a more hands-on layer than most self-improvement communities at this price point offer.

The pitch explicitly positions itself against "motivational fluff," aiming instead at concrete, trackable changes across the major areas of a young man's life rather than pure mindset content.

## Why it's grown

Ahmed's growth curve on Skool is best explained by an existing audience that most creators building a paid community from scratch simply don't have: over a decade of YouTube content and 300 million-plus views gave him a large, pre-qualified pool of viewers already familiar with his voice and framework before Skool became the paid layer on top of that free content. That's a fundamentally different growth path than a coach building trust from zero inside Skool's discovery feed.

The breadth of the curriculum, covering fitness, dating, money, and mindset together rather than a single narrow topic, also mirrors the range of subjects his YouTube channel already covers, making the paid community feel like a natural next step for viewers rather than a pivot into unfamiliar territory.
