# Elliott Pierret and IA France: Inside Skool's AI Community for French Speakers

IA France is a free Skool community with roughly 8,700 members, positioned as an exclusive space where French speakers can trade advice, news, and discussion about artificial intelligence. It's run by Elliott Pierret, an AI educator who built his audience by teaching everyday people how to use tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and Claude without any technical background.

## Who is Elliott Pierret

Elliott maintains an active public presence across YouTube, TikTok, and LinkedIn, where his profile lists professional experience at Microsoft ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/elliottpierret/)). His YouTube channel covers topics like Microsoft's AI strategy and deep dives into specific models such as Claude 4, aimed at demystifying enterprise and consumer AI tools for a general audience ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@elliottpierret)). According to the community's own description, he trained more than 12,000 people in 2024 alone and has built a YouTube following of over 50,000 subscribers.

He also sells standalone digital products built around the same teaching approach, including a ChatGPT-focused guide distributed through Gumroad, which extends the same "AI Master" positioning he uses across his free content ([Gumroad](https://elliottpierret.gumroad.com/l/ChatGPTMaster)). His YouTube output leans toward explainer-style videos on specific tools and companies, breaking down how a given AI system actually works rather than just listing prompts to copy, which sets his content apart from a lot of surface-level "AI hacks" channels.

## What members actually get

IA France centers on live discussion and shared resources rather than a single structured course: members get Elliott's running commentary on AI news, practical advice on integrating tools like ChatGPT and Claude into daily work, and a place to ask questions of both Elliott and each other. The framing throughout is accessibility, breaking down what can feel like a technical, gatekept field into something a total beginner can act on immediately.

## Why it's grown

Elliott's growth pattern mirrors a lot of successful AI-education Skool groups: a personal YouTube and TikTok following built first, with the free Skool community acting as the retention layer that turns one-off video viewers into a recurring audience he can talk to directly. His Microsoft background gives him a credibility hook that a purely self-taught AI creator wouldn't have, and the fact that AI tooling changes monthly means a community format, where he can post timely updates, fits the subject matter better than a static course would. At close to 8,700 members in a crowded French-language AI education space, IA France has become one of the more established communities in that niche.
