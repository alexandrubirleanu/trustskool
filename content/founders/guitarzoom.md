# Dan Denley, Steve Stine, and GuitarZoom: A Guitar Teaching Brand's Skool Classroom

GuitarZoom's Skool community has grown to about 3,428 members on the strength of a teaching brand that predates Skool by well over a decade. It's the online home for GuitarZoom.com, a guitar instruction business built around one instructor, Steve Stine, and founded by Dan Denley.

## Who is behind GuitarZoom

Dan Denley founded GuitarZoom.com after discovering Steve Stine's guitar lesson videos on YouTube and calling him directly to propose a partnership, according to a profile in FSHN Magazine ([FSHN Magazine](https://www.fshnmagazine.com/fshn-spotlight-steve-stine-guitar-zoom/)). That call turned Stine into the face and sole instructor of the platform starting around 2014.

Stine himself has a teaching résumé that's unusually deep: a bachelor's degree in music from Minnesota State University, more than 15 years as a Montessori music instructor, a stint as a professor at North Dakota State University, and, per the same profile, a private teaching practice that at its peak had him instructing more than 100 students a week in person before he ever filmed a course. His GuitarZoom YouTube channel has passed 500,000 subscribers, and Guitar World has covered his lesson series releases directly ([Guitar World](https://www.guitarworld.com/news/guitarzoom-introduces-play-songs-with-steve-stine-lesson-series)).

## What members actually get

Inside the Skool community, the pitch is "a community of guitarists who break through barriers, crush goals, and grow together," which functions as the free front door into GuitarZoom's much larger paid course library covering everything from beginner technique to genre-specific lead and rhythm playing. The Skool space hosts the Steve Stine Guitar Podcast, giving free members direct access to Stine rather than just funnel content pointing at a paywall.

## Why it's grown

GuitarZoom didn't need Skool to build an audience. It arrived with a YouTube channel already in the hundreds of thousands of subscribers and a founder-instructor pairing, Denley on the business side and Stine on teaching and camera work, refined since 2014. What Skool offered was a lower-friction community layer than GuitarZoom's own website, a place where students who already trust Stine's teaching style from YouTube can ask questions and get a reply from him or his team instead of just consuming another pre-recorded video. That's a common pattern among the more durable Skool communities: they're not building an audience from scratch, they're re-housing one that already existed, in this case one measured in decades of teaching and hundreds of thousands of subscribers.
