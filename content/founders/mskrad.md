# Dr. Christoph Agten and MSK Radiology School: A Fellowship-Trained Radiologist's Speed-Reporting Classroom

MSK Radiology School is a Skool community of roughly 3,700 radiologists and residents built around a narrow, practical promise: report musculoskeletal imaging studies faster without sacrificing diagnostic accuracy. It's run by PD Dr. Christoph Agten, a fellowship-trained musculoskeletal radiologist whose reporting method is documented in his own published books rather than just marketing copy.

## Who is Dr. Christoph Agten

Agten is the author of two books sold on Amazon, "SPEED - MSK Radiology: How to report more studies faster" and "The Confident Radiologist," both aimed at working radiologists rather than a general audience ([Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/SPEED-Radiology-report-studies-faster/dp/1074826930)). His personal site lists more than 40 peer-reviewed scientific publications to his name, a YouTube channel that has passed 33,000 subscribers, and a LinkedIn newsletter called "The Last Radiologist" with roughly 6,000 subscribers ([agten.org](https://www.agten.org/)). The same site states he has coached more than 400 radiologists through his SPEED method, and he separately runs a Virtual MSK Fellowship for radiologists working toward subspecialty expertise.

## What members actually get

Inside the free Skool community, the pitch matches the books: free MSK reporting templates and a "Calibration Masterclass" designed to help radiologists report 3.82 times faster, a specific figure Agten cites directly in the community's own description, without losing diagnostic accuracy. That's a narrower and more technical promise than most professional Skool communities make, aimed squarely at working and training radiologists.

## Why it's grown

Radiology is a field where credibility is unusually easy to check. Publications are indexed, board certifications are verifiable, and a "faster reporting" claim from someone without real clinical standing would get picked apart quickly by peers. Agten's community works because the claims are backed by a visible publication record and a specific, named methodology rather than vague promises of efficiency. For radiologists under real production pressure to report more studies per shift, a free community built around templates from someone with Agten's actual clinical and academic credentials is a much lower-risk source of workflow advice than a generic productivity course would be.
