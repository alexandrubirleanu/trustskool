# K.E. On The Track and Free Gold Level: Inside Skool's 2,700-Member Music Business Community

Free Gold Level Private Group promises something specific to independent musicians and producers: strategies for growing streams, fans, and income, taught by someone with an actual discography behind major-label records rather than a marketing consultant who's never placed a beat. The free community sits at roughly 2,769 members.

## Who is K.E. On The Track

K.E. On The Track is the stage name of Kevin Erondu, an American hip-hop producer born in Tampa, Florida and raised in Valdosta, Georgia, who has been active since 2008 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K.E._on_the_Track)). His break came in 2009, producing "Swag Surfin'" for Fast Life Yungstaz, which reached No. 62 on the Billboard Hot 100. From there his production credits expanded into a genuine major-label catalog: five tracks on Roscoe Dash's 2010 debut album including both singles, "You the Boss" for Rick Ross featuring Nicki Minaj in 2011, "Magic" on Future's debut album *Pluto* in 2012, "I'm So Blessed" on DJ Khaled's *Kiss the Ring*, "The Devil Is a Lie" on Rick Ross's *Mastermind* in 2013, and work on Fetty Wap's "Jimmy Choo" in 2016 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K.E._on_the_Track)). He received a Grammy nomination for co-producing Tamar Braxton's *Love and War* album in 2014, and in January 2015 he founded Beat Mechanics Entertainment LLC to sign and develop artists and producers of his own.

## What members actually get

The community is framed around teaching independent artists the mechanics of growing an audience and turning that audience into income, drawing on lessons from a career that ran through the major-label system rather than around it. Given Erondu's own history, that positioning has real weight: he's someone who built a catalog of charting, radio-play records for artists on major labels, then built his own label and publishing entity after seeing that side of the business from the inside.

## Why it's grown

Music business education on Skool tends to split into two camps: aspiring producers teaching from theory, and working producers with genuinely limited time to run a community at all. K.E. On The Track's discography, spanning Billboard Hot 100 entries, a Grammy nomination, and production work for artists like Rick Ross, Future, and DJ Khaled, is independently documented rather than resting on his own claims, which sets a credibility floor that's rare in this specific niche. A free entry point also fits the audience: independent musicians and producers are often early in their careers and cash-constrained, and a no-cost community removes the barrier to accessing insight from someone whose records actually charted, before any paid upsell (Beat Mechanics signings, production mentorship, or similar) enters the picture.
