# David Drazil and Sketch Like an Architect: A Trained Architect Teaching the World to Draw

Sketch Like an Architect is a free Skool community with roughly 4,380 members positioned as a hub for people who want to build sketching confidence and rediscover the habit of drawing by hand. It's run by David Drazil, a trained architect who's spent close to a decade turning architectural sketching instruction into a full teaching business.

## Who is David Drazil

Drazil studied and worked as an architect across the Czech Republic and Denmark before shifting into content and education. He began publishing sketching tutorials in 2017, and his own site says that work has since grown into a following of more than 350,000 fans of sketching and drawing across platforms ([sketchlikeanarchitect.com/about](https://www.sketchlikeanarchitect.com/about)). He's published three books on architectural sketching, including "Sketch Like an Architect: Step-by-Step From Lines to Perspective," which is listed for sale on Amazon under his name ([Amazon listing](https://www.amazon.com/Sketch-Like-Architect-Step-Step/dp/8090762808)), and he's been profiled by Archipreneur on how he turned a niche architectural skill into a teaching business ([Archipreneur profile](https://archipreneur.com/architectural-sketching-david-drazil/)). His site describes the origin story behind the project in his own words in a dedicated post explaining why he started Sketch Like an Architect ([sketchlikeanarchitect.com blog](https://www.sketchlikeanarchitect.com/blog/-why-i-started-sketch-like-an-architect)).

## What members actually get

Per the community's own description, Sketch Like an Architect functions as a go-to hub for anyone who wants to grow as a sketcher, whether that's an architecture student, a working designer, or a total beginner picking up a pen for the first time. The broader Sketch Like an Architect platform, which the Skool community feeds into, includes over 15,000 students across Drazil's paid online courses, plus sketching retreats and other in-person events for more advanced students.

## Why it's grown

Drazil's advantage is credibility that's hard to fake: he's an actual working architect teaching a skill that's core to the profession, not a hobbyist who picked up drawing and decided to monetize it. That professional grounding, combined with a decade of consistent content since 2017 and three published books, gives Sketch Like an Architect a level of authority that's rare in a fairly crowded online drawing-instruction space. The free Skool community functions as the entry point into that ecosystem, letting new sketchers sample the teaching style before deciding whether to invest in the paid courses or retreats.
