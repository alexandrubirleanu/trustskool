# Sabine Lackner and Learn to Draw With Pastels: Inside Skool's 2,700-Member Art Community

Learn to Draw With Pastels is a free Skool community built around beginner-friendly animal and nature drawing lessons, and it's reached roughly 2,718 members without charging for entry.

## Who is Sabine Lackner

The community is run by Sabine Lackner, an Austrian animal portrait artist who has spent more than 20 years drawing realistic animals with pastel pencils ([Sabine Lackner Fine Art](https://sabinelacknerfineart.com/about-new/)). She runs a long-running YouTube channel under her own name, where she posts step-by-step tutorials on drawing subjects like cat portraits, giraffe fur, parrot feathers, and skies with pastel pencils ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCpRJoXnu4Flq3VlRbTsQxXQ/videos)), and maintains an active Facebook page under Sabine Lackner Fine Art with the same body of tutorial content ([Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/SabineLacknerFineArtcom/)). Her personal site lists paid courses and classes alongside the free content, describing her as an award-winning pet portrait artist ([Sabine Lackner Fine Art](https://sabinelacknerfineart.com/courses-classes/)).

## What members actually get

The community's own description frames it as a space to discover the joy of pastel drawing through free animal and nature lessons, tips, and resources aimed at helping beginners grow their confidence rather than jumping straight into advanced technique. That matches the format visible across her YouTube catalog: short, focused tutorials on a single subject or technique, drawing a giraffe's fur, a cat's face, parrot feathers, a bright sky, rather than long-form courses, which suits people testing out pastel drawing for the first time. Her own site lists paid multi-day workshops built around a single subject, such as a four-day cat portrait class, positioned as the next step for people who've worked through the free community content first.

## Why it's grown

Skool's fitness and business categories are crowded, but hobbyist art communities are thinner, and pastel drawing specifically, as opposed to general sketching or digital art, is a narrow enough niche that Lackner faces relatively little direct competition on the platform. Two decades of consistent output under one name across her own website, YouTube, and Facebook gives newcomers an unusually long track record to evaluate before joining, compared to art instructors who show up on Skool with no prior public teaching history. Free access also matches how most people discover a hobby like pastel drawing: through a single tutorial video that solves an immediate problem (how do you get fur or feathers to look realistic), with the Skool community positioned as the next step for people who want ongoing feedback rather than a one-off video.
