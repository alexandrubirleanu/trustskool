# Michael Baumgartner and the Zebracat AI Community: An AI Video Startup's Skool Classroom

The Zebracat AI Community is a free Skool group with roughly 2,250 members, positioned around mastering AI video creation, growing an audience, and driving revenue, with a support email (support@zebracat.ai) built right into the group description. The company's own about page names Michael Baumgartner as the one who set it up.

## Who is Michael Baumgartner

Baumgartner is the CEO and co-founder of Zebracat AI, a company whose product converts text or audio into fully edited marketing videos. According to his Crunchbase profile, this is his second venture as a founder: before Zebracat, he was CEO and co-founder of Augmento, a social-analytics platform built for quantitative traders ([Crunchbase profile](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/michael-baumgartner-f160); [Augmento company page](https://www.f6s.com/company/augmento)). He holds a master's degree in Organisational Innovation & Entrepreneurship from Copenhagen Business School, completed between 2014 and 2016. He's active discussing AI video and startup building on X under the handle @baumgartnerMG ([X profile](https://x.com/baumgartnerMG)) and on LinkedIn ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/michael-baumgartner/)).

## What members actually get

Per the group's own description, members get access to exclusive content and events tied to Zebracat's AI video tooling, framed around three outcomes: mastering AI video creation, growing a following, and turning that into revenue. Unlike many solo-creator Skool groups, support here routes through a company inbox rather than a personal DM, a small but telling sign that this is a product community first, built to onboard and retain paying users of the underlying software rather than to sell a course or a personal brand.

## Why it's grown

This community's growth pattern looks different from most creator-led Skool groups. Rather than an individual's personal audience feeding into the group, membership here scales alongside Zebracat's own customer acquisition as a funded AI video-generation startup. Baumgartner's track record as a repeat founder, having already built and run Augmento before this, likely helped him raise and operate with the kind of resourcing that supports running both a SaaS product and its own dedicated Skool-based education layer simultaneously. His formal training in organisational innovation and entrepreneurship at Copenhagen Business School also shows up in how the company frames its own founding story, describing Zebracat as born out of the frustration of having to create large volumes of video content for his own previous ventures. It's a useful reminder that not every well-run Skool community is a solo creator's side project, some are customer-success functions for venture-backed software companies wearing a community wrapper.
