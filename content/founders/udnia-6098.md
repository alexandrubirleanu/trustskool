# Rodrigo Alfaro and UDNIA: The Dubai-Based Founder Behind a Skool Games Winning Community

UDNIA calls itself the largest digital community in the Spanish-speaking world, teaching artificial intelligence applied to real business use cases. At $49 a month with just over 3,070 members, it's run by Rodrigo Alfaro, based in Dubai.

## Who is Rodrigo Alfaro

Alfaro documents his UDNIA business publicly under the handle rodrigo.alfaro.s on Instagram, where he posts member success stories and progress updates from inside the community ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/rodrigo.alfaro.s/)). UDNIA's own website, udniadigital.com, describes itself as "la primera universidad de Inteligencia Artificial aplicada," the first applied AI university, and positions Alfaro as the figure behind that brand ([UDNIA](https://udniadigital.com/)). A YouTube video titled "$100,000 dólares en SKOOL, Primeros latinos en ganar los Skool games," walks through his account of becoming one of the first Latin American operators to win a Skool Games cup, and he's referenced elsewhere as a three-time Skool Games champion ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_IkZgBE3k00)).

Skool Games is Skool's own recognized competition for community operators, so an externally awarded track record there functions as a harder-to-fake credential than most self-reported "top creator" claims in the space.

## What members actually get

UDNIA teaches two main paths: social automation, aimed at monetizing YouTube, TikTok, and Facebook through AI and automated systems, and a separate Skool-specific track that teaches members how to launch their own community in under a day using AI tools. Alfaro teaches alongside ten additional instructors, and the community includes two books offered as bonuses covering the same curriculum.

## Why it's grown

Winning Skool's own competition multiple times gives Alfaro a form of validation that's difficult to manufacture, it's judged by the platform itself rather than self-reported by the creator, and that credibility likely carries real weight with Spanish-speaking creators evaluating which "how to build a Skool community" teacher to trust. Teaching both a general AI-monetization path and a Skool-specific path inside the same community also lets UDNIA capture two overlapping but distinct audiences, people chasing AI income broadly and people specifically trying to replicate his own community-building results.

For an underserved Spanish-speaking creator market, a recognized, competition-validated operator based in a major business hub like Dubai gives UDNIA a credibility edge over the many unverified "AI guru" accounts targeting the same audience.

The two-path structure also means UDNIA isn't purely dependent on the Skool niche staying popular. If interest in "how to build a Skool community" cools off, the broader social automation track built around YouTube, TikTok, and Facebook monetization still gives Alfaro a durable reason for members to stay, which is a more resilient setup than a single-topic community tied entirely to one platform's trend cycle.
