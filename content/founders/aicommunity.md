# Atul Kumar and K21Academy: The Cloud Educator Behind Skool's AI, Data & Cloud Community

AI, Data & Cloud @ K21Academy is a free Skool community that has crossed 3,000 members, pitched simply as the place to learn AI, Data, DevOps, and Cloud skills toward certification and higher paying jobs. It's the Skool arm of K21Academy, a cloud training platform run by Atul Kumar, who is credited as the community's founder directly on its Skool about page.

## Who is Atul Kumar

Atul Kumar is described on K21Academy's own about page as a Multi-Cloud and DevOps expert and published author who built the academy after watching talented people struggle to break into or advance in tech careers because they lacked practical, hands-on experience rather than theoretical knowledge ([K21Academy About Us](https://k21academy.com/about-us/)). That observation, according to the company's own account, is what pushed him to combine theoretical teaching with mentorship, community support, and outcomes tied to real jobs.

He maintains an active LinkedIn presence under the handle tied to K21Academy, posting bootcamp invitations and cloud certification content aimed at professionals moving into Azure, AWS, and multi-cloud DevOps roles ([Atul Kumar on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/atulk21academy_free-bootcamp-invite-multi-cloud-devops-activity-7150427616578002945-DqnB)). K21Academy itself has built a substantial public track record around his teaching, with the company citing partnerships as an Oracle Gold, Microsoft Silver, and DevOps Institute registered partner alongside a large base of certified learners.

## What members actually get

The Skool community is framed as the free layer of a larger paid training business, K21Academy runs structured, fee based bootcamps and courses in AWS, Azure, GCP, Kubernetes, Terraform, and DevOps, and the free group functions as both a lead-in to those programs and a standalone resource for people early in a cloud or AI career. Inside, the promise is straightforward, a path to industry certifications and job readiness without requiring a coding background or prior tech experience.

That structure, free community plus paid bootcamp, mirrors how K21Academy operates its broader business, which has run since well before Skool existed and has built its reputation primarily through certification prep and hands-on labs rather than community discussion alone.

## Why it's grown

K21Academy's advantage here is that Kumar isn't building an audience from scratch inside Skool, he's routing an existing training business, with over a decade of certification courses and reported reach across multiple social platforms, into a free discovery layer. For a topic as fast-moving and certification hungry as cloud and AI skills, a free community that doubles as a funnel into paid bootcamps is a natural fit, and it lets K21Academy capture people who are cloud curious before they've committed to a specific paid path.

The niche itself, practical AI, data, and cloud certification training, is also one of the most in-demand corners of tech education right now, which gives a founder with an established teaching brand like Kumar's a real head start over newer, unproven cloud educators launching cold on Skool.
