# Kia Ghasem and AI Marketing by Kia: Inside Skool's 21,000-Member AI Automation Community

AI Marketing by Kia is a $1-a-month Skool community with roughly 21,000 members focused on teaching no-code AI automation using n8n. It's run by Kia Ghasem, an Australia-based co-founder of an enterprise AI automation company, which gives the community a somewhat different flavor from many creator-led automation groups: the person teaching it also runs a company selling the same category of service to businesses directly.

## Who is Kia Ghasem

Kia Ghasem is the co-founder of Kamexa, an AI automation company founded in 2023 that builds custom AI agents for enterprise clients ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kia-ghasem/); [Kamexa AI author page](https://kamexa.ai/author/kia-ghasem/)). He holds a Bachelor of Actuarial Studies and Computer Science, with a focus in Artificial Intelligence, from UNSW (the University of New South Wales), and has past involvement with the UNSW Artificial Intelligence Society. He's based in Australia and has spoken publicly about his approach to AI agencies and client work, including an appearance on the "Fueled By Progress" podcast discussing "the truth about AI agencies, saying 'no' to clients, and building for scale" ([Fueled By Progress, Ep. 92](https://fueledbyprogress.com/episodes/kia-ghasem-the-truth-about-ai-agencies-saying-no-to-clients-and-building-for-scale-ep-92)).

That combination, actuarial and computer science training plus a live enterprise AI agency, is a more technically credentialed background than is typical among Skool's AI automation educators, many of whom come from marketing or agency backgrounds rather than formal computer science training.

## What members actually get

The community centers on n8n, the open-source workflow automation tool that has become the default platform for no-code AI agent building across the Skool AI-automation niche. Members get access to what the community describes as "n8n automation templates that actually work," valued in its own materials at more than $50,000 worth of template work, beginner-friendly tutorials for getting started with automation, a network of other builders, team-based competitions, and a short weekly two-minute AI news digest to keep members current on a fast-moving tool landscape. Ghasem also promotes a separate "AI Automation Accelerator" program aimed at helping members launch an agency within 28 days, positioned as the next step up from the $1/month community.

## Why it's grown

The $1/month price point is about as low a barrier to entry as a paid Skool community can have, and it functions less as meaningful revenue and more as a qualification filter (distinguishing people willing to enter payment details from pure lurkers) while keeping the top of the funnel as wide as possible. That's a common structure among AI automation communities specifically, where the real business model tends to sit in higher-ticket coaching or agency-building programs sold to community members later, rather than in the community subscription itself.

Ghasem's operating enterprise AI business (Kamexa) also gives the community a source of real, current client work to draw examples and templates from, rather than relying purely on hypothetical or toy use cases — a meaningful differentiator in a niche where a lot of automation education content is built by people who've never actually delivered automation work for a paying business client.
