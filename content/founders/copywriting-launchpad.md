# Matthew Volkwyn, Nabeel Azeez, and Copywriting Launchpad: Inside Skool's $5 Copy-Skills Hub

Copywriting Launchpad is a Skool community of roughly 5,447 members charging $5 a month for what its own tagline calls a place "where copywriters launch their careers and master high-paying copywriting skills." Rather than one guru figure, it's co-run by two working copywriters: Matthew Volkwyn and Nabeel Azeez.

## Who is Matthew Volkwyn

Volkwyn runs his teaching business out of Japan, where he built two flagship offers, Copy Systems and Copy Dojo, alongside his Skool presence ([Skool profile](https://www.skool.com/@matthewvolkwyn)). The story he tells about his own path, retold in a YouTube video titled "From Cleaning Toilets To Millionaire Copywriter: Matthew Volkwyn's Story," traces a route from a minimum-wage job at Tim Hortons to what an independent course-review site describes as "multiple six-figure businesses," built on client work that reportedly "generated tens of millions in sales" ([Scam Risk review](https://www.scamrisk.com/copywriting-systems-review-is-matthew-volkwyn-legit/); [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p0bcRpd3LNg)). His LinkedIn profile lists him as a copywriter and marketer working with personality-driven brands ([LinkedIn](https://ca.linkedin.com/in/matthewvolkwyn)).

## Who is Nabeel Azeez

Azeez works out of Dubai as a direct response copywriter. His own site lists three active ventures: the agency Dropkick Copy, a training program called CopySkills built for copywriters in the Global South, and a media brand called MuslimMan ([nabeelazeez.com](https://www.nabeelazeez.com/)). He's appeared on The Copywriter Club's podcast to talk through what he calls the "Troll Framework" for cutting through inbox noise ([TCC Podcast #116](https://thecopywriterclub.com/troll-framework-nabeel-azeez/)), and his site carries endorsements from other established copywriters, including one from Stefan Georgi ranking him "in the top 10% of copywriters in the world." On Medium, he describes himself, among other things, as a "1-0-1 White Collar Boxer" ([Medium](https://medium.com/@NabeelAzeez/about)).

## What members actually get

At $5 a month, the price signals volume over exclusivity. This isn't a high-ticket mastermind, it's a low-friction entry point meant to get copywriters moving on fundamentals before anything bigger gets pitched. Given the description's focus on launching careers and high-paying skills, the likely funnel runs from Launchpad into Volkwyn's paid Copy Systems curriculum and Azeez's CopySkills program, two higher-priced products both founders already run independently outside Skool.

## Why it's grown

Neither founder built this audience from a standing start on Skool. Volkwyn arrived with a public rags-to-riches narrative and an existing paid course business, and Azeez arrived with a running agency, a training brand, and a network of recognized copywriters willing to publicly vouch for him. Pairing two operators with separate followings, instead of relying on one, effectively doubles the organic top-of-funnel feeding a $5/month community, a price low enough that neither founder needs it to carry real revenue on its own. It's a relationship-builder that points toward their pricier flagship products.
