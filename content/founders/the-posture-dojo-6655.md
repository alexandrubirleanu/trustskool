# Sam Miller and The Posture Dojo: Healing an 85-Degree Spine Curve Without Surgery

The Posture Dojo Community is a $11-a-month Skool community with just over 4,000 members, centered on posture correction, scoliosis, and what its founder calls a "practice" rather than a quick fix. Live sessions rotate through specific, unusual topics for a fitness community, things like the psychosomatic side of posture and whether a curve is really aging or just stuck fascia.

## Who is Sam Miller

Sam Miller is credited as the founder on the community's Skool About page, and he's built a public media presence around his own posture story under the name The Flowwolf, hosting a podcast called "The Posture Dojo with Sam Miller" that's been running long enough to attract guest experts and cross-promotion from other practitioners in the space ([Spotify for Creators](https://creators.spotify.com/pod/profile/flowwolf); [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/the_flowwolf/); [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@SamFlowwolF)).

His personal story is the foundation of the brand: a near-drowning accident left him with a smashed T10 vertebra that gradually developed into an 85-degree kypho-scoliosis curve. Unable to undergo surgery, he spent years working through breathing exercises, tailored flexibility work, and coordination and strength-building routines aimed at healing the condition without an operation, eventually gaining back roughly two inches of height in the process, a story detailed in a guest feature on another posture practitioner's blog ([Heal Your Posture](https://healyourposture.com/blog/2024/9/5/posture-dojo-podcast-with-sam-miller-84mdl)).

## What members actually get

Inside the Posture Dojo Community, the live sessions are structured around working through material together in real time rather than passively consuming pre-recorded lessons. Topics cycle through both physical and less conventional angles on posture, including sessions explicitly framed around listening to the body and psychosomatic perspectives on why posture problems develop in the first place, alongside more standard technical content on fascia and structural changes tied to aging.

That mix, technical bodywork plus a more mind-body framing, reflects Miller's own recovery process, which by his account involved more than pure mechanical correction.

## Why it's grown

The Posture Dojo's growth traces directly back to Miller's own recovery story functioning as proof of concept. An 85-degree curve is a serious structural condition, and demonstrating meaningful improvement without surgery gives the community an origin story that's difficult for a generic posture-correction program to match. His podcast has also served as a natural audience-building tool ahead of and alongside the Skool community, letting him build trust with listeners over time through guest interviews and case discussions before ever asking anyone to join a paid space.

At $11 a month, the pricing is also low enough to remove most of the friction for someone curious about his approach, which likely helps convert podcast listeners into paying community members at a higher rate than a more expensive offer would.
