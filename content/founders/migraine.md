# Kevin Wissman and Tame The Migraine: Inside Skool's Root-Cause Migraine Community

Tame The Migraine Course is a $97 one-time-payment Skool community with roughly 2,245 members, built around a specific promise: stop managing migraine symptoms and instead retrain the nervous system that produces them. The person behind it is Kevin Wissman, a pharmacist who spent two decades on the other side of the same problem before turning his recovery into a teaching practice.

## Who is Kevin Wissman

Kevin Wissman holds a Doctor of Pharmacy degree and is a Board Certified Pharmacist Specialist, alongside training as an Empowered Relief instructor ([kevinwissman.com](https://www.kevinwissman.com/)). By his own account he battled severe migraines for more than 20 years, a stretch he describes bluntly on his site: "the relentless pain, isolation, and disruptions to work and family led me to a very dark place of depression and even suicidal thoughts." He says he eventually found his way out through what he calls perseverance and a deliberate search for solutions, and now works full time helping other migraine sufferers do the same.

He operates his coaching business through an entity called Tame The Pain LLC, and has talked through his recovery approach in outside conversations as well, including a podcast appearance discussing a mind-body approach to becoming migraine-free ([Life with Purpose podcast episode, Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/episode/2nXbWXLFyvENJuN0PVwcju)). His public content lives on Instagram under the Tame The Migraine name ([@tame.the.migraine](https://www.instagram.com/tame.the.migraine/)), where he posts the same nervous-system-focused material that anchors the paid course.

## What members actually get

For $97, paid once rather than monthly, members get a structured curriculum rather than a loose collection of tips: modules on migraine foundations, gut health, hormones, sleep, and genetics, all framed around the idea that migraines are downstream of a dysregulated nervous system rather than something to be white-knuckled through with medication alone. That framing draws directly on Wissman's clinical background as a pharmacist, layered with the pain-neuroscience and brain-retraining language that runs through his personal site. The community format adds a peer layer on top of the curriculum, letting members compare notes with others working through the same root-cause approach rather than going it alone.

## Why it's grown

The growth logic here is fairly legible. Chronic migraine is a condition millions of people manage for years without ever getting a satisfying answer from a ten-minute doctor's visit, and Wissman is selling specificity: not "wellness" in general, but a pharmacist's take on why the standard advice keeps failing. A one-time $97 charge is a low-friction way to test that promise compared to ongoing specialist visits or a subscription app, and his own multi-decade migraine history gives him a credibility angle that a purely academic expert wouldn't have. Pairing a clinical credential (PharmD, board certification) with a lived, specific, and fairly dark personal story is the kind of combination that tends to convert skeptical, symptom-fatigued buyers who have already tried the generic advice and are looking for someone who has actually been where they are.
