# Dusan Cvetkovic and How to Rhino: Teaching 37,000 Architects Parametric Design

Rhino and Grasshopper are two of the most widely used tools in computational architecture, and also two of the most frustrating for newcomers to learn from documentation alone. How to Rhino built a free community of over 37,000 architects around closing that gap, with a paid tier layered on top for people who want structured courses and live feedback.

## Who is Dusan Cvetkovic

Cvetkovic co-founded How to Rhino alongside his partner Lazar, and works as a practicing architect with more than 13 years of hands-on experience using Rhino across international projects, including consulting and freelance work in Poland, Italy, Portugal, and Denmark. How to Rhino operates as an Authorized Rhino Training Center, a status granted by Robert McNeel & Associates, the company behind Rhino, which places the school inside an official training network rather than an independent third-party course ([howtorhino.com](https://howtorhino.com/)).

The site credits the team with training more than 886 students through structured paid courses, on top of the free Skool community, and Cvetkovic maintains a public presence on Instagram and X under his own name and handle, @dusan_cv, where he shares Rhino and Grasshopper work directly ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/howtorhino/)).

## What members actually get

The free Skool community functions as daily office hours: members ask questions, get resources shared in response, and interact directly with the How to Rhino team rather than working through a static archive. For people ready to go further, the paid side of the business includes dedicated Rhino and Grasshopper courses for architects, along with monthly workshops, mini courses, live Q&A sessions, and project reviews where members get direct feedback on their own work.

The instructional focus stays specific to architecture rather than general 3D modeling, which matters in a tool as broad as Rhino: a lot of official documentation and generic tutorials are written for product design or industrial use cases that don't map cleanly onto architectural workflows.

## Why it's grown

The Authorized Rhino Training Center status is doing real work here. In a niche where credentials are unusually easy to fake, an official designation from the software's own maker gives How to Rhino a level of institutional trust that a purely self-taught YouTube channel doesn't have, even a very good one. Combined with over a decade of Cvetkovic's own project experience across several countries, the community reads as a genuine professional resource rather than a side hustle built on top of a hobby.

The free Skool tier also lowers the risk of trying the teaching style before paying for a full course, which matters in a field like architecture where practitioners are often cautious about spending on training that might not translate to their specific software version or workflow.
