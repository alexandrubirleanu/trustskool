# Loïc Schnekenburger and Glute Games' Club: Inside Skool's 5,700-Member French Fitness Community

Glute Games' Club is a free Skool community of about 5,730 members, built as a virtual gym dedicated specifically to women training glutes, with a program structured around three weekly sessions and a focus on lean muscle building rather than general weight loss. It's run by a sibling pair of certified trainers, with Loïc Schnekenburger as the community's named founder.

## Who is Loïc Schnekenburger

Loïc Schnekenburger's Skool profile is directly credited on the Glute Games' Club about page, alongside his sister Pauline, both described there as certified coaches ([Glute Games' Club on Skool](https://www.skool.com/glute-games-club/about)). Independent of Skool, he's listed as a Head Trainer at Basic-Fit, a major European gym chain, on the company's own site, which documents his professional qualifications, including a DEUG and Licence in STAPS, France's official sports science degree track ([Basic-Fit trainer profile](https://www.basic-fit.com/fr-fr/fitness/personal-support/details?trainerid=loic-schnekenburger&clubid=8a3b45312f894b8fa7d26292ab568f2b)). He also runs his own site, musculation-fessiers.com, dedicated specifically to glute training content ([musculation-fessiers.com](https://musculation-fessiers.com/)), and the sibling duo posts training content together under the Instagram handle @glute_games ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/glute_games/)).

Having a Basic-Fit staff listing, independent of anything he says about himself, is a meaningfully stronger credential check than most fitness-coaching Skool founders offer, since it comes from an employer verifying his role rather than from his own marketing.

## What members actually get

Glute Games' Club is free to join and offers a new training program each month built around three glute-focused sessions per week, drawing on more than 150 documented glute exercises according to the siblings' own program description. The Skool community functions as a free version of their broader Glute Games coaching brand, with members getting workout programming, technique guidance, and content featuring both Loïc and Pauline directly demonstrating exercises.

The program's specificity, targeting one muscle group with real training-science backing rather than a generic "get fit" promise, matches Loïc's formal STAPS training background, and gives members a level of technical precision that a general fitness influencer without that credential typically can't offer.

## Why it's grown

At 5,730 members and free to join, Glute Games' Club benefits from a founder whose credentials are checkable through an actual employer, Basic-Fit, rather than resting solely on self-reported expertise. The sibling structure also broadens the coaching's appeal: having both a brother and sister presenting the content together, with client testimonials describing specific measurable results, gives the community a relatable, family-run feel that's distinct from the typical solo fitness-influencer model.
