# Elliott Hulse and Alpha School: Inside Skool's Closed-Door Community for 'Alpha' Men

Alpha School is a free Skool community with roughly 2,400 members that isn't currently taking new sign-ups. Its own description tells prospective members to "hit the join button to get notification when enrollment opens again," which is an unusual growth mechanic on a platform where most groups are begging for members. Despite the name, this has nothing to do with the AI-powered private school chain founded by MacKenzie Price that's been in the news lately. This Alpha School is run by Elliott Hulse, and it's built for "busy, high performing men who are ready to shred fat, get jacked and be Alpha for life."

## Who is Elliott Hulse

Hulse is a strongman-turned-YouTuber whose career in public fitness content goes back nearly two decades. He founded Strength Camp in 2007 as a training system built around becoming "the strongest version of themselves," not just physically but through meditation and self-knowledge, and the following year he was crowned Florida's Strongest Man in a 2009 title run ([Greatest Physiques bio](https://www.greatestphysiques.com/male-physiques/elliott-hulse/)). His YouTube channel grew into one of the platform's biggest fitness destinations, passing 2.5 million subscribers and 360 million views, which earned him a "YouTube Fan's Favorite" award in 2015 ([channel stats via us.youtubers.me](https://us.youtubers.me/strength-camp/youtuber-stats)).

His public arc has shifted over the years from pure strength coaching toward a broader self-improvement message. In an interview with strength-sports outlet BarBend, Hulse traces that shift back to a 21-day Osho active meditation retreat he attended in New York during a period of job loss and depression, which he describes as producing "a complete 180" in his life, and which later became the seed for his "Grounding Camp" retreats blending bioenergetics, philosophy and active meditation ([BarBend interview](https://barbend.com/elliott-hulse-interview/)).

## What members actually get

The community's pitch is narrow and direct: fat loss, physique transformation, and what it calls Alpha habits, positioned as "the ONLY place to access Elliott Hulse off YouTube." That framing, access to Hulse himself rather than a generic curriculum, is presumably why the group can afford to close its doors once it fills up rather than chase unlimited growth.

## Why it's grown

Hulse didn't need to build an audience from scratch to fill this community. He already had one of the largest fitness followings on YouTube, cultivated since 2007 through Strength Camp, and Skool became the members-only layer sitting on top of content he'd already been publishing for free for years. The closed-enrollment model reinforces that scarcity: rather than an open funnel, Alpha School behaves more like a waitlist for a creator whose main audience lives elsewhere. That's a pattern worth watching for on Skool generally, creators with a large off-platform following tend to treat their community as a capped, high-touch add-on rather than a lead-generation machine.
