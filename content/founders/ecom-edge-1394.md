# Nishkarsh Sharma, Ishan Suri, and Ecom Edge Accelerator: India's E-Commerce Classroom on Skool

Ecom Edge Accelerator is a Skool community of roughly 3,563 members built around a specific, national pitch: teaching Indian entrepreneurs to build profitable e-commerce and dropshipping businesses. It's run by Nishkarsh Sharma and Ishan Suri, two Indian e-commerce operators who built their own businesses before turning to teaching.

## Who runs Ecom Edge

Sharma has worked in e-commerce full time since 2017, starting with dropshipping t-shirts from China to the US before founding Kalmin Ross, an India-based international clothing brand his company's own site credits with more than ₹11 crores in revenue ([ecomedge.in](https://ecomedge.in/our-mission/)). He has taught e-commerce since 2019 and, per the same page, has built a combined following of more than 900,000 across YouTube and Instagram. Independent review sites like GrowthNavigate and StartupBooted refer to him by name as one of the two operators behind EcomEdge, walking through the accelerator's curriculum and student outcomes in detail ([GrowthNavigate](https://www.growthnavigate.com/ecom-edge-accelerator-review); [StartupBooted](https://www.startupbooted.com/nishkarsh-sharma-ecomedge-accelerator-review)).

Suri has run e-commerce full time since 2021, starting with global dropshipping before moving into the Indian direct-to-consumer space. Ecom Edge's own site credits his ventures with more than ₹35 crores in gross e-commerce revenue and says he has scaled D2C operations to roughly ₹1.8 crore a month. The two have taught together since 2021, and the company's own materials say their combined students have generated more than ₹120 crores in revenue since 2019.

## What members actually get

The Skool community functions as the entry point into Ecom Edge's broader accelerator program, which positions itself as helping entrepreneurs move from dropshipping into more defensible direct-to-consumer brands. Members get community discussion, mentorship touchpoints, and training built around the constraints and opportunities of the Indian e-commerce market specifically, rather than generic Shopify advice aimed at a US audience.

## Why it's grown

Ecom Edge's pitch works because Sharma and Suri are teaching a market they operate in themselves, India-focused D2C and dropshipping, rather than repackaging US-centric e-commerce advice for an Indian audience. Both founders point to specific, named businesses they built, Kalmin Ross for Sharma among them, rather than only citing aggregate revenue figures, and independent review sites naming both founders add a layer of third-party visibility that's uncommon in the crowded "ecom guru" space. For entrepreneurs specifically targeting India's market, that combination of local market experience and named, checkable track records is a meaningfully different pitch than most Skool e-commerce communities make.
