# Joshua Sickenberger and the Homeostasis Diet: A Paid Health Community Built on 1.1 Million Followers

Homeostasis Diet is a $47-a-month Skool community of roughly 3,426 members organized around teaching a specific eating and lifestyle framework its creator calls the Homeostasis Diet. It's run by Joshua Sickenberger, whose LinkedIn describes him as the "Creator of the Homeostasis Diet" with more than 1.1 million followers across social platforms ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joshua-sickenberger-395080182/)).

## Who is Joshua Sickenberger

Sickenberger's own account of his background centers on more than a decade building marketing agencies before turning that audience-building skill set toward health content. According to his LinkedIn profile, he spent 11-plus years building multiple marketing agencies, launching eCommerce brands, and developing sales and funnel systems for other businesses before growing the Homeostasis Diet brand into an account with more than a million followers. He posts regularly under the handle @usernamejosh on Instagram ([Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/usernamejosh/)) and runs a dedicated YouTube channel under the Homeostasis Diet name ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@HomeostasisDiet)). His official site, homeostasisdiet.com, hosts the brand's community and course content ([homeostasisdiet.com](https://homeostasisdiet.com/)).

## What members actually get

The paid Skool tier gives members structured access to the Homeostasis Diet framework itself, which the brand's own materials describe as built around gut, genomic, and blood testing rather than generic calorie or macro tracking. The community sits alongside Homeostasis Diet University, a separate course library covering fasting and other protocol-specific modules, and members get ongoing access to Sickenberger directly rather than just the free content he posts to social platforms.

## Why it's grown

Sickenberger's path mirrors a pattern that shows up repeatedly among the more durable paid Skool communities: a founder who already knew how to build and monetize an audience, in his case from more than a decade running marketing agencies, applying that skill set to a specific health niche rather than starting from zero as a health creator first. The 1.1-million-follower social presence he built under the Homeostasis Diet brand functions as the top of a funnel that the $47-a-month Skool community then monetizes directly, converting free-content followers into paying members rather than relying on Skool itself to generate the initial audience.
