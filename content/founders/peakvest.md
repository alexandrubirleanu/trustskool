# KV Dema and Peakvest Society: Inside Skool's 12,600-Member Trading Community

Peakvest Society has grown to around 12,600 members on Skool, with a top VIP tier priced at $150 a month, built around a pitch of structure, accountability and "real results" for serious traders. The community is the current form of what began as Get Funded University, and its Skool page names Kejvi "KV" Dema as its founder.

## Who is KV Dema

Kejvi Dema, who goes by "KV" across his trading content, runs a Skool profile under the handle @tradelikekv, which Skool itself lists as the founder identity behind Peakvest Society. Separately from Peakvest, Dema is the named founder of Illyrian Options and Dema Capital LLC, a trading and mentorship business, and a Medium profile piece on him describes a BBA in Finance & Investments, more than five years of experience in equities, and a stint working for a major Canadian investment bank in New York City before he moved into independent trading education ([Medium profile](https://kennethparkerwrites.medium.com/kejvi-kv-dema-options-day-trader-and-mentor-4168b756946a)). That same piece credits him with a Top 10 Stock Trading Mentor recognition from Influencive in 2021 and notes media coverage in Yahoo Finance and Benzinga, though it discusses his Illyrian Options and Dema Capital work specifically rather than Peakvest by name. Illyrian Options operates under the Badtrading.com brand, which independent reviews on Trustpilot confirm as a real, actively reviewed trading education business ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/illyrianoptions.com)).

## What members actually get

Peakvest's standard tier is free, with Premium and VIP upgrades adding live trading calls, mentorship access and deeper community features. The community carries over social proof from its earlier life as Get Funded University, including a "Wall of Love" testimonial page with more than 1,000 member screenshots and, according to the community's own page, over 300 five-star Trustpilot reviews tied to the broader Badtrading ecosystem. The pitch is squarely aimed at traders who want accountability structures and mentorship rather than a purely self-directed course.

## Why it's grown

Peakvest's size reflects a rebrand strategy rather than a cold start: Get Funded University had already built its member base and review history before becoming Peakvest Society, which means the jump to nearly 13,000 members carries forward years of accumulated trust rather than starting from zero under a new name. That's a common move among trading-education operators once an older brand name has run its course or needs repositioning.

Trading communities also tend to lean harder than most Skool niches on visible, numbers-based social proof, screenshots, review counts, mentor rankings, because the underlying promise (helping people make money trading) is inherently harder to verify than a course teaching a concrete skill. Dema's parallel track record in Illyrian Options and Dema Capital, independently reviewed and covered in financial media, gives Peakvest a layer of founder credibility that newer, unnamed trading communities on Skool typically lack.
