# Brehan Crawford and Gut Brain Synchrony: Inside Skool's 12,000-Member Chronic Illness Community

Gut Brain Synchrony describes itself as the clinical community for people with chronic illness who are "done being gaslit and are ready to recover," and it has attracted more than 12,000 members on Skool at a $50-a-month price point. The community's own about page names Brehan Crawford as its founder.

## Who is Brehan Crawford

Brehan Crawford is a licensed acupuncturist, MAcOM, who runs Crawford Wellness in McMinnville, Oregon. He earned his Master's in Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine from the Oregon College of Oriental Medicine in 2009, and spent five years afterward studying Chinese herbal medicine under Dr. Heiner Fruehauf at the Hai Shan Clinic, according to his practice's own biography and confirmed by independent provider directories including Healthgrades and Sharecare ([Healthgrades](https://www.healthgrades.com/providers/brehan-crawford-x8582); [Sharecare](https://providers.sharecare.com/doctor/brehan-crawford-x8582)). His clinical focus centers on chronic Lyme disease and its coinfections, fibromyalgia, chronic pain and oncology support, and patient review platforms like Birdeye list dozens of reviews under his name and license ([Birdeye](https://reviews.birdeye.com/brehan-r-crawford-lac-157121535648523)). He co-runs Crawford Wellness with his wife, Jocela Mae Crawford, and has built a following of more than 100,000 on TikTok discussing traditional Chinese medicine approaches to chronic illness.

## What members actually get

The community is built around clinicians, described on its own page as people who "go where conventional medicine stops," offering members what the group frames as a working understanding of their own body rather than another supplement stack or protocol. It's explicitly aimed at patients who feel let down by conventional treatment for chronic conditions, alongside practitioners and the simply curious. Crawford Wellness also sells Chorus, a line of herbal gut-support products inspired by classical Chinese formulas, giving the community a commercial arm that sits alongside its educational content.

## Why it's grown

Crawford's approach leans on genuine clinical credentials, a real license, a real clinic, more than a decade of practice, in a wellness space on Skool that's often dominated by influencers without formal medical training. For an audience of chronically ill patients who describe feeling dismissed by conventional medicine, that credential-backed positioning, reinforced by his TikTok following and his practice's public review history, is likely a meaningful differentiator from communities built purely around a personal transformation story.

Pricing the community at $50 a month, rather than offering it free, also filters for a more committed, higher-intent membership than the free tiers common elsewhere on Skool, which may explain how a relatively narrow niche, chronic illness recovery through a gut-brain lens, has still been able to reach 12,000 paying-adjacent members.
