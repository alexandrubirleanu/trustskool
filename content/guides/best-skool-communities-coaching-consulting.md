## How to Pick Between Them

Member count alone doesn't tell you which of these fits your situation. An 8,000-member community and a 200-member one can both be genuinely active, the larger group just has more volume in the feed, not necessarily more relevant conversation for your specific niche of coaching. Price works the same way: a $997 one-time program isn't automatically better than a $9/month group, it usually just means the operator is targeting a different buyer, someone with an established practice and a bigger budget for a deeper system, versus someone just getting a coaching business off the ground.

What's worth checking before joining any of these, paid or free, is whether the community is still active now. Look at the most recent posts in the feed, not the join date on the landing page, and see whether the founder or admins are actually present and answering questions, since a coaching community lives or dies on that level of access. If a paid group is charging a premium one-time fee, it's also reasonable to expect a visible classroom or curriculum section, not just a chat feed, before you pay.
