## How to Pick Between Them

The first split worth noticing is between the broad "wealth academy" style communities at the top of this list and the channel-specific agency communities further down. The big free ones (Online Wealth Academy, Go International, Ultimate Wealth Academy) are built for volume and broad appeal, useful if you're still deciding what kind of agency to run. The smaller, paid, channel-specific ones (Meta ads, Google Ads, SEO) exist because their operators are already running agencies in that channel and are charging for access to what they've learned doing it, which is a different value proposition entirely.

Member count matters less here than it looks like it should. A 10,000-member free community and a 234-member paid one aren't competing for the same thing, one is a wide funnel, the other is a narrower, paid operator group. What's more useful is checking whether the specific claims in a community's pitch, like ad spend numbers or client counts, show up anywhere else independently, and whether the group's actual feed backs up the pitch with current, specific discussion rather than recycled screenshots.

Price is a similarly weak standalone signal: $9/month and $297/month can both be reasonable depending on what you need and where you are, a beginner picking a channel doesn't need a $297/month group built for agencies already doing eight figures in managed spend, and vice versa. The more reliable way to compare any two of these is to look past the sales page at actual growth and engagement data, which is what TrustSkore is designed to surface.
