## How to Pick Between Them

**Sort out whether you're an agent, an investor, or neither, before you look at price.** The biggest split in this niche is between communities for licensed agents building a client base, like BAMx, and communities for investors doing wholesaling or rental deals with no license needed, like Wholesaling Real Estate or One Rental at a Time. Joining the wrong type wastes both the fee and your time, no matter how good the community actually is.

**A free community with 4,000 members isn't automatically better than a paid one with 500.** Wholesaling Real Estate has the widest reach on this list, but a narrower, paid community like The Aigent Lab or Real Estate AI Solutions may serve you better if your goal is specific, applying AI tools to a real estate business, for instance, rather than general education.

**Treat promise-driven descriptions as marketing, not verified outcomes.** Several communities here lead with an aspirational hook: no money or experience needed, a deal in 30 days, seven simple steps. Those are framings pulled from the community's own listing copy, not independently confirmed results, so weigh them as positioning rather than guarantees.

**Check engagement and growth before paying anything.** A stale feed under a large member count is a common trap in real estate communities specifically, since a lot of them were built around a single course launch and then went quiet. TrustSkore growth data tells you whether a community is actually active now, not just how big it once got.
