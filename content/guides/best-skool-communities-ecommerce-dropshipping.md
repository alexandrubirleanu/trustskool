## How to Pick Between Them

**Work out which specific business model you're actually pursuing first.** "Ecommerce" and "dropshipping" describe genuinely different businesses: Amazon FBA and private label, Shopify branded dropshipping, print on demand, and TikTok Shop all run on different playbooks. A community built around one of these won't transfer cleanly to another, so match the community to your actual model rather than the umbrella label.

**A free entry point at the top of this niche is common, not a red flag.** Three of the largest communities here, Ivy League Free Course, Ecom Edge Accelerator, and SheProfits, are all free, and all three sit among the biggest in the niche by member count. Free often means the real business sits in a paid tier, a done-for-you service, or an affiliate offer one step behind the free community, not that the free content itself is thin.

**Watch for pricing language in the description that doesn't match a clean, stable rate.** A few communities in this niche describe pricing that's actively moving, "current price," "increasing soon", inside their own listing copy. Worth noting before you join, since the price you see today may not be what a member paid last month, or what you'll pay next month.

**Member count matters less here than in bigger niches.** With only 28 communities tracked in this category, a group with a few hundred members can still be one of the more established options relative to its niche, so don't discount smaller communities just because the number looks small next to a 5,000-member group at the top.
