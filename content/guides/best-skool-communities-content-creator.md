## How to Pick Between Them

The right community here depends heavily on which specific promise matches what you're actually trying to build, general YouTube growth, faceless automation, UGC deals, or AI-assisted production, since this niche splits into fairly distinct sub-strategies rather than one shared curriculum.

Don't treat member count as the deciding factor by itself. Amplify Views and Faceless Creators are large because their premise is broad and appeals to anyone chasing YouTube growth, while a smaller, cheaper community like Faceless Youtube with AI can be more useful if its specific angle matches your goal exactly.

Weigh price against what's actually being taught, not against the size of the number. A $49/month community that includes a money-back guarantee, like UGC & TikTok Creator Academy, is making a different kind of commitment than a free group funding itself through affiliate percentages or an upsell elsewhere.

Look past the pitch and into the actual activity. A community built around "faceless" or "AI-generated" content should show recent, specific examples from members, not just a promise that the system works, and the same applies to any group claiming rapid subscriber or revenue growth.

Watch for how a community actually makes money too. Some, like AI Content Creators and Faceless Youtube with AI, list a public affiliate percentage alongside their subscription price, which isn't a red flag by itself, but it's a detail worth knowing since it shapes what admins are incentivized to promote inside the group.

Before joining anything paid, check the TrustSkore. Growth trend and posting activity over time tell you far more about whether a content-creator community is delivering than a single member count or a landing page ever will.
