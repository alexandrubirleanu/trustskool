# How Skool's Affiliate Program Actually Works (Platform-Level and Per-Community)

Search "Skool affiliate program" and you'll get results describing two completely different systems, often without saying so. One is Skool's own program for referring people to sign up for the platform itself. The other is a feature individual community owners can switch on to pay their own members for referrals. They have different commission rates, different eligibility rules, and different payment rails. Here's how each actually works.

## System One: Skool's Own Platform Affiliate Program

This is the program you join to earn money when someone signs up for a Skool subscription (the $9 Hobby or $99 Pro plan) because of your link. It pays 40% recurring commission on that subscriber's plan for as long as they stay subscribed, with a 60-day cookie window on referral clicks ([Skool Affiliate Program](https://www.skool.com/affiliate-program); [Codingoblin](https://codingoblin.com/blog/skool-affiliate-program)).

Tracking and payouts run through FirstPromoter, a third-party affiliate platform widely used by SaaS companies, with payouts issued monthly once your accrued balance crosses a $100 threshold ([Codingoblin](https://codingoblin.com/blog/skool-affiliate-program)). The math is straightforward: refer someone who stays on the $99 Pro plan and you earn $39.60 every month they remain subscribed. Ten referred Pro subscribers works out to roughly $400/month, or close to $4,700 a year, for as long as they keep paying ([Codingoblin](https://codingoblin.com/blog/skool-affiliate-program)). The program is free to join and includes pre-made banners, swipe copy, and email templates ([Codingoblin](https://codingoblin.com/blog/skool-affiliate-program)).

This system has nothing to do with any specific community. You're being paid by Skool the company, not by a community owner, for growing Skool's own subscriber base.

## System Two: Individual Community Owners' Affiliate Programs

Separately, any Skool community owner can turn on their own affiliate program for their paying members to refer new paying members into that specific community. The owner sets the commission rate from a fixed menu: 0%, 10%, 20%, 30%, 40%, or 50% of the referred member's payment, configured from the Affiliates tab in the community's own settings ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/192-how-to-set-up-member-referrals-for-your-community)).

A few rules distinguish this from the platform-level program:

- **It's not available to owners and admins for their own group** — the feature exists for members to refer friends into a community they already belong to, not for the operator to pay themselves ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/187-how-to-setup-affiliate)).
- **You have to be a member of the community** to get an affiliate link for it at all. There's no way to promote a community's paid tier as an outsider unless you've joined it.
- **Commissions apply only to genuinely new referrals** — someone who cancels and later rejoins using the same affiliate link doesn't generate a new commission ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/192-how-to-set-up-member-referrals-for-your-community)).
- **The owner can change the rate at any time**, and a rate change applies to all current and future affiliates, not just new ones going forward ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/192-how-to-set-up-member-referrals-for-your-community)).

## The Key Differences at a Glance

| | Platform-Level Program | Per-Community Program |
|---|---|---|
| Who pays you | Skool (the company) | The community owner |
| Commission | 40% recurring, fixed | 0-50%, owner's choice |
| Cookie window | 60 days | Set by the community |
| Payment rail | FirstPromoter, $100 minimum | Handled inside Skool/Stripe |
| Eligibility | Anyone | Members of that community only |
| What you're promoting | Skool itself as a platform | One specific paid community |

## Which One Should You Actually Pursue?

If your audience is creators, coaches, or course-sellers who are shopping for a platform to run their own business on, the platform-level program is the natural fit for review content, tutorials, and "how I built my community" case studies, since the commission is fixed and predictable at 40% recurring.

If you're already a genuine paying member of a specific community and believe in the results it delivers, the per-community program can be more lucrative on a per-referral basis, especially inside higher-priced communities where 30-50% of a $97-$297/month plan adds up fast. The tradeoff is that this income only exists as long as you stay a member, and only for the one community you're in.

## A Word of Caution

Because commission rates on the per-community program are entirely up to each individual owner, and the feature itself is optional, you can't assume every paid community has an active affiliate program running, or what its rate is, without checking that community's own Affiliates tab directly. There's no independent, centralized registry of which communities pay what, so treat any third-party claim about a specific community's affiliate terms as unverified until you see it inside the community's own settings.

Whether you're trying to earn from referrals or just trying to find a community actually worth joining in the first place, the same underlying question applies: is this community growing for real reasons, or is its size mostly a byproduct of aggressive referral incentives?
