# Best Skool Communities for Freelancers and Remote Workers in 2026

Freelance and remote work is a broader tent on Skool than most niches, covering Upwork-specific client acquisition, general work-from-home job leads, digital nomad lifestyle groups, and narrower subgroups like neurodivergent freelancers. Our dataset tracks 21 communities under this umbrella, and the size gap between the largest and smallest is wider here than in almost any other niche we've measured, from a 25,120-member free group down to communities still under 100 members. All ten below list English as their primary language, so location isn't a filter you need to think about going in. Here's the real breakdown.

## The Top Communities

**[Upscale (Free)](https://trustskool.com/community/upwork-outbound-free-9857)** is by far the largest community in this niche, with 25,120 members and no membership fee. The founder built the group after achieving what the listing describes as "financial, time, and location freedom" through Upwork, and the community exists to help other freelancers and agencies do the same.

**[6 Figure Tech Freelancer](https://trustskool.com/community/9-5-to-dream-life)** comes in second with 6,527 members, also free to join. It's built around escaping a 9-5 job through coaching, community support, and resources, explicitly marketed as free access to guidance that would otherwise cost money.

**[Global Digital Nomads](https://trustskool.com/community/nomads)** has 4,709 members and no cost to join, describing itself as "the #1 Digital Nomad FREE community to network and learn how to work abroad."

**[The Official WFH Club](https://trustskool.com/community/wfh-community)** is the largest paid community on this list, with 2,612 members at $47/month. Its listing is specific about the problem it solves: real leads, templates, and support so members "stop guessing" their way into a remote job.

**[FREELANCE with ERICA](https://trustskool.com/community/freelancewitherica)** has 630 members and charges $19/month, with an unusual guarantee built into its own pitch, "first paying client or it's FREE," aimed at people new to freelancing or stuck getting nowhere on Upwork.

**[Remote Paradise University](https://trustskool.com/community/remote-entrepreneur-paradise-6219)** has 484 members and no listed price. Its stated long-term project is building a "glamping village for digital nomads," alongside a free 100-hour course available in its classroom tab.

**[WFH Girl Hub](https://trustskool.com/community/wfh-pixie-vip-7029)** has 477 members and is the only community on this list charging a one-time fee rather than a subscription, at $8. It's centered on daily work-from-home job leads and what its listing calls "VIP opportunities" for remote work seekers.

**[NeuroSpicy Freelancers](https://trustskool.com/community/neurospicy-freelancers)** has 395 members and is free, running what its description calls a "5-Day Neurodiversity Reset" aimed specifically at neurodivergent freelancers and solopreneurs.

**[Freelance To Freedom](https://trustskool.com/community/freelancetofreedom)** has 352 members and no listed price, with a description that narrows its focus to camera-based income: "turn your camera into consistent income."

**[Unemployed Abroad](https://trustskool.com/community/unemployed-abroad)** closes out the list at 263 members, free to join, and bills itself as "the #1 most ACTIVE digital nomads community" for learning to make money online while traveling.

## How to Pick Between Them

Seven of the ten communities here cost nothing, and the three that charge, The Official WFH Club at $47/month, FREELANCE with ERICA at $19/month, and WFH Girl Hub at a one-time $8, all sit in the middle of the size range rather than at either end. The single largest community and the single smallest are both free, so member count alone won't tell you whether a group is worth paying for.

The free communities at the top of this list are worth joining almost regardless of your situation. They cost nothing, and even a 25,000-member group can surface a useful lead or template you wouldn't have found on your own. But size alone doesn't tell you whether a community fits your specific goal. Upscale is built for Upwork specifically, Global Digital Nomads and Unemployed Abroad are built around the travel-and-remote-income lifestyle, and The Official WFH Club and WFH Girl Hub are built for landing a traditional remote job. Those are three different goals wearing the same "freelance and remote work" label.

Price matters less here than specificity. A $47/month community that hands you real job leads every week can be worth more than a free group where you're left to find them on your own, and a free group with thousands of active members can outperform a small paid one if that paid group has gone quiet. Before joining anything on this list, check whether a community's growth has actually held up on TrustSkool, since a listing description tells you the pitch, not whether the group is still delivering on it.
