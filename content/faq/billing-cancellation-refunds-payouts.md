# Skool Billing, Cancellation, Refunds & Payouts: Every Question Answered

Skool's billing system splits into two separate relationships that people constantly mix up: the community owner's subscription to Skool itself (Hobby or Pro plan), and a member's subscription to an individual paid community. Most cancellation and refund confusion, including most of the complaints below, comes from not knowing which of those two relationships is actually the one causing the problem. Here's a direct answer to every common question about both, sourced from Skool's own help documentation and its published payment terms.

## How do you cancel a Skool group subscription or free trial (as an admin)?

Go to your profile picture, open **Settings**, click **SETTINGS** for the group in question, then open the **Billing** tab and click **Manage subscription**, followed by **Cancel subscription** and the confirmation prompt ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/36-how-to-cancel-my-skool-subscription)). Nothing is deleted: your group's content, courses, and member data are preserved as-is, and you can come back and reactivate the group at any time by resuming billing. Two things trip people up here: on mobile you often need to scroll the settings tab bar horizontally to even see Billing, and on desktop a high browser zoom level can clip the Billing tab off-screen entirely, so zooming out (Cmd/Ctrl and minus) is the fix if the tab seems to be missing rather than actually broken.

## How do you cancel your membership to a paid community (as a member), and can you get a refund if you cancel?

Click your profile picture, go to **Settings**, open the **Communities** tab, click **SETTINGS** next to the community, then **Manage membership** and confirm cancellation ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/99-how-to-cancel-my-subscription-to-a-community)). On the mobile app it's the three-dot menu, then **Group Settings**, then **Manage Membership**. You're removed at the end of your current billing cycle, and Skool recommends cancelling at least 24 hours before your rebilling date so the cancellation registers before the next charge fires. Cancelling by itself does not refund anything you've already paid: Skool's default is no refunds, and getting money back for a past charge requires directly asking the community's admins, who grant refunds entirely at their own discretion (see the refund-request steps below).

## Why can't I cancel, when I'm confronted with an error instead?

A few concrete causes, roughly in order of how often they show up. The cancel flow is more reliable on desktop web than inside the mobile app, so if a mobile attempt throws an error, try skool.com in a regular browser first. If you don't get a confirmation within about five minutes of cancelling, it likely didn't actually process, so repeat the steps rather than assuming it worked and moving on. If your browser's zoom level is hiding part of the Billing tab, you may be clicking a control that isn't the real cancel button. And if the subscription is already fully cancelled or the group is already archived, clicking cancel again can throw an error simply because there's nothing left to cancel, so check the group or membership's current status before retrying. If none of that resolves it, open the original receipt email in your inbox (search for "receipt" plus the community name), scroll to the bottom, and click **Manage subscription** then **Cancel plan**, which routes you into Stripe's own billing portal directly and bypasses Skool's interface entirely.

## Why does Skool keep charging me after I clicked 'Leave Community' or already cancelled?

The single most common cause: **Leave Community** and **Cancel membership** are two different actions. Leaving removes you from the member list and community feed, but it does not touch the underlying Stripe subscription, so people leave a group, assume they're done, and keep getting billed because they never actually went through Manage Membership to cancel. Second most common: you cancelled within 24 hours of your rebilling date, and one more charge fired before the cancellation finished processing, exactly why Skool recommends cancelling a full day ahead. Third: you joined the community through the iOS app and it uses Apple's in-app billing, which Skool's own cancel button cannot stop (covered next). Fourth: the cancellation attempt itself silently failed, per the error section above, so no confirmation email means it never actually took. Check your payment history for the charge date versus your cancellation date before contacting the admin: if a charge posted after you genuinely completed cancellation, that's a clean case for a refund request.

## If a community owner enabled Apple In-App Purchase, why doesn't Skool's own cancel button stop the charge?

Because that specific subscription is billed and controlled by Apple, not by Skool's own Stripe pipeline. If you joined a paid community from inside the iOS app and the owner has Apple In-App Purchase turned on for it, Skool has no authority to stop or refund that charge from its own dashboard: the billing agreement exists between you and Apple. To actually cancel it, open the **Settings** app on your iPhone, tap your name at the top to open your Apple ID, tap **Subscriptions**, find the community or Skool listing, and tap **Cancel Subscription**. You keep access through the end of the period Apple already billed you for, and it ends after that. This is the specific reason a lot of iOS members report cancelling inside Skool and getting charged again anyway: they cancelled the wrong subscription.

## Why is it so hard to find the cancel button or get a response from support when trying to cancel?

Two separate problems get bundled into this complaint. First, discoverability: the cancel control isn't on the main dashboard, it's three menus deep (Settings, then the group's own SETTINGS, then the Billing tab, then Manage subscription, then Cancel), and that Billing tab can be clipped by browser zoom or require horizontal scrolling on mobile, so a first-time attempt genuinely can fail to locate it. Second, support responsiveness: Skool's public review profile carries a persistent pattern of complaints about billing disputes and cancellation requests going unanswered for extended periods, which is a major driver of its low aggregate Trustpilot score. The practical workaround is to not rely on Skool's support queue at all: use the receipt-email-to-Stripe-portal route described above, which processes the cancellation directly through Stripe, and message the community admin in parallel, since they have their own financial reason to resolve it and can typically act faster than Skool's own support channel.

## How do you seek a refund for the most recent transaction, and how do you refund a member's payment (as an admin)?

As a member, contact the group admins directly, either through Skool Chat or the support email listed on your receipt, explain the situation, and request the refund; Skool's own guidance is that admins will refund you once you've also cancelled your subscription, so do both, not just one ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/116-how-to-seek-a-refund-for-the-most-recent-transaction)). Approved refunds return to your original payment method in roughly 7 to 10 business days. As an admin issuing a refund, go to the **Members** tab, find the person, click **MEMBERSHIP SETTINGS** next to their name, open the **Payments** tab, click the specific charge date, and select **REFUND PAYMENT**, entering a partial amount there if you don't want to refund the full charge ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/84-how-to-refund-a-members-payment)). One real constraint worth planning around: you can only refund up to your current available Stripe Express balance, so if this week's payout already cleared to your bank, you may need to wait for the next payout cycle before the refund actually goes through. Refunding a charge does not automatically cancel or remove that member; that's a separate step.

## Why was I charged (e.g. $39/month) without warning after being told the price had changed to $0?

A few things typically produce this exact complaint, and the honest answer is that your actual invoice record, not a verbal or chat promise from the owner, is the only thing that determines what you're billed. It's possible the owner made a specific *course* or one-time product free while your ongoing *membership* tier kept its original recurring price, so "the price changed" described a different product than the one billing you. It's possible you're grandfathered at your original signup price (see below) and what the owner announced as "$0" or a new lower price applied only to brand-new members joining after the change, not to you retroactively. It's possible a limited-time promotional price simply expired and reverted to the group's normal list price. And it's possible the owner genuinely hasn't finished pushing a plan change through their own Billing settings yet, so the old price is still live on Skool's side regardless of what they told members. Check **Settings > Payment history** for the exact invoice amount, date, and plan description: if it doesn't match what you were told, that discrepancy is solid grounds to request a refund from the admin directly, since Skool itself won't arbitrate a pricing claim made outside its own system. This is also exactly the kind of instability TrustSkool's price-stability signal is built to flag before you ever pay: a community that changes its listed price often shows up in that data ahead of time.

## What is Skool's refund and payment terms policy?

Per Skool's published payment terms, subscription fees are non-refundable once paid, and they accrue from the first day of each billing term (and every renewal) regardless of whether the group owner or a member actually uses the group during that period ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/227-payment-terms-and-policy); [Skool policies](https://www.skool.com/legal?t=transaction)). Subscriptions auto-renew indefinitely at the same interval, monthly stays monthly, annual stays annual, until either the group owner or Skool itself terminates it. Skool's own platform-level default is to issue no refunds at all, but it explicitly allows individual community owners to offer refunds to their members at their own discretion, meaning actual refund eligibility is whatever terms the group's own admins choose to honor, not a Skool-wide guarantee. If a payment method fails, Skool reserves the right to modify, suspend, or cancel access without prior notice to the account holder. On cancellation, access runs through the end of the already-paid billing period, after which an owner's group is archived (not deleted) and a member's access ends.

## How do you view and customize your invoice history?

As an admin, open the group's **SETTINGS**, click **Billing**, click **Manage subscription**, then **View payment history** to see a list of monthly invoices with the card charged for each, clickable by month ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/75-how-to-view-my-invoice-history)). As a member, it's your profile picture, **Settings**, then **Payment history**. To customize what appears on the invoice itself, go to that same Payment history screen and click the cogwheel icon, then fill in your business details (name, address, tax information) and save ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/164-how-to-customize-my-invoice)). Note that this customization is universal rather than per-invoice: whatever you enter applies retroactively across both the invoices your group issues to its members and the payout invoices Skool issues to you, and to past transactions as well as future ones.

## How do you update your payment method (as a member) or payment info (as an admin), and remove your card?

As a member paying for a community, go to your profile picture, **Settings**, then **Payment methods**, and click **ADD CARD**; once a card is saved, its three-dot menu lets you remove it or move your active subscriptions over to a different card on file ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/98-how-to-update-my-payment-method)). As an admin paying for your own group's Skool subscription (Hobby or Pro), go to the community's **SETTINGS**, open **Billing**, and click **Update payment method** to swap the card Skool charges you on ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/48-how-to-update-payment-info)). These are genuinely separate cards and separate relationships: a member's card pays the community owner for access, while an admin's card pays Skool for the platform itself, so if you're troubleshooting a decline, confirm which of the two you're actually dealing with before changing anything.

## How do you see your payment history, and how do you review credit card declines for your members?

As a member, your own transaction history lives at profile picture, **Settings**, then **Payment history** ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/97-how-to-see-my-payment-history)). As an admin checking a specific member's billing status, open the **Members** tab and find them (a member with a recently failed card often shows up under the **Churned** sub-tab), then open **MEMBERSHIP SETTINGS** and their **Payments** tab, which lists every charge attempt and its outcome. What actually happens behind the scenes on a decline: Skool automatically retries the card and emails the member repeatedly over a 7 to 10 day window, while that member sees a red sticky notification bar in their own account telling them the payment failed until they fix it. If the card is never updated inside that window, Skool removes the member from the group automatically, so a member who seems to have vanished from your community is very often a quietly expired card, not something you did.

## What happens to existing members if you cancel your subscription (grandfathering), or to your group generally?

These are two different mechanisms that get talked about under the same word. Price grandfathering means that when an owner raises a group's price, everyone already subscribed keeps paying the rate they originally signed up at, indefinitely, and only new members joining after the change pay the new, higher price; this is Skool's stated default behavior, not something an owner has to turn on. Separately, when the group **owner** cancels their own Skool subscription, the group is archived at the end of the current billing period: existing content (posts, courses, chat history) remains intact and readable, but nothing new can be posted, no new members can join, and payment collection stops entirely for everyone in it. The group is not deleted, and existing members are not individually notified or refunded as part of the switch to archived. The owner (or a re-promoted admin) can reactivate it later simply by resuming billing, and everything resumes as it was.

## How do you transfer group ownership or delete your Skool group?

Ownership transfer is a Pro plan-only feature. Add the intended new owner as a member, promote them to admin via their **MEMBERSHIP** settings, then use that same menu again to make them the group's new owner ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/190-how-to-transfer-group-ownership)). The moment ownership transfers, the group is automatically archived, the previous owner becomes a regular admin, and the new owner has to add their own payment information to reactivate it, meaning ownership does not automatically carry billing responsibility with it. To delete a group entirely, it generally needs to be archived or cancelled first, then **SETTINGS > Billing > Delete group** completes permanent removal ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/230-how-to-delete-a-skool-group)). Treat deletion as irreversible: unlike a normal cancellation, which just archives the group, deleting it removes it outright.

## How do you make an admin a Billing Manager?

By default, Skool's regular Admin role can see and manage almost everything in a community except billing, which is deliberately walled off from that role. To grant it, open the **Members** tab, filter to show admins, click **MEMBERSHIP SETTINGS** next to the person you want to elevate, and give them billing access from there ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/88-how-to-make-an-admin-a-billing-manager)). Once granted, that person's own admin menu gains a billing and payments section that a regular admin never sees. This is the standard way to let a co-founder, ops hire, or partner manage pricing, invoices, and payouts day-to-day without handing them your actual Stripe login.

## How do you change plans (as a member vs. as a group owner)?

As a member, go to your profile **Settings**, click the **MEMBERSHIP** gear icon for the community, and choose a different tier or billing interval to upgrade, downgrade, or switch from monthly to annual ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/193-how-to-upgrade-to-annual)). If you're still inside a 14-day free trial, the same settings area has a "skip trial / upgrade now" shortcut that starts the paid plan immediately instead of waiting the trial out. As a group **owner**, "changing plans" means something entirely different: it's your own Hobby-versus-Pro subscription to Skool itself, changed from the community's **SETTINGS > Billing** area, not from the member-facing membership menu. Mixing these two up is common: a member changing plans affects what they personally pay the community; an owner changing plans affects what the whole community pays Skool.

## How do you set up payouts for your community, change your payout bank account, and check payout status?

To set up payouts, open the community's **SETTINGS**, click the **Payouts** tab, click the gear icon, select the country where your actual bank account is located (not your citizenship or business registration country), and continue into Stripe Express onboarding, which requires legally mandatory identity verification before it activates ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/78-how-to-setup-skool-subscriptions)). You need an ordinary domestic bank account in that country; fintech-only accounts like Wise or Chime aren't accepted. To change the bank account later, go back into Payouts settings, open the **Stripe dashboard** link, click your profile icon inside Stripe, go to **Payout details**, and add the new account, a change that requires an SMS verification code for security ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/126-how-to-change-my-bank-account)). To check status, the Payout tab shows your current available balance once you have any MRR or course sales on the books; payouts run automatically every Wednesday, your first payout typically takes 8 to 14 business days end-to-end, and subsequent ones land 1 to 3 business days later for US banks or 3 to 7 for international ones ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/85-how-to-check-the-skool-subscriptions-payout)). A connection stuck on "pending" for longer than that usually clears up if you disconnect and reconnect via "Start again here."

## How do you connect Skool to Stripe for subscriptions and one-time payments, does it appear as a separate Stripe account, and is there a roadmap for deeper Stripe integration?

Setting up payouts, described above, is the same action as connecting Stripe: there's no separate integration step. Skool specifically uses **Stripe Express**, not a standard Stripe account, and you cannot link a Stripe account you already use elsewhere. Skool always spins up a brand-new Express sub-account tied specifically to your Skool payouts, even if you already run Stripe for a separate business like a Shopify store. One connection covers both recurring subscription billing and one-time course or product purchases; both payment types flow through the same Stripe Express account and the same weekly payout, not two parallel systems. On whether there's a roadmap for letting creators connect their own pre-existing Stripe account instead: it's a recurring, vocal request in Skool's own community, but there is no officially confirmed roadmap commitment or timeline for it as of now, so treat "bring your own Stripe" as a wishlist item rather than a stated upcoming feature.

## Should Skool be held responsible when creators collect payment without delivering value?

Skool's own stated position, visible in how it responds to Better Business Bureau complaints, is no: payment for community access is a transaction between the member and the individual creator's own billing setup, and Skool treats refund disputes as a private matter between those two parties rather than something it will arbitrate or force a resolution on, even when a member reports a creator went unresponsive after being paid. That's the accurate, if unflattering, answer, and it's why BBB and Trustpilot both carry a recurring complaint pattern of members paying for a community, the founder going quiet, and neither the creator nor Skool support providing a resolution. Practically, this means the due diligence has to happen before you pay, since there's little structural recourse after. The signals worth checking are whether a community's member activity is actually growing over time rather than just its ad spend, whether its category ranking has moved in a way that looks organic rather than suspiciously sudden, and whether its price has stayed stable rather than being repeatedly changed, since frequent repricing often correlates with a founder squeezing revenue rather than steadily building value. That combination of growth trend, discovery-rank movement, and price stability is exactly what TrustSkool's automated TrustSkore is built to surface, precisely because there's no review layer or platform-level accountability from Skool itself to lean on otherwise.

## Is there any way to reach Skool support besides leaving an app-store review?

Yes, three routes that actually reach a person or a queue, roughly in order of how fast they tend to work in practice. Email **help@skool.com** directly, Skool's documented support address. Use the chat widget inside the Help Center at help.skool.com. And, often faster than either, message the community's admin directly: they have their own financial and reputational stake in resolving a billing dispute and can process cancellations or refunds from their own side without waiting on Skool's support queue at all, as covered above. An app-store review isn't routed to a support ticket system at all; it's public feedback with no guaranteed reply, so if you actually need the issue fixed rather than just documented, use one of the three channels above instead.
