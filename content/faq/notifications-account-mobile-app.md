# Skool Notifications, Account & Mobile App Issues: Every Question Answered

Skool's notification system has three separate layers that don't turn off together, and its mobile app has a handful of well-documented bugs that Skool itself has never issued a public fix or timeline for. Below are direct answers to every common question in this cluster, sourced from Skool's own help center and from community and app-store reports where Skool hasn't documented the behavior itself. None of these nine questions overlap enough to merge — the general notification-settings question and the push-notification-bugs question look similar but cover different problems (settings not sticking vs. delivery failing outright), so both get their own answer.

## Notifications & Push Alerts

### How do you manage notifications, and why do you keep getting notified after turning them off in settings?

Skool splits notification control across three independent layers, and turning off one doesn't touch the other two — that's almost always why "I turned off notifications" doesn't actually stop them. Go to your profile picture → **Settings** → **Notifications** to manage **group-level** preferences (digest emails, daily "unread activity" emails, event reminders, and — if you're an admin — membership-request and reported-content alerts); click "Change" next to any group to mute or customize it individually, per [Skool's help center](https://help.skool.com/article/95-how-to-manage-my-notifications). Separately, if you follow specific members you'll keep getting notified of their posts until you unfollow them from **Profile → Following**, and separately again, any thread where you tapped the bell icon will keep pinging you until you tap that same bell again to unfollow just that thread. On mobile, there's a fourth layer on top of all this: your phone's OS-level notification permission for the Skool app, which overrides the in-app toggles entirely — if push is still arriving after you've muted everything in-app, check iOS Settings → Notifications → Skool (or Android's app info → Notifications) next, since that's outside Skool's own settings screen. Community members hitting this exact confusion have written their own workarounds, like the [DIY Bookkeeping Support post on cutting Skool notifications down](https://www.skool.com/diy-bookkeeping-for-businesses-7060/if-you-dont-want-all-of-the-notifications-from-skool-here-is-what-you-do).

### Why do push notifications behave inconsistently — one admin gets notified but not another, notifications reflect last-opened time instead of real time, no push notifications arrive at all, or the badge doesn't show what triggered it?

This is a delivery reliability problem, not a setting you're missing — Skool has not published an explanation or fix for it. Co-admins in the same community have reported that when one admin posts, some other admins get a push and others simply don't, with no pattern tied to their individual notification settings, which points to a server-side or token-registration bug rather than user error (see the [Skool Community "no push notifications" thread](https://www.skool.com/community/no-push-notifications) and the related ["notifications problem" thread](https://www.skool.com/community/notifications-problem)). Separately, some users report the app's unread/badge state effectively resets to whenever they last opened it rather than reflecting real-time activity, so the badge count and the bell/inbox can disagree with what actually happened since your last visit — and because Skool doesn't differentiate push-notification types clearly (a like, a comment, and a DM can all surface as the same generic badge), you often can't tell what triggered the badge without opening every section. Because this gap is well-known enough that third-party tools like [PingBell](https://pingbell.io/solutions/skool-push-notifications/) exist specifically to relay Skool activity into more reliable channels, treat native push as "best effort": if a device stops receiving push entirely, log out and back in to force a fresh push-token registration, keep the app updated, and don't rely on the badge count alone for anything time-sensitive.

## Account & Chat Settings

### How do you turn off Skool Chat?

As a member, go to your profile picture → **Settings** → **Chat**, and use the dropdown next to each group to turn Skool Chat on or off for that specific community — it's a per-group toggle, not a single global switch, so if you're in multiple communities you'll need to repeat this for each one, per [Skool's help center](https://help.skool.com/article/96-how-to-turn-off-my-skool-chat). If you're the community owner and want to restrict chat for everyone rather than just yourself, go to **Settings → Plugins → "Unlock chat at selected Level"** and set the required level artificially high (level 9 is the common trick, since almost no member ever reaches it) — this effectively disables chat community-wide without removing the plugin outright, a workaround discussed in threads like [Disable Chat Feature](https://www.skool.com/community/disable-chat-feature).

### Do you need a profile picture to join a community?

Yes, at the account level — Skool requires a profile picture and a short bio before your account is even usable, stating plainly that "[a profile pic and a bio are mandatory for a Skool account](https://help.skool.com/article/92-do-i-need-a-profile-pic-to-join-a-community)" because communities need faces, not anonymous handles, to build trust. That requirement is separate from what individual paid communities additionally screen for at the approval stage: admins commonly manually reject or ignore join requests with a generic or blank-looking photo because it's the fastest signal that an applicant might be a bot, a scraper, or someone with no real intent to participate (see [Community Builders: "Please upload a profile photo of your face"](https://www.skool.com/communitybuilders/please-upload-a-profile-photo-of-your-face)). For evaluating a community before you join, though, a strict photo policy is at best a weak proxy for how carefully a community is moderated — it tells you nothing about whether the group is actually growing, holding its price, or losing members quietly. That's the gap TrustSkool's TrustSkore is built to close: it scores communities on real, observable growth trend, discovery-rank movement, and price stability rather than manual screening habits or star ratings, so use it alongside your own gut-check on the join page, not instead of it.

### How do you manage multiple Skool accounts, and log out of all devices?

Skool's own guidance is to avoid multiple accounts entirely: use one account to join and create every community you're part of, and if you get invited through a different email address, just log into your existing account when you click the invite rather than creating a second one, per the [multiple-accounts help article](https://help.skool.com/article/222-how-to-manage-multiple-skool-accounts). There is no account-merge feature, so if you've already ended up with two or more accounts, the fix is manual: pick one to keep, rejoin your groups under it, tell any admins you need continuity with (moderation history, purchased courses) that you're switching, and cancel any paid subscriptions on the account you're abandoning before you rejoin under the surviving one. To log out of every device at once — useful if you suspect a session is compromised, or you're doing that account consolidation on a shared computer — go to **Settings → Account settings**, find the **Sessions** section, and click **LOG OUT EVERYWHERE**, which kills all active sessions immediately, per the [log-out-of-all-devices article](https://help.skool.com/article/94-how-to-log-out-of-all-devices); Skool's [account security page](https://help.skool.com/article/186-account-security) covers related password-reset steps if you're locking things down after that.

## Mobile App Bugs

### Why does the mobile app glitch while typing or commenting (can't type, can't load images, message won't post if you're slow)?

This is a documented, ongoing composer bug rather than a device-specific fluke — one widely-shared community post put it bluntly: users "[literally can't type, can't copy to start over, or can't load an image 40% of the time](https://www.skool.com/bos/is-it-me-or-is-the-skool-app-buggy-af)," and several reviewers note the problem got worse, not better, after a recent app update. The pattern is a text-input rendering conflict: the composer's text box locks up mid-edit, especially once you try to reposition your cursor or select text before posting, and there's no autosave, so if you're pulled away mid-comment (a call, an app switch) or simply type slowly, the draft can silently fail to submit and you lose it. The reliable workaround is to compose anything longer than a one-liner in your phone's native Notes app and paste it in, and if the composer freezes, force-close the app rather than fighting it — reopening almost always clears the frozen state, whereas waiting it out usually doesn't.

### Why do videos pause randomly or turn black in fullscreen in the mobile app?

Skool doesn't host classroom video itself — it embeds from four partners (YouTube, Vimeo, Wistia, and Loom), and its own [video troubleshooting guidance](https://help.skool.com/article/178-how-to-troubleshoot-video-issues) only covers YouTube-specific fixes: log into your Google account in the same browser, disable any VPN, switch networks (e.g., from 5G Wi-Fi to LTE), and disable browser extensions. For Vimeo, Wistia, or Loom-hosted lessons, there's no first-party fix published, and Skool's guidance simply points you to your group's admin instead. Community reports describe a specific, repeatable failure: pause a video to switch apps, and it won't resume when you return, on both the mobile app and mobile Safari, a hosting-partner-and-mobile-browser interaction rather than something the group creator did wrong. Separately, on the Android app specifically, the fullscreen control bar is missing a genuine fullscreen toggle altogether — users report only speed, search, and picture-in-picture controls, which is a Skool player-UI gap, not a hosting issue. Until Skool addresses either, the practical move is to avoid backgrounding the app mid-video and, for stubborn lessons, watch on desktop web instead.

### Why did the app show an all-white screen for days with no way to reach a developer except an app-store review?

App Store reviewers describe the exact same failure mode: the SKOOL logo loads, then the screen goes completely white and nothing renders — for some users this has persisted for multiple days straight, across different Wi-Fi networks and different devices, which is why affected users concluded it was a client-side rendering bug rather than a connectivity problem on their end. What makes it worse is that Skool's only listed support channels are an email address (help@skool.com, per [skool.com/support](https://www.skool.com/support)) and its help center — there's no in-app crash reporter, live chat, or status page, so when the app itself won't render past that white screen, there's no support surface reachable from inside the broken app. That's exactly why frustrated users escalate through App Store reviews instead: it's often the only place their report is guaranteed to be read. If you hit this, don't wait it out — force-quit the app, then fully offload or reinstall it (a soft reopen usually doesn't clear the corrupted local cache causing this), use the mobile web version at skool.com as a stopgap, and email help@skool.com directly rather than relying on a store review to reach anyone.

### Why can't you start your own group from the mobile app?

Because community creation has never been built into the mobile apps — it's confirmed as a hard platform limitation, not a bug: you can post, chat, browse the classroom, and manage an existing community from your phone, but the actual "create a new community" flow (plan and pricing setup, payout/Stripe connection, initial branding) only exists in Skool's desktop-oriented web onboarding wizard. This is a known point of friction since both app-store listings market the apps as carrying "all of Skool's features," which creation isn't. If you want to launch a community, open a browser — mobile Safari or Chrome technically works, but the multi-step wizard is easier to get through on a laptop screen — and once it exists, you can go back to managing and posting in it from the app as normal.
