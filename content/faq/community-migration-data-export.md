# Skool Community Migration, Data Export & Account Deletion: Every Question Answered

Nobody signs up for Skool while researching how to eventually leave it, so this cluster of questions is usually only searched once someone is already stuck: a paid customer list from another platform that will not drop cleanly into Skool's import tool, a member export missing a third of its emails, or a community someone has decided to shut down and needs to empty out first. Skool's own help center only documents a thin slice of this, moving members in and exporting a CSV, and the rest of the picture comes from the migration guides, third-party importers, and community threads written by creators who have actually gone through it. Below are the eight questions that come up most, answered directly and sourced throughout.

## Bringing Members and Content Into Skool

### How do you import members into a new Skool group, and can you assign them to a specific course or tier during that import?

Go to **Settings → Invite**, click **IMPORT**, then **UPLOAD FILE**, per Skool's own guidance on inviting members, which describes the feature as built for "importing old customers or members in batches" and notes that bulk invites grant instant access without anyone going through a join request or purchase flow ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/14-how-do-i-invite-members-to-my-community)). Skool's own advice for genuinely large lists is blunt: it recommends inviting through a CRM integration instead of one giant upload. What the article does not offer is any way to route imported members into a specific course, tier, or paid level as part of that same upload, and it is a frequent enough gap that creators bringing over an existing paid list keep asking about it directly in Skool's public community ([Import Members via CSV and assign to Course/s](https://www.skool.com/community/import-members-via-csv-and-assign-to-courses)). Everyone imported this way lands as a plain member at your group's default access, and sorting people into the right course or tier afterward is a separate, manual pass through each member's **MEMBERSHIP** settings. If you are migrating a tiered membership from somewhere else, budget time for that second step; it is not part of the import itself.

### Can you migrate an existing Facebook Group, Discord server, or other community's membership into Skool automatically?

No, and this is one of the most repeated questions in Skool's own community, with creators asking variations of it directly ([How to Migrate Your Facebook Group Members to Skool](https://www.skool.com/community/how-to-migrate-your-facebook-group-members-to-skool); [How to move your Facebook Group to Skool](https://www.skool.com/community/how-to-move-your-facebook-group-to-skool)). The real obstacle sits on the other platform's side rather than Skool's: Facebook does not give group admins any way to export members' names or email addresses, membership in a Facebook Group has never included consenting to hand your contact details to the admin, and scraping around that restriction breaches both Facebook's terms and, for EU or UK members, GDPR itself ([KoffeeKlatch on exporting Facebook group member data](https://koffeeklatch.co.uk/how-to-export-your-facebook-group-members-data-and-comply-with-gdpr/)). Since Skool's bulk import only works off a list of emails you already have, the actual migration path is social rather than technical: post in the old group with a direct join link and ask members to opt in themselves, spread the move over a few weeks instead of one announcement, and treat whatever emails you legitimately collected some other way, a lead magnet, a paid course roster, as the only list you can bulk-import from day one. Discord and Circle have the same structural limit: neither hands you a portable member-email list either, so moving people, as opposed to content, is always a re-invite rather than a transfer.

## Getting Your Data Out of Skool

### What can you actually export from Skool before you cancel or migrate away, and what has no export option at all?

Skool's first-party export options are narrower than most creators expect, which is worth knowing before you need one under time pressure. On the **Members** tab, an **Export** button downloads your member list, including email addresses, as a CSV, though Skool restricted this to paid groups when it rolled the feature out; free groups do not get the same button ([Skool: you can now export your members' email addresses in paid groups](https://www.skool.com/educate/you-can-now-export-your-members-email-addresses-in-paid-groups)). A second, separate Export button on the **Membership Questions** tab pulls everyone's survey answers into a CSV of its own ([Skool Help Center](https://help.skool.com/article/148-where-can-i-find-members-answers-to-membership-questions)). Those two buttons are close to the entire list of what Skool lets you pull out in one click. Everything else, community posts, comment threads, chat history, leaderboard and points history, and the classroom's video or lesson content, has no built-in export at all, and migration guides written by creators who have actually moved off Skool describe the same workaround for all of it: copy lesson text into Docs or Notion by hand, download videos from whichever host actually stores them (YouTube, Vimeo, Wistia, or Loom), and save anything else worth keeping one piece at a time ([SchoolMaker's Skool migration guide](https://www.schoolmaker.com/blog/skool-migration)). If losing a post thread or your leaderboard history would sting, that has to happen before you cancel, not after.

### Why does Skool's member CSV export show blank emails for a chunk of the list?

This is a documented gap in the export itself rather than something wrong with your account. One creator who audited an export of more than 700 members found the email field blank for roughly a third of the list (about 32%), concentrated among members who had joined through Skool's Discovery page rather than a direct invite, since Discovery sign-ups do not always populate the same profile field the CSV export pulls from ([DEV Community: exporting 700+ Skool members to CSV](https://dev.to/cristiantalasanchez/how-i-export-700-skool-members-to-csv-with-one-api-call-email-tier-ltv-survey-1p85)). Skool's own help content does not acknowledge the gap, so there is no official fix to point to. If you need a complete list for a migration, cross-check the Members export against your Membership Questions export first, a member's email often shows up there in a typed survey answer even when the profile-level field is empty.

### Are there tools that migrate an entire Skool group, members and posts together, into another platform automatically?

Yes, and they exist specifically because Skool itself has no group-to-group migration feature. GoHighLevel publishes both a general migration guide, which walks through manually copying member data, course content, and community posts into a GHL Community, and a dedicated Skool Importer that automates the members-and-posts transfer in one pass instead of by hand ([Skool to HighLevel Migration Guide](https://help.gohighlevel.com/support/solutions/articles/155000003309-skool-to-highlevel-migration-guide-); [GoHighLevel: Skool Importer](https://help.gohighlevel.com/support/solutions/articles/155000005150--skool-importer-seamlessly-migrate-your-skool-group-to-ghl-community)). Either way, member lists and course content (video links, descriptions, resources) are the parts that move cleanly; automations like welcome messages and event reminders still have to be rebuilt from scratch on the new platform rather than carried over as-is. A similar tool exists for Mastermind Communities: point it at your Skool group's slug and admin login, and it imports both members and posts ([Mastermind Community Importer](https://mastermind.helpscoutdocs.com/article/944-community-importer-for-skool-group-post-importer)). The catch is the same across all of these tools: since Skool has no official way to hand data to a third party, they authenticate using your actual Skool admin email and password rather than an API key or an OAuth grant, so vetting the specific tool and vendor matters more here than it would with a sanctioned integration.

### Is there an official Skool API for pulling your own community's data programmatically?

No. Skool has never published a public API or developer documentation for one, and every tool built around Skool data, from export helpers to full migration importers, says as much outright. One such tool's own documentation states it plainly: "Skool does not provide a public API. This actor reverse-engineers Skool's internal endpoints" ([Skool API on Apify](https://apify.com/cristiantala/skool-all-in-one-api)). Because there is no key-based authentication to grant, these tools log in the same way you do, typically through an automated browser session using your Skool email and password, then reuse the resulting session cookie for a few days before repeating the login. A handful of independent projects, including a standalone SkoolAPI service, have built more polished wrappers around the same reverse-engineered endpoints, but none of them are sanctioned by Skool, and Skool could change its internal page structure at any time and break them without notice.

## Deleting Your Account and Your Data

### Can you get a copy of your personal data from Skool, or have Skool delete your account and data entirely?

Skool's privacy page includes a formal **Data delete request** form, which is the closest thing Skool documents to a GDPR-style request ([skool.com/privacy](https://www.skool.com/privacy?rt=udt)). What it does not include anywhere in the product is a personal data export: there is no "download everything I've posted" button, so if you have written something in a community worth keeping, a course-completion note, a long post, a testimonial, you copy or screenshot it yourself before requesting deletion ([tools4skool: how to delete a Skool account](https://tools4skool.com/how-to/how-to-delete-skool-account)). Deleting the account itself is not a single obvious button either: it sits under **Settings → Account** as a small text link rather than a prominent control, and Skool requires you to confirm the request by email within 24 hours or it cancels automatically, with no 30-day undo window afterward the way Google or Facebook offer. You also cannot delete your personal account while you still own a paid community; you have to transfer ownership or delete the group first, so Skool does not orphan paying members mid-transaction. Once deletion completes, your old posts and comments stay visible to everyone else but get re-attributed to "Deleted user," and any direct messages you sent remain in the recipient's inbox with your identity stripped out of them.

### What should you back up before you delete, not just cancel, a Skool group, since deletion is irreversible?

Deleting a group (**Settings → Billing → Delete group**) is a harder, more final step than cancelling or archiving one, and Skool treats it as permanent with no recovery path once it is done. Before you do it, pull the three things that have no export button and no undo afterward: the member CSV from **Members → Export** while the group is still on a paid plan and active, since that button is a paid-plan feature that stops being reachable once billing lapses; a manual copy of any course video links and lesson text worth reusing elsewhere, since Skool neither hosts nor exports the underlying files itself; and screenshots or copy-pasted text of any posts, member wins, or testimonials in the feed you would otherwise lose the moment the group disappears. None of this is Skool-specific caution for its own sake; it is the same short list every migration guide converges on once you strip out the platform-specific tooling and ask what is actually unrecoverable ([SchoolMaker's Skool migration guide](https://www.schoolmaker.com/blog/skool-migration); [tools4skool: how to delete a Skool account](https://tools4skool.com/how-to/how-to-delete-skool-account)).
