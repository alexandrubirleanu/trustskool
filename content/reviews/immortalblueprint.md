# Valorant Immortal Blueprint Review 2026: Is the $19/Month Coaching Worth It?

## What's Actually Inside

The core offer is a structured 12-week program, which implies a defined curriculum with a beginning, middle, and end rather than an open-ended content stream, likely covering mechanics, game sense, positioning, and rank-specific strategy needed to climb toward Immortal. Because the goal (Immortal+) is a specific, verifiable in-game rank, this is one of the more measurable outcomes a gaming coaching community can promise, players either climb toward that rank or they don't, which makes it easier to judge results than vaguer "improve your gameplay" pitches. At $19/month, the price sits in the affordable range for competitive gaming coaching, well below what individual coaching sessions typically cost per hour.

## Pros

- A defined 12-week structure gives a clear timeline and endpoint rather than an indefinite subscription with no defined program arc.
- Targets a specific, measurable rank goal (Immortal+), making it easier to judge whether the coaching is working for you personally over the 12 weeks.
- At $19/month, it's priced well below the cost of individual Valorant coaching sessions from platforms that charge per hour.

## Cons

- No independent reviews found beyond the platform listing, so the "proven" claim in the description reflects the community's own framing rather than third-party verified results.
- Reaching Immortal+ depends heavily on individual mechanical skill, time invested, and current rank starting point, all of which affect how realistic the outcome is regardless of coaching quality.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Valorant players currently below Immortal rank who are willing to commit to a structured 12-week program and put in deliberate practice time between coaching content.

## Who Should Skip It

Casual players not focused on competitive rank progression, or players already at Immortal+ looking for higher-level (Radiant or pro-adjacent) coaching.
