# Templo de Ingeniería Musical Review 2026: Inside Skool's Priciest Music Production Community

Templo de Ingeniería Musical is a Spanish-language Skool community with 489 members that pitches itself as the only place combining making professional-quality songs, earning money from them, working alongside established names in the industry, and folding AI into the process. Its listed price is unusually steep at $100,000 a month, by far the highest figure we've seen attached to a Skool community's public listing, so it's worth confirming directly with the group before assuming that number reflects the real entry price.

## What's Actually Inside

Going by its own description, the community organizes around four connected promises: building pro-level production skills, turning those skills into income, creating pathways to work with major players in the music industry, and integrating AI tools into the songwriting and production workflow. That combination, skill plus monetization plus networking plus AI, is broader than most single-focus production or mixing courses, positioning this more as an all-in-one hub for a music career than a narrow tutorial series.

The AI integration piece is worth calling out specifically, since it suggests the curriculum is being kept current with how AI tools are reshaping music production workflows, from mixing assistance to generative composition aids, rather than teaching only traditional DAW-based production techniques. Combined with the promise of connections to established industry names, the overall pitch reads as an attempt to compress skill-building, income generation, and industry access into a single membership, which is an ambitious scope for any one community to deliver on well.

## Pros

- Covers skill-building, monetization, and industry connections together rather than just one of the three.
- Explicitly folds AI tools into music production, relevant for producers trying to stay current with the technology shift in the industry.
- Serves a Spanish-speaking audience in a space where a lot of well-known music production education is English-only.

## Cons

- The listed price of $100,000/month is so far outside typical Skool pricing that it should be independently verified with the community before anyone takes it at face value; it may reflect a data or listing error rather than the real cost.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, so claims about working "with the greats" aren't verifiable from the outside.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking musicians and producers who want a combined approach to skill, income, and industry connections, and who are prepared to verify pricing and terms directly before committing.

## Who Should Skip It

Beginners or hobbyists on any kind of budget, and anyone who wants a straightforward, single-topic production course rather than a broad, multi-part offer.
