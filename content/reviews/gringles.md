## What's Actually Inside

The specificity here is notable: a named proficiency target (C2), a timeframe (under one year), and a stated method (direct practice with US native speakers) rather than a vague "learn English" pitch. That combination suggests structured conversation practice and immersion-style coaching aimed at learners who already have some English foundation and want to push to true fluency, rather than absolute beginners starting from zero.

Naming C2 specifically also matters because it's the level most language courses don't even attempt to promise, most commercial language apps and courses are built around getting learners to conversational or intermediate levels (roughly B1 to B2), leaving the jump to near-native fluency underserved. A community built explicitly around that higher bar, with native speaker access as the delivery mechanism, is targeting a specific and somewhat underserved segment of language learners.

Absolute beginners with no English foundation, since the C2-in-a-year framing suggests the program is built for learners further along, not a starting-from-scratch curriculum.
