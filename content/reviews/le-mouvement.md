# Organic Scale Review 2026: Is This Free Community for Infopreneurs Worth Joining?

Organic Scale is a free, French-language Skool community with 871 members, aimed specifically at infopreneurs, people building and selling online courses or digital information products. The community's own description sets a clear target: help members really learn to excel organically on social media, with a stated goal of adding 3,000 to 15,000 euros a month.

## What's Actually Inside

The word "organiquement" (organically) in the description matters here, it signals the content is built around unpaid, content-driven growth rather than paid advertising, which is a meaningfully different skill set. Being specifically aimed at infopreneurs, rather than e-commerce sellers or local businesses, also suggests the tactics taught are likely tailored to selling knowledge products and building an audience around a personal brand or area of expertise.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier to testing whether the organic growth approach fits.
- Specifically targeted at infopreneurs rather than general "grow on social media" advice for any business type.
- Organic-only focus may particularly suit members without an advertising budget to spend.

## Cons

- The 3,000 to 15,000 euro monthly figure comes directly from the community's own marketing description, not from any independent or verified source, and should be read as an aspirational target rather than a typical or guaranteed outcome.
- We found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.
- The description doesn't name which social platforms the organic strategy centers on, nor which stage of infopreneur journey (pre-launch, early sales, or established) the content is built for, so those specifics are worth confirming directly.

## Why the Organic-Only Framing Stands Out

Most infopreneur-focused communities lean at least partly on paid acquisition, given how quickly organic reach can plateau on major platforms. A community built specifically around organic growth is choosing a harder, slower path by design, which can be a genuine advantage for members without ad budgets, but it also means results likely depend more heavily on consistent content output over time than a paid-traffic approach would.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking infopreneurs who are early in building an audience and specifically want organic, non-paid tactics for growing their following and revenue.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-French speakers, and infopreneurs who prefer paid acquisition channels (ads, sponsorships) over organic content strategy as their primary growth lever.
