# Elite Setters Community Review 2026: A Free Spanish-Language Hub for Appointment Setters

Elite Setters Community has quietly built a base of more than 10,500 members without ever charging for access, and it does so around a narrow, specific role rather than a broad "make money online" pitch. Its own description, written in Spanish, calls it a community "de Appointment Setters y Dueños de Negocios," a community of appointment setters and business owners, built around three things: finding work, getting trained, and networking.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the community serves two sides of the same job market at once. Appointment setters, people who book sales calls for other businesses, come looking for work and skill development. Business owners come looking to hire setters for their teams. Layered on top of that is a networking function, connecting members with each other beyond a simple job board. What the training actually covers, whether it's call scripts, objection handling, CRM workflows, or something else, isn't spelled out in the listing itself.

## Who Runs It

Skool lists Mateo Jiménez as the community's owner. No further background on him is included in the data reviewed here.

## Pros

- Free membership at a scale of over 10,500 people shows the format has found real traction without a paywall.
- Serves both sides of the appointment-setting hiring pipeline in one place, rather than only setters or only agency owners.
- Networking component adds value beyond a static training library, useful for a role that depends heavily on shared scripts and tactics.

## Cons

- The description doesn't detail what the training material actually consists of, so it's hard to judge depth or quality before joining.
- No independent reviews of this specific community were found beyond the Skool listing.
- Free, open communities connecting job seekers and employers can vary widely in how well opportunities are vetted, and that isn't addressed in the description.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking appointment setters actively looking for placements or training, and business owners who want to recruit setters from a dedicated pool, particularly anyone comfortable operating day to day in Spanish, since that's the community's working language.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who doesn't read or write Spanish will struggle to participate meaningfully, since the description and presumably most posts are in that language. Setters or agency owners looking for a broader sales training curriculum beyond appointment setting specifically should also look for a more general resource.
