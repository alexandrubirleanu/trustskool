# Comunidad Japón (FREE) Review 2026: A No-Cost Way to Start Japanese

Comunidad Japón (FREE) is exactly what its name promises: a free Skool community, with 1,123 members, built for Spanish speakers starting Japanese from zero. According to its own description, the community exists to help members learn the basics, get their questions answered, and take their first steps into the language without paying anything.

## What's Actually Inside

The description frames this as an entry point rather than a full curriculum: basic content for absolute beginners, plus a space to ask questions along the way. That's a common structure for free language communities on Skool, they exist partly to build trust and audience before a paid, more advanced tier is introduced, though nothing in the description confirms whether that's the plan here. For a first-steps resource, the combination of basic content plus a place to get questions answered covers the two things most complete beginners actually need, something to study and somewhere to ask what they don't understand, even without a full grammar course attached.

## Pros

- Zero cost makes it an easy way to test whether a Skool-based learning format actually works for you before committing money anywhere.
- Explicitly aimed at absolute beginners ("desde cero"), so it should be approachable for people with no prior Japanese exposure.
- The stated Q&A element means questions and doubts have somewhere to go rather than being left unanswered.

## Cons

- As a free, beginner-only community, it likely has a ceiling: members progressing past the basics may need to look for paid or more advanced instruction elsewhere.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond the Skool platform listing itself, and no teacher or founder is credited publicly.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking beginners who want to dip a toe into Japanese without any financial commitment and who value having a place to ask basic questions as they go.

## Who Should Skip It

Learners who already have some Japanese under their belt, or who want a structured, paced curriculum with assessments, may find a free, beginner-focused space too thin for their level.
