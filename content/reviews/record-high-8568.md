# Record High Review 2026: Free Taiwanese English Exam Prep Community

Record High (高中學測英文得高分全攻略社) is a free Skool community with 377 members, built specifically for Taiwanese high school students preparing to score well on English in their monthly exams and the "學測" college entrance exam. The description positions it as the fastest, most effective way to prepare for these tests from home.

## What's Actually Inside

According to the description, members get access to classmates from high schools across Taiwan working toward the same goal, guidance from two experienced cram-school (補教) teachers who accompany students through the process, free course resources, and emotional support alongside broader information about student life. That combination, peer community, teacher access, and free materials, mirrors the structure of Taiwan's private cram-school (補習班) culture but delivered through a Skool community rather than in-person classes. The description doesn't name the two teachers or detail exactly which exam-prep materials (past exams, vocabulary lists, writing practice) are included.

The emphasis on emotional support alongside academic content is notable given how much pressure Taiwanese high schoolers face around the 學測 entrance exam, a high-stakes test that shapes university admission. Framing the community as offering both peer solidarity and teacher guidance suggests it's trying to address exam-related stress as directly as it addresses English proficiency itself.

## Pros

- Completely free, lowering the barrier for students juggling exam prep costs alongside cram-school tuition.
- Two named "資深" (senior/experienced) cram-school teachers are cited as directly involved, rather than the content being purely self-study materials.
- Combines academic resources with peer support and general student-life information, addressing exam stress as well as content mastery.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Taiwanese high school students preparing for monthly English exams and the 學測 entrance exam who want free resources, peer accountability, and access to experienced cram-school teachers without additional cost.

## Who Should Skip It
