# AI Cyber Value Creators Review 2026: Is It Worth $47?

AI Cyber Value Creators is an 8,875-member Skool community built around "Secure Vibe Coding," the practice of using AI coding assistants like Claude Code without shipping security holes along with them. It's priced as a one-time $47 lifetime access fee rather than a recurring subscription, which already sets it apart from most of the communities in this niche. The pitch is straightforward: cybersecurity fundamentals meet AI tooling, aimed at founders and consultants who want to build and ship faster without getting burned by the AI-generated code they didn't fully audit.

## What's Actually Inside

The core of the community is the Secure Vibe Coding Masterclass, which walks members through using Claude Code and similar tools to launch AI-powered SaaS products while building in security practices from the start rather than bolting them on afterward. Alongside that sits a Personal AI Assistants course and an "AI Powered Delivery" framework, both aimed at helping members turn AI fluency into billable consulting work or their own products.

Members also get access to a library of Claude skills, prompts, and reusable tools, plus a running schedule of live workshops. One recent session covered how to start a business from scratch using the group's frameworks, which is typical of the cadence: less passive video-course consumption, more working sessions tied to a specific skill (threat hunting, zero trust architecture, cloud security, blockchain security) applied through an AI lens.

## Who Runs It

The founder is Dr. Allen Harper, a retired Marine officer with more than 35 years in cybersecurity. He's the lead author of "Gray Hat Hacking: The Ethical Hacker's Handbook, 6th Edition," a widely used reference text in the field, and has held cybersecurity executive roles at large companies as well as a teaching post at Liberty University [(allenharper.com)](https://allenharper.com/) [(LinkedIn)](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dr-allen-harper/). That's a genuinely unusual level of documented, pre-existing authority for a Skool community founder. Most cybersecurity-adjacent Skool groups are run by people who built their credibility inside the platform; Harper built his over three decades in the field first.

## Pros

- **Real, verifiable subject-matter authority.** A published, credentialed cybersecurity author leading the curriculum is rare in this space and easy to confirm independently.
- **One-time pricing.** At $47 for lifetime access (not a recurring monthly charge), the downside risk of trying it is low compared to nearly every other community in this review batch.
- **Practical focus.** The emphasis on securing AI-generated code addresses a real, current gap: a lot of vibe-coded products ship with avoidable vulnerabilities.

## Cons

Independent reviews of AI Cyber Value Creators specifically are thin. We found the founder's professional credentials well-documented across LinkedIn, his personal site, and Medium, but no third-party community reviews, complaint threads, or outcome case studies beyond what the community itself publishes. At 8,875 members and a low entry price, it's also worth noting that the free-tier-adjacent pricing model means the active, engaged core of contributors is likely smaller than the headline number suggests, which is typical for any low-cost, high-membership Skool group rather than a specific knock on this one.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Founders and consultants who are already shipping AI-assisted code and want a structured way to close the security gap, without paying a recurring fee to do it. Developers curious about the intersection of AI tooling and security practice will get real value from the masterclass structure. It's also a low-risk way to sample Dr. Harper's teaching before considering any of his other paid offerings.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners with no coding or business background may find the material assumes more context than a typical "AI for beginners" community. If you're looking for a large, highly active daily-discussion community rather than a course-plus-workshops structure, the relatively small admin team (two admins for nearly 9,000 members) suggests discussion volume may be lighter than the member count implies.
