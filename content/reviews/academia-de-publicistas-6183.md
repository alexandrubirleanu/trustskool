# Academia de Publicistas Review 2026: Free Path to Recurring Clients Abroad?

Academia de Publicistas is a free, Spanish-language Skool community with 1,218 members built around a specific professional identity: becoming a "publicista" (a marketing and advertising services provider) serving clients outside your home country. The community's own description makes a bold positioning claim: "Somos la #1era academia que forma publicistas" (we're the first academy that trains publicistas).

## What's Actually Inside

The stated goal is concrete: helping members land recurring clients who pay for services delivered from abroad ("clientes recurrentes que ofrece servicios en el exterior"). That framing points toward a remote-services business model, likely marketing, ads management, or related creative services sold to clients in wealthier markets, rather than a general "learn marketing" course. The recurring-client emphasis also suggests retainer-style work is the target outcome, not one-off freelance gigs.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing cost as a barrier to exploring the remote-services business model being taught.
- A specific target outcome (recurring international clients) rather than a vague "learn digital marketing" pitch.
- Spanish-language delivery aimed directly at a market often underserved by English-first freelance and agency education.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which particular services (ads, social media management, design, or something else) publicistas are trained to sell, so the exact skill set taught isn't fully clear from the listing alone. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool page, and "#1era academia" is a self-made claim rather than an independently verified ranking.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking marketers or aspiring freelancers who want to build a client base of international, recurring-revenue clients and are comfortable starting with a free community before any paid upsell.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone not interested in serving clients abroad specifically, or looking for a detailed, published curriculum before joining, will want more specifics than the public description currently offers. People without any marketing or advertising background at all may also find the jump to landing international clients steeper than the pitch implies.

Given the "#1era academia" claim is self-reported, weighing it against how the membership base has actually grown, and how active it stays, offers a more grounded read than the tagline alone.
