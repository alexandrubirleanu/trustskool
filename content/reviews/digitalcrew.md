# Digital Crew Review 2026: Is $10/Month Worth It for This Referral-Based Community?

Digital Crew is an English-language Skool community with 563 members priced at $10 a month, and its description states the core mechanic directly: earn $5 for every person you invite, framed as a way to earn passive income and learn to create more income streams.

## What's Actually Inside

The description names two components: a direct referral commission of $5 per invite, and separately, education on building additional income streams. This two-part structure, get paid to recruit plus learn other income ideas, is worth understanding clearly before joining, since the referral-commission piece works differently from a pure skills-education community: income here is tied at least partly to bringing in new paying members rather than to an external product or service being sold.

## Pros

- Transparent about the referral mechanic upfront, $5 per invite is a specific, stated number rather than vague "earn money" language.
- Low monthly cost limits downside if the referral income doesn't materialize as hoped.
- The added "learn to create more income streams" framing suggests some educational content beyond the referral program itself.

## Cons

- Recruitment-based commission structures, earning primarily by inviting others into the same paid community, carry a different risk profile than income built on an external skill, product, or service, so members should understand exactly how many invites are needed to offset the membership cost, and where that referral income actually comes from, before joining.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, and the "income streams" content isn't detailed beyond the referral mechanic.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People comfortable with referral or invite-based earning models who understand the mechanic clearly and want a low-cost way to test it.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who prefers income tied to an external skill, product, or client rather than recruiting new members into the same community, or people who want the broader "income streams" curriculum detailed before paying.
