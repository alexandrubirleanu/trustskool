# MB Conciergerie Academy Review 2026: Is This Free Concierge Business Community Worth Joining?

MB Conciergerie Academy is a free, French-language Skool community with 349 members positioned as "the club for concierge businesses that scale," covering the operational side of running and growing a short-term rental concierge company.

## What's Actually Inside

The description lists four concrete pillars: structuring the business, acquisition of clients or properties, recruitment of staff or partners, and real case studies, delivered alongside live sessions. It also mentions a free training plus private community as the entry offer, which suggests the Skool group functions as an ongoing extension of a lead training rather than a standalone course sitting entirely on its own. Covering recruitment alongside acquisition and structuring suggests the community is aimed at businesses that have moved past a single-property setup and are actively trying to build a team, not just automate a solo operation running out of one apartment.

That progression, from solo operator to team-based business, is a meaningfully different stage than most beginner-level real estate or hospitality content addresses, which typically stops at landing the first client or property.

## Pros

- Covers the full operational stack of a concierge business, structure, acquisition, and recruitment, rather than just marketing tips alone
- Includes real case studies and live sessions, which can be more concrete and useful than theory-only content delivered as static text
- Free entry point via community access plus a complimentary training lowers the barrier to evaluating the offer before spending anything

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify how much of the deeper content sits behind the free training versus requiring a further paid step later
- No independent reviews or member outcome data were found beyond the community's own listing describing its own program

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking operators already running, or actively launching, a short-term rental concierge or property management business who want practical, case-based guidance on structuring and scaling are the clearest fit. It also suits people evaluating whether to enter the conciergerie space before committing further resources, or existing operators specifically trying to move from solo work to a small team.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-French speakers will hit a language barrier immediately, since the community operates entirely in French. People outside the short-term rental or property concierge niche, or those looking for a fully self-contained course rather than a community tied to a lead training, may also find the fit less direct.
