# Méthode Trident Review 2026: Is This Free French Investing Community Worth Joining?

Méthode Trident is a 1,290-member French-language Skool community organized around a specific promise: spend 5 minutes a week to grow your savings across 15 of the best companies in the world. No membership fee is listed.

## What's Actually Inside

The headline hook here is the time commitment, 5 minutes weekly, paired with a fixed scope of 15 named companies rather than an open-ended universe of stock picks. That's a much more concrete promise than most generic "learn to invest" communities make, though the description alone doesn't say which 15 companies or how they were chosen.

## Pros

- No listed membership fee lowers the barrier to checking it out.
- The 5-minutes-a-week framing directly targets people who want exposure to investing without a large ongoing time commitment.
- A fixed, named scope of the 15 best companies is a more concrete promise than vague investing education, giving members a clear idea of what they're being shown.

## Cons

- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own platform listing.
- The description doesn't name the 15 companies or explain the selection methodology, so it's hard to judge from the listing alone whether this is educational content or a specific stock-picking service, a distinction worth confirming given the different risk profile of each.
- A fixed set of 15 companies, whatever they are, is inherently less diversified than a broad index fund, so members should understand the concentration risk that comes with following any small, named list rather than assuming it's automatically safer because it's curated.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking savers who want a low-time-commitment, structured approach to long-term investing in a defined set of companies. It's a reasonable starting point for someone who wants to be more hands-on with their savings than a robo-advisor but doesn't want to spend hours a week researching individual stocks.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-French speakers, and investors looking for actively managed or broadly diversified strategies rather than a fixed list of companies. Anyone uncomfortable with concentration risk, or who wants formal, regulated financial advice tailored to their personal situation, should treat this as general education rather than a substitute for that.
