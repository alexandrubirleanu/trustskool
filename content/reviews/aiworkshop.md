## What's Actually Inside

That two-part framing, build first, then sell, positions this closer to a service-business or agency-building community than a personal AI productivity course. Targeting AI agents and automation as a service to sell to businesses taps into a currently active market, where many entrepreneurs are exploring AI implementation as a paid offering rather than just a personal tool. The build-then-sell model has become a fairly common structure across AI-focused Skool communities over the past year or two, positioning members as AI implementation consultants for local or small businesses rather than as end users of AI tools themselves. That framing means success in this kind of community often depends as much on sales and client acquisition skills as on the technical build side, something worth keeping in mind since the description only names the building and selling outcomes without detailing how much of the curriculum covers each half.

- Targets a real, currently in-demand niche: AI agent building as a service for businesses.
- $77/month sits in a reasonable mid-range relative to many AI agency-style offers that charge considerably more.

## Cons

"AI employees" is marketing language rather than a defined technical concept, so it's worth clarifying exactly what tools and tech stack are taught before joining. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People who want to build a service business around AI automation or agents for clients, rather than just using AI for their own personal productivity.

Anyone wanting AI purely for personal use, with no interest in selling automation services to businesses. Complete beginners with no comfort at all working with software tools may also find the jump straight to building and selling AI agents steep without some prior groundwork.
