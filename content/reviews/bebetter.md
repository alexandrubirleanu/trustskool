# BeBetter Growth Academy Review 2026: Is the $30/Year Skool Worth It?

BeBetter (成長學院, "Growth Academy") is a Chinese-language Skool community with 552 members priced at $30 a year, an unusually low annual rate for a structured health program. It targets people who repeatedly regain weight, feel hungry easily, or feel they don't have time for typical diet plans, with a promised timeline: visible results in 28 days, life change in 90. Yo-yo weight regain is one of the most common frustrations reported by people who've tried multiple diet programs, so naming that specific pattern up front, rather than a generic "lose weight" pitch, signals the program is speaking directly to repeat dieters rather than first-timers.

## What's Actually Inside

According to the community's own description, the program runs on a weekly cadence: one task, one video, and one action card per week, combined with community support so members aren't managing the process alone. It also lists a nutritionist available to answer questions, regular health-focused livestreams, level-based unlockable content, and a "3R transformation" weekly task course. The described philosophy, "do it first, understand it later, then do it again for results," suggests a practical, action-first approach rather than heavy upfront theory.

## Pros

- $30/year is a notably low price point for a program that includes nutritionist access and weekly structured content.
- Explicit weekly structure (task, video, action card) gives clear, bite-sized commitments rather than an overwhelming all-at-once program.
- Addresses a specific, common frustration (yo-yo weight regain, hunger, lack of time) rather than generic "get healthy" messaging.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify the nutritionist's credentials or how much direct access members get, so the depth of professional support is unclear from outside.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform's own listing, so real member results over the stated 28/90-day timelines aren't independently verifiable.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Chinese-speaking members who have struggled with weight regain or feel they lack time for complicated diet plans, and who respond well to weekly, bite-sized tasks with community accountability rather than a rigid meal-by-meal plan.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone needing individualized medical nutrition therapy for a diagnosed condition should work with a licensed provider directly rather than a group program. Non-Chinese speakers will also find the community inaccessible given its language.
