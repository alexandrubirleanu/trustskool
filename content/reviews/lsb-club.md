# LSB Club Review 2026: Is This Free German Networking Community Worth Joining?

LSB Club is a free, German-language Skool community with 790 members. Its description positions it as the network for psychosocial counselors, referred to by the abbreviation LSB, built around growing together, networking together, and making the world a little better together.

## What's Actually Inside

The description names a clear, specific professional audience, psychosocial counselors, rather than a general self-help or coaching crowd. Three stated goals repeat the "together" theme: professional growth, networking, and a shared sense of collective impact, suggesting this functions as a peer-support and networking space for people already working in this field, rather than a training program for people entering it.

Professional networks built around a specific credential or job title tend to function differently from general-audience communities: the value often comes less from content delivery and more from peer connection, referrals, and shared professional identity among people navigating similar day-to-day challenges. Given the emphasis on "gemeinsam" (together) repeated three times in the description, that peer-support framing looks like the primary draw here rather than a structured curriculum.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier for professionals in the field to connect.
- Clearly defined professional audience rather than a general self-improvement community.
- Explicit focus on both growth and networking, not just passive content consumption.
- A profession-specific network can surface referral opportunities and shared challenges that a general-audience community wouldn't address.

## Cons

The description doesn't explain what qualifies someone as an "LSB" or what training the term implies, so outsiders to the German psychosocial counseling field won't immediately understand the credential. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing. It's also unclear from the description how active the group's discussion actually is day to day, versus functioning mainly as a directory of members.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking psychosocial counselors looking for a professional peer network to grow and connect with others in the field, particularly those working independently or in smaller practices who may otherwise lack easy access to peers facing similar day-to-day challenges.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone outside the psychosocial counseling profession, and non-German speakers who'd need networking content in another language. Those wanting formal continuing-education credits or a certification pathway may also want to confirm whether this network offers that or is purely informal peer connection.
