# AI Store Builder Review 2026: Is the Free Wix AI Store Community Worth It?

Instead of teaching ecommerce from scratch, AI Store Builder offers something more direct: sign up through aistorebuilder.com and get a free Wix AI-built store, already stocked with 20 trending products. That pitch has pulled in over 19,000 members to this free Skool community, which bills itself as the "#1 Free E-Commerce Community."

## What's Actually Inside

The core offer described is a done-for-you starting point rather than a course: a Wix-powered, AI-generated online store, pre-loaded with 20 products described as trending, accessed by signing up on an external site. The Skool community itself appears to function as the support and discussion layer around that tool, rather than a place delivering a full curriculum on its own. What's not specified is how "trending" products are selected, how often they're updated, or what happens after the free store is set up, whether hosting, fulfillment, or further customization requires a paid step.

## Who Runs It

Ramin Popal is listed as the owner of this community. No further background on him is available from the information reviewed here.

## Pros

- Free to join, with a concrete deliverable (a pre-built store with products already loaded) rather than just educational content.
- Removes a common early barrier to ecommerce, picking products and setting up a storefront, by automating both.
- A sizable community, over 19,000 members, for troubleshooting and comparing notes with others using the same tool.

## Cons

- The description doesn't explain what happens after the free store is created, so ongoing costs for hosting, apps, or product sourcing aren't disclosed here.
- No independent reviews were found to confirm how well the AI-selected "trending products" actually convert or sell.
- Relying on an external site (aistorebuilder.com) to access the core offer means the actual product experience isn't reviewable from the Skool listing alone.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Complete beginners to ecommerce who want the fastest possible path to a live-looking store without picking a niche or products themselves, and who don't mind using Wix as the underlying platform.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who wants to learn ecommerce fundamentals, like product research or supplier vetting, rather than skip straight to an automated store should look elsewhere. Sellers already committed to a different platform than Wix, or who are wary of "free" offers that may lead to paid upsells, should also read the fine print carefully before signing up.
