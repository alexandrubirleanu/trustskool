# Vegan Life Club Review 2026: Worth $9 for Long-Term Vegan Community?

Vegan Life Club is a $9 one-time-payment Skool community with 354 members built specifically for long-term vegans, not people just starting out. The listing describes it as a space for community, connection, vegan life discussions, friendship, support, and conversation that goes beyond the basics of "why go vegan."

## What's Actually Inside

The key distinction here is the audience: this targets vegans who already have the diet and ethics figured out and are looking for ongoing community and discussion, not beginner-level nutrition or transition advice. That framing suggests conversations around long-term lifestyle, social dynamics, and shared experience rather than "how to start" content.

Long-term vegans often describe a specific kind of fatigue with online vegan spaces that stay permanently focused on convincing newcomers or debating omnivores, rather than just being a place to talk about life as someone who's already made that choice years ago. A community explicitly positioned "beyond the basics" is speaking directly to that frustration, though how active the discussion actually is day to day isn't something the listing itself can confirm. At a one-time $9 price, the financial risk of testing that fit for yourself is about as low as it gets for a paid community.

## Pros

- Clear audience targeting (long-term vegans, not beginners), which filters out a lot of the entry-level content that dominates many vegan spaces online.
- Very low, one-time price ($9) rather than a recurring subscription, minimizing ongoing cost.
- Explicit focus on friendship and support, not just information, which may appeal to vegans feeling isolated in non-vegan social circles.

## Cons

- The listing doesn't detail specific content formats (events, challenges, resource libraries) beyond general discussion and connection.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Experienced, long-term vegans looking for community and social connection with people who share the lifestyle, rather than instructional content about veganism itself. It should also suit vegans who feel like the odd one out in their existing social circle and want a space where that isn't the case.

## Who Should Skip It

People new to veganism looking for beginner guidance, meal plans, or nutrition basics, since the community is explicitly positioned "beyond the basics." Anyone still deciding whether to go vegan at all would likely be better served by an introductory resource first.
