# Skoot Review 2026: Is It Worth $99/Month?

Its own description positions Skoot as "the Skool CRM," built to make running a Skool community easier while increasing its monthly revenue.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, this is a tool-plus-community bundle aimed specifically at people who already run a Skool community and want CRM-style features on top of it, tracking members, automating follow-up, or surfacing revenue opportunities that Skool's native interface doesn't handle. The community itself likely functions as support and product updates for paying CRM users, rather than a course teaching a separate skill.

The market for Skool-specific tooling has grown alongside Skool itself, with a handful of third-party CRMs and automation layers now competing to fill gaps in Skool's native admin and analytics tools. Anyone evaluating Skoot against alternatives should compare specifically on integration depth, how directly it syncs with Skool's own member and payment data, and total cost relative to the incremental revenue or time savings it's expected to generate for a community of a given size.

- Purpose-built for a specific, well-defined user, existing Skool community owners, rather than a generic audience.
- Directly tied to a stated business outcome (increasing monthly revenue) rather than abstract education.
- As a tool-focused community, members likely get direct product support alongside access.

## Cons

The description doesn't detail specific CRM features, integrations, or how it connects to Skool's own platform, so confirming exact functionality before paying is worth doing, especially whether it works via Skool's official API or some other integration method. We also found no independent reviews of this tool beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People who already run a paid or free Skool community and want CRM-style tools layered on top to manage members and revenue more systematically.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone not currently running a Skool community has no obvious use for this, and community owners happy with Skool's native tools may not need an added CRM layer. Very small or brand-new communities may also find the cost hard to justify until member volume grows enough to need dedicated CRM support.
