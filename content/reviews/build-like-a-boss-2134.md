# AI CodeHer Collective Review 2026: Free Tier or $17/Month Premium?

AI CodeHer Collective is a 2,153-member Skool community with a two-tier structure spelled out directly in its own description: "build AI tools and income, without coding, join free or upgrade to Premium for $17/month for courses, templates, and step-by-step training." The base community is free to join, with an optional paid layer on top.

## What's Actually Inside

The core promise is building AI tools and generating income from them without writing code, aimed at non-technical members. The free tier presumably offers community access and discussion, while the $17/month Premium tier is described as unlocking courses, templates, and step-by-step training, a more structured learning path than free discussion alone.

The no-code angle is a meaningful differentiator in the broader AI-education space, where a lot of content still assumes at least some comfort with APIs or scripting. Pairing that accessibility with a genuinely low $17/month price point for the structured tier keeps the overall financial commitment modest even for members who do decide to upgrade past the free level.

## Pros

- **Low-risk entry point.** Free access lets members evaluate the community before deciding on the $17/month upgrade.
- **No-code framing** removes a common barrier for people interested in AI tools who don't have a development background.
- **Affordable upgrade price.** At $17/month, the Premium tier is a low financial commitment relative to many paid AI communities.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify exactly what's included at the free level versus what's Premium-only, so the practical difference between tiers is unclear from the listing alone.
- No independent reviews of this specific community were found beyond the platform listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Non-technical people who want to build AI tools without coding and are comfortable testing free content first before deciding whether the $17/month Premium tier's courses and templates are worth the upgrade.

## Who Should Skip It

Developers who already code and want more advanced or technical AI training will likely find the no-code framing too introductory. People who want a single, clearly bounded course rather than an ongoing community-plus-upgrade structure may also prefer a more traditional one-time-purchase program instead.
