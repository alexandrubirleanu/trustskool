# Learn Power BI Review 2026: Is the $59/Month Power BI Community Worth It?

Learn Power BI is a paid Skool community, $59 a month, with 506 members, aimed at people who want to build real Power BI skills through practice rather than passive video-watching.

## What's Actually Inside

The description lays out four specific components: hands-on projects to practice through actual dashboards rather than theory alone, applying AI inside Power BI workflows, individual feedback on the dashboards members build, and building an outstanding portfolio, which points toward this being positioned partly as a career or job-search tool for aspiring data analysts, not just a hobbyist skills course.

The emphasis on individual feedback is worth noting specifically, since it implies a real person reviews member work rather than the community running purely on pre-recorded lessons. That kind of one-to-one review is typically the most valuable and the most resource-intensive part of any paid learning community, so it's also the piece most worth confirming before paying.

## Pros

- The project-based, feedback-driven structure is a concrete, verifiable claim about format, not vague marketing language.
- The portfolio-building focus suggests direct relevance for people trying to land data analyst roles, not just hobbyists.
- Including AI-in-Power-BI content keeps the curriculum current with where the tool and the job market are heading.

## Cons

- $59/month recurring adds up for a skill that many free tutorials also cover, so the value depends heavily on the quality of the individual feedback promised.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, so it's not possible to verify how responsive or in-depth that feedback actually is.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People actively trying to build a Power BI portfolio for job applications or freelance work, who want direct feedback rather than self-paced videos alone, and who are willing to put in the project work needed to actually get that feedback.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners not ready to commit monthly spend to a tool they haven't tried yet, and anyone hoping for a one-time purchase rather than an ongoing subscription. Analysts who already have a strong portfolio and just need a specific advanced technique may also find a targeted tutorial faster than a full membership.
