# Escape The 9 to 5 Review 2026: Is This Free Skool Community Worth Joining?

Escape The 9 to 5 has pulled together 10,903 members around a straightforward promise, without charging for access: help people quit their jobs and "design their life" while earning income in just one to three hours a day. Its own description opens with a specific line, "Freedom isn't earned through burnout," positioning it against hustle-culture messaging rather than alongside it.

## What's Actually Inside

The stated mission centers on replacing a traditional job with income that takes a limited number of hours daily, one to three, rather than promising full passive income with zero effort. That's a more measured claim than some "escape your job" communities make, though it's still an ambitious target that the description doesn't back up with specifics on which business models, offers, or skills members are taught to build that income around. Whether the path is freelancing, digital products, coaching, or something else entirely isn't named directly in the available description.

## Who Runs It

Brad Clemmons Jr is listed as the community's owner in the data reviewed. No further biographical detail is available here.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no upfront cost to see what the community offers before committing further time or money.
- The "1-3 hours a day" framing sets a more grounded expectation than promises of fully passive income.
- Explicitly rejects burnout-driven hustle as the model, which may resonate with people who've tried and rejected that approach before.

## Cons

- The description doesn't name the specific business model or income path being taught, which makes it hard to judge fit before joining.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, so outcomes and typical timelines to reach that "1-3 hours" state aren't verifiable.
- "Design your life" and "earn freedom" are broad, motivational phrases that could describe almost any income-focused community.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People currently working a traditional job who want to explore whether a lower-time-commitment income path is realistic for them, and who don't mind starting with a free community before deciding whether to invest further.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a fully passive, zero-effort income promise should be skeptical, since even a 1-3 hour daily commitment is still real, ongoing work. People who need a clearly named business model spelled out before joining anything may want more specifics than the current description provides.
