# Novolabs Startup School Review 2026: Free Startup Validation Community

Novolabs Startup School is a free Skool community with 1,155 members, run in Spanish and aimed at founders in Latin America and Spain. The core pitch is narrow and practical: validate your idea before you build it, backed by what the description calls daily content, real cases, and no theory.

## What's Actually Inside

The validate-before-you-build framing targets a specific and common founder mistake: spending months building a product before confirming anyone actually wants it. The description's emphasis on real cases over theory suggests the content leans on examples from actual startups rather than abstract frameworks, and daily content implies an ongoing stream of material rather than a one-time course members complete and then leave. The explicit LATAM and Spain focus also suggests the examples and market context are tailored to those regions specifically, rather than defaulting to US-centric startup advice translated into Spanish, which matters given how different fundraising, regulation, and customer behavior can be across those markets.

## Pros

- Free to join, which matters for early-stage founders who may not have revenue yet to spend on paid startup education or accelerator fees.
- A specific, actionable focus (idea validation) rather than trying to cover the entire startup journey at once.
- Regional specificity (LATAM and Spain) means examples and context are more likely to reflect the market realities founders in those regions actually face, compared with generic global startup content.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify the format of the daily content (short posts, video, live sessions), so the actual time commitment and depth aren't clear from the listing alone. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing, so real founder outcomes from using the validation approach aren't independently verified here, and founders should treat the daily content as a supplement to, not a replacement for, talking to their own actual customers.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Early-stage founders in Latin America or Spain who haven't built their product yet and want a free, example-driven approach to validating their idea before investing time and money in development.

## Who Should Skip It

Founders who have already built and validated their product, or those outside the LATAM and Spain focus looking for region-specific advice, may find less direct relevance here.
