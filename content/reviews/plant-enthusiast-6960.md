# Houseplant Enthusiasts Review 2026: Is This Free Skool Plant Community Worth It?

Houseplant Enthusiasts has quietly become one of the bigger free hobby groups on Skool, pulling in 16,735 members around a simple pitch: a supportive, informative space for plant people, whatever stage they're at. There's no course to buy and no funnel to opt into, just a community built around a shared interest.

## What's Actually Inside

The community's own welcome message keeps things broad on purpose. It says the mission is to create "a supportive and informative environment" for anyone interested in houseplants, explicitly welcoming people who are "just starting out" alongside those who consider themselves experts. That framing suggests a group organized around discussion, questions, and shared knowledge rather than a structured curriculum, more of a place to post a photo of a struggling fiddle-leaf fig and get advice than a step-by-step growing course.

Beyond that welcome statement, the description doesn't spell out specific features, whether there are plant ID threads, propagation swaps, or care guides for particular species. Anyone deciding whether to join is largely trusting the size of the membership and the stated tone rather than a detailed syllabus.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no financial risk in checking whether the community fits your interests.
- A large, established membership (16,735 people) suggests active participation rather than an empty forum.
- Explicitly welcoming to both beginners and experienced growers, rather than gatekeeping around skill level.

## Cons

- The description gives no detail on structure, features, or how discussions are organized inside the group.
- No independent reviews of the community were found beyond the Skool listing itself, so claims about the level of support can't be independently verified.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Anyone who owns or wants to own houseplants and would like a casual, ongoing place to ask questions, troubleshoot problems like yellowing leaves or pests, and swap tips with other growers. It's a reasonable first stop for beginners who don't yet know which plants suit their space, and for experienced collectors who enjoy helping newcomers.

## Who Should Skip It

People looking for a structured, paid course on plant care with defined lessons and milestones should look elsewhere, since this appears to be an open discussion community rather than a curriculum. Anyone hoping for niche specialization, such as a group focused only on rare aroids or bonsai, may also find the general "houseplants" framing too broad for their specific interest.
