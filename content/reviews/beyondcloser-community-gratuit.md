- Narrowly focused on closing specifically, rather than a broad "everything about sales" curriculum, which can make it easier to evaluate fit.
- The stated combination of skills and perceptions suggests attention to the mental or interpretive side of selling, not just memorized scripts.

## Cons

- Being free, it's also worth checking whether the group serves as a funnel toward a paid coaching offer, since the description doesn't state either way.

## What "Gratuit" Signals

The word "gratuit" (free) is even built into the community's own slug, which suggests the operators are deliberately positioning this as a no-cost entry point into their broader sales training ecosystem, rather than a one-off standalone resource. That's a common structure in the sales-training space generally: a free community for exposure and goodwill, alongside a separate paid program or coaching offer for members who want deeper support. None of that is confirmed here, but it's a reasonable expectation to bring into a first look at the group.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking salespeople, including closers working in high-ticket or appointment-based sales, who want to sharpen closing technique and are comfortable with a community-driven, discussion-based learning format.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-French speakers, and salespeople working in long-cycle, committee-based B2B environments where "closer" style techniques may translate less directly, might get more out of training built specifically around their sales context instead.
