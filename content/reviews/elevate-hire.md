## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the community centers on career transition support into specific, named tech roles, rather than teaching coding from scratch or general tech literacy. The "$100M+ in salaries" figure and "5+ years of wins" framing suggest the community leans on its own track record as social proof, implying a structured process for helping career-changers land roles in the named fields, with Tech Sales, Cybersecurity, and QA cited as example paths, "and more" suggesting others aren't listed.

Tech Sales, Cybersecurity, and QA are all frequently cited as accessible entry points into the broader tech industry for career-changers without a computer science background, since they typically emphasize domain knowledge and certifications over deep programming ability. A community organized around helping people break into those specific roles is targeting a well-established career-change pathway rather than inventing a new one, which at least suggests the target outcome itself is realistic and commonly pursued.

## Pros

- Free to join, appropriate for a career-transition community where cost shouldn't be a barrier for job seekers.
- Names specific target roles, Tech Sales, Cybersecurity, QA, rather than a vague "get into tech" pitch.
- Cites a track record, over $100M in member salaries, 5+ years, as part of its positioning.

## Cons

The $100M+ salary figure and "5+ years of wins" claims come from the community's own description and weren't independently verified for this review. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People looking to transition into tech careers in sales, cybersecurity, QA, or adjacent roles, who want a free community built around that specific transition.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone seeking to become a software engineer or developer specifically, rather than moving into the named non-engineering tech roles, may find the focus here doesn't match their goal. People who already work in tech and are looking for advancement or leadership content, rather than an entry-level career pivot, would also likely find the focus mismatched.
