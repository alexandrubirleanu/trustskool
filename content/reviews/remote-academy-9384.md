# Remote Academy Review 2026: Is This Free German Community Worth It for Going Remote?

Remote Academy is a 1,268-member German-language Skool community aimed squarely at beginners: you want to work remotely and live more flexibly but don't know where to start, and the community promises the steps and the community support needed to get going. No membership fee is listed.

## What's Actually Inside

The description explicitly targets people at the very beginning of considering remote work, not knowing where to start is the operative phrase, and pairs a step-based framework with peer community rather than positioning itself as a pure course. That combination, structure plus people, is a reasonable fit for a transition that can otherwise feel isolating. The emphasis on flexibility, not just remote work itself, also suggests the community frames the goal as lifestyle change broadly, rather than narrowly focused on landing a specific remote job title.

## Pros

- No listed membership fee removes financial risk for people just starting to explore the idea.
- It explicitly targets total beginners, rather than assuming existing remote-work experience.
- Combining structured steps with a community, rather than just a course, may help with the accountability and isolation issues that often come with a career transition.

## Cons

- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own platform listing.
- The description doesn't specify which remote career paths are covered, freelancing, remote employment, or building an online business, so how directly applicable the steps are to a specific goal isn't confirmed.
- A no-fee community aimed at total beginners can sometimes attract more people asking questions than people with answers, so it's worth checking how experienced the active membership actually is before expecting expert-level guidance.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German speakers early in exploring remote work who want both a starting framework and a peer community for support. It's probably most useful for people still employed in a traditional in-office role who are exploring whether and how to make the shift, rather than someone already working remotely.

## Who Should Skip It

People who already work remotely and want advanced career-scaling content, or non-German speakers. Anyone with a specific remote career path already chosen, say, a particular freelance skill or industry, may find more targeted value in a niche-specific community than a general "how to go remote" group.
