# Club Raw Review 2026: Is $39/Month Worth It for Raw Vegan Coaching?

With 646 members, it sits in a narrow lane within the broader plant-based and wellness space, raw veganism specifically, rather than vegan or vegetarian eating more broadly. That narrowness is likely the point: someone already committed to raw food gets a community built entirely around that specific way of eating, rather than diluted with general vegetarian content.

## What's Actually Inside

The community's own description points to three components: proven recipes for raw vegan meals, live coaching sessions, and a social element built around connecting with other people pursuing the same lifestyle. The word "proven" suggests the recipes have been tested with existing members, though the description doesn't say how many recipes are available or how frequently live coaching sessions run, and it doesn't clarify whether coaching happens in a group setting, one-on-one, or through recorded sessions members can watch on their own schedule. The heart symbol in the tagline signals a warmth-and-support tone rather than a strictly clinical or academic approach to raw veganism.

## Pros

- Focuses on a specific, less common dietary niche rather than generic plant-based content.
- Live coaching adds a real-time, interactive element beyond static recipes or videos.
- The community framing ("like-minded souls") suggests social support is part of the offer, which matters for a lifestyle change as demanding as going fully raw.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify how many recipes, how often coaching happens, or who leads it, so the actual volume of content is unclear before joining.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People already committed to or seriously exploring a raw vegan diet who want structured recipes plus live support, rather than trying to figure out raw veganism entirely on their own.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for general vegan or vegetarian content without the strict raw-food angle will likely find this too narrow. People who prefer self-paced, written material over live coaching sessions may also find the format less useful, and anyone with specific dietary or medical restrictions should confirm with a doctor or dietitian before adopting a fully raw diet based on community content alone.
