# Rich Credit Academy Review 2026: Is It Worth $89/Month?

Rich Credit Academy charges $89 a month for a 471-member community pitched as a wealth-building academy, offering free credit repair guidance plus step-by-step education on saving, budgeting, credit, homeownership, and business credit.

## What's Actually Inside

The description lists several distinct financial topics, credit repair, saving, budgeting, credit building, homeownership, and business credit, suggesting a broad personal-finance curriculum rather than a single-issue credit repair service. Notably, the description says credit repair guidance itself is free, which raises the question of what exactly the $89/month covers if repair guidance is included at no cost. It's likely the structured, step-by-step education across the other topics, or ongoing coaching and community access, though the listing doesn't spell out that distinction directly. Covering both personal topics (saving, budgeting, homeownership) and business credit in the same community suggests it's aimed at members trying to build wealth on multiple fronts at once, not just fix a credit score.

At 471 members and $89 a month, this sits toward the higher end of the price range for a financial education community, which typically signals more hands-on coaching, live sessions, or done-with-you support rather than a purely self-serve content library. Anyone considering the cost should weigh it against the specific goals they're bringing in, since a broad program covering six or more financial topics may deliver less depth on any single one of them than a narrower, dedicated credit-repair or homeownership-specific service.

## Pros

- Covers a genuinely broad set of financial building blocks, credit, saving, budgeting, homeownership, and business credit, in one place.
- Framed around step-by-step guidance, which should appeal to people who want structured direction rather than scattered financial tips.
- Positions credit repair help as free, which could lower the perceived risk of the paid tier if that guidance is a meaningful part of the offer.

## Cons

- The description doesn't clarify exactly what's included in the $89/month tier versus what's free, so the actual paid value is hard to judge from the listing alone.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People actively working on multiple financial goals at once, credit repair, saving habits, budgeting, and possibly homeownership or business credit, who want structured, step-by-step guidance in one community.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a narrow, single-issue credit repair service rather than a broader financial education program, or those unwilling to commit to a recurring $89 monthly cost without more clarity on what it includes.
