# The Porch Review 2026: Free Community for Old House Restoration

The Porch is a free Skool community with 1,143 members built for a very specific type of person: someone who loves old houses and wants to learn how to restore one, ideally alongside other people doing the same thing. The name itself, evoking a literal front porch, fits the community's stated purpose of connection as much as instruction.

## What's Actually Inside

The description is short but clear: this is a community for old house lovers who want to learn to restore their homes and connect with others. That two-part framing, learning plus connecting, suggests the value here is both practical and social. On the practical side, expect restoration knowledge likely covering things like period-appropriate materials, structural issues common to older homes, and DIY versus contractor decisions. On the social side, it's a place to talk to other people tackling similar projects, which matters a lot in a hobby where mistakes can be expensive and isolating. At 1,143 members and free to join, the community appears built to gather as many old-house owners as possible rather than restrict access, which fits a hobby where more members generally means more varied restoration experience to draw on.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no cost barrier to seeing whether the community matches your specific restoration project or house era.
- Clear, narrow niche of old house restoration specifically, rather than general home improvement, which tends to produce more relevant advice for period homes.
- Explicit emphasis on connection, not just content, useful for a hobby that can otherwise feel like a solo grind.
- No membership fee means there's no pressure to justify ongoing costs, making it low-friction to join even just to browse before starting a project.

## Cons

- No independent reviews or outside coverage were found beyond the Skool listing itself, so activity level and expertise depth aren't independently verifiable.
- The description doesn't specify what era, style, or region of homes the community focuses on, so fit may vary depending on your specific house.
- As a free, member-driven space, the depth and accuracy of restoration advice likely varies by who happens to be active and responding at any given time.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Owners of older homes who are restoring, or planning to restore, them and want both practical guidance and a community of people doing the same work fit this community's stated purpose directly.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone working on new construction or modern renovation, rather than restoration of an older home, is outside this community's stated focus.
