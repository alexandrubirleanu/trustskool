# Writers Block Review 2026: Is This Free Community Worth It for Finishing Your Book?

Writers Block is free to join, has 625 members, and makes an unusually concrete promise, per its own description: helping aspiring and struggling authors complete and publish their book within 90 days, rather than leaving "someday I'll finish it" open-ended.

## What's Actually Inside

The description frames the offer around a specific outcome and timeline, going from an unfinished manuscript to a completed, published book in 90 days. It explicitly names "struggling" authors alongside aspiring ones, which suggests accountability and unblocking are part of the pitch, not just fresh instruction for people starting from zero. It doesn't specify genre focus or whether guidance leans toward self-publishing or traditional routes.

The specific call-out of "struggling" authors is notable because it signals the community is designed to address writer's block and stalled projects directly, rather than assuming every member is starting a manuscript from a blank page. At 625 members and free to join, Writers Block appears built to scale through word-of-mouth among writers who've tried and stalled elsewhere, a different audience than a pure beginners' course.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier to testing the accountability structure.
- A clear, time-boxed outcome, 90 days, rather than open-ended "eventually" writing advice.
- Explicitly targets both aspiring and stuck writers, a wider net than beginner-only programs.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify what happens after 90 days if a manuscript isn't finished.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, so completion rates are unverified here.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Authors with a manuscript already in progress, or ready to start one, who want a structured deadline and accountability to actually finish it. It's a particularly good match for people who've started and stalled on a book before, since the "struggling authors" framing directly speaks to that experience.

## Who Should Skip It

Writers who prefer an unstructured, deadline-free creative process may find the 90-day framing counterproductive. Anyone specifically seeking literary agent or traditional publishing house guidance, rather than a completion-focused peer program, should look elsewhere too, since the description doesn't mention agent connections or querying support.
