## What's Actually Inside

Naming three specific business models, coaching, consulting, and infoproduct businesses, rather than a general "entrepreneurs" label, signals the content is tailored to service-based and knowledge businesses rather than e-commerce, agencies, or SaaS. The phrase "à fort impact" (high-impact) implies the community isn't aimed at people just starting out, but at practitioners who already have some traction and are now trying to move from working directly with clients to a more scalable structure, whether that's productized offers, group programs, or a team. "Mettent le cap sur le scaling" (setting a course toward scaling) reinforces that this is framed as a forward-looking, in-progress journey rather than a finished playbook, so the content is likely process-oriented around the transition itself. That transition, from trading hours for client work to building something that grows independently of the founder's own calendar, is a distinct enough challenge that a community built specifically around it could offer more relevant peer support than a general entrepreneurship group where most members are still at the client-acquisition stage.
