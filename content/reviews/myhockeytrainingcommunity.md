## What's Actually Inside

The weekly classes detail is the most concrete part of the pitch, it implies a recurring cadence of instruction rather than a static library of resources that never gets refreshed. Paired with free resources and a community element, the structure looks aimed at players who want ongoing, structured practice rather than a one-time course they complete and leave. Free training hubs built around a weekly cadence like this typically post drill breakdowns, off-ice conditioning tips, and game-review discussion between sessions, though the description doesn't confirm exactly which of those show up here or whether the weekly classes are live, recorded, or written.

## Pros

- No cost to join, so players and parents can try it before deciding whether it's worth their time.
- Weekly classes suggest a regular rhythm of new content rather than a stagnant resource dump.
- The "supportive community" framing signals a social, accountability-driven element alongside the technical training.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify skill levels covered, whether this suits beginners, competitive players, or both, so it's worth checking a few posts before assuming fit.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, and no lead coach or instructor is named publicly.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Hockey players, or parents of young players, looking for a free, ongoing source of training content and a community of other players working on the same goals.

## Who Should Skip It

Elite or highly competitive players seeking individualized coaching or advanced tactical breakdowns may find a general, free training hub too broad for their needs.
