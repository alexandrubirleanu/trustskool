# Love With Integrity Review 2026: Is Silvy & Bryan's $7/Month School Worth It?

Love With Integrity describes itself as a modern relationship school run by Silvy Khoucasian and Bryan Reeves, with 522 members paying $7 a month for access. The pitch is straightforward: learn the most essential skills for healthy, fulfilling relationships.

## What's Actually Inside

The description centers on skills rather than quick fixes, framing the community as a school rather than a support group or dating-advice feed. That word choice suggests structured teaching, likely covering communication, conflict, and connection patterns that apply whether someone is single, dating, or partnered. Having two named facilitators rather than one also implies the content may draw on two perspectives, which can be useful in a relationship-focused space where different viewpoints (and sometimes different genders) matter to how advice lands.

At $7 a month, the price point is low enough that it reads more like a low-commitment entry point than a premium coaching program, which fits the "school" framing of ongoing, incremental learning rather than a one-time intensive.

Calling itself a "modern relationship school" also signals a departure from more traditional, faith-based, or purely academic relationship education. That framing usually appeals to people who want practical, contemporary language around attachment, communication, and connection rather than dated dating-advice tropes, and it fits with a low-cost, ongoing membership model rather than a single big-ticket course purchase.

## Pros

- Very low monthly cost ($7) makes it accessible to try without a major financial commitment
- Two named facilitators suggest a blended teaching perspective rather than a single coach's viewpoint
- Positioned around teachable skills rather than vague inspiration, which tends to translate into more actionable content

## Cons

- No independent reviews were found beyond the Skool listing, so there's no outside confirmation of how the curriculum plays out in practice
- The description doesn't specify whether content is aimed at singles, couples, or both, so prospective members may want to check the community preview before joining to confirm it matches their relationship stage

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Anyone wanting affordable, skills-based relationship education from two named facilitators, especially people who prefer structured lessons over open-ended discussion groups.

## Who Should Skip It

Those looking for one-on-one coaching or couples therapy, since a $7/month group community is built for shared learning, not individualized clinical support.
