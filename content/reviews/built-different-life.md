# Built Different Review 2026: Is This Free Strength Training Community Worth Joining?

Built Different is a free, 632-member Skool community built around a specific training philosophy rather than a generic "get fit" pitch: ego-free training, joint-friendly volume, and mind-muscle focus, paired with what its own description calls "straight talk on what actually works." It has also expanded into physical products, with a Tempered Formulas supplement line now available alongside the community.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the training approach deliberately avoids ego lifting, chasing heavy numbers at the expense of joint health, in favor of higher training volume delivered in a joint-friendly way, combined with a mind-muscle connection focus more associated with bodybuilding-style hypertrophy training than powerlifting. The addition of a branded supplement line, Tempered Formulas, suggests the community also functions as a funnel toward a related product business, not purely educational content.

Ego-free, joint-friendly training philosophies have gained real traction in recent years as more lifters in their thirties, forties and beyond look for ways to keep training hard without accumulating the same wear and tear as heavier, ego-driven lifting styles. The Tempered Formulas supplement line being available alongside the community is common in fitness-education spaces, but it's worth treating supplement recommendations as a separate purchasing decision from the training philosophy itself, and evaluating each on its own merits.

## Pros

- Free to join the core community, with a clearly stated training philosophy rather than vague general fitness advice.
- Joint-friendly, ego-free framing specifically targets people who've been injured or burned out by aggressive lifting styles elsewhere.
- Mind-muscle focus aligns with a body of exercise science around hypertrophy training, rather than just chasing one-rep maxes.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify coaching format, whether that's live check-ins, pre-recorded programs, or one-on-one access, and the connection to a paid supplement line is worth being aware of before joining. We also found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Lifters dealing with joint pain or burnout from aggressive training styles who want a philosophy built around sustainable volume and mind-muscle connection.

## Who Should Skip It

Powerlifters or strength athletes specifically chasing one-rep max numbers, rather than hypertrophy-style training, are likely a mismatch with this approach.
