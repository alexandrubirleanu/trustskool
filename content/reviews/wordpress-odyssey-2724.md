# WP Odyssey Review 2026: A Free WordPress Basics Community

WP Odyssey (FREE TRIAL) is a free Skool community with 819 members built for people learning WordPress from the ground up. Its description keeps the promise simple and concrete: learn WordPress basics, build a first real website, and get weekly help inside the community, three clearly scoped goals rather than an open-ended "learn web development" pitch.

## What's Actually Inside

The three named elements point to a beginner-focused, project-based structure: foundational WordPress skills, likely covering the dashboard, themes, plugins, and basic site structure, a guided path to actually publishing a real website rather than only completing exercises, and a recurring weekly help session or thread for members stuck on specific problems. That combination, learn plus build plus ongoing support, is a reasonable structure for absolute beginners who need both instruction and troubleshooting help as they go.

WordPress runs a large share of websites worldwide, but its flexibility, themes, plugins, page builders, and settings that can be configured many different ways, is also what makes it confusing for first-timers who don't yet know which choices actually matter. A community explicitly built around getting one real site published, rather than covering every possible WordPress configuration, is a reasonable way to keep beginners from getting stuck in decision paralysis before they've built anything at all.

## Pros

- Free to join, appropriate for WordPress beginners not ready to invest money before knowing if the platform suits their needs.
- Outcome-focused goal, build your first real website, rather than just consuming lessons passively.
- Weekly help built into the offer, giving beginners a recurring point of contact rather than a one-time course with no follow-up support.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify whether hosting, domain costs, or premium themes and plugins are covered, all of which carry their own costs beyond the free community itself. No independent reviews of this specific community were found beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Complete WordPress beginners who want to build a first real website with structured guidance and ongoing weekly support, without paying for a course upfront.

## Who Should Skip It

Experienced WordPress users or developers looking for advanced customization, custom theme development, or performance optimization content will likely find the basics focus too introductory. Anyone expecting hosting or domain costs to be included for free should clarify that before joining.
