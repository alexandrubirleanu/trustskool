# Generational Revival Review 2026: Is This Free Homesteading and Faith Community Worth Joining?

Generational Revival has quietly built a community of over 27,000 members without charging a cent for access. Its stated mission, in its own words, is to help a million people "reconnect with what's real" and eventually build a homestead village to live the way its founders believe God intended.

## What's Actually Inside

Two threads run through the description: a faith-based worldview and a practical homesteading goal. This isn't just a spirituality discussion group, it's oriented toward an actual outcome, a shared homestead village, which implies the community likely covers land, self-sufficiency skills, and traditional living alongside faith content. That combination of the spiritual and the practical is fairly distinct within Skool's spirituality category, where many groups stick to one lane or the other.

What isn't clear from the description is how far along the "homestead village" project actually is, whether it's an active land project members can join, a long-term aspiration, or something in between, and what proportion of the community's activity is discussion versus hands-on planning.

## Who Runs It

The data lists Paul Ziegler as the community's owner. Beyond that, no additional background is available in the fields provided for this review, so no claims are made here about his history or credentials.

## Pros

- Free to join, which removes the financial barrier for people simply curious about the homesteading and faith angle before committing further.
- A clearly stated, ambitious mission (reaching a million people, building an actual homestead village) rather than vague self-improvement language.
- Combines practical self-sufficiency goals with a shared faith framework, which may appeal to members who want both in one place rather than two separate communities.

## Cons

- The description doesn't explain what stage the homestead village project is actually at, so members hoping to physically participate should ask directly before assuming it's ready-made.
- No independent reviews were available to confirm whether the community delivers practical homesteading skills or leans more toward discussion and inspiration.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People with a Christian faith orientation who are drawn to homesteading, self-sufficiency, and traditional community living, and who want to connect with others working toward similar goals rather than going it alone.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a secular homesteading or off-grid living community, since faith is explicitly woven into the mission here, will likely find this framing doesn't match what they want. Members expecting an already-built, move-in-ready homestead village rather than a developing community project should also temper expectations until more details are confirmed.
