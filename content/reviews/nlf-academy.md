# NLF Academy Review 2026: Is It Worth Joining?

NLF Academy, short for "Learn French," is a free, 938-member Skool community with a specific pitch: an all-included French course and practice, plus training for the TEF, TCF, and CLB7 exams. Those three tests are commonly required for Canadian immigration, permanent residency, and work permit applications, which gives the community a concrete practical goal rather than a general "learn French" promise.

## What's Actually Inside

The "all-included" framing suggests course content and practice material are bundled together rather than sold as separate add-ons, with a specific focus on the exam formats used by immigration authorities. That's a meaningfully different product than a conversational French club: TEF, TCF, and CLB7 prep implies structured practice tests, scoring criteria, and format-specific strategy, not just casual chat practice. Because these tests are tied to concrete immigration deadlines for many learners, a community built around them likely also carries a fair amount of shared urgency and mutual accountability among members working toward similar test dates. That shared context, everyone working toward the same kind of exam under similar life pressure, is arguably as valuable as the course material itself for many immigration-track learners.
