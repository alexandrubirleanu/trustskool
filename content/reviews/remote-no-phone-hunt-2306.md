# The No Phone Hunt Review 2026: Is $35 Worth It for No-Phone Job Leads?

The No Phone Hunt is a Skool community with 1,793 members, priced at a one-time $35 rather than a recurring subscription. It targets a specific, underserved slice of the remote-work job market: work-from-home roles that don't require phone calls, posted daily according to the community's own description, along with prefilled resume templates included as part of membership.

## What's Actually Inside

The core value proposition here is narrow and practical: a daily feed of remote job leads specifically filtered for roles that avoid phone-based tasks, think chat, email, or written-communication roles rather than call-center-style positions. The inclusion of "prefilled" resumes suggests members also get resume templates or examples geared toward these no-phone roles, saving some of the application prep work. The description doesn't specify how leads are sourced, how exclusive they are, or how quickly they go stale, details that matter a lot in any job-leads community.

## Pros

- A one-time $35 payment rather than an ongoing subscription, which caps the cost regardless of how long you use it.
- Addresses a specific, real pain point: finding remote work that avoids phone-based communication, a genuine subset of the broader work-from-home market.
- Prefilled resume templates included, reducing some of the friction in applying to the leads posted.

## Cons

Job-lead communities live or die on how fresh and exclusive the leads actually are, something the public description can't verify on its own. We also found no independent reviews of this community, so there's no outside confirmation of lead quality or how often the "daily" postings are actually happening.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People specifically seeking remote roles that avoid phone-based tasks, who want a curated daily feed of leads plus ready-made resume templates rather than searching general job boards themselves.

## Who Should Skip It

People comfortable with phone-based remote work will have access to a much larger overall pool of job leads elsewhere and don't need this narrower filter. Anyone wary of paying for job leads without first confirming lead freshness and quality should ask current members directly before paying.

People who've already built a strong personal network for remote job referrals may also find less marginal value here compared to someone starting a remote job search from scratch.
