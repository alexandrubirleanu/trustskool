# ContractorBnB Mentorship Review 2026: Is It Worth $97/Month?

ContractorBnB Mentorship is a UK-focused property community with more than 2,343 members, built around a specific niche inside serviced accommodation: renting properties to contractors on long-term stays instead of running traditional tourist Airbnbs. It's run by Ryan Luke and priced at roughly $97/month (listed as £77 on the community's own Skool page), with a 7-day free trial that lets new members cancel before day eight with no charge ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/thecontractorbnbcampus/about)).

## What's actually inside

The model taught is straightforward and narrow by design: instead of competing on seasonal, review-dependent tourist bookings, members target contractors who need 90 to 365+ night stays, which the community argues produces steadier occupancy and less price competition than short-let tourism. The page states the target outcome is an £8,000-£10,000-per-month ContractorBnB business, and describes an associated lending arrangement offering 100% financing with no deposit for qualifying purchases. Content is exclusively scoped to the UK property market, so anyone outside the UK, or targeting a different country's rental laws and mortgage products, isn't the intended audience. The about page itself is relatively thin on a detailed module-by-module breakdown, leaning more on the business-model pitch than a syllabus.

## Who runs it

Ryan Luke has a substantial, independently verifiable public track record predating this Skool community. His own materials describe generating more than £20 million in Airbnb revenue since 2018, having personally run as many as 450 tourist Airbnb units before pivoting the bulk of his portfolio to the ContractorBnB model in 2021, and currently running a leaner operation of 8-10 high-profit houses acquired per year with a team of seven ([ryan-luke.com](https://ryan-luke.com/about)). He has an active YouTube channel built around the ContractorBnB brand specifically and states he's helped more than 1,700 students adopt the model ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCIB8yF53rjKtNzADdXkGFhQ)). His broader e-commerce shop and course business also carries public Trustpilot reviews, giving outside parties a way to sanity-check his customer service track record beyond this one community ([Trustpilot](https://uk.trustpilot.com/review/ryan-luke.com)).

## Pros

- Ryan Luke's track record (specific revenue figures, a named pivot story, an ongoing YouTube presence under his own name) is more independently checkable than most single-founder Skool communities in this price range.
- The ContractorBnB niche itself is a genuinely differentiated angle on serviced accommodation, addressing real, well-known pain points of tourist-Airbnb hosting (seasonality, review dependency, race-to-the-bottom pricing).
- A real 7-day free trial with a clean cancellation window before any charge is a lower-risk way to sample the material than a no-refund policy.

## Cons

- Strictly UK-only: the property, tax, mortgage, and lending specifics taught don't transfer to other countries, so international searchers should look elsewhere.
- The £8-10k/month outcome is the pitched target, not a stated average across the full membership, and the about page doesn't break down what a typical (versus best-case) member actually earns.
- The Skool about page itself is light on granular curriculum detail, so prospective members are relying more on Ryan Luke's outside reputation than on a clear syllabus before paying.

## Who it's a good fit for

UK-based property owners or aspiring investors, especially ones already running or considering short-let Airbnb properties who are feeling the seasonality and review-treadmill problems of tourist-focused hosting, are the clearest fit. It also suits people who put real weight on being able to verify a founder's track record outside the sales page itself.

## Who should skip it

Anyone outside the UK, or without access to UK property and financing, should skip this one entirely, since the model is built around UK-specific mechanics. Complete beginners with no property at all yet may also want a broader UK property-investing foundation before narrowing straight into this specific niche.
