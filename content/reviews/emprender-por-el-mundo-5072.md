# Emprender Por El Mundo Review 2026: Free Community for Migrant Women Entrepreneurs?

Emprender Por El Mundo is a free Skool community with 413 members built around a specific and somewhat unusual audience: women entrepreneurs who have emigrated to other countries around the world. The description frames it as a unique opportunity to meet, share, and draw inspiration from other migrant women entrepreneurs, exchange experiences, and form alliances.

## What's Actually Inside

The described audience combines two identities at once, being an entrepreneur and being a migrant, an intersection that shapes the kinds of challenges members likely share, from navigating business regulations in a new country to building networks from scratch after relocating. Exchanging experiences and creating alliances suggests the community leans toward peer support and potential collaboration between members rather than formal, structured business training delivered top-down. Given no single country is specified as the community's base, it likely draws Spanish-speaking women entrepreneurs from across many different destination countries rather than being tied to one region. That range of destination countries could be a strength, exposing members to a wider variety of regulatory and market conditions, or a limitation, since advice that works for someone who emigrated to Spain may not transfer cleanly to someone who emigrated to Mexico or the United States.

## Pros

- Free to join, lowering the barrier for a specific, often underserved audience: migrant women entrepreneurs.
- Speaks directly to the compounded challenges of building a business as a migrant, rather than treating entrepreneurship and migration as separate topics.
- Frames alliance-building as an explicit goal, suggesting potential for real collaboration between members beyond passive content consumption.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify which countries or regions members are based in, so the practical relevance of shared advice may vary by destination country.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond the platform listing itself, and no member success stories or business outcomes are cited in the description.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking women entrepreneurs who have emigrated to another country and want a peer community that understands both the entrepreneurial and migrant experience together.

## Who Should Skip It

Entrepreneurs who haven't migrated, or non-Spanish speakers, are likely to find less of the shared context that makes this community's peer support relevant. People looking for formal, credentialed business mentorship rather than peer exchange between women in similar life circumstances may also want a different kind of program.
