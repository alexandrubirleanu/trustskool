# Freedom Creator Club Review 2026: Is $99/Month Worth It for YouTube Digital Products?

Freedom Creator Club is a 717-member Skool community priced at $99 a month, built around teaching members to create and sell their own digital products using YouTube as the growth engine. Notably, the community's own description flags that this price is temporary, stating it will rise to $198 a month "soon," so anyone considering joining should factor in that the current rate may not last.

## What's Actually Inside

The pitch centers on a specific combination: digital product creation paired with YouTube as the distribution and audience-building channel, aimed at people who want what the description calls a "freedom-focused business." That suggests content covering both the product side, building something sellable, and the channel side, growing a YouTube presence that can sell it, rather than treating those as two separate skills taught elsewhere.

YouTube-driven digital product businesses typically rely on the channel to build trust and an audience over time, with the products themselves, templates, courses, guides, sold to that built-up audience rather than through cold advertising. If that's the underlying model here, members would likely need patience for the channel-growth side of the equation, since audience-building on YouTube tends to compound slowly before a digital product launch actually converts well.

## Pros

- Specific channel focus, YouTube, rather than a scattershot, platform-agnostic approach to digital products.
- Transparent pricing history: the community discloses the planned price increase rather than burying it.
- Targets people wanting a "freedom-focused" business model, appealing to those prioritizing flexibility over scale.

## Cons

At $99 a month with a stated increase to $198 coming, this sits at the pricier end for a digital-products community, and the value has to be weighed against that trajectory. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing to verify results members are getting.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Aspiring creators who already have or want to build a YouTube channel and are looking to monetize it through digital products, and who are comfortable with a premium monthly price that's set to rise.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone not planning to use YouTube specifically, or who's price-sensitive given the planned jump to $198 a month, may want to look at lower-cost alternatives first. Creators without any existing audience or channel who want a faster path to revenue may also find the timeline here longer than expected.
