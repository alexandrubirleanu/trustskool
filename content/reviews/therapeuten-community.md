# Therapeuten Community Review 2026: Is It Worth Joining?

Therapeuten Community is a free, 930-member German-language Skool community pitched as a community of therapists on the path to growing personally, professionally, and entrepreneurially.

## What's Actually Inside

The key detail is who this is for: therapists themselves, not their clients. The description lays out three distinct growth tracks, personal development, professional or clinical skill, and the business side of running a therapy practice, suggesting the community serves as a peer network for practitioners navigating all three at once, including the entrepreneurial side of practice-building that many clinicians get little formal training in. Framing all three tracks together, personal, professional, and business, also suggests the community treats a therapist's own wellbeing as connected to their clinical effectiveness and practice sustainability, rather than treating those as unrelated concerns. That combined framing is fairly distinct from most professional communities, which tend to focus narrowly on either clinical skill-building or business growth, rarely both together with personal development as a third explicit pillar.

## Pros

- Free to join
- Narrowly targeted at practicing or training therapists as professionals, keeping discussion relevant to practice-building rather than general self-help
- Explicitly covers the business side of running a practice, an area many clinical trainings skip over

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify which therapy modalities, licensing bodies, or countries are represented among members, which matters for how directly relevant regulatory or business discussion will be
- No independent reviews found beyond the platform listing

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Therapists and counselors wanting a peer community focused on both clinical growth and the business realities of running a practice. It should also suit therapists early in private practice who want peer input on the business side that clinical training programs typically don't cover.

## Who Should Skip It

People looking for therapy or client-facing mental health support themselves, since this community is built for practitioners, not their clients.

## Bottom Line

Free membership and a clear practitioner-only focus make this worth trying for German-speaking therapists specifically, though the value will depend heavily on how many active members are working in a similar regulatory or practice context to your own. Therapists just starting to build a private practice are likely to get the most immediate value from the business-focused thread of discussion here, and since membership costs nothing, testing that fit carries no financial downside.
