# Agentic AI for Founders Review 2026: Is It Worth $97/Month?

Agentic AI for Founders is a 3,845-member Skool community teaching founders to build AI agents that function like "digital employees," living inside the tools a business already uses rather than requiring a separate platform migration. Membership is $97/month, with the community's own about page stating the price rises to $147 once membership hits 4,000, a detail worth knowing if you're weighing joining now versus later.

## What's Actually Inside

The curriculum is built around step-by-step modules for identifying which workflows in a business are actually worth automating, then building AI agents to handle them, covering content, customer support, operations, and growth functions. Members get live group sessions, peer feedback on agent builds, and worked examples pulled directly from founder Luke Cutting's own "Jarvis" AI agent setup, which he describes as the origin story for the whole community. There's also an emphasis on OpenClaw-style agent orchestration for chaining multi-step workflows, and the community occasionally features guest experts and references an associated AI Agents Conference for founders wanting to go beyond tool use into running AI-native operations.

## Who Runs It

Luke Cutting is a software engineer and co-founder of Azaris AI. According to his own account, after a $10,000 marketing team underdelivered for his app, he built Jarvis, an AI operator managing a team of specialized agents handling content, support, and product work, and says it tripled his app's revenue within a month [(lukebuildsai.com)](https://www.lukebuildsai.com/) [(Instagram: @lukebuildsai)](https://www.instagram.com/lukebuildsai/). A video about that experience is what grew his following into the current 3,800+ member community. That's a specific, checkable origin story rather than a generic "AI expert" claim, though the revenue tripling figure itself is self-reported and not independently audited.

## Pros

- **Concrete, first-person case study at the center of the teaching.** The Jarvis build is a real, specific example members can study rather than an abstract framework.
- **Founder-led with an identifiable public presence**, including a personal site and active social accounts, rather than an anonymous handle.
- **Practical workflow-identification focus.** Teaching founders how to spot which tasks are worth automating, before touching the technical build, addresses a step many automation courses skip.

## Cons

We found the founder's public presence and origin story well-documented, but the core revenue claim ("3x'd his app's revenue") is self-reported without independent verification, standard for this kind of founder-led marketing but worth treating as a claim rather than a fact. Independent third-party reviews of this specific community (beyond its own about page and social presence) were limited in our research, so prospective members are relying more heavily on the founder's public track record than on outside member testimonials.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Founders who already run a business with real operational workflows (content, support, ops, or growth processes) that could plausibly be automated, and who want to learn agent-building applied directly to their own company rather than as a service they'd sell to others. It's a more founder-facing angle than the agency-building communities elsewhere in this niche, which target people building automations for other businesses.

## Who Should Skip It

People without an existing business or clear workflows to automate may find the material less immediately actionable, since so much of the teaching is anchored in applying agents to a founder's own operation. Anyone specifically wanting to build a client-facing automation agency, rather than automate their own company, will likely find more directly relevant material in agency-focused communities in this same space.
