# Networth Builders Review 2026: Is It Worth $1?

Networth Builders is a 642-member Skool community focused specifically on rental property investing, run by Wale Lawal, a licensed Houston real estate agent and investor. Entry is priced at just $1, which the community's own page frames as a spam-filter rather than a real revenue mechanism ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/networthbuilders/about)) — worth noting since the task brief lists this as a recurring monthly charge while the page itself describes it as lifetime access, a detail worth confirming directly before joining.

## What's actually inside

The centerpiece is a proprietary "Buy Box, Offer, and Close" framework alongside a Rental Deal Sourcing Course the page values at $3,497 as a standalone product. Members get weekly live sessions with what the page describes as seven-figure investors, direct deal feedback and analysis, and a 60-day roadmap aimed at getting a member to their first rental property purchase. The toolkit includes calculators, checklists, scripts, and templates, plus resources aimed at automating and systematizing property management once a unit is acquired, rather than stopping at the "buy your first deal" stage.

## Who runs it

Wale Lawal has an unusually well-documented, independently verifiable public track record for a Skool community founder. He's a licensed Houston-area real estate agent and broker with his own investing portfolio of 30+ rental units, and public profiles across LinkedIn, HAR.com, and Yelp corroborate both his license status and a base of real client reviews describing him as a strong negotiator and communicator ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/adewale-lawal/), [HAR.com](https://www.har.com/adewale-lawal/agent_walel), [Yelp](https://www.yelp.com/biz/wale-lawal-networth-builders-houston)). He's been recognized as a top social-media agent in Texas and as a 2024 top agent by RateMyAgent, according to his own site, and has helped more than 400 clients invest, per his company bio ([networthbuilders.com](https://networthbuilders.com/about/)). This is a materially stronger, more checkable public footprint than most single-founder Skool communities in this review batch.

## Pros

- The founder's credentials (an active real estate license, a documented personal rental portfolio, and independent third-party reviews on LinkedIn, HAR.com, and Yelp) are unusually easy to verify compared to most Skool community leaders.
- A near-zero entry price removes essentially all financial risk to trying the community.
- The 60-day roadmap to a first purchase gives a concrete, time-bound goal rather than an open-ended "learn real estate" promise.
- Weekly live access to the founder and other experienced investors is a meaningful level of support for a community this affordable.

## Cons

- At 642 members, this is one of the smaller communities in this review batch, so peer discussion volume and shared deal-flow activity will be thinner than in larger groups.
- Because entry is essentially free, it's unclear from the public page how the community sustains itself financially long-term, or whether some content is gated behind Lawal's separate paid coaching or brokerage services.
- The near-$1 price point in the task data doesn't fully match the "lifetime access" framing on the community's own about page; prospective members should clarify current pricing and terms directly before assuming it's a recurring monthly cost.

## Who it's a good fit for

Aspiring rental property investors, especially those in or near the Houston market who value working with someone whose license and track record can be independently checked, are the clearest fit here. The near-zero cost also makes it a low-risk way for total beginners to get a first look at a structured rental-investing framework.

## Who should skip it

Investors outside Houston who want strategies specifically tuned to markets Lawal doesn't operate in directly may get less localized value, even if the general framework still applies. Those wanting a large, highly active peer community with heavy day-to-day discussion volume may find this group quieter than some larger alternatives.
