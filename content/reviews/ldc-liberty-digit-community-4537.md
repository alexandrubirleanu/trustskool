# LIBERTY DIGIT Review 2026: Is $118/Year Worth It for This French Digital Marketing Program?

LIBERTY DIGIT is a 668-member French-language Skool community priced at $118 a year, which works out to under $10 a month when annualized. Its own description frames the group as an official shortcut for mastering digital marketing and, in its words, creating your freedom.

## What's Actually Inside

The framing here connects two ideas directly: digital marketing skill and personal financial freedom, positioning the program as a means to an end rather than marketing education for its own sake. "Votre Raccourcie Officiel" (your official shortcut) suggests a structured, condensed path rather than an open-ended library of loosely organized content, implying some kind of defined program or curriculum, though the description doesn't specify which digital marketing channels (paid ads, content, email, affiliate) make up the core teaching. The annual pricing structure, rather than monthly, suggests a program designed around a defined learning arc with a start and endpoint, rather than an ongoing subscription-style community. That structure is worth keeping in mind when comparing this to monthly-billed alternatives, an annual commitment assumes the content is stable enough that a full year of access makes sense upfront, rather than being updated so frequently that monthly flexibility matters more.

## Pros

- Annual pricing at $118/year is relatively affordable when averaged monthly, compared to many paid marketing programs charging monthly fees.
- Explicit "official shortcut" framing suggests a structured program rather than scattered, unorganized content.
- Operates in French, serving French-speaking learners directly rather than through translated content.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which digital marketing channels or tactics form the core curriculum, so it's worth requesting more detail on the actual syllabus before paying for a full year upfront. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking beginners to intermediate marketers who want a structured, defined program toward digital marketing skills, and who are comfortable with an annual pricing commitment, fit this well. People motivated by the freedom and independence framing specifically, rather than pure technical marketing skill for its own sake, are likely to connect with the program's stated positioning.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-French speakers would face an obvious language barrier. Anyone preferring to pay monthly rather than commit to a full year upfront, or advanced marketers seeking channel-specific deep dives rather than a general program, may also want to look elsewhere.
