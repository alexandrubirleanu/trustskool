# Fly Sussex Community Review 2026: Is This Free Paragliding Student Community Worth Joining?

Fly Sussex Community is a free Skool community with 390 members, created specifically by Fly Sussex instructors for their paragliding students. The description positions it as a place to meet fellow pilots at various stages of the paragliding journey and get help with theory.

## What's Actually Inside

This is explicitly tied to a specific paragliding school, Fly Sussex, rather than being a general, open paragliding forum, which suggests the content and member base are aligned with whatever curriculum and progression Fly Sussex instructors already teach in person. The stated focus on theory support implies the community exists to reinforce classroom or ground-school learning between actual flying sessions, and to connect students who might otherwise only interact briefly during lessons.

Paragliding training combines physical flying time with a meaningful body of theory, weather reading, airspace rules, equipment checks, that's easy to forget between lessons if it's only covered once in person. A dedicated online space for reinforcing that theory, and for students at different stages to compare notes, fills a fairly specific gap that a general aviation or paragliding forum wouldn't necessarily cover in the same school-specific way.

## Pros

- Built directly by instructors for their own students, so the theory content should align closely and consistently with what's actually taught in in-person lessons.
- Free to join, with no cost barrier for students already paying for flight training.
- Creates ongoing peer connection between lessons, useful in a sport where flying opportunities are often weather-dependent and spaced out.

## Cons

Membership value is closely tied to already being a Fly Sussex student, so its usefulness for outsiders or paragliders training elsewhere is limited. No independent reviews were found beyond the platform's own listing, and the description doesn't say whether non-students can join or view content at all.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Current or prospective Fly Sussex paragliding students who want theory support and peer connection between lessons, especially in the gaps between weather-dependent flying days.

## Who Should Skip It

Paragliding students training with a different school, since the community is tied specifically to Fly Sussex's curriculum and instructors, and anyone not currently learning to paraglide who wouldn't have context for the school-specific discussion happening there.
