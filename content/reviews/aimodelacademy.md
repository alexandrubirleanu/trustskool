# AI Model Academy Review 2026: Is It Worth $97/Month?

AI Model Academy is a Skool community with more than 3,258 members built around a single business model: launching an "AI agency" that creates and sells AI-generated model imagery and content. It's run by Scotty Scales and currently lists access at $97, with the community's own about page framing that as a one-time entry fee rather than a recurring charge, before pricing is set to move to $347 once the group hits 3,000 members ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/aimodelacademy/about)) — a threshold this community appears to have already passed, which is worth clarifying with the team before you pay.

## What's actually inside

The curriculum is built around what the community calls an A-Z AI agency blueprint: creating consistent AI models and characters, generating content at scale, and then finding buyers or building an audience around that output. Members get a marketing playbook covering multiple traffic-generation strategies, ongoing updates as new AI tools and models are released, and access to a private Telegram channel the community describes as having 2,000+ members for faster support outside of Skool itself. Priority support is positioned as part of the offer, along with regular content refreshes rather than a static, one-and-done course library.

## Who runs it

Scotty Scales is the named founder and lead admin on the community's Skool page, and the about page presents him as the source of the AI-agency methodology being taught. Beyond the Skool listing itself, we could not find an independent, verifiable public profile, prior business track record, or third-party coverage of Scotty Scales specifically — his presence appears concentrated inside this community and its own marketing rather than documented elsewhere. That's a meaningful gap for a community charging close to $100 for access, and prospective members should ask directly for verifiable proof of results before joining rather than relying on the page's own claims.

## The claims, and what to weigh

The about page states the community has "helped 3,434+ start AI brands in 10 months," with individual claims of going from $0 to $100k in 90 days and a first dollar of income possible within 30 days. These are the kind of specific, aggressive numbers that are common across the AI-agency Skool niche, and none of them are independently verifiable from outside the community. Take them as marketing copy to be tested against your own results, not as a guarantee.

## Pros

- A tightly scoped niche (AI model creation plus a traffic/marketing system) rather than a vague "learn AI" grab-bag, which makes it easier to evaluate whether the specific skill is one you want.
- Regular tool and blueprint updates are built into the pitch, addressing the real problem that AI-tool guidance ages quickly.
- A large enough member base (3,200+) that there should be genuine peer activity and shared experience inside the group, not a ghost town.

## Cons

- No refunds after access is granted, per the community's own stated policy, so there's no way to test the material risk-free before committing.
- The pricing structure itself is ambiguous on the public page (one-time fee language sitting alongside a stated future price increase, which normally implies a recurring or tiered model) — get this in writing before paying.
- No independent verification of the founder's background or of the community's headline income claims was found outside the platform's own marketing.

## Who it's a good fit for

People who already understand the basic mechanics of AI image/video generation and want a packaged system for turning that into a saleable service, and who are comfortable evaluating aggressive earnings claims critically rather than at face value, are the best fit here. The Telegram add-on and ongoing tool updates suggest genuine value for someone actively building in this space day to day.

## Who should skip it

Total beginners looking for hand-held, verified mentorship with a track record they can check independently should be cautious. With no refund policy and unverified founder credentials, this is a "do your own diligence first" purchase rather than a low-risk trial.
