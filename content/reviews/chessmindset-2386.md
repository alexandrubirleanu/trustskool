# Dr. Mike's Chess Academy Review 2026: Worth Joining to Climb Elo?

Chess improvement content is everywhere, from YouTube channels to paid courses, but Dr. Mike's Chess Academy pitches itself narrowly: get to 1,000-2,000+ Elo, and do it fast. It's a free Skool community with 1,454 members, calling itself the "#1 community for online chess players." Whether it truly holds that top spot is the community's own claim rather than an independently verified ranking, but the member count itself is substantial for a niche this specific.

## What's Actually Inside

The description's core promise is rating-focused rather than general chess appreciation: "Skyrocket to 1,000 - 2,000+ Elo ASAP." That's a specific, measurable range, which suggests the community is squarely aimed at players below or around that band who want a faster path upward, rather than titled players or people simply looking to enjoy the game socially. That's a common gap in chess improvement content generally, since plenty of high-level analysis exists for stronger players, but material aimed squarely at the sub-2,000 range, where most online players actually sit, is comparatively harder to find in one dedicated place.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no cost to see whether the rating-focused approach clicks with how you study chess.
- A clearly defined target audience (players aiming for roughly 1,000 to 2,000+ Elo) rather than a vague "improve your chess" pitch aimed at everyone.
- A solid member count of 1,454, suggesting there's an actual community of players at a similar stage to compare notes with.

## Cons

The description doesn't detail the actual training method (puzzles, opening theory, endgame study, game review, or some mix), so it's worth browsing the community's posts to see what "skyrocket ASAP" looks like in practice before assuming a specific format. We also didn't find independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool page, so it's worth asking current members what a typical week of study looks like before assuming any one method dominates.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Players currently under roughly 2,000 Elo who want a community specifically organized around rating improvement, rather than a general chess discussion space or content aimed at advanced/titled players.

## Who Should Skip It

Players already above 2,000 Elo, or those more interested in chess as a casual hobby than as a rating-chasing project, may find the community's ASAP-improvement framing isn't quite aimed at them.
