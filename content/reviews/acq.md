# ACQ Vantage Review 2026: Is $1,000/Month Worth It for $1M+ Owners?

ACQ Vantage charges $1,000 a month and doesn't pretend to be for everyone. Its description sets the entry bar explicitly: "For $1M+ business owners looking to scale past $10M+." With 1,209 members, it sits at a price point far above most Skool communities, positioning itself as a room for operators already past the startup phase.

## What's Actually Inside

The description is narrow by design: it names a specific revenue floor (already at $1M+) and a specific target ceiling to break through ($10M+). That combination filters out both pre-revenue founders and hobbyist business owners, the entire framing assumes members are running an established company and are looking for the next scaling threshold, not general startup advice. At $1,000 a month, the price itself acts as an additional filter, likely deterring anyone not seriously operating at that revenue level.

## Pros

- Extremely specific eligibility framing ($1M+ scaling to $10M+), which should mean a peer group at a genuinely comparable stage rather than a mixed bag of business sizes.
- A price point high enough to filter for serious, funded operators, which can mean a higher-quality room of peers for those who qualify.
- Clear, unambiguous positioning rather than vague "for entrepreneurs" language.

## Cons

The description doesn't detail specific mechanisms, mastermind calls, frameworks, or 1:1 access, so it's unclear exactly what the $1,000 monthly fee covers beyond community access. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing, which matters more at this price point than at a low-cost community.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Business owners already generating $1M or more in revenue who are specifically trying to break through to $10M+ and want a peer group at that same stage, and who are comfortable paying a four-figure monthly fee for it.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone below the $1M revenue mark, or business owners not focused on aggressive scaling specifically, will likely find both the price and the peer group mismatched to their stage. Founders more interested in lifestyle businesses than aggressive growth targets may also find the "scale past $10M+" framing pushes in a direction they don't actually want to go.

At $1,000 a month, this is exactly the kind of listing where checking real membership numbers and growth trends before paying matters most, since the price alone won't tell you whether the peer group and content actually deliver on the eligibility bar it sets.
