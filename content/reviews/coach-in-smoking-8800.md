# Coach in Smoking Review 2026: Is This Free Youth Football Coaching Community Worth It?

Coach in Smoking is a free Italian-language Skool community with 384 members, and despite the name, its description is squarely about football (soccer) coaching. It's built for coaches working with youth and amateur teams who want to grow and improve specifically through comparing notes with peers, "confrontandosi," as the description puts it, rather than through a formal top-down certification course.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the core mechanism here is peer exchange rather than a top-down instructor-led curriculum. Youth and amateur football coaching involves a lot of situational judgment, managing different age groups, motivating amateur players, dealing with parents, and structuring training sessions with limited resources, and a community built around coaches comparing approaches with each other is well suited to that kind of practical, situation-by-situation learning. That kind of exchange also tends to surface solutions to problems that vary a lot by club, budget, and player age group, which a rigid standardized course can struggle to address.

## Pros

- Free to join, making it accessible for coaches at grassroots and amateur levels who may not have big budgets for coaching education.
- Peer-comparison framing ("confrontandosi") suggests real coach-to-coach exchange rather than one-directional lecturing.
- Specific to youth and amateur football rather than professional-level tactics, which better matches what most member coaches actually deal with day to day.
- Italian-language delivery serves Italian grassroots and amateur football coaches directly in their own working language.

## Cons

- No independent reviews found beyond the platform listing, so the quality and experience level of the coaches participating can't be verified from outside.
- A peer-exchange format depends heavily on how active and experienced the existing membership is, so the value can vary based on who's currently participating.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Youth and amateur football coaches in Italy or other Italian-speaking contexts who want to trade practical coaching ideas and experiences with peers facing similar age-group and resource challenges.

## Who Should Skip It

Coaches working at a professional or highly competitive level looking for advanced tactical systems, or anyone outside football coaching entirely, will likely find this peer-focused youth and amateur community a mismatch for their needs. Non-Italian speakers would also face a language barrier that limits how much they can get out of the discussion.
