# AI Marketing Forum Review 2026: Is It Worth $28/Month?

AI Marketing Forum is a paid Skool community that has built up 1,174 members around a compact promise. It bills itself as the community to help you "exponentially increase the value of YOU and your company with AI." Membership runs $28 a month, which puts it well below the price of most AI-focused business communities on the platform.

## What's Actually Inside

The group's public description doesn't break out a syllabus or list specific modules, but its framing points to two things running in parallel: personal skill-building with AI tools and applying that skill toward business or marketing outcomes. That "value of you and your company" phrasing suggests members are meant to walk away with both an individually marketable AI skillset and something they can apply directly inside their own business or client work. Because the listing is short on specifics, anyone evaluating it should expect to learn more about the actual curriculum, live call cadence, and resource library only after joining or asking current members directly.

## Pros

- Priced at $28/month, it sits at a lower entry point than many AI-focused paid communities, which lowers the cost of testing whether the content fits.
- The framing explicitly ties AI skills to marketing and business value rather than treating AI as a purely technical or hobbyist pursuit.
- At over 1,100 members, there's a large enough base that discussion threads and peer activity are more likely than in a brand-new group.

## Cons

The public listing is essentially a single tagline, so it's hard to know exactly what's taught, how often live sessions happen, or what format the content takes before paying to join. We also found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing, so prospective members are relying mostly on the group's self-description and member count rather than outside verification.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Marketers and small business owners who want a low-cost way to start experimenting with AI in their work, and who are comfortable joining with limited upfront detail about the exact curriculum, are the most natural fit here.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who wants a detailed breakdown of course modules, live call schedules, or instructor credentials before paying should look elsewhere first, since none of that is spelled out in the public description.
