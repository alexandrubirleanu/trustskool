# Fundamentals of Tattooing Review 2026: Is It Worth $14/Month?

Fundamentals of Tattooing runs 561 members at $14 a month, aimed at people learning to tattoo from scratch rather than working artists refining an existing style. That's a meaningfully different audience than most tattoo-adjacent content online, which tends to be either portfolio inspiration for clients or advanced technique videos aimed at artists who already know the basics. This community's stated focus is the ground-floor fundamentals: machine setup, lining, and shading.

## What's Actually Inside

The description lists three concrete technical building blocks, machine setup, lining, and shading, plus an emphasis on safe practice, which matters given the health and hygiene risks involved in tattooing untrained. The phrase "community behind you" suggests peer support and possibly feedback on practice work, not just prerecorded lessons, which lines up with how tattoo apprenticeships traditionally work: a mix of technical instruction and ongoing critique from people further along.

## Pros

- Names specific technical skills (machine setup, lining, shading) rather than vague "learn to tattoo" marketing language.
- Explicitly addresses safe practice, a real concern for anyone learning tattooing outside a formal apprenticeship.
- Community-based support format may help beginners get feedback that's hard to find learning entirely alone.

## Cons

- Tattooing involves real health risks (infection, bloodborne pathogens) if practiced incorrectly, and the description doesn't specify what equipment, model skin, or supervision is available for hands-on practice.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond the platform listing itself, so instructor credentials and outcomes for members aren't independently verified.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Complete beginners who want a structured, community-supported starting point for learning tattoo fundamentals before or instead of a traditional in-shop apprenticeship.

## Who Should Skip It

Working tattoo artists who already have machine setup, lining, and shading down will find this too basic. Anyone unable to verify safe, sanitary practice conditions for hands-on skin work should treat any online tattoo training, this one included, as a supplement to proper in-person training and licensing requirements in their area, not a replacement for them.
