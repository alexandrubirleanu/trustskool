# Industry Set Review 2026: Is It Worth Applying To?

Industry Set is a free, 1,199-member Skool community for bass, house, techno, and UKG producers, but it isn't an open sign-up. The group's own description states it's by application only, and aimed squarely at producers who are "signed," "touring," or already building viral traction, not hobbyists dipping a toe into electronic music production.

## What's Actually Inside

Because entry is gated by application, the group is positioned less as a beginner tutorial hub and more as a peer network for producers who already have some professional traction in the bass, house, techno, or UK garage scenes. The four-genre framing suggests a broad electronic music focus rather than a single-subgenre niche, and the emphasis on "signed, touring, viral" in the description reads as a filter for members already working at a semi-professional or professional level, rather than a course for people starting from zero.

That gatekeeping detail also shapes what the community is likely for once you're in: less step-by-step production tutorials, more shop talk between people who already have labels, bookings, or an audience, comparing notes on the business side of the scene rather than the fundamentals of making a track. For a genre cluster this specific, that peer-level framing matters, bass, house, techno, and UKG each have their own production conventions and scenes, and a community spanning all four suggests members cross-pollinate ideas across those styles rather than staying siloed in one.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no financial risk in applying to see if you're accepted.
- Application-only gating means members are self-selected for a baseline level of activity in the scene, which can raise the quality of peer conversation compared to open communities.
- Covers four related but distinct genres in one place, useful for producers who work across styles rather than a single sound.

## Cons

- Because it's application-only, prospective members can't preview the content or community activity before applying, so you're committing time to an application without seeing what's inside first.
- We found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing, so there's no outside confirmation of what "signed, touring, viral" actually looks like in practice among current members.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Producers already working in bass, house, techno, or UKG who have some existing traction, a release, a touring history, or a viral moment, and want a peer network of others at a similar stage.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners to music production, or anyone without existing traction to point to in an application, may not get past the gate, and should look for more open, entry-level production communities instead.
