# Lipecare Club Review 2026: Is $24/Month Worth It for Lipedema Support?

Lipecare Club charges $24 a month and has grown to 1,017 members around a specific medical condition: lipedema, a chronic disorder affecting fat distribution that predominantly affects women. The community describes itself as educational, offering classes and shared experiences under guided support, aimed at Portuguese-speaking women navigating the condition.

## What's Actually Inside

According to its own description, the community combines three elements: classes, member sharing, and guided support from a physician who specializes in lipedema along with invited specialists. That structure suggests a hybrid model, part structured education about the condition and its management, part peer community where members share their own experiences, backed by input from someone with clinical background in the specific condition rather than general wellness coaching. For a condition that's often under-recognized or misdiagnosed by general practitioners, having content specifically guided by someone focused on lipedema is a meaningful distinction from a purely peer-run support group.

Lipedema is frequently misdiagnosed as simple obesity or lymphedema, and many women report years of dismissive medical encounters before getting an accurate diagnosis. A community that pairs peer sharing with physician-guided classes is directly addressing that gap, giving members both the emotional validation of a peer group and a more reliable source of condition-specific information than generic weight or wellness advice that doesn't account for lipedema's distinct physiology.

## Pros

- Content described as guided by a physician with a specific focus on lipedema, rather than general wellness advice applied to a specific condition.
- Combines structured classes with peer sharing, giving both educational content and emotional community support.
- Speaks directly to Portuguese-speaking women managing a specific, often misunderstood condition, rather than a broad international group where language and healthcare context may differ.

## Cons

- The description doesn't detail exactly how much of the $24/month experience is live classes versus self-paced content versus peer discussion.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, so member outcomes and the depth of clinical guidance can't be verified from the outside.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Portuguese-speaking women managing a lipedema diagnosis who want combined education and peer support with some level of clinical guidance, rather than a purely peer-run forum.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone seeking one-on-one medical treatment should treat this as a supplement to, not a replacement for, direct clinical care, since it's a group community rather than individualized medical treatment. Non-Portuguese speakers may also want to look for an equivalent group in their own language.
