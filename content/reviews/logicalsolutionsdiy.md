## What's Actually Inside

Traditional credit repair companies typically charge ongoing monthly fees for essentially the same core service, reviewing a credit report and filing disputes on the member's behalf, so a DIY model that hands members software to do it themselves, backed by live weekly calls for guidance, is directly targeting that cost structure. Whether AI-assisted dispute software performs as well as a human credit repair specialist is a fair question the listing itself doesn't answer, but the price gap between the two approaches is real and substantial.

- Sets a specific, measurable target (750+ score) so members have a clear benchmark for whether it's working.

## Cons

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People with credit issues who want to handle repair themselves with software and community support rather than paying a traditional credit repair company, especially those comfortable being hands-on with their own paperwork and disputes each week.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone with complex credit situations, identity theft, active bankruptcy, or extensive legal disputes, who may need a licensed credit or legal professional rather than a DIY community approach.
