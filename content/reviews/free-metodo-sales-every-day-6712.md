## What's Actually Inside

The description centers on two words worth unpacking: "automatizzato" (automated) and "prevedibile" (predictable). Together they suggest the system is built to reduce manual prospecting and outreach work in favor of a repeatable process, likely involving some combination of lead generation, a sales sequence, and follow-up, though the specific channels aren't named. The stated audience, coaches, consultants, and training schools, is a coherent group that typically sells higher-ticket services and depends on consistent lead flow rather than one-off transactions, which lines up with the "predictable" framing.

That combination of audience and promise also tells you something about the price point the system is built for: hitting 30,000 euros a month generally implies selling a higher-ticket offer to a relatively small number of clients each month, rather than a high-volume, low-price product, which shapes what "automated acquisition" likely means here compared to an e-commerce or low-ticket digital product context.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing financial risk for coaches and consultants testing whether the system fits their business model.
- Names a specific target audience (coaches, consultants, training schools) rather than a generic "anyone can do this" pitch.
- Revenue range stated upfront (5k to 30k euros/month) gives a concrete benchmark to evaluate against, rather than an open-ended promise.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which acquisition channels make up the "automated" system, so it's hard to judge the actual mechanics from outside. No independent reviews beyond the platform's own listing were found, so the stated revenue range should be read as a target rather than a documented average outcome for members.

Italian-speaking coaches, consultants, and training school operators who already have an offer to sell and want a more systematic, less manual approach to client acquisition than relying on referrals alone.

People without an existing coaching, consulting, or training offer will find the material hard to apply directly. Non-Italian speakers, and anyone looking for a specific tactical playbook rather than a broader system, may also want to look elsewhere.
