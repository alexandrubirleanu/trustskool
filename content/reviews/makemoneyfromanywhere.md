# Make Money From Anywhere Club Review 2026: What's Behind the Free Access?

Make Money From Anywhere Club is a free Skool community with 1,812 members, pitched at "ambitious and aspiring entrepreneurs" who want to grow a business, secure funding, and use AI to increase their income. The community's own copy calls itself the "#1" group for this combination, a claim worth treating as marketing language rather than a verified ranking.

## What's Actually Inside

Three distinct threads run through the description: general business growth, access to funding, and using AI to boost income, specifically the promise to "10x" it. That's a broad combination, and the public listing doesn't break down how those three pieces connect in practice, whether funding means grants, loans, investors, or something else, or what specific AI tools or tactics are taught for the income side. Members should expect a wide-angle entrepreneurship community rather than a narrowly focused, single-skill curriculum, at least based on what the description states outright.

## Pros

- Completely free, so there's no financial barrier to checking it out.
- Combines funding access with AI income tactics, two topics that are frequently taught separately elsewhere.
- Positioned for people at the "aspiring" stage, which suggests a beginner-friendly entry point.

## Cons

"#1 community" and "10x your income" are both self-asserted claims without supporting data in the description, and phrasing like this is extremely common across "make money" communities on Skool, which makes it hard to judge how this one actually differentiates itself in practice. We found no independent reviews of this specific community.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Aspiring entrepreneurs early in their journey who want a free, broad-strokes introduction to business growth, funding options, and AI-assisted income strategies in one place, rather than committing to a paid course before understanding the basics. It's also a reasonable low-cost starting point for people who simply want to see how the funding and AI angles are discussed before deciding whether to invest further elsewhere.

## Who Should Skip It

Experienced entrepreneurs looking for a narrow, advanced curriculum in one specific area (funding, AI, or otherwise) may find the broad scope here too shallow relative to more specialized alternatives built for their stage. Anyone who has already been burned by "make money" communities making bold, unverified promises should also weigh that history before joining another one built on similar language.
