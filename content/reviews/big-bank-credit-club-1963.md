# Big Bank Credit Club Review 2026: Is It Worth $67/Month?

Big Bank Credit Club is a 349-member Skool community built around one promise: "turning your credit into freedom." At $67 a month, it sits well above the price of most Skool communities in the credit space, which puts real pressure on the group to deliver more than generic tips.

## What's Actually Inside

The community's own pitch is specific about what it is not: it says it doesn't just "fix" credit, it builds it, structures it, and positions members to reach their financial goals. That framing suggests an ongoing process rather than a one-time repair job, likely involving structuring credit profiles over time rather than a single round of dispute letters. For anyone whose credit situation is more about building from scratch or restructuring existing debt than erasing a handful of negative marks, that's the angle this group is going after. The recurring monthly price also implies a service that expects members to stick around across multiple billing cycles as their credit profile changes, rather than a one-off transaction.

## Pros

- Clear, goal-oriented positioning ("Reach Your Goals") rather than vague promises of instant fixes
- Explicitly distinguishes itself from simple credit "fixing," suggesting a longer-term, structural approach
- A defined monthly price point makes it easy to budget for compared to opaque one-off credit repair fees

## Cons

- At $67/month, it's a meaningful recurring cost, and the public description doesn't spell out exactly what members receive week to week (calls, templates, coaching, etc.)
- No independent reviews or outcome data were found beyond the platform listing itself

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People actively working to build or restructure their credit profile, rather than just remove a couple of negative items, are the clearest match. If you want an ongoing framework and community accountability around credit as part of a broader financial rebuild, the pitch is aimed at you, and the recurring price suggests it's meant to be used consistently rather than dipped into once.

## Who Should Skip It

If you're looking for a single, low-cost fix for a specific credit report error, or you'd rather work directly with a licensed credit repair organization or attorney, this community's broader "build and structure" positioning may not match what you need. Anyone unwilling to commit to a recurring $67/month cost should also think twice before joining, since the value depends heavily on consistent participation over time.
