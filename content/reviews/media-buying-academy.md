# Media Buying Academy Review 2026: Is This Free Skool Community Worth Joining?

With over 14,500 members and no price tag, Media Buying Academy has built one of the larger free followings on Skool. Its own description keeps things broad, calling the group "your gateway to opportunities, collaborations, and continuous growth in digital marketing," language that promises connection and momentum more than a fixed curriculum.

## What's Actually Inside

Going strictly by the listing, the community positions itself around three things: opportunities, collaborations, and growth, all inside digital marketing generally rather than media buying specifically, despite the name. That's a notable gap between the group's title and its stated pitch. Members joining expecting a deep-dive media buying curriculum, ad platforms, bidding strategy, creative testing, may find the actual content is more general marketing networking than paid traffic instruction. The scale, over 14,000 members, suggests an active space, but size alone doesn't confirm depth of instruction or how much direct access to expertise a typical member gets.

## Who Runs It

The data reviewed lists Abderrahim Khalid as the owner. No further background on him is included in the information available for this review, so no claims are made here about his career history or credentials beyond that attribution.

## Pros

- Free to join, which removes the financial barrier for anyone curious about digital marketing networking.
- Large, active-looking membership base of over 14,500, which can mean more discussion partners and shared opportunities.
- Framed around collaboration and opportunity-sharing, useful for people looking to network rather than just consume static lessons.

## Cons

- The name promises media buying specifically, but the description only mentions digital marketing broadly, so the actual focus on paid ad strategy is unclear from the listing.
- The listing is tagged as an Arabic-language community, so even though its Skool blurb reads in English here, expect discussions, posts, and calls to run primarily in Arabic.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, so member outcomes and the quality of "opportunities" mentioned can't be verified from here.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Arabic-speaking marketers and freelancers looking for a free space to network, find collaborators, and stay exposed to digital marketing trends, especially those comfortable that the group's actual focus may be broader than pure media buying.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who needs the content in English, or anyone specifically after a structured, step-by-step media buying course rather than general networking and opportunity-sharing, should look elsewhere or confirm the format before joining.
