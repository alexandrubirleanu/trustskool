# Authentic Sales Review 2026: Is This Free Skool Community Worth Joining?

Authentic Sales is a free Skool community with 1,750 members, operating in German. Its description positions it as a peer meeting point: "Der Treffpunkt für alle, die sich im Verkauf und Cold Calling austauschen, motivieren und gemeinsam besser werden wollen" (the meeting point for anyone who wants to exchange ideas about sales and cold calling, motivate each other, and get better together).

## What's Actually Inside

This reads as a peer-support and exchange community rather than a structured, instructor-led course. The description highlights three activities: exchanging experience, mutual motivation, and collective improvement, with a specific focus on sales and cold calling as disciplines. That framing suggests value comes primarily from other members' real-world sales experience rather than a single expert's curriculum.

Cold calling is a skill many salespeople find isolating to practice alone, since rejection is frequent and immediate feedback is rare outside of a supportive peer group. A community built specifically around mutual motivation for this particular skill addresses a real, commonly cited pain point in sales training more directly than general business communities typically do.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier for salespeople curious about peer exchange on cold calling specifically.
- Narrow, practical focus, sales and cold calling rather than business in general, should keep discussion relevant to a specific, high-stakes skill.
- Explicit emphasis on mutual motivation suggests an accountability-oriented culture, useful for a skill many salespeople find personally difficult to sustain.

## Cons

- The description doesn't indicate whether there's a lead instructor, structured content, or purely open peer discussion, which affects how much guidance new or struggling salespeople can expect.
- No independent reviews of this specific community were found beyond the Skool platform listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking salespeople and cold callers who want a peer community for motivation, experience-sharing, and accountability around cold outreach specifically.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-German speakers, and anyone looking for a formal, credentialed sales training program rather than peer exchange, should look elsewhere. Prospective members should also confirm whether the community includes any structured curriculum at all or functions purely as an open discussion space, since that distinction matters for salespeople who learn better from a defined program than from open-ended peer conversation.
