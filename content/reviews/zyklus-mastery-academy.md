# Zyklus Mastery Academy Review 2026: A Free German Community for Cycle Coaching Training

Zyklus Mastery Academy has built a 438-member, free community around a two-part promise, stated in its own description: help members holistically understand their menstrual cycle, and train them to guide other women as a mentor while building meaningful income from that work. "Zyklus" is simply the German word for cycle, and the academy framing signals a training program rather than a casual discussion group.

## What's Actually Inside
The description positions this as more than a personal-education group; it's framed as training for a coaching business. Members appear to work through material on understanding their own cycle first, then move toward learning how to mentor other women on the same topic, with income-building as an explicit part of the pitch. That two-stage structure, learn it yourself, then teach it professionally, is a common shape for practitioner-training communities.

## Pros
- Free to join, unusual for a community that frames itself around eventually building paid mentorship income
- Clear progression implied in the description: personal understanding first, then mentor training, then income, rather than jumping straight to a business pitch
- Serves German-speaking women directly in a niche (cycle coaching) that's still relatively underserved in that language
- The "academy" framing suggests some structured progression rather than a loose, unorganized discussion feed

## Cons
- The description doesn't specify certification, credentialing, or what qualifies a member to call themselves a "mentor" after going through the material
- No independent reviews outside the Skool listing were found to verify income claims implied by the "great income" framing
- Building a mentoring income around any health-adjacent topic carries real responsibility, and prospective mentors should think carefully about the line between coaching and medical advice before working with clients

## Who It's a Good Fit For
German-speaking women interested in deeply understanding their own menstrual cycle and potentially building a coaching practice around that knowledge, who want a free starting point before any paid tier or certification and are comfortable with a holistic, mentorship-based teaching style.

## Who Should Skip It
Non-German speakers, since the community operates in German, and anyone looking for clinical or medical guidance on menstrual health rather than a holistic, mentorship-oriented approach, since the two frameworks start from different assumptions about the body and shouldn't be mixed up when a real diagnosis is needed.
