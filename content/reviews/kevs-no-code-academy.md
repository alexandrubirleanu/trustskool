# Kev's No-Code Academy Review 2026: Is It Worth $10/Month?

Kev's No-Code Academy has grown to 2,067 members by targeting a very specific audience: people with zero coding background who want to build AI SaaS products and AI automations anyway. Membership runs $10 a month, and the group doesn't hedge on its positioning, calling itself "the BEST community in the world for learning A.I SaaS & A.I Automations as a COMPLETE Beginner."

## What's Actually Inside

Going by the description, the curriculum is aimed squarely at beginners, covering both building AI-powered SaaS products and setting up AI automations, all without requiring prior coding skills. The "complete beginner" framing repeated in the group's own pitch suggests the content starts from fundamentals rather than assuming familiarity with no-code tools or automation platforms.

That said, "AI SaaS" is a fairly ambitious end goal for a beginner-oriented community: it implies building and potentially selling software products, not just automating a single personal task. The description doesn't clarify how far the $10/month curriculum takes a member toward an actual launched product versus teaching the underlying concepts.

## Pros

- At $10/month, the price is low enough to test the material for a couple of months without much financial risk.
- Explicitly targeting complete beginners removes the guesswork about whether prior technical experience is required.
- Combining AI SaaS building and AI automation under one roof means members get exposure to two related, in-demand skill areas rather than just one.

## Cons

- No independent reviews were found outside the Skool listing itself, so the "BEST community in the world" claim in the description is the group's own marketing language, not a verified ranking.
- Beginner-focused content may move too slowly for anyone who already has some no-code or automation experience.
- The description doesn't name specific no-code platforms or AI tools covered, so members won't know the exact toolset until after joining.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People with no coding background who want a low-cost, beginner-oriented on-ramp into building AI SaaS products and AI automations.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who already has intermediate or advanced no-code or automation experience, since content explicitly aimed at complete beginners is unlikely to offer much new material for them. People wanting a rigorous, code-based path into software development should also look elsewhere, since the community's whole premise is avoiding code entirely.
