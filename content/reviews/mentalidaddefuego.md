## What's Actually Inside

Naming all three groups, athletes, parents, and coaches, rather than just athletes alone suggests the community treats sports performance as a shared ecosystem, where a parent's or coach's mindset matters as much as the athlete's own. That's a somewhat different angle than a typical sports-performance program aimed only at competitors, potentially useful for families navigating youth sports together. The focus on transforming the human being points toward mindset and identity work as the primary lever, ahead of physical training or tactics, though the description doesn't specify the actual coaching format, live sessions, structured courses, or one-on-one work, behind that philosophy.

- A clear, stated philosophy, mindset transformation drives results, giving members something concrete to evaluate rather than vague motivational content.
- At 582 members, likely enough peer activity for parents and coaches to compare notes, not just athletes.

## Cons

$100/month is a real recurring cost, and the description doesn't specify the actual delivery format, calls, courses, one-on-one coaching, or how it differs for the three named audiences. No independent reviews of this community exist beyond the Skool listing, and mindset-transformation claims in sports performance are hard to verify from outside.
