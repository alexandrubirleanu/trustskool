# MasterGrief Review 2026: Is This Free Grief Support Community Worth Joining?

MasterGrief is a free Skool community with 761 members built around a single, clearly stated premise: grief deserves to be witnessed with real presence rather than managed, minimized, or rushed through. The community's own description frames its purpose simply as learning "to grieve with more love than pain," positioning it as a space for processing loss rather than a course promising to make grief disappear.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the core offering is presence-based support: a place where members are witnessed in their grief rather than handed a fixed timeline or a checklist for "moving on." The phrase "more love than pain" suggests the community's approach treats grief as something to be carried differently, not eliminated, which is a meaningfully different promise than many self-help spaces make. There's no mention in the description of structured modules, courses, or a named grief methodology, so the format leans toward community and companionship over curriculum. Many bereavement resources are built around staged models of grief or a timeline for "moving on," and a community built instead around simply being witnessed represents a different philosophy, one that may suit people who've found rigid recovery timelines unhelpful in the past. Whether that translates into daily check-ins, shared rituals, or open discussion threads isn't spelled out in the description, so the actual day-to-day mechanics of participation are worth asking about directly before joining.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing a financial barrier at a moment when people are often already under strain.
- A clear, specific emotional framing ("more love than pain") rather than vague wellness language.
- 761 members suggests a community large enough to offer regular presence and response, without feeling anonymous.

## Cons

- The description doesn't name who facilitates the community or what training, if any, informs the support offered, so anyone with acute grief or trauma should treat this as peer and community support, not clinical care.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the community's own Skool listing, so claims about the quality of "presence" here are the community's own framing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People processing grief who want to be heard and witnessed rather than pushed through a structured recovery timeline, and who are comfortable with a free, community-based support format.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone needing licensed grief counseling, crisis support, or clinical mental health care should look for a credentialed provider first. A description-only listing like this also isn't enough for someone who needs to verify facilitator qualifications before opening up in a group setting.
