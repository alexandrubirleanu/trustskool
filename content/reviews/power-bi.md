# Power BI 新手村 Review 2026: Can You Really Build a Report in 2 Hours?

Power BI 新手村 (roughly, "Power BI Beginners' Village") is a free, Chinese-language Skool community with 1,203 members, and it's unusually specific about its structure compared to most communities in this batch. The description spells out an exact format: "用 2 小時快速課 + 10 日小挑戰，從 0 做出第一份基礎報表" (with a 2-hour quick course plus a 10-day mini challenge, build your first basic report from zero).

## What's Actually Inside

This is one of the more concretely scoped communities available: a defined 2-hour course paired with a 10-day challenge, aimed at taking someone from zero Power BI experience to a completed basic report. That's a specific, time-bound outcome rather than an open-ended "learn data analytics" promise, and the structure (short course plus a multi-day challenge for reinforcement) suggests a deliberate onboarding sequence rather than a loose content library members have to piece together themselves.

## Pros

- A named, time-boxed curriculum (2-hour course, 10-day challenge) rather than a vague promise, giving a clear sense of the time commitment involved.
- Explicitly built for beginners starting from zero, so there's no assumed prior Power BI or data background needed.
- Free to join, matching its framing as a beginner on-ramp rather than a premium professional certification.

## Cons

The description focuses on absolute basics (a first, basic report), so more experienced Power BI users or those needing advanced DAX, data modeling, or enterprise deployment skills will likely outgrow the content quickly. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Chinese-speaking beginners with zero Power BI experience who want a short, structured on-ramp with a defined outcome rather than an open-ended, self-directed learning path.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone already comfortable with basic Power BI reporting and looking for advanced or intermediate content will likely find this beginner-focused curriculum too introductory. People who prefer official Microsoft learning paths or paid certification tracks may also want a more formally credentialed option than a community-run challenge.
