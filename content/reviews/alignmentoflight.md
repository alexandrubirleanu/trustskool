# Alignment of Light Community Review 2026: A Free Weekly Meditation Circle

Alignment of Light Community is a free Skool community with 1,811 members, built around spiritual practice and guided meditation rather than business, fitness, or money topics. Its own description promises "practical tools and teachings" for both the spiritual and human sides of a member's journey, paired with a specific recurring format: weekly community guided meditations with a live Q&A component.

## What's Actually Inside

Two elements stand out from the description. First, the weekly guided meditation sessions come with a Q&A attached, which suggests real-time interaction and discussion rather than a passive, pre-recorded-only experience. Second, there's a stated daily practice component delivered via video, giving members something to engage with between the weekly live sessions rather than a week-long gap. Beyond that structure, the description doesn't name a specific spiritual tradition, teacher, or lineage the practices draw from, so members should expect an eclectic, "practical tools" framing rather than a single defined philosophy or school of thought.

## Pros

- Free to join, with a clearly recurring format (weekly live sessions) rather than a one-off resource dump.
- Combines live interaction (Q&A) with daily video content, giving members both community touchpoints and something to do solo.
- Explicit "practical tools" framing suggests actionable practices rather than purely abstract spiritual discussion.

## Cons

No facilitator name, credentials, or specific tradition is mentioned in the public description, so it's hard to assess the teaching background behind the sessions. We also found no independent reviews of this community outside its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People on a spiritual or personal growth journey who want a free, live weekly touchpoint (guided meditation plus Q&A) along with daily practice content to stay engaged between sessions. It also suits people who prefer a group setting for meditation over a solo app-based practice, since the weekly Q&A adds a layer of real-time connection that most meditation apps don't offer.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a strictly secular, clinical mindfulness or meditation program, without the broader spiritual framing, is likely to find this community's language and approach a mismatch. People wanting one-on-one guidance rather than a group format should also look elsewhere, since everything described here is built around a shared, live group setting rather than individual sessions.
