# Investeringsklubben Review 2026: Free Investment Education, Worth It?

Investeringsklubben ("The Investment Club") is a free Skool community with 1,227 members, aimed squarely at Swedish speakers who already have some grounding in investing and want to go further. The description sums up the pitch in one line: "Programmet för dig som vill ta din kunskap inom investeringar till nästa nivå," a program for those who want to take their investment knowledge to the next level.

## What's Actually Inside

The "next level" framing suggests this isn't a beginner's introduction to what a stock or an index fund is, but rather a step up for people who already understand the basics and want to sharpen their approach. Calling itself a "program" implies some structure, sequential material or a defined path, rather than a purely open discussion forum, though the description doesn't specify what asset classes or strategies are covered.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no cost to testing whether the level of content matches where you're actually at.
- Positioned specifically for people past the basics, which can mean less noise than a beginner-heavy general investing group.
- Swedish-language delivery, useful for a market that's smaller and less served by English-first investing communities.
- A "program" framing implies at least some sequencing to the material rather than scattered, unrelated posts.

## Cons

The description is short on specifics: it doesn't name asset classes, strategies, or the credentials behind the program, so members are trusting the pitch more than any documented curriculum. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Swedish-speaking investors who already have a working understanding of investing basics and want a free, structured next step rather than another beginner's guide.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners looking for foundational investing education, or non-Swedish speakers, will likely find this community either too advanced or inaccessible in its current language. Anyone expecting personalized portfolio advice rather than general education should also temper expectations, since nothing in the description suggests individualized financial planning is part of the offering.

Since "next level" is a relative claim without a defined starting point, it's worth checking how the community has actually grown and how engaged its members are before assuming the level of content matches your own.
