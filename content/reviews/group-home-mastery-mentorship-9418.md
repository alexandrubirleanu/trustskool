## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the program runs for 8 weeks of live mentorship, positioning it as a cohort-based, time-bound program rather than an evergreen, browse-anytime community. The emphasis on a business that "actually cash flows" suggests the content is oriented toward operational and financial viability, presumably licensing, staffing, referrals, and reimbursement mechanics that make group homes profitable, though the description itself doesn't itemize the curriculum module by module.

## Pros

- Clear time-bound structure, 8 weeks, rather than an open-ended, hard-to-finish program.
- Named instructor with a specific self-described credential, billed as "America's #1 Group Home Coach" in the program's own marketing, gives prospective members a person to research before paying.
- Niche focus on cash flow specifically, rather than just launching a group home, addresses a real pain point in this industry.

## Cons

- At $7,500 one-time, this is a high-ticket commitment, and the "#1 Group Home Coach" claim is the program's own marketing language rather than an independently verified title.
- We found no independent reviews of this specific mentorship beyond its Skool listing.
