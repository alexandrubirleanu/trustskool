# AI Automation Society Plus Review 2026: Is It Worth $99/Month?

AI Automation Society Plus (AIS+) is Nate Herk's paid Skool community for people trying to build, or scale, an AI automation agency, primarily using n8n alongside newer tools like Claude Code. With 3,849 members and a $99/month price (or a discounted annual rate around $839/year), it positions itself as the serious, paid tier that sits above Herk's larger free "AI Automation Society" community.

## What's Actually Inside

The centerpiece is an "Agent Zero" foundations course paired with an n8n Masterclass built around more than 100 workflow templates. Beyond the course library, members get weekly live Q&A calls with Herk, unlimited technical support, and what the community calls a "100% Resolution Guarantee," a commitment that support requests get resolved rather than left hanging. The stated goal isn't abstract AI automation literacy, it's specifically framed around landing client contracts in the $5,000 to $20,000 per month range and building toward $1M+ in annual agency revenue, the same trajectory Herk says he took with his own agency.

The community is intentionally described as smaller and more committed than the free AIS tier, with admins and senior members reportedly answering questions in under 24 hours according to third-party review coverage [(ippei.com)](https://ippei.com/ai-automation-society-plus/) [(skoolmakers.com)](https://skoolmakers.com/communities/ai-automation-society-plus/).

## Who Runs It

Nate Herk has a substantial public track record: north of 650,000 YouTube subscribers, over 30 million total views, and a real operating agency, Uppit AI, that he states does roughly $1M/year in revenue [(ippei.com)](https://ippei.com/ai-automation-society-plus/). That combination of a large public audience and an operating business gives the founder credibility that's independently checkable, rather than resting solely on the community's own claims.

## Pros

- **Founder with a verifiable public track record**, not just Skool-native marketing claims.
- **Refund guarantee on annual plans**, with requests honored within 7 days according to the community's stated policy.
- **Active, well-reviewed community.** Multiple independent review sites (ippei.com, aifunnelinsider.com, communityhunter.com, skoolmakers.com) rate it around 4.4/5, converging on similar conclusions rather than a single outlier opinion.

## Cons

Independent reviewers are consistent on one point: the AI automation agency business model itself is increasingly crowded. Reviewers note the headline income claims (the $5k-$20k/month client contracts) set an expectation that's harder to hit in practice, since acquiring clients is genuinely difficult in a saturated market, and most of a member's time and budget still goes into client acquisition rather than the technical build itself [(ippei.com)](https://ippei.com/ai-automation-society-plus/). Monthly plan sales are also final, only annual members get the refund window, so trying it risk-free effectively means committing to the yearly rate upfront.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Someone who has already decided to build client-facing AI automation work using n8n, has some existing technical or sales aptitude, and is prepared to spend real effort on client acquisition rather than expecting the templates alone to generate revenue. It's a good fit for people who want a structured curriculum plus an active support system rather than building an agency entirely from scratch.

## Who Should Skip It

Passive learners looking to dabble in AI automation without a serious intent to sell services should look elsewhere, reviewers are blunt that this community rewards committed builders, not casual browsers. Anyone unwilling to commit to the annual plan should also weigh the fact that monthly purchases carry no refund option, since testing the community risk-free effectively requires paying for a full year upfront rather than a single low-commitment month.
