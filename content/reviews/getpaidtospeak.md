# Get Paid To Speak Community Review 2026: Worth Joining for Free?

Get Paid To Speak Community costs nothing to join and has built up 1,030 members around a single shared goal: helping each other get booked and paid as speakers. The group's own pitch is collaborative rather than instructional, framed as documenting learnings, asking questions, and networking with speakers who are already succeeding, with the stated aim of helping "make the world our speaking stage."

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, this reads less like a course and more like a peer exchange. Three activities are named directly: documenting what members learn as they go, asking questions when they get stuck, and networking with speakers who are already making money on stage. That combination suggests the value here comes from the collective experience in the room (people further along sharing what worked) rather than from a fixed curriculum owned by one instructor. For anyone trying to break into paid speaking, that kind of real-time peer knowledge, pricing a keynote, landing a first booking, handling a difficult event, can be hard to find outside a dedicated community.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no financial risk in testing whether the community's discussions match what a working or aspiring speaker actually needs.
- Framed around global networking, which matters in an industry where opportunities often come through referrals and word of mouth rather than cold outreach.
- The "document your learnings" angle suggests an active culture of members sharing real experience, not just consuming static content.

## Cons

- The description doesn't name a specific curriculum, event calendar, or instructor, so it's unclear how structured the experience is beyond open discussion and networking.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, so member satisfaction and how active the group actually is can't be confirmed from the outside.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Speakers, aspiring or working, who want a free space to trade notes with peers on getting booked and paid, rather than a formal paid course with a set syllabus.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a structured, instructor-led program with modules and deadlines should treat this as a supplement rather than a replacement, since the description points to peer networking, not a packaged curriculum. Speakers who already have a strong professional network in the industry may find less new value here too.
