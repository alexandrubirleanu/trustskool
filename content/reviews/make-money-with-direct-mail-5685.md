# Make Money With Direct Mail Review 2026: Is the $35/Month Postcard System Worth It?

## What's Actually Inside

According to the description, the core mechanic is "shared postcards," a direct mail model where advertising costs (and presumably revenue or leads) are split across multiple businesses or participants on a single mailer, rather than one business paying for and running its own campaign. The promise is a specific, moderate income range, an extra $1,000 to $3,000 per month, rather than a vague "financial freedom" pitch, which at least sets a concrete bar members can measure their own results against.

Direct mail as a channel has real, decades-old precedent in marketing (it predates digital ads by a long way), so the underlying mechanic isn't inherently far-fetched, though the description doesn't detail how members actually source clients, design mailers, or handle logistics like printing and postage.

## Pros

- $1,000 to $3,000/month is a specific, bounded income claim rather than an open-ended promise, which makes it easier to hold the program accountable.
- "Shared postcards" is a genuine cost-sharing mechanic in direct mail marketing, not an invented buzzword, giving the pitch a plausible operational basis.
- At $35/month, the entry cost is modest relative to many "start a marketing agency" style programs.

## Cons

- The description doesn't explain who handles printing, mailing, and client acquisition, or what upfront capital (beyond the $35 membership) is needed to actually run campaigns.
- No independent reviews found beyond the platform listing, so the stated $1,000 to $3,000 monthly income range hasn't been verified by outside sources.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People interested in direct mail as a side-income channel who want a specific, bounded income target and are comfortable with an offline, physical-marketing business model rather than a purely digital one.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone expecting a fully digital or passive system, since direct mail inherently involves physical logistics, or anyone who needs the full cost breakdown (printing, postage, client acquisition) spelled out before committing $35/month.

The $1,000 to $3,000/month target is concrete enough to evaluate, but confirm the full cost and logistics picture with the community directly before assuming the $35 membership is the only expense involved.
