# AINAUTEN Review 2026: Is the $995/Year AI Automation Community Worth It?

AINAUTEN (AI Automation Experts) is a German-language Skool community with 1,076 members, priced at $995 per year. According to its own description, the pitch is broad: learning to automate your life, your work and your ideas using AI plus automation, with a linked resource at ainauten.com/automation for further context. At roughly $83 a month when annualized, it sits well above the free-to-cheap end of the Skool AI-community market.

## What's Actually Inside

The description frames the community around applying "AI + Automation" across three areas: personal life, work, and idea execution, rather than narrowing in on one specific automation niche, such as only client-facing agency automations or only content automation. That breadth can be a strength for members wanting a general toolkit, or a drawback for people hoping for a laser-focused curriculum in one specific automation use case.

## Pros

- **Clear external resource link.** The community points to ainauten.com/automation, suggesting there's a dedicated site backing the community rather than just a Skool page with no outside presence.
- **Annual pricing** at $995/year works out to less per month than many monthly-billed AI communities in a similar price range, if a member intends to stay a full year.
- **Broad automation framing**, covering life, work and ideas, could suit generalists who want AI automation skills applicable across multiple areas rather than one narrow workflow.

## Cons

At $995/year, this is a meaningful financial commitment, and the community's own description doesn't specify what's included beyond the general "learn how to automate" pitch: no mention of live calls, specific tools taught, or a curriculum outline in the listing itself. We also found no independent, third-party reviews of AINAUTEN beyond its own Skool and website presence.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking founders or professionals who want a broad, ongoing AI-and-automation education relationship and are comfortable committing close to $1,000 upfront for a year of access without a detailed curriculum preview.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone wanting a narrow, specific automation skill, like just cold-email automation or just one platform, may find the broad "life, work, ideas" framing too general. Budget-conscious members should also weigh this against the many free or low-cost AI automation communities elsewhere on Skool before committing to the annual fee.
