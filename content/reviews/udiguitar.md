# The Art of Guitar Composition Review 2026: Is $7/Month Worth It for Songwriters?

The Art of Guitar Composition charges $7 a month and has built a 440-member community around a specific frustration: guitarists who can play other people's songs note for note but freeze up when asked to write something original. The tagline says it plainly, from guitarist to composer, stop copying songs and start creating your own instrumentals. At that price, it sits well below what most one-on-one composition lessons would cost for even a single session.

## What's Actually Inside
The description targets a mindset shift as much as a technical one. Rather than teaching more scales or licks in isolation, the community frames itself around the idea that anyone can create original music, including people who don't consider themselves "creative" in the traditional sense. That suggests the content likely walks through composition frameworks, ways to generate original musical ideas, and structure for turning improvised playing into finished instrumental pieces.

## Pros
- Very low monthly cost ($7) makes it low-risk to try, especially compared to private composition lessons
- Addresses a specific, common gap for guitarists (performing vs. composing) rather than being a generic "learn guitar" catch-all
- The "even if you think you're not creative" framing suggests the material is built for people without a formal music theory or composition background

## Cons
- At $7/month with 440 members, this is a small, niche paid community rather than a large ecosystem with many active sub-groups
- No independent reviews outside the Skool listing were found, so it's worth sampling free content from the creator elsewhere before committing to a recurring charge
- The description doesn't specify whether there's live coaching, feedback on submitted compositions, or purely pre-recorded material, which affects how "coached" the experience actually is

## Who It's a Good Fit For
Guitarists who are comfortable playing but have never written original instrumental music and want a structured, affordable way to start, especially anyone who's been stuck only ever learning other people's songs and wants a nudge toward original composition.

## Who Should Skip It
Guitarists who are already actively composing and releasing original instrumentals and need advanced arrangement or production help rather than a foundational shift from player to composer, or anyone looking for a broader multi-instrument songwriting community rather than a guitar-specific one.
