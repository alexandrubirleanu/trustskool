## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the community's core promise centers on two connected steps: learning how to write a book, and learning how to publish it, which addresses a common gap for creative writers who can produce prose or poetry but haven't necessarily navigated the self-publishing or traditional publishing process. The invitation to "spin the tales of time" and the specific mention of poets alongside storytellers suggests the community welcomes both narrative fiction and poetry, rather than being narrowly focused on one form.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing cost as a barrier for writers exploring whether a dedicated community fits their needs.
- Explicitly covers publishing, not just writing, addressing a step many creative writing resources skip.
- Welcomes both storytellers and poets, giving it a slightly broader creative scope than book-writing-only communities.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify whether "publish it" refers to traditional publishing, self-publishing, or both, so the actual publishing guidance offered is unclear before joining.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond the platform listing itself.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Aspiring authors and poets who have a manuscript or body of work in progress and want both a community of fellow writers and guidance on getting published.

## Who Should Skip It

Readers looking for book discussion or recommendations rather than a writing-focused community should look elsewhere, since this is aimed at people producing work, not just consuming it. Writers already under contract with an agent or publisher may also find less new value in the publishing guidance offered.
