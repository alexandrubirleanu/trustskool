# Cashflow 101 Online Review 2026: Is This Free Community Legit?

Cashflow 101 Online is a free Spanish-language Skool community with 677 members. Its description invites members to simulate their escape from the "rat race" using Cashflow Online, and to learn to think like an investor, with sessions specifically scheduled for the 2nd and 4th Friday of each month.

## What's Actually Inside

The "rat race" and "Cashflow" language points directly to Robert Kiyosaki's well-known Cashflow board game and financial-education framework, which uses simulated financial decision-making to teach investing concepts like passive income, asset versus liability thinking, and building toward financial independence. The specific recurring schedule named in the description, the 2nd and 4th Friday of every month, suggests a structured, predictable cadence of live game sessions rather than an always-on, self-paced course, which matters for anyone trying to figure out how much time commitment this actually requires.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing cost as a barrier to trying a simulation-based approach to financial education.
- A defined, predictable schedule (2nd and 4th Friday monthly) makes it easy to plan around, compared to communities with vague or inconsistent session timing.
- Learning investor thinking through a game format can make abstract financial concepts (cash flow, assets versus liabilities) more concrete and memorable than lecture-style content alone.

## Cons

- We didn't find independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing, so we can't verify session quality, facilitation consistency, or how faithfully sessions represent the original Cashflow framework.
- A twice-monthly schedule means the pace of engagement is relatively slow, so members wanting frequent, ongoing content between sessions may need to supplement with outside material.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking beginners to investing concepts who learn well through simulation and game-based formats, and who are comfortable working within a fixed, twice-monthly schedule, are the clearest fit.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-Spanish speakers will find limited value here, since sessions are conducted in Spanish. People wanting daily or weekly engagement, rather than a twice-a-month cadence, may also find the pacing too infrequent for their learning style.
