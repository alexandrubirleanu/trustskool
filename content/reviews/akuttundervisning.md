# Akuttundervisning Review 2026: A Free Portal for Healthcare Workers and Students

Akuttundervisning, a name that translates from Norwegian roughly to "acute" or "emergency training," is a free Skool community with 765 members. Its description states the ambition plainly: to be the best portal for healthcare personnel and students, making everyday work and study easier, and even lists a phone number for technical support issues.

## What's Actually Inside

The name itself points toward emergency and acute-care medical training specifically, which suggests content likely covers time-critical clinical scenarios, protocols, and decision-making relevant to emergency departments or acute care settings, aimed at both working healthcare professionals and students still in training. Framing itself as a portal rather than a course or coaching program suggests a resource-hub structure, likely organized reference material, guidelines, or case-based learning, rather than a single linear curriculum. The inclusion of a direct technical support phone number in the public description is a practical, unusually concrete detail that signals an actively maintained, operational platform rather than a loosely run hobby community.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing cost as a barrier for healthcare workers and students seeking acute-care educational resources.
- Specifically targeted at healthcare personnel and students, a professional audience with real stakes in accurate, well-organized clinical content.
- The listed technical support line suggests an actively maintained platform with a real operational team behind it, rather than an abandoned or dormant group.

## Cons

Medical and clinical content requires a high bar for accuracy, and the public description doesn't specify what credentialing or review process, if any, governs the material shared, so healthcare professionals should independently verify any clinical guidance against their own institution's protocols. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Norwegian-speaking healthcare workers and students specifically interested in acute and emergency care training and reference material in a free, structured portal format.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-Norwegian speakers, and healthcare professionals outside acute or emergency care settings looking for content in their own specialty, may find this focused on a narrower clinical area than what they need.
