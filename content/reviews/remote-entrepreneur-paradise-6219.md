# Remote Paradise University Review 2026: A Free Community Building a Glamping Village for Digital Nomads

Remote Paradise University is a free English-language Skool community with 484 members organized around an unusually concrete project: building an actual glamping village for digital nomads, rather than just running an online course about remote work.

## What's Actually Inside

Alongside the physical village project, the community offers a free 100-hour course, accessible through its classroom tab, on how to become a digital nomad. That's a substantial amount of structured content bundled at no cost, and pairing it with a real-world building project rather than purely digital content is a distinctive angle compared to most remote-work or digital nomad communities on Skool, which tend to stick to online coaching and courses.

The combination also raises a natural question for anyone evaluating it: whether to treat the community mainly as a free education resource (the 100-hour course) or as an early-stage stakeholder or follower of the physical glamping village itself, since those are two fairly different reasons to join, and the listing doesn't clarify how directly the two are connected, for instance, whether course completion leads to any involvement in the village project.

## Pros

- Free access to a 100-hour course is a large amount of structured content for zero cost.
- The glamping village project gives the community a tangible, real-world goal beyond just content consumption, which could appeal to people wanting an actual physical destination or project to be part of.
- Targets the digital nomad lifestyle specifically, a well-defined audience with shared logistical and lifestyle questions.

## Cons

- The listing's own description is unfinished ("how to become on"), which makes it hard to judge how polished or actively maintained the group's materials are.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People interested in the digital nomad lifestyle who want free foundational training plus the novelty of following, or potentially visiting, an actual glamping village project.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a fully polished, professionally produced course experience, since a rough edge in the community's own description is a signal worth weighing before joining. People who want a purely digital, location-independent community without any tie to a physical project or destination may also prefer a more conventional remote-work community instead.
