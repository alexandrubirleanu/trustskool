# Immo Investment Club Review 2026: Worth Joining for Real Estate Learning?

Real estate investing communities tend to split between deal-chasing hype and slower, wealth-building education, and Immo Investment Club plants its flag firmly in the second camp. It's a free, German-language Skool community with 1,457 members, and its own tagline promises to teach members "A-Z" how to invest successfully in property and build serious, long-term wealth. At close to 1,500 members, it's built up a fairly substantial audience for a niche, language-specific real estate community.

## What's Actually Inside

The description leans on two ideas: a full A-to-Z learning path (implying the community tries to cover the whole journey rather than one narrow tactic) and a long-term, "seriös" wealth-building framing rather than a get-rich-quick pitch. For a German-speaking audience navigating a property market with its own regulatory and tax quirks, that combination, structured education plus a patient time horizon, is the community's core promise. It reads as a group built for people planning a multi-year property portfolio rather than chasing a single quick deal.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no upfront cost to see whether the "A-Z" structure matches how you like to learn.
- Positioned around long-term, sustainable wealth building rather than short-term flipping hype, which the description explicitly calls out ("seriös").
- A German-language space specifically, useful for investors who want market context relevant to Germany or German-speaking Europe rather than US-centric real estate content.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which real estate strategies are covered (rentals, development, REITs, or something else), so it's worth checking the community's posts directly to see what "A-Z" actually includes before assuming it matches your specific interest. We also didn't find independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing, so it's worth asking current members directly about which strategies get the most attention.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking beginners to intermediate investors who want a structured, long-horizon approach to real estate rather than a fast-flip or speculative angle, and who'd rather learn within a community than piece together information from scattered sources.

## Who Should Skip It

Investors looking for aggressive, short-term strategies, or anyone outside the German-speaking market who needs region-specific guidance for a different country's property rules, may find this community's focus doesn't line up with their situation, and would likely be better served by a market-specific alternative in their own country.
