# Growth Consulting Academy Review 2026: Can Ordinary People Really Earn €100,000/Year?

Growth Consulting Academy makes a bold claim right in its description: that growth consulting is "the profession letting completely normal young people earn up to €100,000 a year" by leveraging the success of established companies. It's a free, Italian-language community with 361 members, positioning growth consulting less as a skill to learn and more as a career path to step into.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the community's angle is that members act as growth consultants for other businesses, presumably advising or executing on marketing, sales, or operational growth for companies that already have some traction, rather than building products of their own. The €100,000/year figure is the community's own framing and isn't something this review can independently verify, so it's worth treating as the pitch rather than a guaranteed outcome. Being free also raises a fair question about how the community sustains itself financially, whether through an affiliate structure, paid upsells, or another model not mentioned in the description, which is worth asking about directly once inside.

## Pros

- Free to join, which lowers the barrier for anyone curious about what growth consulting as a profession actually involves.
- The framing around "normal people," rather than marketers with years of agency experience, suggests the material is aimed at beginners rather than assuming prior expertise.
- Growth consulting is a real, definable service category, so the niche itself isn't vague even if the earnings claim is unverified.

## Cons

- The €100,000/year figure comes directly from the community's own marketing copy and isn't independently verifiable from the data available here.
- The description doesn't specify which industries or types of "success" members are meant to be leveraging, so the actual scope of the consulting work is unclear before joining.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Italian-speaking beginners curious about growth consulting as a career and willing to treat the income claim as aspirational marketing rather than a guarantee.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who has already tried growth consulting or agency work and is looking for advanced tactics may find beginner-oriented material too basic, and people uncomfortable with big income promises in marketing copy should apply healthy skepticism before joining.
