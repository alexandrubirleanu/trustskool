# The Nurse in Progress Club Review 2026: Free Nursing Student Support?

The Nurse in Progress Club is free to join and has reached 1,018 members with a specific promise: a structured, supportive space for future nurses who want consistency, confidence, and community, without going through nursing school alone.

## What's Actually Inside

The description names three specific things members are meant to gain: consistency, confidence, and community. That combination maps closely to common pain points in nursing programs, inconsistent study habits under heavy workloads, anxiety around clinical performance and exams, and the isolation that can come with an intense academic path. The "without doing this alone" framing suggests the group's core value is peer support and shared experience among people going through the same stage of training, rather than a formal instructor-led curriculum on nursing content itself.

Nursing programs are widely known for high attrition tied less to raw academic ability and more to burnout, isolation, and inconsistent habits during clinical rotations. A free community explicitly built around consistency and confidence, alongside peer companionship, is targeting exactly that gap rather than trying to compete with a nursing school's actual coursework. For someone already enrolled and studying, that kind of accountability and shared experience layer can matter as much as any additional academic content would.

## Pros

- Free to join, which matters given nursing students already face significant program and textbook costs.
- Speaks directly to the emotional and structural challenges of nursing school (consistency, confidence, isolation) rather than just academic content.
- A tightly defined audience (future nurses specifically) should mean more relevant peer experiences than a general student community.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify whether the community includes structured study plans, exam prep, or clinical guidance, or whether it's primarily peer support and encouragement.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, so actual activity levels and member engagement can't be confirmed from the outside.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Nursing students who want a free peer community for encouragement, accountability, and shared experience through the demands of their program.

## Who Should Skip It

Students specifically seeking structured exam prep, NCLEX-style question banks, or clinical skills instruction should confirm the community covers that before assuming it does, since the description emphasizes support and consistency more than academic content delivery.
