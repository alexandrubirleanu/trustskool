# Otero Airbnb Academy Review 2026: Is a $3,000 Spanish-Language Airbnb Course Worth It?

Otero Airbnb Academy is a $3,000 one-time-payment Skool community with 355 members, billed in its own listing as the #1 Spanish-language Airbnb academy. The stated promise is a step-by-step path from zero experience to a six-figure Airbnb business.

## What's Actually Inside

The description positions this as a structured, guided program rather than a loose discussion group, walking members from a starting position through to a six-figure outcome specifically in the Airbnb short-term rental business. The "#1 in Spanish" framing suggests the community targets the Spanish-speaking short-term rental market as its primary differentiator, alongside the guided, step-by-step structure.

Airbnb-specific education is a crowded space in English, but far less saturated for Spanish-speaking hosts, particularly across Latin America and Spain, where local regulations, property sourcing, and management logistics can differ meaningfully from US or UK markets. A program built specifically for that audience, rather than a translated version of an English course, is a real differentiator if the content is actually localized to those markets, though the public listing doesn't confirm which countries or regulatory environments it covers. Given short-term rental rules vary significantly by city and country, prospective members would be wise to ask directly whether the curriculum addresses their specific market before paying the full $3,000.

## Pros

- A single, large one-time payment ($3,000) rather than an ongoing subscription, which some buyers prefer for a defined program with a clear endpoint.
- Specific outcome target (six-figure Airbnb business) rather than vague income claims, giving a benchmark to evaluate against.
- Positioned specifically for the Spanish-speaking market, a differentiator from the many English-language Airbnb courses already out there.

## Cons

- At $3,000, this is one of the higher-priced communities in its category, and the public listing doesn't break out exactly what's included (calls, templates, software, ongoing support) to justify that price.
- The "#1 academy" and six-figure outcome claims come from the community's own marketing and haven't been independently verified.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking entrepreneurs serious enough about entering the Airbnb business to commit a substantial one-time payment for a structured, guided program.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone wanting to test the Airbnb business model cheaply before committing real money, or English-speaking students, since the program and community operate in Spanish.
