# COA Kunden auf Autopilot Review 2026: A Free but Exclusive Business Program?

COA - Kunden auf Autopilot is a German-language Skool community with 327 members, listed as free to join. Its own description, though, pitches something far more specific and ambitious: reaching a five-figure online business in 90 days, described as guaranteed ("garantiert"), with new spots capped at 10 per month.

## What's Actually Inside

Three concrete, checkable claims sit at the center of the description: a 90-day timeline, a five-figure ("5-stelligen") revenue outcome framed as guaranteed, and a hard limit of 10 new members admitted monthly. That combination, a capped, cohort-style structure, suggests some level of structured onboarding rather than an open, unlimited group, and the specificity of the numbers stands out compared to vaguer "build your business" pitches.

## Pros

- The described capped, monthly-cohort format suggests more structured onboarding than an unlimited open community.
- The stated timeline and revenue target are unusually specific rather than left vague.
- Framing itself as exclusive and limited-capacity is at least a clear, checkable claim members can hold the community to.

## Cons

A guaranteed five-figure revenue outcome within a fixed 90-day window is a strong claim, business results depend on far too many variables, market conditions, individual effort, existing skills, for any program to honestly guarantee them, and that's worth real skepticism regardless of price. It's also worth noting that the free listing here sits oddly next to language of exclusivity and limited spots, so it's worth confirming directly whether real costs exist beyond the Skool community itself. We found no independent reviews of this community's outcomes beyond its own listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People evaluating online-business programs who want to scrutinize a guaranteed-revenue claim directly with the community, and confirm actual costs and terms, before making any further commitment.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who treats "guaranteed" income claims as an immediate red flag, a reasonable and common position, especially tied to a fixed 90-day window, should be cautious here. Non-German speakers will also face a language barrier.
