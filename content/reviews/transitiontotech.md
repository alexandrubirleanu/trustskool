# Transition to Tech Review 2026: Free Community for Breaking Into Tech

Transition to Tech is a free Skool community with 493 members built around helping people move into a tech career. The description bundles three elements together, learning, skills, and community support, and adds an unusual extra: a "REFER N EARN 40%" program that lets members earn passive income by referring others, layering an affiliate incentive on top of the career-focused content.

## What's Actually Inside

The core pitch is career transition support, positioned as an all-in-one space for the learning and community aspects of breaking into tech, though the description doesn't specify which tech roles or skill tracks are covered, whether that's software development, IT support, product management, or something else. The 40% referral program is worth understanding clearly before joining, since it means part of the community's growth model runs on members recruiting other members, which is a different dynamic than a community funded purely by course fees or sponsorship.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing financial barriers for people already navigating a career change.
- Combines learning, skills development, and community support in one place rather than requiring members to piece those together separately.
- The referral program offers a direct, transparent way for members to earn from bringing others into the community, if that appeals to them.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which tech career paths or skill areas the community actually focuses on, so it's unclear how deep or specialized the content is for any single track. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing, and the referral incentive is worth factoring into how you read member endorsements.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People early in a tech career transition who want free access to learning resources and peer support, and who don't mind a community that also runs on a referral-based growth model.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone seeking a specific, accredited technical certification track should confirm the exact curriculum first, since the description doesn't name one directly. People who'd rather avoid communities built partly around referral incentives may also prefer a purely educational alternative.
