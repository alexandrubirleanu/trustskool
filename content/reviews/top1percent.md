# The 1% in AI Review 2026: Is It Worth $97/Month?

The 1% in AI is a 632-member Skool community priced at $97 a month, with a headline promise that leaves little room for interpretation: learn how to start and build an AI business to $50,000 a month. That's a specific, aggressive revenue target, and the entire pitch is built around reaching it through an AI-based business model.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the community is structured around the journey from starting an AI business to scaling it to five figures in monthly revenue, though the listing doesn't specify what kind of AI business, whether that's AI automation agencies, AI content services, AI SaaS, or something else. The $50k/mo figure functions as the north star for the curriculum and community discussion rather than a guarantee.

Communities built around a specific dollar target like $50k/mo tend to attract members at very different starting points, from people with no existing business to those already running something adjacent who want to add an AI angle, and the value of the community likely varies a lot by which group a given member falls into. It's worth asking directly, before paying, whether the curriculum assumes an existing audience or client base, or whether it also covers the earlier step of finding first customers from zero.

## Pros

- Specific, measurable target ($50k/mo) gives members a clear benchmark to work toward rather than vague "make money with AI" messaging.
- Positioned around building an actual business rather than just learning AI skills in the abstract.
- At $97/month, priced in line with many niche business-building communities rather than steep mastermind-level pricing.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which type of AI business model is taught, so it's worth confirming that detail matches your interests before paying. We also found no independent, verified case studies or reviews of members reaching the $50k/mo target, and results in any income-focused community depend heavily on individual execution.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People who already want to build a business around AI specifically, and want a community oriented around aggressive revenue targets, rather than a slower, more general AI-literacy path.

## Who Should Skip It

Beginners who just want to learn what AI tools do, without an immediate intent to build a business around them, are likely better served by a free, less business-focused community first.
