## What's Actually Inside

Two parallel tracks come through in the description: one aimed at people wanting to automate their own existing business operations, and another aimed at people looking to build an AI-automation service and sell it to clients. Supporting both, the group says it provides more than 100 pre-built blueprints along with daily live support, suggesting a template-plus-coaching model rather than a purely theoretical course.

At 403 members, Automatable has built a meaningfully sized paid community relative to its $70 monthly price, which sits among the higher tiers for AI-automation-focused Skool groups. That combination, a substantial fee and several hundred paying members, suggests the community has found enough traction to sustain itself at this price, though prospective members should still weigh the cost against their own timeline and need for daily support specifically.

## Pros

- Daily live support is a meaningfully high level of ongoing access compared to communities offering only weekly or occasional calls.
- 100+ blueprints, if delivered as described, could significantly cut the time needed to build out common automations rather than starting from scratch.
- Dual focus (internal automation and client acquisition) gives value to two different types of members: operators and aspiring consultants.

## Cons

- The 60-day/80% and 30-day/first-client framing are stated targets, not verified average outcomes across the membership, so individual results will vary.
- At $70 a month, this is a meaningful recurring cost, and we found no independent reviews verifying the blueprint count or support quality beyond the community's own description.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Business owners wanting to automate operations quickly, or aspiring AI automation consultants looking for templates and daily support to help land their first client.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone unwilling to pay $70 monthly without first seeing proof of the stated 60-day and 30-day outcomes, or who prefers a slower, more exploratory learning pace, may want to look elsewhere first.
