# SC2 Academy Review 2026: Is the $99/Year Guard Training Community Worth It?

SC2 Academy is an English-language Skool community with 370 members, priced at $99 per year, built around a narrow and specific promise: teaching basketball guards how to dominate on the court.

## What's Actually Inside

The description positions the academy around a single claimed differentiator: it calls itself the only NBA player teaching guards how to dominate. That's a strong claim worth treating as marketing language from the listing itself rather than an independently confirmed credential, since no player name, team history, or verification is included in the description. Support is offered through a listed email address, which suggests some level of direct access to whoever runs the program rather than a fully self-serve course.

Beyond the guard-specific positioning, the description doesn't detail curriculum specifics, drills, or whether content is delivered through video breakdowns, live sessions, or written material. The niche itself, guard play specifically rather than general basketball training, is narrow enough to be a genuine differentiator if the coaching lives up to the pitch.

The annual billing structure ($99/year rather than a monthly fee) is also worth noting: it's a lower ongoing commitment than most monthly-fee coaching communities, which can suit members who prefer a single yearly charge over a recurring monthly one, though it also means less flexibility to cancel quickly if the content doesn't fit.

## Pros

- Narrow, specific positioning (guard play, not generic basketball training) rather than a broad "get better at basketball" pitch.
- A direct support email is listed, suggesting members can reach whoever runs the community.
- At $99/year, the price is modest relative to many one-on-one basketball coaching alternatives.

## Cons

- "The only NBA player teaching guards" is a self-applied claim in the listing, not independently verified for this review.
- No detail on curriculum format, drills, or session structure, and no independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Guards, and coaches of guards, at any level who want position-specific training rather than general basketball instruction, and who are comfortable paying $99/year without independent verification of the instructor's NBA background.

## Who Should Skip It

Players at other positions (forwards, centers) since the content is guard-specific, and anyone who needs verified credentials before committing money to a sports training community.
