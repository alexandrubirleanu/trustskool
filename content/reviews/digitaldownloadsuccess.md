# DCT AI Community Review 2026: Is It Worth $17/Month?

DCT AI Community is a 5,393-member Skool group built around a specific promise: helping members move from passively consuming AI content to actively implementing it. At $17/month, it's one of the more affordable entries in this batch, and it's aimed less at developers or agency builders and more at experienced professionals in fields like education, healthcare, consulting, and corporate training who want to apply AI tools to work they already know how to do.

## What's Actually Inside

The community runs a 30-day transformation structure where members typically walk away with a credential, a new practical skill, and a completed project built using AI in their own field. Rather than generic prompt tutorials, the framing is deliberately about combining AI capability with the "professional discernment" members already have, which shows up repeatedly in the community's own positioning. The about page states plainly: "DCT ranks in the top 300 AI communities on Skool. Not because of marketing. Because members do the work," an emphasis on action over content consumption that runs through the whole structure.

Related DCT-branded offerings outside this specific Skool community include a more advanced "DCT Accelerator" mentorship track for creators ready to build and sell AI-powered digital products at scale, hosted on Teachable rather than Skool [(ct-learning-series.teachable.com)](https://ct-learning-series.teachable.com/l/pdp/dct-accelerator-mentorship-program-for-advanced-ai-users). Worth knowing if you're deciding between the $17/month Skool tier and any upsell you encounter once inside.

## Who Runs It

The founder is Dr. Mesha Bazemore, a Certified AI Consultant and Certified Therapeutic Art Coach who runs MBazemore AI Solutions. Her public materials, including a Teachable instructor page, TikTok presence, and published books on Lulu, consistently position her work around AI education for established professionals, particularly women in midlife navigating a second career act with AI [(cocoatwins.com)](https://cocoatwins.com/pages/about-mbazemore-ai-solutions) [(TikTok)](https://www.tiktok.com/@meshabazemore). That specific audience focus is unusual in the crowded "AI community" space, where most groups default to a generic entrepreneur or marketer audience.

## Pros

- **Genuinely differentiated audience focus.** Targeting professionals over 40 with established expertise, rather than the standard "make money with AI" crowd, is a distinct and clearly-stated niche.
- **Low monthly cost with a free trial.** At $17/month with a trial option, the barrier to testing it is minimal.
- **Action-oriented structure.** The 30-day project-based format gives members a concrete deliverable rather than an open-ended content library to work through alone.

## Cons

We found solid documentation of the founder's credentials and teaching history, but limited independent, third-party reviews of this specific Skool community from members outside the group's own marketing. As with many niche-audience communities, it's worth confirming during the free trial whether the pace and content level match your own background before committing past the trial period, since the target audience (experienced professionals new to hands-on AI use) is fairly specific.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Professionals with real domain expertise, especially in education, healthcare, consulting, or corporate training, who want a structured, supported way to start actually using AI tools rather than just reading about them. It's also a reasonable fit for anyone who feels talked down to by younger, hype-driven AI marketing and wants a community that treats existing professional judgment as an asset rather than something AI replaces.

## Who Should Skip It

Developers, agency builders, or anyone looking for technical, code-heavy AI automation training will find this community's focus mismatched to that goal, there are other Skool communities in this niche built specifically around Claude Code and n8n automation work. Complete beginners to any professional field, with nothing yet to apply AI to, may also get more out of a more generic starter community first.
