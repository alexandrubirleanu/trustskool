# Boss Babe Academy Review 2026: Is It Worth $47/Month?

Boss Babe Academy is a 457-member Skool community teaching real estate wholesaling to beginners, priced at $47/month. It's run by Destiny Haywood and explicitly positions itself as a beginner-friendly, no-license, no-credit path into real estate: members are told they don't need to buy property to make money in the space ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/boss-babe-academy-6942/about)).

## What's actually inside

The stated curriculum is step-by-step training in wholesaling fundamentals: finding discounted properties, contracting with sellers, and assigning those contracts to buyers for a fee, without ever taking ownership of a property. Members get lifetime access to materials, weekly live coaching sessions, deal review and feedback, daily community support, and a library of recorded replays for anyone who misses a live session. The community frames itself with an explicit mission around "breaking generational curses through real estate and digital assets," aiming at first-time investors building income-producing assets rather than seasoned wholesalers looking to scale an existing operation.

## Who runs it

The community's Skool page names Destiny Haywood as founder and lead admin. That doesn't mean the content isn't valuable, but it does mean prospective members should ask directly for verifiable proof of results before paying, rather than relying on the page's claims alone.

## Pros

- A tightly scoped focus on wholesaling specifically (rather than a broad, unfocused real estate grab-bag) makes it easier to know exactly what you're signing up to learn.
- Lifetime access to materials plus ongoing weekly live coaching is a reasonable structure at this price point.
- The explicit no-license, no-capital framing is honest about the entry barrier being low, which matches how wholesaling actually works mechanically (you're assigning contracts, not buying property).

## Cons

- We could not independently verify the founder's background, track record, or prior real estate experience beyond the community's own Skool page — a genuine gap worth asking about directly before joining.
- No testimonials, case studies, or detailed module-by-module curriculum are published on the public about page.
- The community explicitly prohibits recording or sharing course materials, with stated legal consequences for violations, which is a stricter policy than most comparable communities publish and limits how much of the actual content can be reviewed secondhand before joining.
- At 457 members, this is one of the smaller wholesaling communities in this review batch, meaning less peer density than larger alternatives at a similar or lower price.

## Who it's a good fit for

Total beginners specifically drawn to wholesaling as a low-capital entry point into real estate, who are comfortable joining a smaller, newer community without an extensively documented founder track record, may still find the weekly coaching and deal-review structure useful.

## Who should skip it

Anyone who weighs founder verifiability heavily in their decision-making should treat this one more cautiously than the larger, more established wholesaling communities in this space, several of which have independently checkable founder track records.
