# Money Broker VIP Review 2026: Is the $97/Month Funding Community Worth It?

Money Broker VIP is a paid Skool community with 534 members and a $97 monthly fee. The pitch, laid out in bullet points by the community itself, covers four things: access to up to $150,000 in 0% funding, weekly bank intelligence, help deploying that capital, and the ability to earn money per referral.

## What's Actually Inside

Reading the description literally, this sits at the intersection of two different offers stacked together. One side is about business credit and funding, helping members access 0% interest financing (commonly done through 0% APR business credit cards) and then deploy that capital, with "weekly bank intel" suggesting regularly updated information on which banks or cards currently offer favorable terms. The other side is a referral income layer, where members can earn money by referring others into the community or program, which functions as its own income stream separate from the funding education itself.

## Pros

- Very specific, concrete promise ($150K in 0% funding) rather than vague "make money" language
- Weekly bank intel suggests ongoing, updated content rather than a static one-time course
- Built-in referral earning option gives members a second way to get value beyond the funding strategies themselves

## Cons

- 0% business funding strategies depend heavily on individual credit profiles and current bank promotions, and the description doesn't disclose success rates or typical outcomes for members
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, and the referral-income component means some of the community's growth and enthusiasm may be tied to the earning-per-referral incentive rather than the funding education alone

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People with reasonably strong personal or business credit who want a structured, updated resource for finding and stacking 0% funding offers, and who are also interested in earning through referrals.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone with poor credit, who is unlikely to qualify for the funding amounts described, or anyone uncomfortable with communities that blend education with referral-based income, should look elsewhere or verify the model carefully before paying.
