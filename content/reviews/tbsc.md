The Better Setters Club charges $20 a month and has built a 437-member community specifically for appointment setters, both new and experienced, who want to sharpen their craft. Its own description frames the group as a private space to master systems and become a consistent, high-performing professional in that specific role. Being described as "private" also suggests some level of vetting or application before joining, rather than an open sign-up.

## What's Actually Inside
Appointment setting is a distinct skill within sales, booking qualified calls rather than closing deals directly, and this community's description positions itself squarely around that role rather than sales more broadly. The "master systems" language suggests members can expect repeatable processes and scripts for setting appointments, alongside peer support aimed at consistency, which is often the hardest part of this job since income is tied directly to daily activity. Setters often work on commission or a per-booked-call basis, so a community built around consistency is really addressing an income-stability problem as much as a skills problem.

## Pros
- Specific role focus (appointment setting, not general sales) means content is likely more directly applicable than a broad sales community
- Low monthly cost ($20) relative to many sales training programs, making it accessible for setters early in their career
- Framed for both new and experienced setters, suggesting a mix of foundational and advanced material

## Cons
- The description doesn't name specific industries, scripts, or tools covered, so it's worth confirming the content matches your particular market (for example B2B, coaching, or agency) before joining
- No independent reviews outside the Skool listing were found to verify claims about consistency or performance improvements
- At 437 members, this is a modest-sized paid community rather than a large-scale training operation

## Who It's a Good Fit For
New or working appointment setters who want a dedicated, affordable community focused specifically on that role rather than general sales training, especially anyone who's set appointments informally and wants a more repeatable, systemized approach.

## Who Should Skip It
Full-cycle sales reps or closers looking for negotiation and closing skills, since this community's stated focus is specifically on the setting side of the sales process rather than the conversation that happens once a call is actually booked.
