# Skate Life Review 2026: Is This Free Skool Skateboarding Community Worth Joining?

Skateboarding communities tend to live on forums and social media, so a dedicated Skool group is a slightly different format for the sport. Skate Life has picked up 15,219 members with a straightforward, no-cost pitch aimed at skateboarders worldwide.

## What's Actually Inside

The community's description is brief and welcoming: "A community of skateboarders worldwide! Click 'Join' to gain free access today!" There's no mention of lessons, trick tutorials, competitions, or gear reviews in that description, just an open invitation framed around shared identity as skateboarders rather than a specific skill-building curriculum.

Given how little detail the description provides, it reads more like a general gathering space, somewhere skaters can post clips, talk about spots, or just connect with others who ride, than a structured coaching program. Anyone expecting a formal syllabus of tricks or progressions should look for signals of that elsewhere before joining.

## Who Runs It

Greg Lutzka is listed as the owner in the data reviewed. No further background about him is included in that data, so this review doesn't speculate about his skating history, career, or credentials beyond the fact that he's attributed as the community's owner.

## Pros

- Completely free, with the description itself emphasizing "free access" as part of the pitch.
- A large membership base (15,219) for a hobby-focused community, suggesting an active pool of people to interact with.
- Global framing ("worldwide") that could suit skaters looking to connect beyond their local scene, especially anyone who doesn't have a strong local skate crew nearby.
- Low commitment to try out, since joining costs nothing and skaters can simply lurk before deciding whether to post or engage.

## Cons

- The description gives no detail on content, structure, or what members actually do inside beyond general community membership.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, so it's hard to verify activity levels or the quality of discussion from outside.
- There's no indication of moderation, community guidelines, or how disputes or spam are handled, details that matter more as a community's membership count climbs into the tens of thousands.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Skateboarders of any level who want a free, casual online space to connect with other riders globally, share progress, or just be around people who care about the sport. It may also suit skaters relocating or traveling who want a way to find community wherever they land.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking specifically for structured trick tutorials, coaching, or a formal training program should confirm that content exists before joining, since the description reads more like an open social community than a skills curriculum. Riders who prefer smaller, tightly-knit local groups over a large worldwide feed may also find this format less personal than what they're used to.
