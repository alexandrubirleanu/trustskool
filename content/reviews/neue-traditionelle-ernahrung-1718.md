# Neue Traditionelle Ernährung Review 2026: A Free German Nutrition Community

Neue Traditionelle Ernährung ("New Traditional Nutrition") is a free, German-language Skool community with 329 members. Its own description sets a clear direction: getting back to what actually nourishes the body, real food, and building a space to learn, cook for yourself, and get healthier.

## What's Actually Inside

The word "traditional" is doing a lot of work in the framing here: rather than positioning itself around a modern diet trend, the community pitches a return to older, whole-food ways of eating. The description explicitly names learning and self-preparation ("Selbermachen") as core activities, suggesting a hands-on, DIY orientation rather than a passive recipe-scrolling group, with getting healthier ("Gesundwerden") stated as the outcome members are working toward. The description doesn't specify exactly which traditional practices are covered, whether that's fermentation, bone broth, sourdough, or something else, so the precise curriculum isn't clear from the listing alone.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier to trying the material.
- Explicit DIY, self-preparation framing rather than just passive content consumption.
- A specific "traditional food" angle that differentiates it from generic diet or macro-tracking communities.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which traditional food practices or cuisines are covered, so it's hard to know the exact curriculum before joining. It's entirely German-language, so non-German speakers will hit a real barrier. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking people interested in whole-food, traditional approaches to eating who want to learn practical, hands-on food preparation skills rather than just follow a meal plan.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-German speakers won't be able to follow the community without translation. Anyone looking for a structured macro-counting or calorie-based approach, rather than a traditional-foods philosophy, is likely to find this a mismatch.
