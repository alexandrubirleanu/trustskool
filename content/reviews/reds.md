# Real Estate Deal School Review 2026: Is It Worth $25/Month?

Real Estate Deal School is a 1,341-member Skool community covering multiple real estate investing strategies: wholesaling, curative title deals, creative finance, rentals, and fix-and-flip. It's priced at $25/month, though members with an active XLeads software subscription get access included free, which signals the community functions partly as a value-add wrapped around that software product ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/reds/about)).

## What's actually inside

The stated pitch is broad: "make money in real estate in 7 simple steps," aimed explicitly at people with no money, credit, or experience. Underneath that framing, the practical structure is eight weekly live coaching sessions plus guidance from five coaches the page describes as earning $1 million or more annually in their own real estate businesses. Members also get deal-analysis tools, scripts, and contracts, and the option to collaborate directly with the team on active deals rather than working entirely alone. Covering five distinct strategies in one community is unusual; most wholesaling-focused Skool groups pick one lane, so this is a wider net than most competitors in the niche.

## Who runs it

Real Estate Deal School is tied to Zach Ginn, an eight-figure real estate investor who, together with his father Rick Ginn, built XLeads.com, an all-in-one lead-generation and CRM platform for wholesalers and investors offering free unlimited skiptracing and AI-scored leads ([XLeads](https://xleads.com/), [resimpli.com](https://resimpli.com/blog/xleads-review/)). Zach Ginn has an active YouTube presence built specifically around teaching XLeads and wholesaling tactics, giving outside parties a way to see his teaching style and track record before joining ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lvN9DymUTLQ)). Because Real Estate Deal School is bundled with XLeads subscriptions, it functions as much as a customer community for that software as a standalone paid course, which is a meaningfully different structure than most Skool wholesaling communities.

## Pros

- The father-son team behind it has a real, ongoing software business (XLeads) with its own independent reputation and reviews, not just a course funnel.
- Five named coaches and eight weekly live sessions is a genuinely high cadence of live access relative to the $25/month price.
- Covering five real estate strategies rather than one gives members flexibility to pivot toward whichever approach fits their market and capital.
- Bundling free access for XLeads subscribers means existing software customers get real added value at no extra cost.

## Cons

- Breadth across five strategies can come at the expense of depth in any single one; members focused specifically on deep wholesaling mastery may prefer a narrower, more specialized community.
- The "$1M+ annually" coach claim and the "no money or experience needed" framing are stated by the page itself without independent, per-coach verification.
- No testimonials or detailed curriculum breakdown are published on the about page itself, so prospective members are relying on Zach Ginn's outside reputation more than a documented syllabus.

## Who it's a good fit for

Existing or prospective XLeads users get the clearest value here, since the community is effectively bundled into that software relationship. Beginners who want exposure to multiple real estate investing strategies before committing to one specific path, and want a high volume of live coaching for a low monthly price, are also a good fit.

## Who should skip it

Investors who already use a different lead-generation platform and don't want the community tied to a specific software ecosystem may find less pull here. Anyone wanting one deep, narrowly focused wholesaling curriculum rather than a five-strategy overview should look at a more specialized alternative.
