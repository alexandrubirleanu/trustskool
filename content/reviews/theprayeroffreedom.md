# The Prayer of Freedom Skool Review 2026: Is the $3/Month Reader Community Worth It?

The Prayer of Freedom is the official Skool community tied to the book of the same name, priced at just $3 a month and currently sitting at 555 members. It's positioned as a companion space for readers, not a standalone course, built around helping members apply the book's teachings to their own lives. A price that low usually signals the fee is there to cover platform costs and filter out drive-by sign-ups, rather than to fund a heavily produced curriculum.

## What's Actually Inside

According to the community's own description, the group exists to bring together readers of The Prayer of Freedom so they can learn healing practices, learn how to help others going through similar struggles, and grow in their Christian faith together. That framing suggests the core activities are likely discussion around the book's material, mutual support among readers, and faith-centered encouragement rather than a separate curriculum. At $3 a month, it reads as a low-commitment add-on for people who already own or are reading the book, rather than a premium coaching program. The "helping others" element also implies the space is meant to function two ways, members aren't just receiving support, they're expected to eventually offer it to newer readers too.

## Pros

- Extremely low price point ($3/month) removes most of the barrier to trying it out.
- Tightly scoped to one specific book and its readership, which usually means more focused, relevant conversation than a broad "faith and healing" catch-all group.
- Explicit focus on both personal healing and helping others, giving it a two-way, service-oriented angle beyond passive reading.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify whether live calls, structured lessons, or just discussion threads are included, so the format is somewhat unclear from the outside.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing itself, so member experience is hard to verify externally.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Readers of The Prayer of Freedom who want a space to process the book's ideas with others, particularly those looking for Christian community around healing and personal growth rather than a generic self-help forum.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who hasn't read the book, or who isn't looking for a specifically Christian framing of healing, will likely find little relevant discussion here. It's also not built for people wanting a structured, multi-week program with defined milestones.
