# Vidaenyates Community Review 2026: Free Way Into the Superyacht World?

Vidaenyates Community is a free, Spanish-language Skool community with 1,227 members built around a very specific question: is a career in the megayacht world actually for you? Per its own description, the goal is helping people "discover if the world of megayachts is for you, and how to start that change."

## What's Actually Inside

This reads less like a full training program and more like an exploratory, decision-stage community, one aimed at people who are curious about superyacht work (crew roles, hospitality, or related positions) but haven't committed yet. The phrase "cómo empezar ese cambio" (how to start that change) suggests the community also offers some first-step guidance once someone decides the industry is a fit, rather than stopping at pure exploration.

## Pros

- Free to join, which matches its stated purpose as an exploratory first step rather than a paid commitment.
- A narrow, unusual niche (megayacht careers) that's genuinely underserved compared to more common career-change communities.
- Spanish-language delivery, useful for Spanish-speaking audiences considering an industry that's often discussed only in English or through unofficial recruiter channels.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify what "how to start that change" concretely involves (certifications, recruiters, specific entry roles), so members exploring a career change may still need to do significant outside research. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking people curious about working in the superyacht industry who want a low-commitment, free way to start learning whether it's realistic for them before investing in certifications or relocating.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone already working in yachting who needs advanced, industry-insider content, or anyone wanting English-language material, will likely find this community's exploratory, beginner-facing framing too introductory. People who've already decided on the career and just need job placement help specifically may also find the "is this for you" framing more preliminary than what they're looking for.

Because career-exploration communities live or die on whether members actually stick around long enough to make a decision, it's reasonable to check how the group has grown over time before assuming it will hold your attention through that process.
