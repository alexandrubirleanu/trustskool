# The Butter Dojo Review 2026: Is It Worth $49/Month?

The Butter Dojo is a $49/month Skool community with 1,770 members, built around one of the more specific niches you'll find on the platform: helping snowboarders "progress faster by mastering movement, board control, and the principles behind every trick," with particular focus on butter tricks, the flat-ground, press-based maneuvers riders use between features.

## What's Actually Inside

The description emphasizes principles over trick-by-trick tutorials. "Movement, board control, and the principles behind every trick" suggests the teaching approach is built around transferable fundamentals rather than a checklist of individual maneuvers to memorize. For a skill like buttering, which depends heavily on balance, timing, and board feel, a principles-first approach can help riders adapt to tricks not explicitly covered rather than only replicating what they've seen demonstrated.

Niche action-sport coaching communities like this one tend to see engagement rise and fall with the season, so it's worth checking whether the community stays active in the off-season with drills, video breakdowns, or planning content, or largely goes quiet until snow returns. That seasonal rhythm is a normal feature of winter-sport communities generally, not necessarily a flaw specific to this one.

- Emphasis on movement principles and board control fundamentals over rote trick-copying is a more durable teaching approach for a balance-dependent sport.
- At 1,770 members and a real recurring price, the community has enough scale to suggest ongoing demand rather than a one-off launch.

## Cons

- $49/month is a real recurring cost for what is fundamentally a seasonal, weather-dependent sport, so value depends heavily on how much you actually ride during snow season.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Intermediate snowboarders who already have basic riding skills and want structured, principle-based coaching specifically for butter tricks and flat-ground movement, rather than general beginner instruction.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners who haven't yet learned to ride confidently should build basic snowboarding skills first. Riders only interested in big-mountain, park, or backcountry riding rather than flatground tricks are outside this community's specific focus.
