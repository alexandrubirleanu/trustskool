# Kick-Ass Job Squad Review 2026: Free, Neurodivergent-Friendly Job Search Support

Kick-Ass Job Squad has grown to 323 members around a specific, practical need: help with CVs, resumes, and job hunting, delivered with support, accountability, and fun. It's free to join, and the community explicitly welcomes "spicy brains," language commonly used within neurodivergent communities to describe ADHD or otherwise nontypical thinking styles, alongside anyone else job hunting.

## What's Actually Inside

The stated goal is direct and measurable: go from no views to interviews. That framing suggests the community likely centers on practical application review, resume and CV feedback, application strategy, and possibly interview preparation, rather than abstract career philosophy. The explicit welcome to "spicy brains" signals an intentional effort to make the space feel safe and understanding for job seekers who may struggle with the executive-function demands of job hunting (staying organized, following up, managing rejection) in ways generic job-search advice doesn't address.

## Pros

- **Free to join**, removing cost as a barrier during what's often an already stressful and sometimes expensive job search.
- **Concrete, outcome-focused goal.** "No views to interviews" gives a clear benchmark rather than vague career coaching language.
- **Explicitly neurodivergent-friendly framing**, which fills a real gap since most job-search content isn't built with ADHD or executive-function challenges in mind.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify whether the "help" comes from peer feedback, a dedicated coach, or a mix of both, so the depth and consistency of support may vary. We also found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Job seekers who feel unseen by generic job-search advice, especially neurodivergent people who want a supportive, understanding space, alongside anyone simply looking for free CV and resume feedback, are the clearest fit here.

## Who Should Skip It

Job seekers who need highly specialized industry-specific placement services (executive search, niche technical recruiting) may find a general community format too broad for their needs. Anyone expecting guaranteed job placement rather than support and accountability should recalibrate expectations first.
