# NORA Review 2026: Is This $17/Month Virtual Assistant Community Worth It?

NORA is a 1,291-member Spanish-language Skool community with a specific promise: learn to make a living online as a virtual assistant within the next 90 days. Membership is $17/month, one of the more affordable price points in this niche.

## What's Actually Inside

The pitch centers on a defined timeframe, 90 days, to go from wherever a member is starting to working as a virtual assistant online. That structure implies a sequenced path, likely covering the core VA skills, client-facing basics, and how to find work, rather than an open-ended library of tips. The Spanish-language focus is notable since most VA-training content online skews English-first, meaning members get both the instructional material and the peer discussion in their own language rather than working through a translated or subtitled English course.

## Pros

- $17/month is low enough to make trying it a low-stakes decision.
- The 90-day framing gives a concrete timeline members can measure progress against.
- A Spanish-language VA community fills a gap, since a lot of the more established VA training exists only in English.

## Cons

- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own platform listing.
- The description doesn't specify which VA skills, software, or specializations are actually taught, so it's worth confirming before joining if you have a specific niche in mind.
- The "live off the internet" framing is an ambitious promise for a $17/month program, and the description doesn't say how many members have actually reached that outcome, so it's best read as the community's goal rather than a guaranteed result.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking people looking for a low-cost, structured, time-boxed path into remote virtual assistant work. It's probably most useful for people starting from zero who want a defined 90-day plan rather than an experienced freelancer looking to refine an existing VA business.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone wanting VA training in a different language, or people looking for a specific VA specialization that isn't confirmed in the community's description. Freelancers already working as VAs and earning steady income may find the beginner-oriented framing covers ground they've already learned on the job.
