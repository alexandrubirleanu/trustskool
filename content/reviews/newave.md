# Consigue tu Trabajo Remoto Review 2026: Is $199/Year Worth It?

Consigue tu Trabajo Remoto ("Get Your Remote Job") is a 377-member Skool community priced at $199 a year, built around one specific outcome stated directly in its description: teaching members how to land remote jobs with international companies. Billed annually rather than monthly, the pricing structure suggests it's designed for members who commit to a full job-search cycle rather than trying it out month to month.

## What's Actually Inside

The description, "Te enseñamos a conseguir trabajo remoto con empresas internacionales" (we teach you to get remote work with international companies), is a direct promise around job placement or job-search skills specifically for the international remote job market, an area where Spanish-speaking candidates competing for English-language or global roles often need help with resume formatting, interview practices, and platform navigation that differ from local job markets. The annual pricing, rather than monthly, implies the program expects a job search to take meaningful time to complete, though the description doesn't detail specific tactics: resume templates, interview coaching, or introductions to specific companies or platforms.

Remote job placement communities in this space tend to vary widely in what "help" actually means, some offer genuine warm introductions to hiring companies, while others limit support to generic resume feedback and job board links. Given the annual commitment involved here, clarifying which end of that spectrum this community sits on before paying is a reasonable step for anyone evaluating the $199 price against their own job-search timeline.

## Pros

- Annual pricing at $199 works out to roughly $17 a month, reasonable for career-focused education if the job-search guidance proves effective.
- Addresses a genuinely difficult challenge, competing for international remote roles from outside the country where the company is based.
- Clear, singular outcome focus (landing a remote job) rather than a broad, unfocused curriculum.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which industries, skill levels, or types of remote roles (entry-level vs. specialized) the guidance targets, which matters a lot for job-search advice. We also found no independent reviews or verified placement outcomes for this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking job seekers actively targeting remote positions with international companies who want structured guidance and are comfortable committing to an annual price rather than a monthly trial.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for local, in-country job placement rather than international remote roles, or those wanting a low-commitment monthly trial before paying for a full year, should look elsewhere.
