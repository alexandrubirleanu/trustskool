# The Jiu-Jitsu Forum Review 2026: Is This Free BJJ Community Worth Joining?

The Jiu-Jitsu Forum makes a specific claim in its own description: it's the "ONLY FREE Jiu-Jitsu COMMUNITY," built to connect practitioners worldwide and unlock more courses and techniques as members participate. With 632 members and no cost to join, it positions itself against paid BJJ instruction platforms that charge for technique libraries.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the community centers on a Classroom section that houses courses and techniques, with access seemingly tied to participation rather than a flat one-time unlock. That participation-gated structure is worth noting, it suggests members who engage more, posting, commenting, showing up, may see more of the technique library than those who join and stay passive.

Free jiu-jitsu communities built around a participation-gated Classroom sit somewhere between fully open YouTube technique libraries and paid instructional platforms like BJJ Fanatics, and the appeal here is presumably a mix of community discussion plus incremental access to curated technique content. Practitioners already paying for a specific instructor's paid instructional platform may find the free technique library here a useful supplement rather than a replacement, since it isn't tied to one coach's particular system.

## Pros

- Free to join, with courses and techniques available through participation rather than a paywall.
- Global, practitioner-to-practitioner framing rather than being tied to one specific gym or instructor's system.
- Explicit Classroom structure suggests organized technique content rather than just an open discussion forum.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which BJJ lineage, gi versus no-gi focus, or belt-level content the courses cover, and the participation-gated access model isn't fully explained, so it's unclear exactly how much activity unlocks how much of the library. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Jiu-jitsu practitioners at any level who want a free, global community to supplement in-person training, and who don't mind engaging actively to unlock more of the technique library.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a single named instructor's structured curriculum, rather than a general community forum, or people expecting instant access to the full technique library without participating, may find this a mismatch. Practitioners who prefer to lurk rather than post or engage may also get less value out of a participation-gated model like this.
