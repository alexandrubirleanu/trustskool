## What's Actually Inside

The three stated functions, connect, equip, encourage, suggest a network model rather than a single training program: connecting members likely means linking people doing similar basketball-ministry work across different locations or organizations, equipping points to practical resources or training for running basketball-based outreach or discipleship programs, and encouraging suggests ongoing peer and community support for people doing this work, which can be relationally demanding. This positions the community for people already involved in, or seriously exploring, basketball as a ministry tool, rather than for basketball players or coaches seeking sport-specific training.

Sports-based ministry is a recognized approach within Christian missions work, using a shared, accessible activity as a bridge to build relationships before introducing explicitly religious content, and basketball in particular travels well across many countries and cultures with minimal equipment requirements. A dedicated network for people running this specific model likely helps members avoid reinventing program structure from scratch, sharing what has and hasn't worked in their own local context instead.
