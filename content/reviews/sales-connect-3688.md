This isn't framed as a pure education community, it's positioned as a talent pipeline. The second part, "conectamos talento en ventas con empresas con estándar alto," suggests an active matching function between trained members and employers, which is a more concrete value proposition than most sales-training communities that stop at teaching techniques.

- The dual training-plus-placement model addresses a real gap: many sales courses teach skills without any path to actually getting hired or placed.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify what "empresas con estándar alto" (high-standard companies) means in practice, industries, company sizes, or geographic focus, so the placement side is vague from the listing alone.
- No independent reviews of this community exist beyond the Skool platform listing, so there's no outside confirmation of how many members are actually placed with companies versus just trained.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Spanish-speaking individuals looking to break into or professionalize an online sales career, particularly those motivated by the prospect of being connected with hiring companies rather than training alone.

## Who Should Skip It

Experienced sales professionals who don't need foundational training, or anyone looking for a purely educational community without an implied job-matching component.

## Bottom Line

Because the community claims an active matching function between trained members and hiring companies, the real test is whether that placement side delivers in practice rather than staying purely aspirational. Since joining is free, a reasonable approach is to ask current or past members directly about actual placements before assuming the "high standard companies" framing reflects a large or active hiring pipeline.
