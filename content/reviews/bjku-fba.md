# AMZ Certification Program Review 2026: Is It Worth $97/Month?

The AMZ Certification Program is Bashar J Katou's Skool community for BJK University, positioned as "the largest FBA training university in the world." With 6,172 members drawn from 47 countries, it teaches Amazon private label selling with the stated goal of getting members certified as AI-powered sellers and launching a profitable product within 12 weeks. Membership runs $97/month, with a free 7-day trial offered at times.

## What's Actually Inside

The community centers on a 12-week structured path toward Amazon FBA certification, backed by 24/7 AI-powered support and product research tools. New members get live onboarding calls with a roadmap customized to their situation, and the ongoing curriculum includes bi-weekly webinars featuring six- and seven-figure sellers as guest coaches. Beyond core Amazon training, the program also covers Walmart and TikTok Shop as additional sales channels, plus quarterly strategy updates to keep pace with marketplace algorithm and policy changes. The group caps new admissions at 12 per month according to its about page, which typically means a waitlist.

This Skool community sits alongside BJK University's flagship paid mentorship, which independent reviewers report runs $3,800 to $5,000 as a separate, higher-tier offer [(ippei.com)](https://ippei.com/bjk-university/) [(scamrisk.com)](https://www.scamrisk.com/bashar-katou/). The $97/month Skool tier appears to be the lower-cost entry point into the same ecosystem rather than the full mentorship program itself, so it's worth confirming exactly what's bundled before assuming Skool membership includes 1:1 coaching.

## Who Runs It

Bashar J Katou founded BJK University with a stated mission to "impact 1 million lives" through e-commerce education. He's built a substantial public profile around Amazon FBA training, with YouTube tutorials and a large enrolled student base across his various programs.

## Pros

- **Established, multi-channel curriculum.** Beyond Amazon, the program folds in Walmart and TikTok Shop, which is more diversified than most single-platform FBA courses.
- **Structured onboarding.** A live, customized roadmap call at the start is a concrete step many lower-cost communities skip.
- **Sizable community with global reach.** Members across 47 countries suggests real breadth, not just a US-only audience.

## Cons

Independent review sites give a genuinely mixed picture here, and it's worth being direct about it. Trustpilot flagged and removed a number of reviews on BJK University's profile for inauthenticity [(Trustpilot)](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/bjkuniversity.com), which undermines confidence in testimonials at face value. Multiple independent reviewers note that at scale, personalized mentor attention becomes harder to get, with some students describing generic responses during group coaching [(ippei.com)](https://ippei.com/bjk-university/). The higher-tier BJK University program also reportedly has no refund policy, which is unusual for a program in this price range and raises the stakes of getting it wrong [(sidehustlesdatabase.com)](https://sidehustlesdatabase.com/bjk-university-review/). None of this makes the $97/month Skool community itself a scam, but it does mean the surrounding ecosystem has real, documented reputation friction worth weighing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Someone who has already decided Amazon FBA private label is the business model they want to pursue, has capital for inventory beyond the course fee, and wants a structured 12-week framework with live coaching touchpoints rather than a self-paced video library.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone comparison-shopping FBA courses purely on price, or anyone who hasn't independently verified current reviews before committing, especially given the flagged review authenticity issues on Trustpilot. If the $3,800 to $5,000 upsell is where the real mentorship lives, budget-conscious buyers should get clarity on exactly what the $97/month tier includes before assuming it matches the marketing.
