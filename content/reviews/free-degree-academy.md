# Free Degree Academy & Coaching Review 2026: Is It Worth $49/Month?

Free Degree Academy & Coaching is a $49/month Skool community with 794 members. Its description bills it as the program for students who want scholarships, financial aid, and money for college so they can graduate without debt, calling itself an exclusive scholarship club.

## What's Actually Inside

The focus here is narrow and outcome-driven: helping students find and win scholarships and financial aid specifically, rather than covering general college advice like choosing a major or study habits. Framing it as an "exclusive scholarship club" suggests some curated access to scholarship opportunities or application strategies, rather than just generic tips easily found through a search engine.

Scholarship and financial aid searching is a genuinely time-consuming and often confusing process for students and families, with opportunities scattered across countless individual organizations, each with different deadlines and requirements. A community built specifically around consolidating that search and coaching members through applications addresses a real, well-documented pain point in the college-funding process, though the description doesn't specify whether the "exclusive" scholarships referenced are curated by the community itself or simply aggregated from public sources.

## Pros

- Specific, high-value outcome focus on avoiding college debt through scholarships and financial aid, not a general student community.
- The "exclusive scholarship club" framing implies curated access to opportunities or strategies.
- At $49/month, the cost is modest relative to the amount of student debt the program aims to help members avoid.

## Cons

"THE #1 program" is unverifiable marketing language from the community's own description, and there's no stated track record of how many members have actually secured scholarships or graduated debt-free. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Current or prospective college students, or their parents, actively searching for scholarships and financial aid strategies to reduce or eliminate college debt, particularly families early in the process who haven't yet built a system for tracking deadlines and applications across multiple scholarship sources.

## Who Should Skip It

Students who've already secured their financing or have graduated, and anyone outside the US-style scholarship and financial-aid system this appears to be built around. Families who've already worked extensively with a school's own financial aid office and a private counselor may also find some overlap with guidance they've already received.
