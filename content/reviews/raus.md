# RAUS Review 2026: Is This Free Auswanderer Community Worth Joining?

RAUS (Auswanderer & Digitale Nomaden) is a free, German-language Skool community that has grown to 1,149 members around one clear audience: people who are traveling, emigrating, or self-employed abroad, with a specific tilt toward digital nomads. The name itself, "RAUS," translates roughly to "OUT," which tells you a lot about the community's orientation before you even read the description.

## What's Actually Inside

Per its own description, RAUS positions itself as a community for travelers, emigrants, and self-employed people, with tips and exchange around successfully emigrating. That framing suggests the value here is less a formal course and more a peer network: people who've already made the move, or are mid-process, sharing logistics, decisions, and lessons with those still planning theirs. Given the digital nomad emphasis, expect discussion that touches on remote income, visas and relocation logistics, and the practical, unglamorous side of living abroad. At 1,149 members and free to join, the community has clearly prioritized reach over gatekeeping, which fits a topic where the value comes largely from the range and diversity of members' own relocation experiences.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no financial risk in testing whether the community and its members' situations match your own emigration plans.
- Clear, specific audience of German-speaking travelers, emigrants, and self-employed digital nomads, which tends to produce more relevant discussion than a broad "expat" catch-all.
- Peer-driven positioning around tips and exchange suggests real lived experience rather than purely theoretical content.
- A community built around a shared language and shared destination challenges tends to surface more actionable, specific advice than a broad, multilingual global-nomad forum.

## Cons

- No independent reviews or outside sources were found beyond the Skool listing itself, so activity level and content quality aren't independently verifiable.
- The community operates in German, which limits accessibility to German-speaking members and rules it out for anyone who doesn't read or write the language comfortably.
- The description doesn't name specific target countries beyond Dubai-adjacent or general "emigration," so members researching one specific destination should confirm relevant expertise exists before joining.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking individuals actively planning or already living through emigration, especially as digital nomads or self-employed professionals, who want a peer community rather than a formal course, are the clearest fit.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-German speakers, and anyone looking for structured, curriculum-style relocation training rather than community exchange, should look elsewhere.
