# The Info Funnel Review 2026: Is the $5,000/Year Coach Scaling Program Worth It?

The Info Funnel is a 652-member Skool community priced at $5,000 per year, a premium tier well above the typical Skool community price point. The pitch is scaling coaches to the next level through what the description calls "scientific methods of traffic, conversions, and fulfillment," combining course content, community, and education under a banner it calls "Scale Info."

## What's Actually Inside

The description breaks the offer into three functional areas that map to how an info-product or coaching business actually operates: traffic (getting attention and leads), conversions (turning that attention into paying clients), and fulfillment (delivering the actual coaching or program so clients get results and stick around). The phrase "scientific methods" signals the positioning is process-and-data-driven rather than purely motivational, aimed at coaches who already have some traction and want a repeatable system to scale further, rather than beginners just starting out. Given the $5,000/year price point, this sits at the higher end of the coaching-and-course-creator Skool market, closer to a mastermind-style offer than a typical low-cost community.

## Pros

- Addresses all three core levers of a scaling coaching business (traffic, conversion, fulfillment) rather than focusing on just lead generation or just sales.
- The "scientific methods" framing suggests a systems-based approach, which can suit coaches who want repeatable processes over one-off tactics.
- Combines course content, community, and education in one offer rather than requiring separate purchases for each.

## Cons

- No independent reviews found beyond the platform listing, and at $5,000/year, the stakes of that gap are much higher than with a typical low-cost Skool community.
- The description doesn't name specific instructors, case studies, or results, making it hard to judge the program's track record from the listing alone.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Established coaches who already have an offer and some client base but want a structured, systems-driven approach to scaling traffic, conversions, and fulfillment, and who have the budget for a premium annual commitment.

## Who Should Skip It

Coaches just starting out without an existing offer or client base, or anyone unwilling to commit $5,000 annually without first seeing detailed proof of results and instructor credentials.
