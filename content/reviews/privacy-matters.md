# The Hacker Academy Review 2026: Is $9/Month Worth It for Learning Real Hacking Skills?

The Hacker Academy charges $9 a month to 397 members with a pitch built on authenticity, "real hackers teaching real skills," according to its own description, backed by free cheatsheets and weekly live sessions. At that price point, it positions itself as an accessible entry into cybersecurity and hacking education.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, members get two concrete, recurring elements: downloadable cheatsheets provided at no extra cost, and a weekly live session cadence, alongside a community described as actively helping members level up. That combination of reference material plus regular live touchpoints suggests a structure built for steady skill progression rather than a one-time content dump.

## Pros

- Very low price point ($9/month) relative to many cybersecurity education offerings.
- Weekly live sessions provide a consistent cadence for engagement and learning.
- Free cheatsheets add tangible, reusable reference material beyond just live content.

## Cons

- The description doesn't specify the skill level assumed, beginner versus advanced, or which specific hacking domains, web, network, or otherwise, are covered in the live sessions.
- We found no independent reviews of this community or verification of instructor credentials.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Beginners and hobbyists interested in ethical hacking and cybersecurity who want an affordable, live-session-based way to start learning, backed by reference cheatsheets.

## Who Should Skip It

Experienced security professionals looking for advanced, specialized training may find an introductory-leaning community less useful than more specialized alternatives.
