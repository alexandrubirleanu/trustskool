# StartFly Review 2026: Is This Free Skool Support Community Worth Joining?

StartFly's Skool community is free to join and has reached 316 members, but it isn't positioned as a course or coaching program at all. According to its own description, it's a support and updates community for people who already use StartFly, described as an all-in-one marketing and sales tool.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, this reads as a product support hub rather than an educational one. Members likely come here to get help using StartFly, hear about new features or updates, and troubleshoot alongside other users, similar to how a number of SaaS companies now run customer support communities directly on Skool instead of a separate help desk.

That approach has real advantages for a software company: instead of scattering support requests across email tickets and social media comments, everything lives in one searchable place, and users can often solve each other's problems faster than a support team working through a queue. The tradeoff is that a support community's usefulness depends entirely on how actively the StartFly team itself participates, answers questions, and posts updates, none of which is detailed in the description available here.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no barrier for current or prospective StartFly users.
- Tied directly to product updates, so members get news about new features as they roll out.
- Community-based support can feel faster and more personal than a traditional support ticket queue.

## Cons

The value here is entirely tied to already using or planning to use StartFly's software, so it offers little to anyone outside that user base. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing, so response times and support quality can't be verified from outside.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Current or prospective StartFly users who want a direct line to product updates, troubleshooting help, and peer support from other users of the tool.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone not using or considering StartFly's marketing and sales software has little reason to join, since the community's entire value proposition is scoped to that specific product rather than marketing and sales education in general. People shopping around for an all-in-one marketing tool and looking for an unbiased comparison should also look elsewhere, since a vendor-run support community isn't the place to get a neutral read on how StartFly stacks up against competitors.
