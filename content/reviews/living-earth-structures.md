# Living Earth Structures Review 2026: Is It Worth $20/Month?

Living Earth Structures is a Skool community with 383 members, priced at $20 a month, teaching natural building techniques. The community is introduced by Sir Cobalot, described as a natural builder with over 25 years of experience, whose stated pitch is helping members build an affordable, sustainable, durable, and beautiful natural structure, whether that's a Hobbit-house-style earthen home, a sauna, an outdoor oven, or similar builds.

## What's Actually Inside

Going by the description, the curriculum spans multiple types of natural structures rather than a single project. Natural building typically uses materials like cob, earthbags, straw bale, or similar low-impact methods instead of conventional lumber and concrete, which lines up with the "affordable, sustainable, durable" framing. The range of example projects (a Hobbit house, a sauna, an oven) suggests the community covers both larger dwelling-scale builds and smaller functional structures, giving members flexibility depending on their own project scope and land access.

## Pros

- Led by someone the community describes as having over 25 years of hands-on natural building experience, which is a meaningful depth of practical background for this kind of skill.
- Covers a range of project types (dwellings, saunas, ovens) rather than teaching just one narrow build, giving members multiple entry points.
- Natural building appeals to people specifically wanting lower-cost, more sustainable alternatives to conventional construction, and the community is framed directly around that value.

## Cons

- No independent reviews found beyond the platform listing, so the instructor's background and the outcomes of past student builds can't be verified from outside sources.
- Natural building projects, especially anything intended as a permanent dwelling, can involve local permitting and building code questions that a general online community may not fully address for every region.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People interested in hands-on, low-cost natural building projects, whether a full earthen dwelling, a backyard sauna, or a wood-fired oven, who want to learn from someone with a long stated background in the craft rather than piecing together scattered internet tutorials.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for conventional stick-frame or modern construction techniques, or who needs detailed region-specific permitting and code guidance rather than general building technique, should treat this as a supplement to, not a replacement for, local professional and regulatory advice. People with no interest in hands-on, DIY-style building projects would also get little use from a community centered on the physical craft of construction.
