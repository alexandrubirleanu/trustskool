# The Photo Project Review 2026: Is $7/Month Worth It for Moms?

For $7 a month, The Photo Project promises something narrower and more concrete than most self-improvement communities on Skool: getting every photo you've ever taken safe, organized, and backed up. It's aimed squarely at moms, with 1,232 members currently working through what the community describes as a step-by-step process backed by "real support every week, until it's done."

## What's Actually Inside

The description frames this as a project with an endpoint, not an open-ended hobby. Members work through organizing and backing up years of accumulated phone and camera photos, with weekly support built in so the process doesn't stall out the way most people's photo backlogs do on their own. The "until it's done" framing suggests ongoing accountability rather than a single course members watch once and never finish, which matters for a task most people know they should do but keep putting off.

## Pros

- Low price point at $7/month, making it an easy trial for a task most people have procrastinated on for years.
- A clearly defined outcome (photos safe, organized, backed up) rather than a vague "get better at photography" promise.
- Built-in weekly support addresses the real reason most people never finish this kind of project alone: it's tedious and easy to abandon halfway through.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which platforms or backup systems are taught (Google Photos, iCloud, external drives, or a specific paid tool), so members won't know the exact technical approach until they join. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Moms (or really any parent) sitting on years of disorganized, unbacked-up photos who've tried and failed to tackle the project alone, and who want a low-cost, accountability-driven nudge to actually finish it.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone already using a photo organization system they're happy with, or looking for creative photography instruction rather than organizational and backup help, won't find much use for this specific community. Dads, grandparents, or anyone else sitting on the same unsorted photo backlog could plausibly benefit too, even though the marketing is aimed squarely at moms.

At $7 a month, it's cheap enough that the real question isn't the price but whether the weekly accountability structure actually gets you to the finish line, which is worth weighing against how consistently the community itself appears to be running.
