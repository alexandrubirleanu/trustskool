# The Cooperation Collective Review 2026: Is This $57/Month Neurodivergent Family Community Worth It?

The Cooperation Collective is a 1,299-member Skool community focused on co-regulation and practical tools for neurodivergent families, hosted by Dr. Chelsey according to the group's own description. Membership costs $57/month, noticeably higher than most general-interest Skool communities, which fits a niche that leans closer to therapeutic support than casual hobby content.

## What's Actually Inside

Co-regulation, helping a child manage big emotions by first regulating your own state as a parent or caregiver, is a specific, well-defined concept in neurodivergent parenting circles, and it's the throughline of this community's positioning. The promise of "practical tools" suggests concrete strategies members can apply in the moment, rather than general awareness content about neurodivergence. Framing the group around a named host also implies a somewhat structured delivery, likely regular content or live sessions from Dr. Chelsey, rather than a purely peer-run forum with no central voice.

## Pros

- The niche is precise. Co-regulation for neurodivergent families is a much narrower and more actionable promise than generic "parenting support."
- The $57/month price signals a community likely aimed at sustained, ongoing support rather than a one-off course, which fits how co-regulation skills tend to be built over time.
- Being organized around a single named host gives the community a consistent point of view rather than a crowdsourced grab-bag of parenting advice.

## Cons

- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own platform listing.
- The description doesn't specify Dr. Chelsey's professional background or discipline, so it's unclear how clinical or credentialed the guidance is meant to be.
- At $57/month, this is priced closer to a specialized support program than a hobby community, which is worth factoring in alongside whatever else a family may already be spending on therapy or coaching.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Parents and caregivers of neurodivergent children looking for practical, in-the-moment co-regulation strategies and a community of others navigating similar daily challenges. It's likely most useful for families who already have some baseline understanding of neurodivergence and want applied tools and peer support, rather than a starting point for learning what neurodivergence is.

## Who Should Skip It

Families needing crisis-level clinical intervention, diagnosis, or licensed therapy, since a Skool community, whatever its quality, is not a substitute for individual clinical care. Anyone expecting one-on-one coaching tailored to their specific child's needs should also confirm what level of individualized support, versus general shared tools, the $57/month membership actually includes.
