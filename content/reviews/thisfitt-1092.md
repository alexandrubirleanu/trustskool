# EPA Training Review 2026: Is This Free Community Worth Joining?

EPA Training is a free, private Skool group with 801 members. Its description is aimed at two audiences at once, athletes and coaches, and names four specific things members get: elite training clarity, real feedback, study reviews, and weekly breakdowns.

## What's Actually Inside

Serving both athletes and coaches in the same space is a notable design choice, it suggests the community is meant to bridge the gap between the people executing training and the people designing it, rather than treating them as separate audiences. The four named offerings, feedback, study reviews, and weekly breakdowns in particular, point toward a recurring cadence of structured input rather than a static library of tips.

Having athletes and coaches in the same room can cut both ways: it may give athletes direct access to coaching-level thinking they wouldn't otherwise see, while giving coaches a closer read on how athletes actually experience and apply training advice. Whether that dynamic plays out well in practice depends heavily on how active and how experienced the coach members actually are, something the description doesn't specify.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier for either athletes or coaches to test the fit.
- Serves both athletes and coaches directly, rather than assuming only one perspective.
- Names concrete recurring offerings (weekly breakdowns, real feedback) instead of vague motivational content.
- A private group structure can foster more candid feedback exchanges than a fully public, open forum where members may be more guarded.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which sport or discipline the training applies to, and "study reviews" isn't defined clearly enough to know if it means film study, research review, or something else. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing. As a free, private group, it's also worth confirming what the actual entry or approval process looks like, since "private" can mean anything from an open application to a more selective vetting process.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Athletes and coaches who want structured, recurring feedback and weekly breakdowns in a smaller, private setting rather than a large public forum.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a community tied to one specific, named sport, or those who prefer open, public networking over a private group format.
