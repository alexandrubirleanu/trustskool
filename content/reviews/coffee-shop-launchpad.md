# Coffee Shop Launchpad Review 2026: Is $49/Month Worth It for Aspiring Cafe Owners?

Coffee Shop Launchpad is a Skool community with 326 members, priced at $49 a month. Its description speaks directly to a specific person: someone who wants to open a coffee shop but doesn't yet know how, offering, in its own words, all the necessary pieces, help, and a like-minded community for the journey.

## What's Actually Inside

The pitch is broad by design, "all the necessary pieces," rather than naming specific deliverables like business plan templates, equipment sourcing guides, or licensing checklists. What is clear is the target stage: pre-opening, for people planning a coffee shop rather than already running one. Community support from others pursuing the same specific goal is named as part of the value, which could matter given how many practical, on-the-ground problems (location scouting, supplier relationships, permitting) tend to come up during this exact stage.

## Pros

- Targets a specific, well-defined life stage, before opening, rather than trying to serve both new and existing coffee shop owners at once.
- A peer community of others pursuing the same goal could be genuinely useful for troubleshooting real, shared problems.
- At $49 a month, the ongoing cost is modest relative to the capital typically required to open a physical coffee shop.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify whether the guidance is built around a particular country's licensing, health codes, or commercial real estate norms, all of which vary enormously and matter a great deal for something this regulated. We found no independent reviews of this community or evidence of member outcomes beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People seriously planning to open a coffee shop who want structured guidance and a peer community through the pre-opening process.

## Who Should Skip It

Current coffee shop owners looking for growth or operations help, rather than opening guidance, are likely to find this misaligned with their stage. Anyone still in the very early idea phase, not yet ready to commit to a monthly fee, may want to wait.
