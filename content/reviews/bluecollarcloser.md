# Blue Collar Closer Review 2026: Is It Worth $49 a Month?

Blue Collar Closer is a 350-member Skool community priced at $49 a month, built specifically for home services technicians and salespeople, plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, and similar trades where selling is part of the job even when the person isn't a career salesperson. The description positions itself against "pushy sales" and "gimmicks," promising instead "real strategies to double revenue and income fast."

## What's Actually Inside

The framing speaks directly to a common tension in home services work, technicians who are skilled at the trade itself but less comfortable or trained in selling upgrades, memberships, or additional services to homeowners. By explicitly rejecting pushy tactics, the community positions its approach as more consultative or relationship-based selling, though the description doesn't detail exactly which sales techniques, scripts, or frameworks are taught.

At $49 a month, the price is positioned as a business expense rather than a casual purchase, which fits the framing of selling as a revenue lever for an existing trade business rather than a hobbyist interest.

## Pros

- Targets a specific, underserved audience, home services technicians and salespeople, rather than generic sales training for any industry.
- Explicitly rejects pushy or gimmicky tactics, which may appeal to tradespeople uncomfortable with aggressive sales approaches.
- States a concrete goal (doubling revenue and income) rather than vague motivational language.

## Cons

The "double revenue fast" claim is the community's own marketing language and isn't something we could verify independently. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Home services technicians and salespeople who want to sell more effectively without adopting high-pressure tactics, and who are comfortable paying $49 a month for structured sales training in their specific industry.

## Who Should Skip It

Salespeople outside the home services trades, or anyone looking for a general sales course not tailored to plumbing, HVAC, electrical, or similar fields, may find the industry-specific focus here less relevant. Solo operators who don't handle in-home sales conversations directly may also find less immediate use for the material.
