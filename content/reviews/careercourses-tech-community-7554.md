# Scale In Tech Review 2026: Is This Free Tech Career Community Worth Joining?

Scale In Tech, run by TITNB, is a free, 350-member Skool community focused on helping people break into or advance within tech careers. Its description names three components directly: "tech career education with job placement support" and "tech events, community and elevation."

## What's Actually Inside

Job placement support is the standout element here, many career-education communities stop at teaching skills, while this one explicitly claims to help connect members with actual roles, though the description doesn't specify what that support looks like in practice, resume review, referrals, direct employer connections, or something else. The mention of "tech events" alongside general community access suggests a mix of structured programming and ongoing peer interaction rather than a purely self-paced course.

The word "elevation" in the description reads as aspirational branding rather than a specific claim, so it's worth focusing evaluation on the two more concrete elements, the career education itself and the job placement support, rather than the broader motivational language surrounding them.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing cost as a barrier to testing whether the career education content matches your specific tech path.
- Explicitly names job placement support rather than stopping at skills training alone, addressing the gap between learning and getting hired.
- Includes tech events as part of the offer, useful for networking beyond just course material.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify which tech roles or skill tracks (software engineering, data, product, IT support) are covered, nor exactly what job placement support entails. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Anyone trying to break into a tech career or advance within one, who wants both educational content and some form of job placement assistance alongside community support.

## Who Should Skip It

Experienced tech professionals who don't need career-transition support, or those looking for deep, specialized technical training in one narrow area, may find a broad career-education community too general. People expecting placement support to mean a guaranteed job offer, rather than assistance with the search, should recalibrate expectations before joining.
