# New Society Review 2026: A Free Community Promising an AI Business in 3 Weeks

New Society is free to join and has grown to 402 members around a bold, time-boxed pitch stated plainly in its own description: "Build & Launch Your AI Business in 3 Weeks." That's the entirety of the group's public description, a short, direct promise rather than an elaborated curriculum.

## What's Actually Inside

Because the description is so compact, what's implied is a structured, fast-paced program aimed at taking someone from idea to a launched AI business inside a three-week window. It doesn't specify what "AI business" means in practice, whether that's a SaaS product, an AI-powered service, an agency, or a content-based offer, so members should expect to learn those specifics only after joining.

At 402 members and no membership fee, New Society has attracted a reasonably sized free audience around its three-week promise, though the brevity of the public description means most of the specifics, format, mentorship, tools used, only become visible after joining. That's worth factoring in for anyone who prefers to evaluate a program in detail before committing time to it.

## Pros

- Free to join, so testing the community carries no financial risk.
- A clear, time-boxed goal, three weeks, gives members a concrete timeline to measure progress against, rather than an open-ended "someday" promise.
- The AI business angle taps into genuine current demand from people wanting to build something in this space.

## Cons

- Three weeks is an aggressive timeline for building and launching any business, and the description offers no data on how many members actually hit that goal.
- The description is extremely brief, leaving format, structure, and specific tools entirely unclear, and we found no independent reviews of New Society elsewhere.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People who want a fast, structured push to get an AI-related business idea off the ground and are comfortable with an aggressive, compressed timeline.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone needing a slower, more thorough approach to business building, or who's skeptical of three-week launch promises without supporting data, should look for a more detailed program first.

Anyone joining primarily to evaluate feasibility before investing serious time should ask current or past members directly about typical outcomes, since the public description alone doesn't provide that context.
