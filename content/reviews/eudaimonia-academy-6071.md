# Eudaimonia Academy Review 2026: Free Community for Health Recovery from Modern Life?

Eudaimonia Academy is a free, French-language Skool community of 375 members. The pitch, in the founder's own words, is helping people who are, translated directly, "destroyed by modernity" recover their health and performance. The name itself, Eudaimonia, a Greek philosophical term generally associated with human flourishing, signals a broader wellbeing framing rather than a narrow fitness or diet program.

## What's Actually Inside

The description names three pillars side by side: "PERIO CONCU," "PRIMAL DIET," and "MENTAL." The primal diet and mental components point clearly toward nutrition and mindset work, in line with the "destroyed by modernity" framing, the idea that modern food and lifestyle habits are the problem, and a return to more ancestral or foundational patterns is the solution. The first term isn't explained anywhere in the public listing, though, so its exact meaning isn't clear without asking directly.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier to see whether the approach resonates.
- Frames health specifically as a reaction against modern lifestyle habits, a positioning some members may connect with more than generic fitness messaging.
- Combines diet and mental components rather than addressing just one, reflecting a more holistic framing than most single-issue wellness communities.

## Cons

One of the three named pillars, "PERIO CONCU," isn't explained anywhere in the public description, making it hard to fully evaluate the offer before joining. The description also doesn't specify whether any content is delivered by a licensed nutritionist, physician, or mental health professional, which matters given the health claims implied by "retrouver leur santé" (recovering their health). No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French speakers who feel their health or performance has suffered from modern lifestyle habits and want a diet-and-mindset framed community to address it, particularly those drawn to a more philosophical or ancestral framing of wellness.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone wanting a fully transparent breakdown of the curriculum before joining should ask directly what each of the three named pillars covers, since the public listing doesn't spell it out. People with diagnosed medical conditions should treat this as a community, not a substitute for licensed medical or nutritional care.
