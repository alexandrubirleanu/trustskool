# Die KI-Werkstatt Review 2026: A Free German AI-Coding Community for Developers

Die KI-Werkstatt (German for "The AI Workshop") is a free Skool community built for German-speaking developers who want to learn AI-assisted coding, with 401 members as of this review and no membership fee attached. Its pitch centers on Claude Code and similar tools, positioned specifically for the DACH region of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland.

## What's Actually Inside

According to its own description, the group offers honest, unsponsored tests of AI coding tools, ready-made templates, and tutorials described as exclusive to members. The emphasis on "ehrliche Tests" (honest tests) suggests a review-style component alongside instructional content, positioning the community as a filter for developers trying to figure out which AI coding tools and workflows are actually worth adopting, rather than a straight how-to course.

## Pros

- Free to join, which lowers the bar for developers who just want to see what the AI-coding conversation looks like in German.
- Explicit focus on Claude Code specifically gives it a clearer identity than a generic "AI tools" community.
- Templates and tutorials described as exclusive suggest some effort toward original resources rather than just link-sharing.

## Cons

- Being free and community-run, there's no guarantee of consistent posting cadence or long-term maintenance, something worth watching in any no-cost Skool group.
- We found no independent reviews of this specific community outside its own Skool page.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking developers, especially in Germany, Austria, or Switzerland, who are actively using or considering Claude Code and want peer discussion, templates, and tool reviews in their own language.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-German speakers obviously won't get much value here, and developers who prefer English-language AI coding communities, of which there are many larger ones on Skool, may find a bigger, more active alternative elsewhere.
