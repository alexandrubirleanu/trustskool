# AI University Review 2026: Is It Worth $67/Month?

AI University has grown to 326 members around a pitch aimed squarely at solo operators: master AI, get clients, and turn ideas into agentic teams and operating systems so one person can do the work a whole team used to do. Membership costs $67 a month, positioning it in the mid-tier range for paid AI communities on Skool, above hobbyist price points but well under high-ticket mastermind territory.

## What's Actually Inside

Going by the community's own description, the offer has two distinct halves. One is technical: building "agentic teams" and "operating systems," language that points toward multi-agent AI workflows rather than single-prompt chatbot use. The other is commercial: "get clients," which suggests the community isn't purely educational but also expects members to monetize what they learn, likely through freelance AI implementation, consulting, or building AI-powered services for other businesses. That dual focus, build the skill and sell the skill, is a common structure for AI communities trying to differentiate from the flood of generic "learn AI" groups.

## Pros

- **Clear positioning on agentic AI**, a more advanced and current framing than basic prompt-engineering content.
- **Built-in monetization angle.** The "get clients" promise means the community isn't just theory, it's oriented toward members actually earning from the skill.
- **Solo-operator framing.** The pitch to "do the work of a whole team solo" speaks directly to freelancers and small operators rather than enterprise teams.

## Cons

The public listing doesn't spell out what tools, platforms, or specific agent frameworks are taught, so it's hard to judge technical depth from the description alone. At $67 a month, prospective members should also weigh this against other AI communities in the same price band since we found no independent, third-party reviews of this specific group beyond the Skool platform listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Freelancers, consultants, and solo founders who already have some baseline comfort with AI tools and want to move toward building and selling agentic workflows are the clearest match. People trying to position themselves as "the AI person" for other small businesses will likely find the client-acquisition framing useful.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners who have never used an AI tool before may find the "agentic teams" language assumes more prior context than a true zero-to-one course would provide. Anyone looking for enterprise-scale AI implementation guidance, rather than solo or small-team use cases, should look elsewhere.
