# BI Family Review 2026: A Free Alumni Community, Not an Open Enrollment Group

BI Family is a free, French-language Skool community with 540 members, but it's important to understand upfront what this actually is: a private group reserved for members who have already completed the "BI" program, built to keep that graduated cohort connected and give them ongoing access to program videos.

## What's Actually Inside

Per its own description, this isn't an open-enrollment learning community, it's a continuation space. Members who finished the underlying BI program can stay in touch with each other and retain access to the videos from that program after completing it. That structure means the actual educational value lives in the original BI program itself, not in this Skool space, which functions more like an alumni network and content archive layered on top.

For anyone reading this review who hasn't already been through the BI program, the honest answer is that this specific listing isn't really evaluable as a standalone product. Its entire value proposition is contingent on having completed something else first, so a review of "is it worth joining" doesn't really apply the way it would for an open community; the relevant question is instead whether the original BI program was worth completing.

At 540 members, the group has at least kept a sizeable number of graduates engaged enough to remain in the space rather than leaving it quiet after finishing, though the listing doesn't say how that compares to the size of the original BI program's total graduating cohort.

## Pros

- Free to join for those eligible, so there's no added cost for graduates to stay connected.
- Preserves access to program videos after completion, which some courses take away once you finish.
- A built-in sense of shared experience since every member has been through the same program.

## Cons

- This isn't an open community; the description makes clear it's reserved for people who completed a specific program, so it's not something an outside reader can simply join to access "BI" content for the first time.
- The description doesn't explain what the underlying "BI" program itself covers, so its value is only meaningful in the context of already having completed it.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Graduates of the specific "BI" program who want to stay connected with fellow alumni and retain access to program materials.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who hasn't completed the BI program and is looking for a way to access its content or teaching for the first time through this group.
