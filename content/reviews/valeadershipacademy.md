# VA Leadership Academy Review 2026: Free Community for Virtual Assistants

VA Leadership Academy bills itself as a private group for the VA Leadership Academy, with 522 members and no cost to join. The community's own description targets both aspiring and active virtual assistants who want to build remote businesses that are profitable and, in its words, "FUN."

## What's Actually Inside

The framing suggests two overlapping audiences under one roof: people who haven't started VA work yet and people already running a VA business who want to grow it. That combination usually means a mix of getting-started guidance (landing first clients, setting rates, structuring services) alongside more advanced business-building conversation for VAs looking to scale past solo freelancing. The emphasis on "profitable" work signals this isn't just a hobbyist or side-hustle space, and pairing that with "FUN" hints at a community culture that leans supportive and social rather than purely transactional.

Because the description calls this a "leadership academy" rather than just a VA training group, it also hints at an ambition beyond entry-level freelancing: helping members grow into positions where they might eventually manage other VAs, build a small team, or otherwise step into more of a leadership role within the virtual assistant space rather than staying a solo operator indefinitely.

## Pros

- Free to join, which lowers the barrier for VAs at any stage to test the community before committing time or money elsewhere
- Explicitly welcomes both beginners and active VAs, so it can serve as a long-term home rather than something members outgrow after their first few clients
- A clear, single niche (virtual assistant businesses) rather than generic freelancing or remote-work advice

## Cons

- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, so there's no outside verification of how active or supportive the group actually is day to day
- The description doesn't specify what kind of VA work is emphasized (admin, social media, bookkeeping, etc.), so members may need to explore inside to see if the mix matches their specialty

## Who It's a Good Fit For

New and established virtual assistants who want a free space to learn how to land clients and grow a remote VA business, particularly those who value a supportive, community-driven approach over a purely course-based one.

## Who Should Skip It

VAs looking for a highly specialized niche (say, only e-commerce VA work or only executive assistants) where the description doesn't confirm that level of specificity is available.
