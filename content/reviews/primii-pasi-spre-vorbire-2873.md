# Primii Pași spre Vorbire Review 2026: Is This Free Romanian Speech Community Worth Joining?

Primii Pași spre Vorbire, "First Steps Towards Speech," is a free, Romanian-language Skool community with just under 1,900 members, built specifically for mothers of children aged one to four who haven't started speaking yet. The community's own description centers on a simple daily commitment: nine minutes a day of exercises aimed at supporting a child's first words.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the group provides simple, structured speech-encouragement exercises designed to be done for a short, specific daily window, alongside peer support for mothers navigating a late-talking child. That kind of small, repeatable daily structure is a deliberately low-friction approach for busy parents, and the peer-support element likely matters as much as the exercises themselves for mothers who may be worried about their child's development.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing any cost barrier for parents wanting to try the exercises.
- A specific, low-friction daily structure, nine minutes, that's realistic for parents already stretched thin, rather than an open-ended, vague recommendation to work on speech more.
- Built specifically for a Romanian-speaking audience, offering support in-language for a topic where local, culturally relevant guidance matters.

## Cons

- This is a peer-support and exercise community, not a substitute for a professional speech-language evaluation, so parents with concerns about a child's speech delay should still consult a pediatrician or speech-language therapist directly, particularly if delays are significant or accompanied by other developmental concerns.
- We found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Romanian-speaking mothers of toddlers who haven't started talking yet and want a simple, low-time-commitment daily practice alongside support from other parents in a similar situation. It suits parents looking for a first, low-pressure step and reassurance from others in the same boat, rather than a replacement for a formal evaluation.

## Who Should Skip It

Parents whose child has been flagged for a more significant developmental or speech concern, who need professional evaluation rather than a peer-support exercise group as the primary intervention.
