# Učionica budućnosti Review 2026: Free Serbian Parenting Community Worth It?

Učionica budućnosti, Serbian for "Classroom of the Future," is a free parenting community that has reached 15,359 members with a message aimed at a specific frustration: parents who feel like they've become their child's daily enforcer around schoolwork rather than a guide.

## What's Actually Inside

Translated, the community describes itself as being for parents of children aged 9 to 15 "who want to stop being everyday controllers and build a different system in which the child takes responsibility for their own learning." That's a specific, well-defined problem statement rather than a generic parenting pitch: it targets the daily friction of nagging, checking, and monitoring homework, and points toward some alternative system meant to shift responsibility onto the child.

The description doesn't spell out what that "different system" actually looks like in practice, whether it's a set of parenting techniques, a communication framework, or scheduled group coaching. Parents considering this community are working from the problem it promises to solve rather than a detailed walkthrough of the method.

## Pros

- A clearly named pain point, constant monitoring and control, that many parents in this age bracket will recognize.

## Cons

- The description doesn't explain the actual method or system referenced, so it's unclear what changes day to day for a parent who joins.
- No independent reviews of the community or its approach were found beyond the Skool listing itself.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Serbian-speaking parents of children roughly 9 to 15 years old who feel stuck in a cycle of daily reminders and checking around schoolwork, and who are looking for a community built around shifting that dynamic rather than more homework-help content.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone who doesn't speak Serbian will have a hard time participating, since the community's description and presumably its discussions are conducted in that language. Parents of children well outside the 9 to 15 range, or those looking for academic tutoring rather than a shift in parenting approach, should also look elsewhere.
