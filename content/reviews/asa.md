# ASA Review 2026: What Is This French MRR Training Community?

ASA is a free Skool community with 1,255 members, positioned as the home base for people who've joined what its own welcome message calls "la formation MRR avec le plus de valeur du marché Français," or the most valuable MRR training in the French market. Unlike most listings, ASA's description reads less like marketing copy and more like a congratulatory note to new members: "well done, you've done the hardest part," it tells people who've just joined.

## What's Actually Inside

The description centers on "MRR," an abbreviation commonly used in French-language digital marketing circles to refer to master resell rights, buying or accessing digital products with the right to resell them to others, though ASA's own listing doesn't spell out the term directly. That business model appeals to people who want to sell digital products without creating them from scratch, since resell rights bundle the product itself with permission to market and keep the proceeds from reselling it. Beyond confirming the community is built around that kind of training and framed as high-value within the French-speaking market, the description doesn't detail specific modules, what products or niches are covered, or how the resell rights system is structured for members.

## Pros

- Free to join, which is notable for a community built around MRR training, a niche where paid upsells are common elsewhere.
- The welcome-message tone suggests an emphasis on member support right from the point of joining, rather than a purely transactional signup.
- Serves the French-speaking market specifically, which may appeal to members who prefer native-language instruction over translated English-language MRR content.

## Cons

- No independent reviews were found beyond the community's own Skool listing, and the description itself doesn't explain what MRR products or systems are actually taught.
- Because the listing reads as a post-signup welcome message rather than a public pitch, prospective members researching from outside have very little to go on before joining.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking people already interested in or exploring master resell rights as a way to sell digital products, who want a community-based support layer around that specific model.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone unfamiliar with resell rights business models should research the concept independently first, since the community's own description assumes members already know what "MRR" means. Non-French speakers, or anyone seeking English-language instruction, likely won't find this the right fit given the explicitly French-market framing.
