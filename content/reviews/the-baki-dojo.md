Having a named method is a meaningful signal: it suggests a structured program rather than a loose collection of tips. The description doesn't detail the specific exercises, programming style, or whether coaching is live or self-paced, so those details would need to be confirmed by joining. At $6 a month, the subscription is priced closer to a content library than a coached program, which is worth keeping in mind if what a member actually wants is direct, one-on-one coaching rather than a self-directed method to follow independently.

People wanting a structured, branded approach to strength and mobility training at very low monthly cost, without needing every program detail spelled out before joining.
