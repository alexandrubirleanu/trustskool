The word "Advanced" in the description signals this isn't pitched as an entry-level or beginner space, it implies members are expected to already have some grounding in spiritual or healing practice before joining. The "Healing and Business" pairing suggests the community likely serves practitioners, energy workers, healers, coaches, who want to combine their spiritual practice with the practical skills of running it as a business, a common niche among intuitive and holistic practitioners looking to monetize their gift without feeling like they're "selling out." This combination of spiritual and commercial content is a recurring tension in the healing-practitioner world: many practitioners are trained deeply in their modality but have never run a business, priced their services, or marketed themselves, so a school that treats both as core curriculum is addressing a real, commonly cited gap. Whether "Advanced" here means advanced healing technique, advanced business strategy, or both simultaneously isn't clarified in the description itself.
