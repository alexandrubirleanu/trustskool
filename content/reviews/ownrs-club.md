# Bricks Academy (Ownrs Club) Review 2026: Is It Worth $19/Month?

BRICKS ACADEMY, also listed as Ownrs Club, has grown to 955 French-language members at $19 a month, positioning itself around a specific promise: helping real estate investors invest with peace of mind. Its own description keeps things simple, listing three pillars: mutual aid, learning, and opportunities.

## What's Actually Inside

Real estate investing communities generally succeed or fail based on how well they connect three things: education on how deals and financing work, a support network of other investors to sanity-check decisions with, and actual deal flow or opportunities to act on. Bricks Academy's description touches all three directly, "entraide" (mutual aid), "apprentissage" (learning), and "opportunités" (opportunities), which suggests the community is trying to be a full-cycle resource rather than just an educational course or just a deal-sharing forum.

The phrase "invest with peace of mind" is worth noting as the emotional core of the pitch. Real estate investing, especially for newer investors, often carries significant anxiety around financing, tenant issues, and market timing, so a community built around reducing that anxiety through peer support and structured learning is addressing a real pain point in the space, even if the description doesn't detail specific markets, financing strategies, or property types covered.

## Pros

- Combining education, peer support, and opportunity-sharing in one place matches how real estate investors typically need to operate: informed, connected, and deal-aware.
- At $19 a month, the price is accessible relative to the scale of financial decisions real estate investing usually involves.
- Framing the community around reducing investor anxiety, not just teaching tactics, speaks to a genuine and common concern among newer property investors.

## Cons

- No independent reviews of Bricks Academy were found beyond its Skool listing, so the quality of the "opportunities" shared and the depth of educational content can't be verified from outside the group.
- The description doesn't specify geographic markets, minimum capital expectations, or financing strategies covered, so prospective members should confirm those details match their own situation before subscribing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French-speaking real estate investors, whether new or experienced, who want a combination of education, peer support, and deal opportunities in one recurring, low-cost membership.

## Who Should Skip It

Investors outside the French-speaking market, or anyone looking for a highly specialized niche like commercial real estate syndication or a single specific financing strategy, may find the general investor-club format too broad for their specific focus.
