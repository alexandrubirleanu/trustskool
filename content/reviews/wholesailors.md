# Wholesailors (Wholesale Skool) Review 2026: Is It Worth $25/Month?

Wholesailors, also listed as Wholesale Skool, is one of the largest wholesale real estate communities on Skool, with more than 3,713 members. It's run by Maximilian Dier, who markets himself as "the Wholesale Pirate," and currently costs $25/month as part of a limited-time promotion, with the community's own page stating price rises to $30/month once membership hits 4,000 ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/wholesailors/about)) — worth factoring in since the group is already close to that mark.

## What's actually inside

The core curriculum is what the community calls the Arbitrage Flipping System (AFS), a 10-step wholesaling methodology covering three deal types: house arbitrage, land arbitrage, and creative arbitrage (seller-finance and subject-to style structures rather than straight cash wholesaling alone). Members get contract templates, call scripts, and skiptraced leads, plus access to AI-based tooling the community bundles in. Support runs beyond a static course library: daily livestreams with Dier himself, a personal accountability coach, and dedicated underwriter support for evaluating deals, which is a meaningfully higher level of hands-on support than a typical Skool course community offers at this price.

## Who runs it

Maximilian Dier is a real estate wholesaler and investor based in Dallas, Texas, and runs MaxDispo, a dispositions company that connects wholesalers' contracts to a nationwide buyer network; MaxDispo's own materials cite over 100 wholesale deals closed with more than $1 million in assignment fees generated through that network ([maxdispo.com](https://maxdispo.com/)). He maintains an active, multi-platform public presence under his own name across YouTube, Instagram, and a personal site describing 250+ completed deals ([maximiliandier.com](https://maximiliandier.com/), [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/maximiliandier/)), which is a stronger independent footprint than many single-community Skool operators have. Wholesailors appears to be the education arm built on top of that operating business rather than a course sold in isolation from any real dealmaking.

## Pros

- The founder has a documented, multi-platform public operating business (MaxDispo) behind the education, not just a Skool page and a sales funnel.
- Genuinely differentiated support layer: daily livestreams, a named accountability coach, and underwriter support go beyond the "course plus Discord" model common at this price point.
- Coverage of three distinct deal structures (house, land, creative/seller-finance) gives members more than one path if straight cash wholesaling in their market is too competitive.
- A large, active member base (3,700+, with dozens typically online) suggests genuine day-to-day community activity rather than a dormant group.

## Cons

- The page leans on "hundreds of student wins" without naming specific, checkable case studies or testimonials, so the scale of typical (versus best-case) outcomes is hard to independently assess.
- The "no experience required, little to no money needed" framing is standard wholesaling-niche marketing; real deals still require real time, negotiation skill, and often some capital for marketing or earnest money, regardless of the system taught.
- Pricing is explicitly promotional and will increase as membership grows, so the $25 entry point is a moving target rather than a stable long-term price.

## Who it's a good fit for

Aspiring wholesalers who want more structure and daily accountability than a typical Skool course offers, and who are willing to actually work leads and scripts daily rather than passively consume content, are the best fit. The multiple deal-type coverage also suits investors in markets where straight cash wholesaling is heavily saturated.

## Who should skip it

Anyone expecting wholesaling to require literally no capital or effort, based on the page's own framing, should recalibrate expectations first; this remains a sales- and negotiation-driven business regardless of the system used. Investors who already have an established wholesaling process and dispo network may find less incremental value here than someone starting from zero.
