# AI Video Creators Community Review 2026: Is It Worth $9/Month?

AI Video Creators Community (AVCC) is a 3,780-member Skool group focused specifically on one skill: making AI video genuinely good, and turning that skill into client work. At $9/month (or $55/year), it's the cheapest community in this review batch, and it carries Skool's own "Top 1% Community" badge along with recognition as a Skool Games winner community.

## What's Actually Inside

The structure is a clear 7-step roadmap that takes members from a first AI-generated image through to a finished, monetizable video, covering image and video generation, audio, editing craft, "going pro," monetization, and staying current with trends. Beyond the roadmap, members get 10+ structured courses, built-in tools including ReelEngine for scripts and ad concepts and PromptEngine for optimized prompts, live feedback and Q&A sessions, and periodic challenges with cash prizes. The monetization layer is a genuine differentiator: the community's in-house studio posts real client briefs that members can take on directly, and the about page states studio owners and brands inside the community actively hire AI creators, rather than just teaching monetization in the abstract.

Reported member outcomes cited by the community include client deals as large as $15,000, months with $5,000+ in earnings, and videos going viral past 1 million views, several starting from zero prior experience. These are self-reported by the community rather than independently audited, standard for this kind of testimonial and worth treating as anecdotal rather than typical.

## Who Runs It

Sergey Kabankov founded and runs the community, with a public Instagram presence (@aivideoskool) documenting his own AI video work. He's positioned as a working AI video creator rather than purely an educator, which lines up with the community's practical, studio-and-client-briefs structure.

## Pros

- **Extremely low cost.** At $9/month, it's easy to justify trying regardless of budget.
- **Specialized, not scattershot.** Unlike broader "AI content" communities that span every format, AVCC focuses specifically on video, which likely means more depth per topic.
- **Real monetization pathway.** Studio-sourced client briefs posted directly to members is a more concrete income bridge than generic "here's how you could monetize this" advice.
- **Independently recognized.** The Top 1% Skool badge and Skool Games win are platform-level signals, not just self-description.

## Cons

We found a YouTube video titled "AI Video Creators Community Skool Review (What To Realistically Expect)," suggesting some independent commentary exists, but detailed, itemized third-party critique of the community was limited in our research. As with any community citing large individual member wins ($15,000 deals, 1M+ views), those figures represent standout outcomes rather than typical results, and prospective members should calibrate expectations accordingly rather than assuming they're representative of the median member.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Anyone specifically wanting to get good at AI video, whether for freelance client work, brand building, or a content career, and who wants a low-cost way to start with structured courses rather than piecing together free YouTube tutorials. The client-brief pipeline makes it particularly relevant for freelancers actively looking for paid AI video work rather than hobbyists.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a broader AI skill set beyond video (images-only, automation, or agency-building) will find more directly relevant material in a community built specifically around that focus. Total beginners uninterested in the freelance/client angle and just wanting to make videos for their own brand may find some of the monetization-heavy framing less relevant to their goals.
