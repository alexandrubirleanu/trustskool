# HIIT Science Community Review 2026: A Free Hub for HIIT Coaches

HIIT Science Community is a free Skool community with 699 members, described as the global hub for coaches and practitioners of "HIIT Science" specifically, rather than high-intensity interval training in a generic sense, to share experiences, grow together, and build lasting connections.

## What's Actually Inside

The naming here matters: rather than a general fitness or HIIT enthusiast forum, the description positions this as tied to a specific approach or body of methodology, "HIIT Science," suggesting members are expected to already have some familiarity with, or interest in adopting, that particular framework. The stated purpose leans heavily on peer connection, coaches and practitioners sharing experiences with each other, rather than a single instructor delivering a course. "Global hub" language also signals an ambition to be the central gathering point for this specific niche, drawing coaches from multiple locations and backgrounds into one space. For coaches, that kind of cross-border peer network can matter more than it first appears: methodology-specific communities tend to surface implementation questions and edge cases that a single trainer working alone might not encounter, simply because more practitioners are applying the same framework across different client populations.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no cost barrier for coaches and practitioners wanting to connect.
- Aimed specifically at coaches and practitioners, a more professional and specialized audience than a general fitness enthusiast group.
- Built around a named methodology (HIIT Science) rather than generic training discussion, which can mean more focused, relevant conversation.

## Cons

The description doesn't spell out exactly what distinguishes "HIIT Science" methodology from HIIT training in general, so it's worth clarifying that framework directly before joining with specific expectations. We also found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing, so verifying the size and activity level of the practitioner base directly is a reasonable step before joining.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Coaches and fitness practitioners already familiar with, or curious about, the HIIT Science approach specifically, and who want a peer network of similarly focused professionals, are the clearest fit.

## Who Should Skip It

General fitness hobbyists looking for a beginner-friendly workout community, rather than a coach-and-practitioner professional network, may find this pitched above their level. Anyone unfamiliar with or uninterested in the specific HIIT Science framework should clarify what that entails before joining.
