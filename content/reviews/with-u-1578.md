# With U Review 2026: What This Free Business Community Offers

With U is a free Skool community with 1,824 members. Its description outlines three components rather than one: education, advisory, and community, all in service of helping members grow their business online independently.

## What's Actually Inside

The word "independently" is doing real work in that description: this reads as support for people who want to run their own business rather than build a team-based or franchise-style operation, likely solo entrepreneurs, freelancers, or small operators. Combining education (structured learning), advisory (more direct, personalized guidance), and community (peer support) suggests a broader support system rather than a single fixed course, though the specific niche or industry the business advice targets isn't named in the public listing, so it appears to apply fairly generally rather than to one specific business type. The name itself, "With U," leans into a supportive, alongside-you tone rather than a top-down expert-teaches-student format, which fits the mix of education, advisory, and community the description lists side by side.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no financial risk to seeing whether the general business-advisory format works for you.
- Combines education, advisory, and peer community rather than just one of those elements alone.
- Framed around independence, which should resonate with solo operators who don't want a team-dependent model.

## Cons

Because the description doesn't name a specific industry, method, or niche, it's hard to know in advance exactly how tailored the advice will be to your particular business. We also didn't find independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Solo online business owners or aspiring entrepreneurs who want general education, advisory input, and peer community, without needing a single narrowly defined method, will find the broad framing here workable. People who've felt isolated running a business on their own, and want a supportive sounding board as much as new skills, may value the "with U" framing as much as any specific advisory content.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a narrowly specialized program built around one specific business model or niche should look for a more targeted community, since this one is framed generally rather than around a single defined system. Members who already have a strong support network and just need one specific tactical skill (a particular ad platform, for instance) may find a more focused, single-topic community a better use of their time.
