# The Fortress Army Review 2026: Worth Joining This Free Women's Community?

The Fortress Army is a free Skool community that has grown to 2,023 members around a specific relationship-focused mission for women. Its own description frames the group as a place "where women come to awaken their feminine power, attract true masculine energy, and build passionate, lasting relationships."

## What's Actually Inside

Reading the description, the content centers on three connected ideas: personal development framed as "feminine power," attracting a specific kind of partner energy, and building relationships meant to last. It reads as a mix of self-development and dating or relationship coaching aimed specifically at a female audience, rather than general relationship advice for any gender.

The "Army" in the name, alongside "Fortress," suggests a community identity built around solidarity and mutual support among members, which fits the "women come to" framing in the description more than a solo, self-paced course format.

## Pros

- Free to join, so members can explore the community's approach without a financial commitment.
- A women-only framing may make some members more comfortable discussing personal and relationship topics candidly.
- The combination of personal development and relationship-building gives the group more than a single narrow focus.
- A community of over 2,000 members suggests active peer discussion rather than a quiet, low-engagement group.

## Cons

- No independent reviews were found beyond the group's own Skool listing, so how the "feminine power" and "masculine energy" concepts are actually taught isn't independently verified.
- The description doesn't specify whether the group includes any coaching credentials or is purely peer-driven discussion.
- The description doesn't clarify whether there's a paid tier or coaching offer beyond the free community, so anyone expecting one-on-one guidance should confirm that before joining.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Women interested in a personal development approach to dating and relationships, particularly those drawn to language around feminine and masculine energy dynamics.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for relationship advice not framed around gendered "feminine/masculine energy" concepts, or men seeking a similarly focused community, since this one is explicitly built for women. People currently working through a relationship crisis that needs licensed therapy should seek that support directly rather than relying on a peer community alone. Anyone skeptical of energy-based relationship frameworks in general is unlikely to find the core premise a good match.
