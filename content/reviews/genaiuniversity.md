# Gen AI University Review 2026: Is This Free Community Legit?

Gen AI University, shortened to GenAIU, is a free Skool community with 678 members. Its description is short but specific: "Where founders build agentic companies. Since 2021."

## What's Actually Inside

Two details in that brief description carry real weight. First, the audience is named precisely as founders, not hobbyists or job seekers, and the goal is building "agentic companies," meaning businesses built around autonomous AI agents rather than general AI education or prompt-writing tips. Second, the "Since 2021" tag places its origin well before the current wave of generative AI hype that followed the late-2022 boom, suggesting the community (or its founders) have been working in this space since a meaningfully earlier point in the AI cycle, before agentic AI was a mainstream term. Beyond those two points, the description doesn't detail specific curriculum, tools, or frameworks covered.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing cost as a barrier for founders wanting to explore the agentic AI space before committing further.
- Explicitly targets founders building companies, rather than a broad "learn AI" audience, which likely means more focused, business-building discussion.
- The "Since 2021" claim suggests longer-running experience in the AI space than communities that appeared only after generative AI's mainstream breakout.

## Cons

- We didn't find independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing, so we can't verify the "Since 2021" claim or the community's track record independently.
- The description is quite brief and doesn't specify what "agentic companies" concretely means in practice, what tools or frameworks are taught, or what stage of founder (pre-idea, pre-revenue, scaling) the content is aimed at.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Founders actively building, or seriously considering building, a company centered on autonomous AI agents, who want to connect with others working in the same specific niche, are the clearest fit. People drawn to the "since 2021" positioning, valuing experience that predates the current AI hype cycle, may also find that framing appealing.

## Who Should Skip It

People looking for beginner-level, general AI education rather than founder-focused, company-building content should look elsewhere, since the description is explicitly aimed at founders.
