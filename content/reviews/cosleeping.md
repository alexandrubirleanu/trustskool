# Happy Cosleepers Review 2026: Is It Worth $15/Month?

Happy Cosleepers: Cosleeping is an 880-member Skool community priced at $15 a month, built around a single, specific topic: supporting families who share a bed or room with their infants and young children. The community describes itself as the new home of "the world's largest cosleeping community," a claim that comes from the listing itself rather than any outside ranking we could verify.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the description, the core offer is straightforward: a place to connect with other bedsharing families around the world, ask questions, and pull from a set of shared resources. That framing suggests this functions more as an ongoing peer-support and Q&A space than a structured course with a fixed curriculum. For a topic like cosleeping, where parents often get conflicting advice from doctors, family members, and online forums, having a dedicated space to ask specific situational questions (how to set up a safe sleep space, how to transition eventually, how to handle nights when it isn't working) is arguably the main value on offer here, alongside whatever reference materials the group has built up over time.

## Pros

- A clearly defined, narrow topic rather than general parenting advice, so members know exactly what kind of support they're signing up for.
- A stated resource library in addition to open discussion, giving members something beyond just message threads to reference.
- Global framing ("families around the world") suggests a broad base of shared experience across different cultural approaches to bedsharing.

## Cons

- We found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing, and no outside way to confirm the "world's largest" claim.
- The description doesn't specify whether resources are vetted by any medical or sleep-safety professional, which matters for a topic like infant sleep; members should ask directly about the sourcing of any safety guidance before relying on it.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Parents currently bedsharing, or seriously considering it, who want peer support, practical setup tips, and reassurance from others navigating the same choice, rather than a lecture-style course.

## Who Should Skip It

Parents who've already decided against cosleeping, or who are looking specifically for formal, credentialed pediatric sleep guidance rather than a peer community, may find this isn't the right fit for their needs.
