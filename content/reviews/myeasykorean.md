# My Easy Korean Review 2026: Worth Joining This Free Community?

My Easy Korean is a free Skool community with 1,342 members built around a specific, recognizable learner problem: understanding more Korean than you can actually speak. The stated format is weekly speaking challenges paired with personal feedback, rather than a general vocabulary or grammar course.

## What's Actually Inside

The comprehension-versus-speaking gap the community targets is a well-known plateau for language learners, especially people who've consumed a lot of Korean media (dramas, music, podcasts) and can follow along but freeze up when they need to produce their own sentences aloud. Weekly speaking challenges suggest a recurring, structured cadence that forces active production rather than passive review, and "personal feedback" implies members' actual speaking attempts get individual correction rather than only generic tips, which is often the missing piece for learners stuck at this stage. The word "Easy" in the community's name is worth noting too, it suggests an approachable, low-intimidation framing aimed at learners who may have felt discouraged by more academic or exam-style Korean courses in the past, rather than a rigorous, test-prep-oriented program.

## Pros

- Free to join, targeting a specific, well-defined problem (understanding more than you can speak) rather than a generic "learn Korean" pitch.
- A weekly challenge structure creates a recurring reason to actually practice speaking out loud, rather than passive review.
- Personal feedback on speaking attempts, if delivered consistently, addresses a genuine gap in most self-study language resources.

## Cons

The description doesn't specify who provides the personal feedback or their qualifications, native speaker, certified instructor, or peer volunteers, which matters for judging feedback quality, especially for learners specifically hoping to correct pronunciation or natural phrasing rather than just grammar accuracy. We found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Korean learners who already understand a good amount of spoken or written Korean but freeze up when trying to speak, and who want a recurring, low-pressure structure to practice producing the language actively.

## Who Should Skip It

Complete beginners with little existing Korean foundation, or learners specifically looking for a full grammar and vocabulary curriculum rather than speaking practice alone, will likely need a different starting resource first. Learners who prefer fully self-paced study with no scheduled challenges or social pressure to participate may also find the weekly format less appealing.
