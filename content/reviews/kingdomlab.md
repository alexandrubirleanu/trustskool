# Kingdom Lab Review 2026: A Free Faith-Based Mentoring Community for Creatives and Entrepreneurs

Kingdom Lab is a free Skool community with 341 members that describes itself as "a laboratory for destiny," mentoring creatives, leaders, and entrepreneurs to grow in their calling, communication, and creativity. The framing blends faith-based language, destiny and calling, with practical skill areas like communication and creative development.

## What's Actually Inside

The description groups three distinct audiences together, creatives, leaders, and entrepreneurs, under a shared mentorship umbrella focused on three growth areas: calling, communication, and creativity. That structure suggests the community is less about a single technical skill and more about identity and purpose-driven development that happens to serve people across different fields. The "laboratory" framing implies an experimental, growth-oriented space rather than a fixed curriculum, though the description doesn't detail specific programs, mentors, or session formats.

## Pros

- Free to join, removing any financial barrier to exploring whether the faith-and-purpose framing resonates.
- Serves multiple overlapping audiences, creatives, leaders, entrepreneurs, which can create a broader network than a single-niche community.
- The explicit focus on "calling" alongside practical skills like communication suggests attention to purpose, not just tactics.

## Cons

- The broad framing, spanning creatives, leaders, and entrepreneurs at once, makes it harder to know exactly how deep the content goes in any one specific area.
- No independent reviews of this community were found beyond its own Skool listing, so there's no outside perspective on mentorship quality or community engagement.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Creatives, leaders, or entrepreneurs who want their professional growth to be explicitly connected to faith and a sense of calling, rather than treated as separate from it.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone looking for a purely secular, skills-only community, since faith and destiny language are core to the pitch rather than incidental. People wanting deep, single-discipline expertise, for example only creative craft or only business strategy, may find the multi-audience framing too broad for their specific needs.
