# The Bike Fit Academy Review 2026: Is This Free Bike Fitting Community Worth Joining?

The Bike Fit Academy is a free, 1,199-member Skool community built around one specific problem cyclists actually have: getting their bike fit right. The community's own description says it's led by professional bike fitters and structured around three concrete activities, posting videos of your own bike fit for feedback, joining educational webinars, and discussing setup questions with other riders.

## What's Actually Inside

The video-feedback format is the most distinctive part of what's described here: rather than reading generic fit guidelines, members post footage of themselves riding so that professional fitters, and presumably other members, can weigh in on saddle height, reach, cleat position, and the dozens of small adjustments that separate a comfortable fit from a painful one. Educational webinars add a scheduled, live-learning component, and the open discussion angle suggests an active back-and-forth rather than a static resource library.

Bike fit is a notoriously individual problem, what works for one rider's body proportions and riding style can be wrong for another, so a community format built on actual footage rather than generic checklists is a meaningfully better match for the subject matter than a static article or video course would be. The webinar format also suggests the "pro bike fitters" mentioned in the description show up live rather than only responding asynchronously to posted videos, which matters if you're hoping to ask a specific, real-time question about your own setup.

## Pros

- Free access to input from people described as professional bike fitters, a service that can otherwise cost real money per session at a shop.
- The video-critique format is a genuinely practical mechanism: cyclists can get individualized feedback rather than generic fit advice.
- A specific, narrow focus on bike fit, not general cycling training or gear reviews, means less noise for anyone with a specific fit problem to solve.

## Cons

- Community-sourced video feedback is not a substitute for an in-person professional fitting session with proper measurement tools, and the community's own framing doesn't claim otherwise.
- We found no independent reviews of this specific community beyond its own Skool listing, so it's worth reading a few posts yourself before assuming the "pro" feedback matches a paid in-shop fitting.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Cyclists troubleshooting a specific discomfort or performance issue on the bike who want a free, low-stakes way to get outside eyes on their setup before booking a paid fitting.

## Who Should Skip It

Riders who need a precise, tool-measured professional fit, for a new bike purchase or a serious injury concern, for example, should treat this as a supplement to, not a replacement for, an in-person fitting.
