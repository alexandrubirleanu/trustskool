# AI First Designer School Review 2026: Is It Worth $99 One-Time?

AI First Designer School is a Skool community of just under 1,900 designers, priced at a one-time $99 fee, built around a specific positioning: helping designers use AI as a tool that extends their work rather than treating AI as a threat that replaces them.

## What's Actually Inside

Per the community's own description, members get proven playbooks and real courses focused on integrating AI into design workflows, alongside a community explicitly framed around members shipping work together, suggesting an emphasis on actually producing and releasing design work, not just consuming lessons passively. The description's own figure of over 1,800 designers roughly matches the total member count in our data, suggesting the community's self-reported size is consistent with what we're seeing.

## Pros

- A one-time $99 fee rather than an ongoing subscription, giving members lasting access without a recurring bill.
- Explicit focus on shipping actual design work within the community, rather than a passive course-consumption model, which tends to produce more tangible skill-building.
- A specific, timely positioning, AI as a design tool rather than a replacement threat, that speaks directly to a real anxiety many designers have right now.

## Cons

- "Proven playbooks" is a claim made in the community's own description, and we found no independent, third-party validation of specific outcomes or case studies beyond that self-description.
- As with many AI-and-design communities launched recently, it's worth confirming how current the specific tool and workflow guidance is, since AI design tools change quickly.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Working designers who want practical guidance on incorporating AI tools into their existing design process and value a community that emphasizes actually shipping work. It's a better fit for designers with an existing portfolio and workflow to augment than for someone still learning core design fundamentals from scratch.

## Who Should Skip It

Designers looking for foundational design-skill training rather than AI-workflow integration, since the community's stated focus is squarely on the AI layer.
