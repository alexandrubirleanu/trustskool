# reTHINK RICH Academy ELITE Review 2026: Is It Worth $29/Month?

reTHINK RICH Academy ELITE is a 393-member Skool community covering a mix of credit repair, business funding, real estate, and general investing education, priced at $29/month. It's run by Richard Prillerman, who brands himself online as "the rich get richard," and positions the group as "the #1 Credit/Funding/Business/Real Estate/Investing Community in the world" ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/rethinkrichacademy/about)). Despite the "real estate" framing in the task brief's niche label, the actual subject matter is broader financial literacy and business-building rather than a real estate-specific curriculum.

## What's actually inside

The community centers on live coaching sessions the page describes as featuring 7- and 8-figure entrepreneurs, covering credit building and repair, business and personal funding strategies, and general investing guidance. Beyond the community itself, Prillerman's broader brand offers tax preparation services (monthly, quarterly, and annual) and business consulting through a separate site, therichgetrichard.com, plus guest-speaking and collaboration opportunities. The about page is light on a detailed, structured curriculum breakdown; the pitch leans more on Prillerman's personal story and access to him and guest entrepreneurs than on a syllabus of specific modules.

## Who runs it

Richard Prillerman describes himself as a US Air Force veteran with an MBA from Harvard, and states his personal story includes being homeless on two separate occasions before becoming a millionaire over more than 20 years of investing ([X](https://x.com/the_rich_get)). He maintains an active, multi-platform public presence under "the rich get richard" across Facebook, Instagram, X, and YouTube, with content specifically covering credit repair, business credit, and funding topics for free before pointing to the paid community ([Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/therichgetrichard/), [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/the_rich_get_richard/?hl=en)). That sustained, multi-platform footprint under a consistent personal brand is a reasonable, if not independently audited, signal of an established public presence rather than a one-off Skool listing.

## Pros

- Richard Prillerman has a consistent, checkable public presence across multiple platforms under the same personal brand, rather than existing only inside this one Skool community.
- $29/month is toward the lower end of the pricing in this review batch, lowering the cost of testing whether the format fits.
- Additional adjacent services (tax prep, business consulting) suggest an operating business behind the brand rather than only a course/community product.
- Broad subject coverage (credit, funding, real estate, investing) suits members who aren't sure yet which specific wealth-building path fits them.

## Cons

- Specific personal claims (the Harvard MBA, the homeless-to-millionaire story, 20+ years of investing experience) come from Prillerman's own social media and the community's about page; we found no independent, third-party confirmation of these specific credentials.
- The about page provides no detailed curriculum structure, testimonials, or case studies, leaning heavily on personal story and access to guest entrepreneurs rather than a documented syllabus.
- At 393 members, this is one of the smaller communities in this review batch, so peer-to-peer activity and shared momentum may be more limited than in larger groups covering similar topics.
- Covering credit, funding, real estate, and investing all at once, similar to Real Estate Deal School's broad approach, trades depth in any single area for breadth.

## Who it's a good fit for

People early in a credit-repair or business-funding journey who want broad exposure to multiple wealth-building topics and value hearing directly from a founder with a relatable personal story are the best fit. The lower price point also makes it a reasonably low-cost way to sample the format.

## Who should skip it

Anyone wanting a real-estate-specific curriculum should look elsewhere, since despite how this community gets categorized, the actual content is broader financial and business education, not real estate investing mechanics specifically. People who weigh independently verifiable founder credentials heavily should treat the personal-story claims here as unverified rather than confirmed.
