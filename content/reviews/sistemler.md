# Ajans Sistemleri Akademisi Review 2026: Free Agency Systems Training?

Ajans Sistemleri Akademisi ("Agency Systems Academy") is a free, Turkish-language Skool community with 1,205 members aimed at agency owners and operators. The description states its purpose plainly: an "eğitim topluluğu" (education community) for people who want to learn and apply "otomasyon, sistemler ve satış altyapılarını" (automation, systems, and sales infrastructure).

## What's Actually Inside

The "ajans" (agency) framing in the name suggests the target audience runs or works inside a marketing, ads, or service agency specifically, rather than being a general small-business owner. The three named pillars, automation, systems, and sales infrastructure, point toward operational backend work: setting up processes and tooling that let an agency run and sell more consistently, rather than creative or client-facing skills like copywriting or design.

## Pros

- Free to join, lowering the barrier for agency operators curious about systemizing their operations.
- A specific target audience (agencies) rather than a generic small-business community, which should mean more relevant peer discussion.
- Three named focus areas (automation, systems, sales infrastructure) give a reasonably clear sense of the content's operational bent.
- Framing the community explicitly as "eğitim" (education) suggests structured learning rather than an open chat forum.

## Cons

The description doesn't name specific tools or platforms taught, so it's unclear whether the automation content centers on particular software or stays tool-agnostic. We also found no independent reviews of this community beyond its own Skool listing.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Turkish-speaking agency owners and operators who want to build out automation, internal systems, and sales infrastructure, and who want a free community focused specifically on the agency operating model.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone outside the agency world, or non-Turkish speakers, will likely find the audience-specific framing and language barrier make this a poor fit regardless of the systems content's quality. Agencies primarily focused on creative output rather than backend operations may also find the automation-and-systems focus less relevant to their day-to-day needs.

Since operational, systems-focused content depends heavily on the material actually staying current as tools change, it's worth checking how the community has grown and stayed active over time rather than assuming a free listing means low commitment from the people running it.
