## What's Actually Inside

The one-line description positions the group as a central hub for people doing LS or LT engine swaps, the practice of dropping a GM LS or LT-series V8 into another vehicle. Being run by a hardware brand rather than an independent influencer suggests the value here is likely product-adjacent: compatibility questions, mounting and bracket advice, and troubleshooting tied to the kinds of parts ICT Billet sells, alongside general swap community discussion.

Calling itself a "headquarters" rather than a course or forum also signals scope: it reads as a gathering point for an existing enthusiast base rather than a structured lesson series with a start and end point. For a mechanically involved project like an engine swap, that ongoing, always-open format can be more useful than a fixed curriculum, since builders tend to hit unpredictable, project-specific snags that a linear course wouldn't anticipate.

## Pros

- Free to join, so there's no financial risk in checking whether the community fits your specific swap project.
- A brand-run hub for a specific, well-defined niche (LS/LT swaps) likely means members can get parts-compatibility answers straight from people close to the hardware.
- LS/LT swaps are a large and active enthusiast category, so there should be a reasonable base of members working through similar builds.

## Cons

- Being run by a parts brand means the community may lean toward product recommendations and brand-specific advice rather than neutral, cross-brand comparisons.
- No independent reviews were found beyond the platform listing, and no owner name beyond the ICT Billet brand affiliation is given in the community record.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Anyone actively planning or mid-way through an LS or LT engine swap who wants a dedicated space for mounting, bracket, and compatibility questions, especially those already using or considering ICT Billet parts.

## Who Should Skip It
