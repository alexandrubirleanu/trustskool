## Pros

- Free to join, making it a low-risk way to connect with other people interested in automation
- Peer-support framing ("on s'entraide," we help each other) suggests an active, collaborative culture rather than passive content consumption
- Niche enough focus (automation specifically) to likely filter out unrelated general business chatter

## Cons

- No independent reviews found beyond the Skool listing, so there's no outside confirmation of how active or knowledgeable the membership actually is
- The description doesn't specify particular tools, platforms, or automation use cases covered, so the exact scope is unclear before joining
- With no stated instructor or curriculum, the value of the community depends heavily on who happens to be active in it at any given time

## Who It's a Good Fit For

French speakers with a general interest in automation who want a free, informal space to trade tips and support with like-minded members, rather than a formal, instructor-led course.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-French speakers will find limited value given the language focus. Anyone wanting a structured curriculum with defined lessons and a clear instructor, rather than open peer discussion, should look for a more formally organized alternative that publishes its syllabus up front.
