# Immo Mentor Community Review 2026: Is the Free German Real Estate Group Worth It?

Immo Mentor Community is a free-to-join Skool group built for German speakers interested in real estate as a path to building wealth. With 708 members, it sits in the mid-size range for niche property communities, and its pitch centers on two things: a free e-book to help new members get oriented, and ongoing content aimed at teaching people how to earn money and eventually build what the group calls a seven or eight-figure net worth through property.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the community's own description, the core offer is educational rather than done-for-you. New members are pointed to a free e-book meant to give them a "perfect start" in real estate. From there, the community frames itself as a place to learn how to generate income and grow wealth specifically through property investing, rather than general entrepreneurship or side-hustle content. Given the German-language framing and the "Immo" (short for Immobilien, German for real estate) branding, this is squarely aimed at the DACH region's property investing audience.

Like many free Skool communities built around a lead magnet, the free e-book is likely the on-ramp into the group rather than the entirety of what's offered. That's a common and reasonable model, but it does mean prospective members should expect the free tier to be an introduction, with the deeper wealth-building content potentially gated behind future paid offers, calls, or coaching that aren't described in the public listing.

## Pros

- Free to join, with no stated membership fee, which lowers the barrier for beginners testing the waters
- A free e-book positioned as an onboarding asset, giving new members a concrete first step rather than just a feed to scroll
- Narrow, specific focus on real estate wealth-building rather than a generic "make money online" grab-bag

## Cons

- No independent reviews found beyond the Skool platform listing itself, so claims about members reaching a 7-8 figure net worth aren't independently verifiable
- The public description doesn't specify what strategies are taught (rental income, flipping, syndications, etc.), so prospective members are joining somewhat blind on methodology

## Who It's a Good Fit For

German-speaking beginners who want a no-cost entry point into real estate investing education, and who are comfortable starting with a free resource before deciding whether to go deeper with any paid offer the community may eventually introduce. It should also suit members who prefer to learn at their own pace from written material, like the e-book, rather than jumping straight into live coaching.

## Who Should Skip It

Non-German speakers, and anyone expecting a fully documented curriculum or audited results before joining, since the public listing doesn't lay out specific methods or outcomes. Members who need concrete proof of past results before investing time in a community, rather than judging it after some hands-on exposure, may also want to wait for more outside data points to surface.
