# Project Management Mastermind Review 2026: Is It Worth $49/Month?

Project Management Mastermind describes itself as the number one project management community on Skool, and with 1,700 members it has real scale behind that claim. At $49/month, it sits in the mid-range for career-focused Skool communities, positioned as a career-development investment rather than a casual hobby group.

## What's Actually Inside

Based on the community's own description, members get training delivered through PMI Authorized Training Partners (ATPs), which is a specific, checkable credential category, PMI ATP status is an official designation from the Project Management Institute, not a generic marketing claim. Beyond ATP-delivered training, the pitch mentions certifications and what it calls "resume backed PM experience," suggesting the community is oriented toward helping members build a credible, job-ready project management profile rather than just learning PM theory in the abstract. The combination of training, certification pathways, and resume-relevant experience in one place is a fairly complete career-development package if it delivers as described.

## Pros

- **PMI ATP-delivered training is a specific, verifiable credential category**, not a vague claim, giving this community more concrete backing than typical career-coaching groups.
- **Certifications plus resume-relevant experience** addresses both the credentialing and the practical-experience gaps that often hold back PM job seekers.
- **Positioned explicitly around career outcomes** (certifications, resume experience) rather than general networking or motivation content.

## Cons

At $49/month, the ongoing cost adds up over a job search or certification timeline, so it's worth mapping out how many months of access a realistic certification path requires before committing. We also found no independent reviews specifically verifying the "resume backed PM experience" claim beyond the community's own description.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

People pursuing a project management career or certification, who want structured training tied to an official PMI-recognized delivery channel and help building resume-ready experience, are the clearest fit. Career-changers specifically targeting PM roles will likely find the combination of training and practical experience useful.

## Who Should Skip It

Experienced, already-certified project managers looking for advanced or niche PM topics may find foundational certification training less relevant to their needs. Anyone not planning to pursue formal PM certification, and just looking for general project-coordination tips, may find a free resource more appropriate than a $49/month recurring cost.
