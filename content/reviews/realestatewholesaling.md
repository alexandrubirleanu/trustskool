# CLOSR Academy Review 2026: Is It Worth $297/Month?

CLOSR Academy is a wholesale real estate community explicitly built for people already doing deals, not beginners, and it charges accordingly: $297/month for a community of just over 1,029 members. It's run by JT Sudderth and is by far the most expensive community in this review batch, positioned around a specific guarantee: close 1-3 extra wholesale deals per month, or the membership isn't worth it ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/realestatewholesaling/about)).

## What's actually inside

The core offer is the CLOSR Sales Process, a scripted system for different lead types plus negotiation tactics, backed by a dispositions system aimed at getting signed contracts through to actual closing rather than stalling at the contract stage, which is where many wholesalers lose deals. Members get daily live coaching from active wholesalers on JT Sudderth's own team, a separate weekly live session with Sudderth himself, more than 10 hours of recorded course training, and practical tools: scripts, contracts, and deal calculators. The page states materials are valued at more than $5,000, and cites an average assignment fee of $29,788 to argue that closing even one additional deal covers the membership cost many times over.

## Who runs it

JT Sudderth states he has closed more than $10 million in off-market real estate deals and built multiple seven-figure companies before packaging that operating experience into the CLOSR/Wholesale CEO program ([Skool](https://www.skool.com/wholesaleceo/about)). He maintains an active YouTube channel and X account under his own name specifically focused on wholesaling and business content, giving outside parties a way to see his track record and communication style directly rather than relying only on the sales page ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@jtsudderth), [X](https://x.com/sudderth_jt)). A related program under the CLOSR/Whop ecosystem shows a 4.5-out-of-5 rating on at least one third-party review aggregator, which is a modestly positive but limited independent signal ([closr-wholesale-academy.tenereteam.com](https://closr-wholesale-academy.tenereteam.com/)).

## Pros

- Explicitly and honestly positioned as "not for beginners" and "not a get-rich-quick scheme," which is more transparent gatekeeping than communities that market to total newcomers with unrealistic promises.
- Daily live coaching plus a separate weekly session with the founder is an unusually high-touch structure for a Skool community.
- The stated average assignment fee ($29,788) and the "close 1-3 extra deals" framing give a concrete, testable benchmark for members to hold the community accountable to, rather than a vague promise.
- JT Sudderth has an active, checkable public presence beyond the Skool page itself.

## Cons

- At $297/month, this is easily the highest-priced community in this batch, and it isn't a fit for anyone still learning the fundamentals of wholesaling.
- The "guaranteed" 1-3 extra deals language is a strong claim; the page doesn't specify what happens if that guarantee isn't met (refund, extension, etc.), which is worth clarifying directly with the team before paying.
- With just over 1,000 members at this price point, the community is smaller than several cheaper alternatives in this niche, so peer density and shared deal-flow discussion may be thinner.

## Who it's a good fit for

Active wholesalers who are already closing some deals but want to add volume, tighten their sales scripts, and get more disposition support to convert signed contracts into closed assignments are the clear target. The price makes the most sense for someone already generating revenue from wholesaling who's optimizing an existing pipeline, not starting one from scratch.

## Who should skip it

Total beginners with zero deal experience should look at a lower-priced, beginner-oriented community first; CLOSR's own positioning agrees with this. Anyone not ready to commit to daily engagement with live coaching calls may not extract full value at this price.
