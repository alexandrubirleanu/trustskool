# PEAQ Review 2026: Is the $97/Month Men's Development Community Worth It?

PEAQ pitches itself with a bold one-liner: become the peak version of yourself. At $97/month, this Skool community has attracted 1,141 members around a broad self-improvement promise built on four pillars, wealth, strength, mind, and magnetism, aimed specifically at what the description calls ambitious men who refuse to be average.

## What's Actually Inside

The four named pillars give some structure to an otherwise broad positioning: wealth, likely financial or business-building content, strength, physical training, mind, mindset or mental performance, and magnetism, social skills, presence, or attraction-adjacent content. That's a wide-reaching mix, closer to a general men's self-improvement brand than a single-skill community, which means the depth on any one pillar is likely shallower than a community dedicated purely to, say, fitness or business. At 1,141 members and $97/month, PEAQ sits toward the premium end of general self-improvement communities, a price point that typically comes with more active coaching or content than a purely free discussion group.

## Pros

- Clear target audience of ambitious men and a memorable four-part framework, wealth, strength, mind, magnetism, that gives some shape to what could otherwise be a vague self-improvement pitch.
- Covers multiple life areas under one membership, which can suit someone who wants a single community rather than separate ones for fitness, business, and mindset.
- At $97/month, the price signals a more premium, ongoing commitment than a one-off course, which for some members indicates a more active or engaged group.
- Billed monthly rather than as an annual package, keeping the commitment flexible if only some of the four pillars end up feeling relevant.

## Cons

- No independent reviews or outside coverage were found beyond the Skool listing itself, so claims about outcomes across any of the four pillars can't be verified.
- Broad, multi-pillar communities like this one often run shallower on any single topic than a community built around one specific skill, something worth weighing against the $97/month price.
- The description doesn't clarify how content is split across the four pillars, so a member primarily interested in one area (say, wealth) can't confirm in advance how much of the monthly content will actually be relevant to them.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Men looking for a single, all-in-one self-improvement community covering finances, fitness, mindset, and social presence, rather than four separate specialized communities, are the intended audience here.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone wanting deep, specialized expertise in just one of the four pillars, such as pure fitness coaching or pure business mentorship, will likely get more focused value from a community built around that single topic.
