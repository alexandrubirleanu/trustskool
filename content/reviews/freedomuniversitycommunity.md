# Freedom University Review 2026: Can This Free Community Really Teach You Remote Sales Work?

Freedom University is a free, Italian-language Skool community with 1,077 members built around a specific promise: teaching people to work remotely as a Chatter, Setter or Closer, even with zero prior experience, and following them through to their first real earnings. That's a narrower, more concrete pitch than a generic "make money online" community, since Chatter, Setter and Closer are specific, named roles inside online sales funnels, typically appointment-setting and high-ticket closing work.

## What's Actually Inside

According to the community's own description, the path is designed for complete beginners and is structured to walk members from no experience through to landing their first paid results in one of three roles: chatting and engagement work, setting appointments, or closing sales. This suggests a role-based curriculum rather than a single generic "sales training" track, which matters for people who want to know upfront which specific skill they're being trained toward.

## Pros

- **Free entry point**, so anyone curious about remote sales roles can join without financial commitment.
- **Beginner-first framing.** The description explicitly targets people with no prior experience, rather than assuming existing sales background.
- **Named, specific roles.** Chatter, Setter and Closer are concrete job functions in the remote sales and appointment-setting world, giving prospective members a clearer idea of what they're training for compared to vaguer "digital marketing" communities.

## Cons

The description promises accompaniment "to first concrete earnings," but the listing itself doesn't specify timelines, success rates, or what proportion of members actually reach paid work. We also found no independent, third-party reviews of this specific community to verify outcomes beyond its own marketing description.

## Who It's a Good Fit For

Italian-speaking beginners with no sales or remote-work background who want a structured entry point into chatting, appointment-setting or closing roles, and who are comfortable learning inside a free community rather than a paid, more formal program.

## Who Should Skip It

Anyone already experienced in high-ticket sales or appointment setting is likely to find the beginner-level framing too basic. People wanting guaranteed income figures or a fully documented track record should treat the "first concrete earnings" promise as a goal the community is oriented toward, not a verified outcome.
