# Best Spirituality Communities on Skool in 2026

Skool's Spirituality category holds a wide range of practices: meditation and mindfulness groups, energy work and Reiki circles, astrology and tarot communities, manifestation and law-of-attraction groups, and faith-specific or interfaith study communities. These groups vary enormously in structure. Some are daily practice pods with guided sessions, others are looser discussion spaces around a teacher's philosophy or a specific book or tradition.

Because spirituality is personal and belief systems differ widely, it's worth being clear about what TrustSkore can and can't tell you. It doesn't evaluate whether a practice, teaching, or belief system is "right," that's not something a ranking system should judge. What it does measure is whether a community is genuinely active and growing (people are joining and staying, not just joining once and drifting away), and whether its pricing has been stable over time rather than shifting unpredictably.

For someone new to Skool, those two signals are still useful even in a category this subjective: sustained growth generally means the format, live sessions, structured courses, ongoing discussion, is actually working for the people in it, and stable pricing tends to correlate with a sustainably run community rather than one built around urgency or scarcity tactics. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What kinds of spirituality communities exist on Skool?
The category includes meditation and mindfulness practice groups, energy work and Reiki communities, astrology and tarot spaces, manifestation and law-of-attraction groups, and faith-based or interfaith study communities. Structure varies a lot: some run daily guided sessions, others are closer to ongoing discussion and Q&A around a teacher's material.

### Are spirituality communities on Skool tied to a specific religion?
Some are, many aren't. You'll find communities built around specific traditions as well as secular mindfulness or "universal" spirituality groups that intentionally avoid any single doctrine, so it's worth checking a community's description and pinned posts before joining to see which it is.

### How much should a spirituality community reasonably cost?
Costs vary widely, from free discussion groups to paid communities bundling live sessions, courses, or one-on-one guidance. A useful gut check is price stability over time: a community charging the same amount for months is more likely funding an ongoing practice than running a scarcity-driven promotion.

### Can you judge a spiritual community by member growth alone?
Growth tells you whether people are finding and staying in a community, which is a genuine signal of engagement, but it says nothing about whether a particular practice or teaching is right for you personally. Use growth and price stability as a filter for legitimacy and activity, then evaluate the actual content and fit yourself.
