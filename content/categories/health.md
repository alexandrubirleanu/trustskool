### Does TrustSkore evaluate whether health advice in a community is medically accurate?

### What types of health communities exist on Skool?
The category includes fitness coaching and training-program communities, nutrition and meal-planning groups, mental health peer-support spaces, biohacking and longevity groups, and recovery-focused communities around sleep, addiction, or chronic condition management. Structure ranges from daily check-in accountability groups to looser discussion-based communities.

### Are paid fitness communities on Skool better than a personal trainer?
They serve different needs: a paid community typically offers programming, peer accountability, and group coaching at a lower price point than one-on-one training, but without individualized correction of your form or a fully personalized plan. A community with steady, sustained member growth is a decent sign that its programming and accountability structure genuinely work for people over time.

### How do you know if a health community is active enough to be useful?
Check whether member growth looks sustained over recent weeks rather than spiked once around a launch and then flat, since ongoing growth usually means current members are getting enough value to stay and refer others. A community that's climbing in Skool's own internal rankings is also generally a sign of real, ongoing engagement rather than a one-time promotional push.

### Should mental health support communities be free?
Not necessarily, some paid communities fund moderation, structured programming, or licensed facilitators that a free peer group can't offer. That said, be cautious of any community, free or paid, positioning itself as a replacement for therapy or crisis support. Peer communities are a supplement, not a substitute, for professional mental health care.
