## Frequently Asked Questions

### What kinds of relationship communities exist on Skool?
The category includes dating and attraction coaching, marriage and long-term partnership communities, parenting and family communities, and communication-skills groups focused on conflict resolution, boundaries, or emotional intelligence. Some are coach-led with structured programs, others are closer to peer support groups organized around a shared life stage or challenge.

### Are relationship coaching communities on Skool a substitute for therapy or couples counseling?
No. They can offer useful peer support, structured exercises, or coaching perspective, but they're not a substitute for licensed therapy, couples counseling, or professional crisis support, especially where safety, abuse, or mental health concerns are involved. Treat community content as a supplement to, not a replacement for, professional help when the situation calls for it.

### How private are relationship communities, given the personal nature of the topic?
Privacy practices vary by community. Some run fully private, member-only spaces with strict rules against screenshotting or sharing outside the group, others are more open. Check a community's stated rules and moderation approach before sharing anything sensitive, and remember that TrustSkore measures growth and pricing behavior, not a community's privacy or moderation standards.

### Are paid relationship communities more effective than free ones?
Not automatically, but paid communities are more likely to include structured coaching, live Q&A, or personalized feedback that a free discussion group typically can't sustain. A useful check either way is whether the community shows steady member growth over time rather than a one-time spike, since people tend to leave quickly if the paid version isn't delivering real support beyond what a free group offers.
