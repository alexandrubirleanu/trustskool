# Skool's 2026 Discovery Overhaul: New Search, Keywords, and Trending Rankings Coming

In episode #52 of Skool News, the hosts lay out why Discovery is becoming Skool's biggest engineering focus for 2026, share some previously unpublished platform numbers behind that decision, and preview a stack of changes to search, rankings, and categories set to roll out over the following 90 days.

## Why Skool is rebuilding Discovery

The hosts open with a stat worth sitting with: roughly 30% of members platform-wide now come from the Skool network itself, not an owner's own marketing. Digging into that traffic turned up two numbers that surprised even the team. First, around a million people visit the Discovery page every day, despite Skool never actively promoting it (one host admits he personally forgets it exists). Second, most of that traffic is search, not browsing: about 70% is people searching within Discovery, roughly 10% is passive browsing, and the remaining 20% comes through user profiles. The hosts are candid that search results currently aren't very good, which is exactly why it's first in line for a rebuild.

## What's changing, and roughly when

- **Search**, the most-used part of Discovery, gets an algorithm rewrite starting in April 2026.
- **Owner-defined keywords**: group owners get up to 11 keywords (think "Facebook ads," "knitting," "photography") that feed the search algorithm, expected within about two weeks of the recording.
- **A new default ranking**: Discovery's homepage has always been static, showing the same big groups indefinitely. The plan is to make **trending** the new default view, changing frequently and giving smaller, currently-buzzing communities real visibility. The existing size-based "top" ranking stays available, it's just no longer the front door.
- **A reworked ranking algorithm**, targeted for May, meant to reward genuine community quality over raw engagement, since the current system can be gamed.
- **New categories**: Skool currently has around nine, set when the platform was four or five times smaller. AI reportedly deserves its own category separate from "Tech," and Real Estate its own slot separate from "Money." Changes are on hold until after the current Skool Games season ends.
- **More accurate attribution**, building on the traffic-sources dashboard already shipped, with further affiliate-link tracking work and less traffic misclassified as "direct." Targeted for May.
- **Notification and settings simplification**, an ongoing effort aimed at cutting notification overload for owners active in multiple communities.

## What this means if you run, or are evaluating, a Skool community

Fill in your keywords once that field ships, it's a direct, low-effort input into how the new search surfaces you. Don't over-index on your current numeric "rank," since the metric behind it is being replaced specifically because it's easy to game. If you're comparing communities on growth data, expect Discovery-driven traffic and attribution numbers to shift across April and May 2026 as these changes land, independent of what any individual community is doing differently.

## School of the week

Two contrasting proof points closed the episode. Sir Cobblot runs **Living Earth Structures**, a $5/month community teaching sustainable earthen hobbit houses, saunas, and ovens, earning almost $2,000/month mostly from Instagram with his Skool link in bio. Matt Separato runs **Miniature Painting School**, a free (freemium) community for painting miniatures like Warhammer figures, pulling in about $1,000/month almost entirely from a YouTube link in bio. Both run the same playbook: make content, link in bio, let the about page convert. The episode also teased, unnamed, a community pulling in close to $600,000/month, saved for the following week.
