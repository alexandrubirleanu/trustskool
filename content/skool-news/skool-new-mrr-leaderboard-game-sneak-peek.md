# Skool's New Game: A Live MRR Leaderboard With Status Badges (Sneak Peek)

Skool News #64, hosted by Skool co-founder/CEO Sam Ovens and Andrew Kirby, gives an early, "not live yet" look at a rebuilt Skool Game, then runs through a short roadmap and two in-progress updates: Growth Boost's efficiency score and a simplified accounting view.

## A first look at the new Skool Game

Rather than talk through the change, the hosts screen-share the actual build inside the Skoolers community. The new leaderboard page reads "Grow your MRR to level up," with a line underneath explaining the mechanic: "Get respect with a status emoji next to your name. Hit $100k MRR to go Platinum + join a secret club."

Above the ranking table sits a row of milestone badges the hosts talk through on screen: a clover at $3K, a rocket at $10K, a crown at $30K, a diamond at $100K ("You know, you go platinum, we're calling it going platinum"), a red diamond at $300K, and a top badge at $1M. The table itself ranks users by rank, name, group, category (Money, Hobbies, Music, Spirituality, Tech, Health, Sports all appear in the demo), traffic, 30-day change, and current MRR, with a note that even creators not near the top of the board still get their own status emoji and progress tracker rather than being invisible below the fold.

It's a real change in shape, not just a lick of paint. The old Skool Games ran as a quarterly, per-category contest that culminated in flying top-five finishers out to visit Skool's HQ as a group celebration, and at least one longtime community owner in the comments flagged that trade-off directly, saying the shift takes away some of the shared, in-person thrill of the old format. What replaces it here is a standing, individual leaderboard tied to MRR growth rather than a seasonal bracket, so status is something you build continuously instead of winning once a quarter.

## Roadmap: freemium signup, locked trial content, faster payouts, more languages

The hosts move to a shorter roadmap segment covering four items still in progress: showing paid plans immediately at signup for freemium communities, locking specific courses or lessons from free-trial members, faster payouts, and additional language support. On the freemium point specifically, one host frames the goal as something that will "dramatically improve the efficiency of premium groups," tying the change back to how Skool scores communities for Growth Boost and internal ad placement.

## Growth Boost efficiency score: still a work in progress

The chapter titled "Growth boost update" is framed as exactly that, an update rather than a finished feature: the hosts say the underlying efficiency score is still being tuned, with a clearer breakdown of why a given community's score looks the way it does slated to arrive later rather than in this episode.

## A quicker look at accounting, and two Skool of the Week picks

A shorter segment covers changes meant to make a community's financials easier to read at a glance inside Skool, without a deep line-by-line walkthrough in this episode. The show closes with three Skool of the Week shoutouts: Sailors Connect, The Wash Game, and the Rich Habits podcast community, all of which show up in the comments thanking the hosts for the mention.

## Why this matters if you run or are evaluating a Skool community

None of this is live yet as of this episode, but the direction is worth tracking regardless of where your community currently sits. If status and visibility inside Skool's own ecosystem move from a quarterly contest to a standing, revenue-based leaderboard, consistent MRR growth becomes the thing worth optimizing for over a single good quarter. And if freemium signup and efficiency scoring really are getting rebalanced, communities running a free tier today may be worth re-evaluating once that rolls out.
