# Skool HQ Office Tour: A Look Inside the Team Behind Skool.com

Skool News #51 breaks from its usual format of covering platform updates and instead gives viewers a walkthrough of Skool's physical headquarters, led by the Skool News host and co-founder Sam Ovens. There are no new features or product changes in this episode, but it offers a rare look at the small team and culture behind the platform that hundreds of thousands of community owners rely on.

## What the tour covers

The walkthrough moves through the office room by room: a ball pit room (the hosts joke that people assume it's a kids' play area, insisting "it's not, it's a tech company"), several meeting rooms, and a wall of Polaroid photos taken of everyone who visits the office. There's a "podcast room" used mainly for executive meetings rather than an actual podcast, a merch fulfillment area where the team ships embroidered products (including a cat mascot named Johnny) ordered through schoolmerch.com, and a kitchen stocked with an exhaustive LaCroix selection.

Outside, there's a school bus Sam bought for $1,000 on eBay and paid another $500 to have driven in from North Dakota, parked next to the actual set where Skool News itself is filmed, complete with the "behind the scenes" camera and lighting gear the team uses to produce the show. The tour ends in the working area, where desks belong to the people actually running day-to-day operations.

## Who's behind the platform

For anyone who only knows Skool as a website, the tour puts a few names and faces to the operation. Mark is named as the Customer Success Manager who helps creators launch their communities on Skool. Two people described as "executive Claire" and "executive Erica" are called out as the ones key decisions route through. Sam Ovens, Skool's co-founder, appears throughout, reportedly commuting to the office by motorbike.

It's a small, informal-feeling team running a platform with real scale, and that context is worth having if you're evaluating Skool as a company to build a community on, not just a piece of software. A platform's roadmap, support responsiveness, and stability all trace back to the size and structure of the team building it, and this episode is the closest thing to an org chart Skool has published publicly.

## Why this episode matters even without a feature update

Most Skool News episodes exist to explain a shipped change: a new discovery algorithm, an attribution system, a live-call feature. This one is different: it's a culture and brand episode, not a product one. That distinction matters if you're using Skool News as a way to track "what's new" on the platform. Skip #51 if you're purely hunting for feature changes; but it's worth watching if you want a sense of who is actually building the tools your community runs on, and how seriously (or not) they take themselves while doing it. The running joke throughout the episode, that Skool's rapid growth and Sam's good looks both come down to a bottle of "aura mist," gives a fair sense of the tone: confident, self-aware, and unmistakably a startup rather than a faceless SaaS vendor.

No new features, pricing changes, or discovery/ranking updates were announced in this episode, so nothing here changes how communities should be run or evaluated. Treat it as background context on the company, not a changelog entry.
