# 11 Uniquely Profitable Skool Communities (And a First Look at Skool's New Search)

Episode 54 of Skool News recaps the quarterly Skool Games event in LA, name-checks 11 real Skool communities the hosts met there with their actual monthly revenue, and closes with a live demo of a rebuilt search feature the team says is already Skool's single biggest source of new members, even in its current, admittedly rough state.

## The event: 40 winners, flown to LA, every 90 days

Skool Games is a recurring, all-expenses-paid event: roughly 40 community owners qualify each quarter and fly to Los Angeles to meet the Skool team and Alex Hormozi in person. The format is deliberately light on lectures and heavy on hanging out, plus a red-carpet photo op and an award. It's how the hosts collect most of the community profiles below, and a recording of the full event was promised for the following week.

## 11 niches making real money

The revenue figures are self-reported on camera, not independently verified, but they're specific enough to be a useful gut-check on what actually sells on Skool:

- **The Photo Project** (school.com/photo) - photography community, about $30,000/month.
- **Grateful Mike** - teaches guitar to Grateful Dead fans specifically, about $4,000/month.
- **AI Video Bootcamp** - the standout: $133,000/month, with the founder getting a sales notification every 30-60 seconds.
- **Club Row** (Gillian) - about $18,000/month.
- **An identity-shifting community**, run in Mandarin, about $12,000/month.
- **Manifestation Mastery** (school.com/manifestation) - about $4,000/month.
- **Holistic gut health** (Australia-based) - about $22,000-$23,000/month.
- **A career-development community** - no revenue figure given on air.
- **A jujitsu community** - about $7,600/month.

That's nine communities with enough detail to name here; the hosts flagged a couple more attendees on camera without catching the specifics, consistent with the episode's "11" framing.

## The pattern: niche is not a ceiling

None of these are in the usual "make money online" lane. A guitar community for one band's fanbase, a jujitsu group, and gut-health coaching sit next to a $133k/month AI tool community, and none of them needed a broad or trendy niche to work. The through-line the hosts push is that a specific audience with a real problem beats a bigger, vaguer one, AI Video Bootcamp's founder mentions getting a new paying member roughly every 30 to 60 seconds at the low end of its pricing, which is as much a pricing and offer story as a niche story.

## The bigger news: search is getting rebuilt

Past the recap, the hosts share a roadmap update and a live before/after demo of Skool's new search, still unreleased at time of publishing but slated for "the start of May." Two demos stand out. First, searching a well-known name in the current search surfaced an unrelated, non-English group instead of the right one; the new search returned the correct match immediately. Second, a deliberate typo ("meat ads" instead of "meta ads") returned literal meat-related results in the current search, while the new version correctly inferred the intended query, an explicit typo-tolerance improvement.

The stat worth remembering: even with search in its current, unfixed state, it's already sending Skool more new community members than Discovery browsing and profile visits combined. The hosts frame better search and a coming trending-based Discovery redesign as the platform's top growth priority for the rest of the year, alongside smaller fixes to notifications, account settings, and eventual language and currency support.

## Why it matters if you run or are evaluating a Skool community

If you're picking a community to join, the niche list is a reminder that self-reported MRR screenshots vary wildly in specificity and are worth treating as anecdotes, not proof. If you run one, the search overhaul is the more actionable item: it's about to become an even bigger discovery channel than it already is, which raises the value of a clear, keyword-matchable community name and about page ahead of the rollout.
