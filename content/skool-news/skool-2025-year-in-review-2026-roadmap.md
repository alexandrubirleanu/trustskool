# Skool's 2025 Year in Review: 20 Million Users, Half the Churn, and the 2026 Roadmap

In place of a normal news roundup, the Skool News hosts used their Christmas episode to walk through Skool's actual 2025 growth numbers, recap every major feature shipped this year, and preview the 2026 build list. It's the closest thing to an official state-of-the-platform address Skool has put out.

## 2025 by the numbers

The headline stats: Skool is now at 20 million users and adding more than a million a month, with 200,000 communities on the platform and over $1 billion in creator earnings to date. Two numbers matter more than the top-line growth, though. First, 30% of all new memberships now come from the Skool network itself, meaning for an average group of 100 members, roughly 30 arrived without the owner running an ad or posting content, just from Skool's own discovery, search, and suggested-communities surfacing them. Second, platform-wide churn has been cut roughly in half this year, from just over 20% at the start of 2025 down to about 15% now, a mix of platform-level changes and owners optimizing retention on their own communities.

On the earnings side, the community "Evolve" became the first group confirmed at $500K/month in revenue and is now reportedly past $540K/month. The hosts also called out what they consider the best community post of the year: a breakdown from a creator running a $300K/month one-person community at a 95% profit margin, which pulled in roughly 4,500 likes and 5,500 comments.

## The niches keep getting stranger, and that's the point

One recurring theme was just how varied Skool communities have become: a donkey hoof-trimming course, a birdwatching meetup group, a shipping-container-living community, a pig-farming group, and a pro skier who requested (and got) a frame-by-frame video scrubbing feature for coaching students on their technique. The hosts framed this breadth, alongside members reporting they've replaced doomscrolling on other apps with time spent on Skool, as evidence the platform is increasingly functioning as a social-media substitute rather than just a course-hosting tool.

## Every major feature that shipped in 2025

The recap listed out the year's product releases in one place: the School Games moved from monthly to quarterly; the separate "Skool Games" and "Skool Community" groups merged into Skoolers; a free 9-module Skool 101 course launched; one-click affiliates went live so members can earn by referring new members; a member location map was added; comment pinning shipped with an email ping to the pinned member; native video hosting arrived (free, unlimited, with chapters, replacing the old workaround of hosting on Vimeo); native live calls and webinars followed, supporting up to 10,000 attendees and turning a finished call directly into a post with one click; a $9/month Hobby plan launched alongside the existing $99 plan, bringing suggested-communities placement, Apple Pay, and Google Pay with it; membership tiers were added; and a full analytics dashboard shipped covering MRR, cohorts, retention, unit economics, and conversion rates.

## What's coming in 2026

Four priorities were named for next year. Traffic-source reporting is first: owners will see exactly which channel (Instagram, TikTok, ads, or the Skool network itself) brought in each member, down to individual member records. Discovery is getting a major overhaul, since the team says it's had almost no direct investment despite already driving 30% of memberships, with better search ranking and new sorting options like trending or newest planned. A risk-score and auto-moderation system is coming for free communities, using AI to flag risky membership requests for auto-approval, auto-decline, or manual review, alongside an optional SMS verification toggle for posting and commenting once a group gets large. Notifications are getting more granular filtering controls. And internationalization is on the roadmap for the first time, with language and currency localization planned in response to surprisingly strong demand from Latin America and parts of Asia.
