# Skool Growth Boost Explained: How Skool's New Paid Ad Engine Grows Your Community

Skool News #60 covers the public rollout of Growth Boost, a new feature that turns Skool itself into a paid growth channel for community owners, and it changes how "organic" community growth on the platform actually works.

## What Growth Boost actually does

Growth Boost has two parts. First, it pushes eligible groups higher in Skool's internal search results for the keywords their ideal members are already searching. Second, and the bigger shift, is that Skool now runs off-site paid ads, on Meta/Facebook, to promote your community to new people. Skool covers the ad spend itself. As one Skool community post summarizing the feature put it plainly: "Skool pays for the ads. Meta decides who gets shown." That second half matters. Skool is fronting the media buy, but it hands targeting decisions to Meta's ad algorithm, so which prospects actually see your community isn't something an owner controls directly.

## The pricing model is performance-based, not pay-to-play

This is the detail community owners seem most focused on. Growth Boost isn't a flat subscription or an upfront ad budget you commit to. Skool only takes a cut when the ad it ran actually converts into a paying member. Reporting on the mechanics puts the commission at 30% of that member's payment, on top of normal processing fees. So if your community is priced at $10/month and Skool's ad brings in a payer, Skool deducts roughly $3 plus fees from that transaction. If a new member joins through any channel Skool didn't pay for, that 30% commission does not apply. You still only pay the standard payment processing fee.

In effect, Skool is betting its own ad dollars on your community's ability to convert, and only gets paid if that bet works out. For an owner with a decent conversion funnel and a sellable price point, that's close to free customer acquisition. For an owner with a weak sales page or an unclear offer, it just means Skool's ad spend converts poorly and nothing is lost on the owner's side beyond the usual processing fee.

## It's on by default, and there's a free trial window

According to the rollout details discussed around this announcement, Growth Boost ships enabled by default for eligible paid communities, and owners can turn it off at any time from Settings > Discovery. Skool also appears to have quietly tested the feature with select communities for several weeks before this public "LIVE" announcement, meaning some owners had real results to react to immediately. In community discussion threads tied to this launch, owners running small paid groups (in one case a $9/month community) mentioned using a free trial window to test Growth Boost risk-free before deciding whether to keep it on.

## Why this matters if you're evaluating a Skool community

If you're a prospective member browsing communities, Growth Boost is worth knowing about because it changes what "this community is growing fast" might actually mean. A spike in new members could reflect genuine demand, or it could reflect Skool's own paid Meta ads sending in traffic that converted. Neither is necessarily good or bad for you as a member, but it's a variable in how communities now acquire people, and it's the kind of shift that raw member counts alone won't tell you about.
