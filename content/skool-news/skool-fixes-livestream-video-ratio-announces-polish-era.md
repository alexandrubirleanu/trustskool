# Skool Fixes Its Live Stream Video Size and Announces a 90-Day "Polish" Push (Skool News #37)

Episode #37 opens with photos from the in-person Q3 Skool Games, then moves into the one feature that actually shipped: live streams and call recordings now fill a proper 16:9 frame instead of sitting in a cramped square with black bars on either side. From there, the hosts lay out what's next: rather than another new feature, Skool is dedicating the coming months to cleaning up everything it shipped in 2025.

## Q3 Skool Games recap

The Q3 Games wrapped with an in-person meetup. Standouts included a Fortnite community, a mindset community, and pro skateboarder Mitchie Brusco's "Skate IQ" community, which teaches tricks like ollies and kickflips. (Brusco holds the world record for the first-ever 1260 landed on a skateboard, still unbeaten.) Nate Herk gets called out for winning three quarters running and going for a fourth. Winners got an all-expenses-paid trip to hang out with Skool's team and trade notes on what's working.

The Q4 Games are live now on the Skoolers leaderboard, closing December 19 at 10am. Top five finishers per category win the same trip; Q3 event recordings are being edited for release soon.

## The fix: no more tiny square video

Previously, live calls and webinars rendered as a small square, wasting most of the frame as black space in both the live room and the recording. Now the video is full 16:9, whether it's a single host, two people on camera, or a shared screen, and the recording matches what viewers saw live. Hosts no longer need to export a call into a separate video editor to make it presentable before uploading. For anyone running paid coaching calls, webinars, or replay libraries inside a Skool community, this closes a real gap between "we have live calls" and "our live calls actually look professional."

## Roadmap: Skool's "polish" era begins

The hosts frame 2025 as a run of major features shipped back to back: native video, live streaming, webinars, tiers, and Analytics v1. Rather than add another headline feature next, the team says it's entering a deliberate "polish" phase, roughly 90 days of cleaning up rough edges, fixing unintuitive flows, and speeding things up before building anything new. Analytics v2, focused on attribution (seeing exactly where members and paying customers come from, including how many arrive via the Skool Network), is confirmed for early 2026, after the polish work.

## What "polish" actually means in practice

A few concrete examples came up:

- **Notifications**, not live yet: one-click mute of "likes" notifications across every group at once, per-group notification tuning (off, never, or custom), and filters so a busy notification feed can be sorted by membership requests, new posts, or recordings and bulk-marked as read. Report notifications specifically will become a toggle admins can turn off entirely if moderators already handle them, rather than a filter.
- **Accidental course deletion**, already shipped: deleting a course now requires typing the course's name to confirm, and the delete option has been removed from inside the course itself.
- **Course module editing**, planned: right now you can duplicate an entire course or copy it to another group, but you can't move a single module between courses. That's coming.

The hosts are explicit that this isn't a marketing-friendly list of shiny new things. It's small, unglamorous fixes to features that already exist, but ones they argue matter more to daily usage than another big launch would.

## Why this matters if you're evaluating Skool communities

If you run or join a Skool community, the live stream fix is worth noting right away, especially if you sell replay access or run webinars as part of your offer. The bigger signal is the roadmap shift itself: after a year of rapid releases, Skool is explicitly pausing to fix rough edges rather than piling on more. For community owners, that's a good sign, a platform investing in reliability and usability tends to be a safer long-term bet than one that only ever ships new logos for the changelog.
