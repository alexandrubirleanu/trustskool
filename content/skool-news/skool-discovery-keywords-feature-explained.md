# Skool's Discovery Keywords Feature Is Live: How It Works (and Why It Doesn't Change Rankings Yet)

In episode #53 of Skool News, the hosts confirm that Discovery keywords, previewed the week before as an upcoming feature, have officially shipped. They walk through how to set them, why they won't actually move your ranking yet, and close with a "Skool of the week" segment profiling four communities at very different revenue levels.

## The feature: up to 11 keywords per community

Every group can now go to **Group Settings → Discovery** and find a new field: "Set keywords to help people find your community." Owners can enter up to 11 keywords or phrases, typing each one followed by a comma. It sits right next to the existing Discovery status panel, which already shows whether a group is showing in Discovery, its category, its numeric rank, and its detected language.

The idea is straightforward: instead of Skool guessing what a community is about from its name and about page alone, owners now get a direct input into how their group gets matched to what people type into Discovery's search box.

## The catch: they don't do anything yet

The most important line from the episode is also the easiest to miss: **keywords will not impact search results until Skool ships the new search.** In other words, this update ships the input field before it ships the algorithm that reads it. Setting keywords today doesn't move your rank or change your visibility this week, it queues you up for whenever the rebuilt Discovery search goes live.

That's consistent with the roadmap Skool laid out the week before in episode #52, where keywords were described as arriving about two weeks ahead of the actual search algorithm rewrite. If you run a Skool community, the practical move is to fill the field in now anyway. It costs nothing, and it means you're not scrambling to backfill it the day search suddenly starts using it.

## How to actually pick good keywords

The hosts' guidance for choosing keywords boils down to three questions:

- What topics is your group actually about?
- What would your ideal member type into a search bar to find something like you?
- Would an irrelevant or stuffed keyword help, or just dilute your relevance?

They walk through a real example, a community called **Grow With Evelyn**, to show what a sensible keyword set looks like: specific enough to match real searches, not a grab-bag of unrelated buzzwords. The warning attached to that last point is explicit: "putting irrelevant keywords in will only harm your results" once the new search is live. Skool is signaling, without spelling out the mechanism, that stuffing won't be free once ranking is on the line.

## What's still coming

The episode closes with a short roadmap update pointing to three things still in motion: the Discovery search rebuild itself, changes to the discovery ranking algorithm, and new categories (Skool's current category list was set when the platform was a fraction of its current size). Keywords are the first visible piece of a larger Discovery rebuild, not the whole project.

## Skool of the week

Four communities closed out the episode, each on a different growth channel:

- **Logan Sendle**, Blue-Collar Biz Accelerator, **$554,586/month**, driven primarily by paid ads.
- **Andy Friedman**, Comedy Society, **$6,742/month**, driven primarily by Facebook.
- **Zoltan Szabo**, Exotic Veterinary Academy, **$26,412/month**, driven primarily by Instagram.
- **Joshua Laborde**, Tuning & Reverse Engineering, **$11,000/month**, driven primarily by YouTube.

The spread is the point: a six-figure-a-month blue-collar training community and a five-figure niche automotive one are both running on Skool, using completely different acquisition channels, well before Discovery search even starts using the keywords owners are being asked to fill in today.

If you're running or evaluating a Skool community, the takeaway is simple: fill in your 11 keywords now, treat it as free positioning homework rather than a ranking lever, and expect the actual search and ranking impact to land once Skool ships the rebuilt Discovery search it's been previewing since episode #52.
