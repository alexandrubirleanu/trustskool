# Skool Adds Granular Notification Filters: Inside the St. Patrick's Day AMA (Skool News #49)

Episode #49 of Skool News trades the usual single-topic format for a green-hat AMA: a short feature update on notifications, one "Skool of the week" spotlight, and then a run of viewer questions fielded live by the hosts about where Skool and its founder are headed.

## The new feature: notification tuning, community by community

The episode opens with a walkthrough of Skool's notification settings page, and the headline change is control. Instead of one blanket setting for every community you belong to, owners and members can now set notifications per group, choosing "Custom" on communities like the ones shown on screen and leaving platform defaults everywhere else. Layered on top of that are two cadence controls that apply account-wide: a digest email you can set to Daily, Weekly, Monthly, or Never, and a separate notifications email with Hourly, Daily, Weekly, or Never options. There's also a notification filter dropdown that narrows what you're looking at in the first place: All, Mentions, Comments, Following, Requests, New posts, or Recordings, so a busy inbox doesn't bury the one comment you actually needed to see.

The hosts frame it as a fix for anyone running or belonging to several active communities at once. On screen, one of them puts it bluntly: without tuning these settings, notifications get out of control fast. For anyone managing more than one Skool group, or a single group with a genuinely active comment section, this is worth revisiting even if you set your notifications once and never touched them again.

## Skool of the week: $5,000/month from Japanese tattoo art

The spotlight segment goes to Ron, whose community is built around Japanese tattoo artistry and pulls in roughly $5,000 a month. It's a reminder of how narrow a niche can still support a real, recurring business on Skool, a theme this channel returns to often: pairing headline seven-figure outliers with much smaller, sustainably profitable niches in the same breath.

## AMA: Sam Ovens on scale, values, and what's next

The rest of the episode is a straight Q&A pulled from community-submitted questions, and a few answers stand out for anyone building on the platform long-term.

- **The path to 1 billion users.** Asked directly how Skool intends to get there, the hosts talk through the scale ambition behind the platform rather than naming a specific feature or date, framing growth as a function of the wider creator-and-community economy Skool is building around.
- **Company values.** A question on what Skool actually stands for gets a direct answer from the team, positioned as the operating logic behind product decisions rather than a wall poster.
- **Sam's favorite framework.** Founder Sam Ovens shares the mental model he leans on most when making decisions, worth watching in full if you follow how Ovens thinks about building and running businesses.
- **Voice messages.** A member asks whether Skool will support voice messages in chat or posts. The hosts address it as a real request on their radar rather than something shipping this episode.
- **Languages and currencies.** With Skool's user base increasingly global, the hosts field questions on multi-language and multi-currency support, relevant to anyone running a community with members or pricing outside the US.
- **Accounting for Skool creators.** The episode closes on practical ground: tips for handling the bookkeeping side of a paid Skool community, from tracking recurring revenue to staying organized for tax season.

## Why this matters if you're evaluating Skool communities

None of this is a platform overhaul. It's a maintenance episode: one genuine quality-of-life feature (notification filters) plus a batch of forward-looking answers about where the platform and its founder are focused next. If you belong to several Skool communities, the notification settings alone are worth five minutes to revisit. And if you're sizing up which communities on Skool are worth joining or running, the signals here, global pricing support, finer-grained admin controls, a values-driven product roadmap, point at a platform still investing in the infrastructure serious communities need.
