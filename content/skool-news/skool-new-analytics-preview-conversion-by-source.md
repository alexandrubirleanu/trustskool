# Skool's New Analytics Preview Shows Conversion Rates by Traffic Source

Episode 29 of Skool News is a sneak peek at Skool's in-progress analytics rebuild (traffic attribution, conversion rate by channel), a clarification on how the Skool Games' "$100 price limit" actually works ahead of the Q4 2025 season starting October 1st, and a tour of standout Q3 communities, including a witch-themed group pulling in $23k/month.

## A first look at attribution: where members actually come from

The hosts show an early, unreleased build of Skool's improved analytics, still being fine-tuned alongside subscription tiers and webinars. The eventual dashboard will surface MRR, churn rate, conversion rate, and a full breakdown of where traffic and members come from: YouTube, the Skool Network, direct, referral (member affiliates), and Google search, both at the group level and for each individual traffic source.

The real-world examples are the interesting part. One anonymized group gets roughly equal traffic from YouTube and the Skool Network, but YouTube converts at just 1.22% versus 3% from the Skool Network, meaning the network sends half the visitors but produces the same number of members because it converts more than double. A second group making $11k/month gets 18% of its traffic from the Skool Network but sees referrals (member affiliates) as its dominant source, converting at 17%, dramatically outperforming cold traffic. A third group at $50k/month gets 62% of its traffic from the Skool Network and converts new visitors at 40%.

The hosts note that most communities still haven't adopted the affiliate feature (shipped around February 2025) beyond turning it on, typically seeing 5-10% of members from referrals. The outlier groups pulling 70%+ from referrals actively train their affiliates: a course in the classroom plus a weekly call reviewing what top affiliates are doing. The dashboard will also show day-by-day stacked traffic charts and separate paid ads from organic, revealing that ad spend appears to lift organic and referral growth too, "one source lifts all the others." No ship date was given, but the hosts call it the biggest analytics change to date.

## Skool Games Q4 starts October 1: the $100 "limit" is really a handicap

Every Skool community is automatically entered in the Skool Games, no signup needed, you just won't appear on leaderboards without enough qualifying revenue. The hosts flag one persistent misunderstanding: the "$100/month price limit" isn't a price cap. You can charge $200, $1,000, or more, but only the first $100 of your price counts toward your Skool Games score, functioning more like a golf handicap than a restriction. Scoring itself is based on new MRR growth over the quarter, not total revenue, which is why repeat winners are notable: you have to keep adding growth every single quarter to keep winning.

## Break-out communities from Q3 2025

The hosts highlight a few standout groups from the quarter. Skate IQ ($49/month, ~$21.5k MRR) ran a collab with Tony Hawk during its launch. Forbidden Knowledge by Billy Carson hit $61k/month after Carson appeared on a Joe Rogan episode with 6.3 million views. AI Automation Society Plus, run by Nate, sits at $22,500/month and is chasing a fourth consecutive Skool Games win. Digital Circle, a French-language AI community run by Anthony Sirius, added a reported $311,000 in new MRR over 90 days, the largest new-MRR figure the hosts say they've seen in a single Skool Games quarter. And the group behind the video's title, a witch-themed community run by "Mickey," makes roughly $23k/month teaching people how to practice witchcraft.

Smaller notes from the episode: the team is debating adding a cat plush to the Skool merch store, and the "Goose World Tour," a member-run series of in-person Skool meetups now sponsored by Skool, is wrapping its world tour with a final stop in Antarctica, with tickets opening the same day this episode aired.
