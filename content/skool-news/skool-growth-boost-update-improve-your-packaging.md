# Skool Growth Boost Update: Why It Works for Some Communities and Not Others

Skool News #62 is a short, practical episode built around one question the hosts say they get constantly: is Growth Boost actually working? The answer, plus how to audit your own community's "packaging" while Skool keeps tuning the feature, and a quick rundown of the final Skool Games leaderboard season, makes up the whole episode.

## "Is it working?" depends entirely on which group you ask

The hosts are blunt that Growth Boost, Skool's automated discovery-and-ads growth feature, is only about a week old at the time of this episode and is still very much an experiment. As one host puts it, asking whether it's working "globally" is "kind of like asking if it's raining... it is in some places and it isn't in others." Some communities are seeing a real lift in paying members; others are seeing close to nothing.

The team's own visibility into this is still limited. They estimate they'll have meaningfully better internal data in one to two weeks, but expect actual optimization, getting the feature reliably "dialed in", to take at least a month, probably longer. In the meantime, they're testing different Discovery and ad variants against traffic splits (their example: 20% of users might see one version, another 20% a different one), so what your own Discovery page looks like may shift without warning.

Skool also showed a rough mockup of a future indicator that would tell owners where their group sits on an efficiency scale, poor, average, good, or excellent, since more efficient groups get sent disproportionately more of the traffic Growth Boost distributes. One caution worth flagging for owners: the hosts said groups already in the "excellent" range have hurt themselves by changing their packaging mid-stream, seeing Growth Boost stop working after "changing everything." If it's working for you right now, that's a reason to be careful about what you touch.

## The best visibility available today: filter Discovery yourself

Until that indicator ships, the hosts' actual advice is manual: go to Skool Discovery, filter for paid groups and your own language, and browse. That removes noise and shows what's genuinely converting right now, patterns the hosts note are consistently lower-ticket with a clearly stated offer. Trending alone skews toward AI and make-money groups, but clicking into hobby categories surfaces plenty of working paid communities in niches like Counter-Strike coaching, painting, and chess.

## Packaging is the lever Skool says matters most

Regardless of where Growth Boost's algorithm lands, the hosts argue none of it works if a group's "packaging" (cover image, group name and description, and about page) isn't good. Two resources were shared for this: Canva cover-image templates from Sal Frances's Template School, and a custom-GPT "about page" tool built on Kirby's NOMAD framework (a clear niche, outcome, mechanism, authority/proof, and deliverables). They also pointed to a Skoolers community post where a member analyzed the top 100 trending about pages on Discovery and broke down the common anatomy of a winning one. The analogy used: bad cover photos on an otherwise-great Airbnb listing kill trust before anyone reads the description, and the same applies to a community's card image on Discovery.

## Games ending, new game teased, and this week's picks

The current Skool Games leaderboard season closes June 28 at 10am Pacific, its last run in the current format, with a new (unspecified) version teased for after. The "Skool of the week" segment highlighted World of Puppets ($1/day, wooden puppet-making, roughly $1,300/month), Tantra Nectar (free, 29,000 members), Start an Ice Cream Shop ($4,000/month at the premium tier), and Angel Archetype Society (a $24,000/month meditation and ritual community).

Given how openly Skool admits Growth Boost's results are uneven and still being tuned, member counts or a single visit to Discovery won't tell you which communities are genuinely compounding versus catching a temporary algorithmic push.
