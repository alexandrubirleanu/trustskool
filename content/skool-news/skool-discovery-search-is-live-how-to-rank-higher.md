# Skool's Rebuilt Discovery Search Is Now Live: What Changed and How to Rank Higher

Episode #55 of Skool News confirms that the rebuilt Discovery search, previewed as a rough demo two episodes earlier and promised for "the start of May," has officially shipped. The episode also walks through what actually moves your Discovery rank today, a smaller update to notification controls for members and admins, and closes with two freemium communities breaking down their real monthly numbers.

## The search rebuild is live, typos and all

Episode #54 showed an early, admittedly unpolished before/after of the new search: type a well-known name into the old search and it could surface an unrelated, non-English group; type a deliberate typo like "meat ads" instead of "meta ads" and the old search returned literal meat results. Episode #55 picks up right where that left off, with a live, working version of the same demo. Typing "meta" into the search box now correctly returns Meta-ads-related communities instead of getting derailed by the typo, and the hosts call out the improved typo tolerance by name as one of the clearest upgrades over the old system. It's the same fix Skool had been signaling since its original Discovery roadmap episode back in April, now actually running in production rather than in a preview clip.

## What actually moves your rank now

With search rebuilt, the episode turns to a practical question: what should a community owner actually do to rank higher? The answer, per the hosts, comes down to three things working together: your About page, your community description, and your Discovery keywords. Keywords themselves shipped a few episodes earlier and, at the time, explicitly did nothing to search results yet, they were positioned as homework to do ahead of the algorithm that would eventually read them. This episode is effectively that moment: with the new search live, a clear, keyword-matchable About page and description are no longer just good practice, they're the input the ranking engine is actually reading from. Owners who filled in their 11 keywords back in April are the ones best positioned to benefit immediately.

## A smaller ship: notification controls get easier to manage

Alongside the search news, the episode covers a notification settings update that lets both members and admins change their notification preferences more easily, rather than digging through nested menus. It's a quality-of-life fix rather than a growth feature, but it's the kind of update that tends to reduce the "why am I getting pinged for everything" complaints that make members mute a community entirely, which quietly protects engagement, one of the same signals Skool's ranking system weighs.

## Why it matters: two freemium communities, two different numbers

The episode closes with two real creator profiles built on Skool's freemium model, a free tier that funnels into a paid one. Jake runs a freemium AI community earning roughly $30,000 a month, and Brehan runs a freemium Gut Health community earning $19,345 a month. Both are useful case studies precisely because they depend on being found: a freemium funnel only works if enough of the right people discover the free tier in the first place, which is exactly the mechanism this episode's search and ranking updates are meant to improve.

## The takeaway

If you run a Skool community, the sequence to act on is straightforward: make sure your About page and description actually describe what your community does in the words a real searcher would use, confirm your 11 keywords are filled in, and check your notification settings now that they're easier to adjust. If you're evaluating communities to join, this episode is a reminder that Discovery search is actively improving, so what you find on the platform today should get more relevant, not less, as Skool keeps shipping against this roadmap.
